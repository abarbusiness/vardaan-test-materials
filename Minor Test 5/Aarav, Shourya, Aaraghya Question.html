Minor Test 05
Class: 7 & 8 Subject: English Grammar Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 50
Topics: Noun, Articles, Pronoun, The Sentence
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly. Number your answers correctly.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Objective/Short Answer - 2 Marks Each)
1.
(The Sentence) Identify the Subject and the Predicate in the following sentence:
"Under the old banyan tree sat a wise monk."
2.
(Noun) Pick out the nouns in the following sentence and state their kind (Common, Proper, Collective, or Abstract):
"The jury was impressed by the honesty of the young boy."
3.
(Pronoun) Fill in the blanks with suitable Relative Pronouns:
(a) The book ________ you lent me is very interesting.
(b) I met the woman ________ daughter won the singing competition.
4.
(Articles) Fill in the blanks with 'a', 'an', or 'the'. Put a cross (X) where no article is required:
(a) He is ________ honest man who always speaks ________ truth.
(b) Gold is ________ precious metal, but ________ iron is very useful.
5.
(The Sentence) Identify the kind of sentence (Assertive, Interrogative, Imperative, Exclamatory):
(a) How gracefully the dancer performed on stage!
(b) Please pass me the dictionary placed on the top shelf.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer/Application - 3 Marks Each)
6.
(Pronoun) Identify whether the underlined pronoun is Reflexive or Emphatic. Give a brief reason for your choice.
(a) The principal himself distributed the prizes.
(b) The thief hid himself in the dark cave.
7.
(Noun) Form Abstract Nouns from the following words and use any ONE of the newly formed abstract nouns in a sentence of your own.
(a) Grow
(b) Brave
(c) Child
8.
(Articles) Correct the errors related to articles in the following sentences:
(a) The sun rises in east.
(b) She gave me a one-rupee coin and a apple.
(c) He is best student in our class.
9.
(Pronoun) Fill in the blanks with the correct form of the Personal Pronoun given in brackets (Subjective, Objective, or Possessive case):
(a) Let Rohan and ________ (I/me) solve this puzzle.
(b) This pen is ________ (my/mine), and that one is yours.
(c) The teacher praised ________ (they/them) for their hard work.
10.
(Sentence Transformation) Change the following sentences as directed in the brackets without changing their meaning:
(a) Is there any sense in crying over spilt milk? (Change to Assertive)
(b) What a beautiful piece of art this is! (Change to Assertive)
SECTION C (25 Marks)(Long Answer/Integrated Grammar - 5 Marks Each)
11.
(Integrated Editing) The following paragraph has one error in each line related to Nouns, Pronouns, Articles, or Sentence structure. Identify the incorrect word and write the correct word. Write them in a format (a) to (e).
A flock of bird was flying in the sky. (a)
They were searching for an place to rest. (b)
Suddenly, it saw a large tree near a lake. (c)
The tree was filled with ripe mango. (d)
Every birds ate happily and rested there. (e)
12.
(Sentence Synthesis) Combine the following pairs of simple sentences into a single Compound or Complex sentence using suitable conjunctions or relative pronouns. (1 Mark Each)
(a) He is very rich. He is not happy.
(b) This is the boy. He broke the window yesterday.
(c) The bell rang. The students rushed out of the classroom.
(d) I respect him. He is an honest man.
(e) You must work hard. You will not pass the examination.
13.
(Integrated Fill in the Blanks) Fill in the numbered blanks with appropriate Articles or Pronouns to complete the passage coherently.
Once upon (1) ________ time, there lived an old woodcutter in a village. (2) ________ woodcutter was very poor. One day, while (3) ________ was cutting a tree near a river, his axe fell into the water. He started crying because (4) ________ was his only means of livelihood. Suddenly, a fairy appeared and offered to help (5) ________.
14.
(Noun & Pronoun Cases) Read the sentence below. Identify the Case (Subjective/Nominative, Objective/Accusative, or Possessive/Genitive) of the underlined words.
"Ram's brother gave me a beautiful gift yesterday, and he bought it from the market."
Identify the case for: (a) Ram's (b) me (c) gift (d) he (e) market
15.
(Creative Application) Write a short paragraph (50-60 words) on the topic: "A Visit to a Magical Garden".

Mandatory Conditions:
1. You must use at least two Abstract Nouns.
2. You must use the Definite Article ('the') at least twice for specific reference.
3. You must include exactly one Interrogative Sentence in your paragraph.
(Underline the Abstract Nouns and the Interrogative Sentence in your answer).
