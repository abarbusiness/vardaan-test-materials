SECTION B: Short Answer/Reasoning (15 Marks)
6.
Error: am having
Correction: have had
Rule: The verb 'have', when used to show possession, is a stative verb and cannot be used in the continuous (-ing) form. Since a specific past time reference ("since 2020") extending to the present is given, the Present Perfect Tense ("have had") must be used. (1 mark error + 1 mark correction + 1 mark rule explanation)
7.
Analysis: The sentence uses Past Perfect ("had left") and Simple Past ("reached").
Reasoning: When two actions occur sequentially in the past, the action that happened first (the train leaving) takes the Past Perfect tense to show earlier completion. The action that happened later (reaching the platform) takes the Simple Past tense. (1 mark for identifying tenses + 2 marks for explaining the sequencing rule)

9.
Corrected Sentence: Pure water boils at 100 degrees Celsius.
Explanation: This is a universal scientific fact. Such facts represent permanent, unchanging states, not temporary actions currently in progress. Therefore, they must always be stated in the Simple Present tense, never continuous. (1 mark for correct sentence + 2 marks for explanation of scientific facts rule)
10.
Difference: Simple Past refers to completed actions at a definite time in the past with no connection to the present. Present Perfect refers to actions completed in the past that have a direct relevance, result, or effect in the present.
Examples:
1. Simple Past: I lost my ID card yesterday. (The action is over, perhaps I found it later).
2. Present Perfect: I have lost my ID card. (I still do not have it right now, which is a problem). (1.5 marks for accurate distinction + 1.5 marks for correct comparative examples)
