Minor Test 05
Class: 7 & 8 Subject: English Grammar (The Sentence) Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly. Check your spellings and punctuation.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Objective/Short Answer - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Identify the Subject and the Predicate in the following sentence:
"The old man with a walking stick crossed the busy street carefully."
2.
Identify the kind of sentence (Assertive, Interrogative, Imperative, or Exclamatory):
(a) Please pass me the salt from the table.
(b) What a magnificent monument the Taj Mahal is!
3.
Identify whether the following sentences are Simple or Compound:
(a) The dog barked loudly at the stranger.
(b) I like to read books, but my brother prefers playing video games.
4.
Fill in the blanks with the correct Coordinating Conjunction (FANBOYS):
(a) He was very tired, ________ he continued to run the marathon.
(b) You can have tea, ________ you can have coffee.
5.
Define a Compound Sentence. Provide one example of your own.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer/Application - 3 Marks Each)
6.
Combine the following pairs of Simple Sentences into a Compound Sentence using an appropriate conjunction:
(a) He is very poor. He is honest and hardworking.
(b) It was raining heavily. We decided to stay indoors.
7.
Change the following sentences as directed in the brackets:
(a) She is a brilliant student. (Change to Interrogative)
(b) Did you complete your homework? (Change to Assertive/Declarative)
8.
Separate the following Compound Sentence into two Independent Clauses (Simple Sentences):
"The sun set behind the mountains, and the birds flew back to their nests."
9.
Use Correlative Conjunctions (Either...or / Neither...nor / Not only...but also) to combine the sentences:
(a) He does not play cricket. He does not play football.
(b) She is intelligent. She is hardworking.
10.
In the sentence below, identify the two independent clauses and the coordinating conjunction used to join them:
"We waited for the train for two hours, yet it did not arrive."
SECTION C (25 Marks)(Long Answer/Complex Application - 5 Marks Each)
11.
Paragraph Editing (Conjunctions): The following paragraph has incorrect conjunctions used to join compound sentences. Identify the incorrect conjunction and supply the correct one. Write them in a format (a) to (e).
Rohan studied hard for the test, or he failed. (a)
He wanted to play outside, so it was raining. (b)
You must leave right now, and you will miss the bus. (c)
She was feeling sick, but she went to the doctor. (d)
I like apples, yet my sister likes oranges. (e)
12.
Transformation Challenge: The following paragraph is written in choppy, simple sentences. Rewrite the entire paragraph by joining them into Compound Sentences. Use different conjunctions (and, but, or, so, yet).
"The alarm rang loudly. John woke up immediately. He was feeling very sleepy. He got out of bed. He brushed his teeth. He took a shower. He could eat breakfast. He could skip it to catch the early bus. He decided to eat. He was very hungry."
13.
Differentiate and Explain:
(a) What is the main difference between a Phrase and a Clause? Give one example of each.
(b) What is the hidden subject in an Imperative Sentence? Explain with a short example.
14.
Creative Writing: Write a short paragraph (50-60 words) describing a visit to an amusement park or a fair. You must use at least three compound sentences connected by different coordinating conjunctions (FANBOYS). (Underline the compound sentences in your paragraph).
15.
Mixed Application: Read the instructions carefully and answer:
(a) Add a suitable predicate to this subject: "A flock of white sheep..."
(b) Rearrange the jumbled words to form a meaningful Assertive sentence: time / stitch / saves / a / in / nine
(c) Turn this into a compound sentence using 'otherwise': Work hard. You will fail.
(d) Identify the sentence type: Alas! We have lost the match.
(e) Punctuate and capitalize correctly: she went to paris london and rome during her summer vacation
