SECTION B: Grammar - Error Correction & Transformation (15 Marks)
6.
Error: went
Correction: go ("He didn't go...")
Rule: In the Simple Past tense negative sentences, the auxiliary verb 'did' is followed by the base form (V1) of the main verb, not the past tense form (V2). (1 mark error + 1 mark correction + 1 mark rule explanation)
7.
Answer: By next month (or any future time reference), the farmers will have been ploughing the fields (for a week/since morning).
(Note: To accurately reflect Future Perfect Continuous, a time reference is ideal, but simply changing the verb structure to "will have been ploughing" is the core requirement.) (3 marks for correct structure: will/shall + have been + verb-ing)
8.
Error: am knowing
Correction: "I know the answer to this complex mathematical problem."
Reason: 'Know' is a stative verb (verb of cognition/state of mind). Stative verbs are generally not used in the continuous (-ing) form. (1 mark error + 1 mark correction + 1 mark reason)
9.
Answer: "She has written a letter to the principal."
Meaning Change: The original sentence (Simple Present) suggests a routine or a factual statement. The new sentence (Present Perfect) indicates an action that happened in the past but has relevance or completion in the present moment (the letter is now finished). (1.5 marks for correct structure + 1.5 marks for explaining the shift from routine to completed action with present relevance)

(Rule implicit: When two actions happen in the past, the earlier action uses Past Perfect, and the later action uses Simple Past.) (1.5 marks for identifying error + 1.5 marks for correct usage of Past Perfect)
