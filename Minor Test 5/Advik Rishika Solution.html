Sample Transformed Paragraph:

(Evaluator Note: Allow reasonable variations. The key is that the choppy sentences are successfully joined using coordinating conjunctions to form compound sentences). (5 marks total. Deduct 1 mark for poor flow or missing conjunctions. Sentences must be compound, not complex).
13.
(a) Phrase vs Clause: A phrase is a group of words that makes some sense but not complete sense because it lacks a subject and a predicate (e.g., in the corner). A clause is a group of words that forms part of a sentence and contains a subject and a predicate (e.g., who is wearing a red shirt).
(b) Hidden Subject: In an Imperative sentence (which gives a command or request), the subject is always 'You', but it is hidden or understood. Example: "Sit down." actually means "(You) sit down." (2.5 marks for part A, 2.5 marks for part B)

(Evaluator Note: Verify the presence of at least three distinct compound sentences joined by FANBOYS and that they are underlined). (3 marks for the 3 compound sentences, 2 marks for coherence and paragraph structure)
