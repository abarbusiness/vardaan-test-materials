Minor Test 05
Class: 8 CBSE Subject: English Grammar (Clause, Sentence & Tense) Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Read the instructions for each question carefully before answering. Ensure your tenses align perfectly within complex sentences.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Objective/Short Answer - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Identify the Principal (Main) Clause and the Subordinate Clause in the following sentence:
"The girl who won the gold medal is my younger sister."
2.
Identify whether the following sentences are Simple, Compound, or Complex:
(a) He is poor, yet he is completely honest.
(b) Although he was exhausted, he continued his journey.
3.
Fill in the blanks with the correct tense of the verbs given in brackets. (Hint: Pay attention to the sequence of events in the past):
When I ________ (reach) the railway station, the train ________ (leave) already.
4.
Identify the kind of Subordinate Clause (Noun, Adjective, or Adverb Clause) in the given sentences:
(a) I know that he is an intelligent boy.
(b) We will wait here until you return.
5.
Convert the following sentence into the Present Perfect Continuous Tense:
"The mechanic repairs the broken car in the garage."
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer/Application - 3 Marks Each)
6.
Synthesis (Complex Sentence): Combine the following pairs of simple sentences into a single Complex Sentence using a Subordinate Clause:
(a) He was very ill. He could not attend the science exhibition.
(b) This is the beautiful village. I was born here.
7.
Tense Transformation in Clauses: Change the tense of the following complex sentence from Present to Past. Ensure both the main and subordinate clauses reflect the correct sequence of tenses:
"She says that she is working hard because she wants to secure the first rank."
8.
Phrase to Clause: Expand the underlined phrase into a subordinate clause to transform the Simple sentence into a Complex sentence. (Maintain the original tense).
(a) I saw a wounded tiger in the forest.
(b) He confessed his guilt to the judge.
9.
Do as Directed: Transform the following sentences without altering their core meaning:
(a) As soon as the sun rose, the fog disappeared. (Begin: No sooner...)
(b) Besides being a brilliant student, he is a great athlete. (Change into a Compound Sentence using 'Not only... but also')
10.
Tense Consistency: The following short paragraph has tense inconsistency errors. Identify the two incorrect verbs and provide the correct forms.
"Yesterday, I visited the zoo with my parents. We see many wild animals there. A huge lion was roaring loudly inside its cage, which terrifies all the little children."
SECTION C (25 Marks)(Long Answer/Integrated Grammar - 5 Marks Each)
11.
Integrated Tense Application: Fill in the numbered blanks with the correct form of the verbs given in brackets to complete the narrative coherently.
Last night, while I (1) ________ (read) a detective novel, I suddenly (2) ________ (hear) a strange noise from the kitchen. I cautiously (3) ________ (walk) downstairs to investigate. To my surprise, I found that my cat (4) ________ (knock) over a jar of cookies. By the time my mother woke up, I (5) ________ (clean) up the entire mess.
12.
Complex Clause Analysis: Read the following sentence carefully. Break it down to identify the Main Clause and the two Subordinate Clauses. Also, state the type of each Subordinate Clause (Noun, Adjective, or Adverb).
"The man who is wearing a black coat told the police that he had seen the thief running away."
13.
Sentence Transformation Challenge: Rewrite the following sentences as directed in the brackets. (1 Mark Each)
(a) In spite of his wealth, he is unhappy. (Change into a Complex Sentence)
(b) If you do not hurry, you will miss the flight. (Change into a Compound Sentence)
(c) The teacher punished the boy who was shouting. (Change into a Simple Sentence)
(d) I was too tired to walk any further. (Change into a Complex Sentence using 'so...that')
(e) What a beautiful painting this is! (Change into an Assertive Sentence)
14.
Editing Passage (Clause & Tense): The following passage has one error in each line, primarily related to conjunctions, clauses, or tense sequence. Write the incorrect word and the correct word in the format (a) to (e).
He works very hard so that he will win the scholarship. (a)
Unless you will not apologize, I shall not forgive you. (b)
He asked me that where I lived. (c)
When I reached the stadium, the match has already started. (d)
She is the girl whom secured the highest marks. (e)
15.
Creative Grammar Application: Write a short paragraph (50-60 words) describing an unexpected event that happened at school.

Conditions:
1. You must use at least one Past Perfect Tense.
2. You must include at least two Complex Sentences (using subordinate clauses).
