SECTION A: GEOGRAPHY (Natural Resources) [20 Marks]
1.
When does a substance from nature become a "resource"? Mention the main factors responsible for this change.
(2)
Solution:
A substance becomes a resource when human beings develop techniques to convert these gifts of nature into useful articles to satisfy their needs.
Main factors: Technology, time, and human needs are the primary factors that change a natural substance into a resource.
2.
Give two examples of ubiquitous resources and two examples of localized resources.
(2)
Solution:
Ubiquitous Resources (found everywhere): Air and Sunlight.
Localized Resources (found in specific places): Gold and Copper.
3.
What is meant by "Sustainable Development"?
(2)
Solution:
Sustainable Development is the judicious and careful use of natural resources to meet our present needs while conserving and protecting them for future generations.
4.
Briefly explain the difference between Potential and Actual (Developed) resources.
(2)
Solution:
Potential Resources: Resources whose total available quantity is not known and which are not being used currently but can be used in the future (e.g., generating hydroelectricity from certain waterfalls in Africa).
Actual Resources: Resources whose total quantity and quality are known, surveyed, and are currently being used by humans (e.g., windmills in Tamil Nadu).
5.
Differentiate between Biotic and Abiotic resources with suitable examples.
(3)
Solution:
Biotic Resources: These are living resources that come from the biosphere. They generally have the capacity to reproduce and are renewable. Examples: Plants, animals, forests.
Abiotic Resources: These come from the physical or non-living environment. They are mostly non-renewable as their total reserve cannot be increased. Examples: Land, water, air, and minerals.
6.
Explain the impact of the Green Revolution on groundwater in Punjab as discussed in your notes.
(3)
Solution:
During the Green Revolution (mid-1960s), the use of high-yielding variety (HYV) seeds demanded huge amounts of water. This led to the overexploitation of groundwater, which was extracted at an unsustainable rate (faster than it could naturally recharge). Currently, Punjab extracts 157% more water than is naturally recharged, leading to a critical and dangerous decline in its water tables.
7.
How did Sikkim's Organic Revolution help in the restoration and regeneration of resources?
(3)
Solution:
To protect soil fertility from harmful chemical fertilizers, Sikkim started an Organic Revolution in 2005. They utilized sustainable farming methods like crop rotation, mixed cropping, and biological pest control. By 2016, this approach successfully increased soil fertility, brought back beneficial soil organisms, and made the local water sources cleaner, thereby regenerating the environment.
8.
What is the International Solar Alliance (ISA)? Briefly describe its "Towards 1000" strategy.
(3)
Solution:
International Solar Alliance (ISA): It is an initiative launched jointly by India and France in 2015 to harness solar energy to combat climate change and reduce reliance on non-renewable fossil fuels.
"Towards 1000" Strategy: This is a goal set by the ISA to install 1000 Gigawatt (GW) of solar energy capacity globally by the year 2030, aiming to provide clean energy and drastically reduce carbon dioxide emissions.
SECTION B: SCIENCE (Force) [20 Marks]
9.
What is a force? State the SI unit of force.
(2)
Solution:
Force: A push or pull upon an object resulting from the object's interaction with another object is called force.
SI Unit: The SI unit of force is the Newton (N).
10.
Bits of paper stick to a plastic comb after it is rubbed on dry hair. Name the force responsible for this. Is it a contact or non-contact force?
(2)
Solution:
Force: Electrostatic force.
Type: It is a non-contact force (it can attract the paper bits from a distance without touching).
11.
Differentiate between mass and weight (give any two points).
(2)
Solution:
1. Definition: Mass is the amount of matter contained in a body, while weight is the gravitational pull exerted by the Earth on that object.
2. Constancy: Mass remains constant everywhere in the universe, whereas weight changes from place to place depending on the local gravity.
12.
State the principle of flotation.
(2)
Solution:
Principle of Flotation: An object will float in a fluid if the weight of the fluid displaced by its immersed part is equal to the total weight of the object.
13.
Differentiate between contact and non-contact forces with one example of each.
(3)
Solution:
Contact Forces: Forces that can only act when two objects are in physical contact with each other.
Example: Muscular force, Frictional force.

Non-Contact Forces: Forces that can act between two objects even when they are not in physical contact (they act from a distance).
Example: Magnetic force, Gravitational force.
14.
Why does an ordinary iron nail sink in water, but a large ship made of the same iron floats?
(3)
Solution:
An ordinary iron nail is solid, meaning its overall density is much higher than water. Its downward weight is greater than the upward buoyant force, causing it to sink. A ship, however, is hollow and traps a large amount of air. This makes the ship's average density less than that of water. It displaces a massive volume of water, creating a large upward buoyant force that balances its weight, allowing it to float.
15.
Explain the "turning effect" of force and provide one everyday example where it is observed.
(3)
Solution:
Turning Effect of Force: The turning effect of a force (also known as torque or moment of force) is the ability of a force to rotate an object around a fixed point or pivot. It depends on both the magnitude of the force applied and the perpendicular distance of the force from the pivot.
Example: Pushing a door open using its handle (which is located far from the hinges/pivot to maximize the turning effect).
16.
Briefly explain how a submarine uses the principle of buoyancy to sink and rise in the ocean.
(3)
Solution:
Submarines have special hollow compartments called ballast tanks.
To Sink (Dive): Water is allowed to enter the ballast tanks, displacing the air. This increases the submarine's average density and total weight. When its weight exceeds the buoyant upthrust, it sinks.
To Rise (Surface): Compressed air is forcefully pumped into the ballast tanks, pushing the water out. This decreases the submarine's average density and total weight. When its weight becomes less than the buoyant upthrust, it rises to the surface.
SECTION C: MATHEMATICS (Algebraic Identities) [20 Marks]
17.
Expand using a suitable identity: $(x + 7)^2$.
(2)
Solution:
Using the identity: $(a + b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2$
Here, $a = x$ and $b = 7$
$= (x)^2 + 2(x)(7) + (7)^2$
$= x^2 + 14x + 49$
Answer: $x^2 + 14x + 49$
18.
Evaluate using the identity $(a - b)(a + b) = a^2 - b^2$:
$(y - 5)(y + 5)$.
(2)
Solution:
Using the given identity: $(a - b)(a + b) = a^2 - b^2$
Here, $a = y$ and $b = 5$
$= (y)^2 - (5)^2$
$= y^2 - 25$
Answer: $y^2 - 25$
19.
Expand using a suitable identity: $(2m - 3)^2$.
(2)
Solution:
Using the identity: $(a - b)^2 = a^2 - 2ab + b^2$
Here, $a = 2m$ and $b = 3$
$= (2m)^2 - 2(2m)(3) + (3)^2$
$= 4m^2 - 12m + 9$
Answer: $4m^2 - 12m + 9$
20.
Evaluate $(101)^2$ using a suitable algebraic identity.
(2)
Solution:
We can write $101$ as $(100 + 1)$.
So, $(101)^2 = (100 + 1)^2$
Using the identity: $(a + b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2$
$= (100)^2 + 2(100)(1) + (1)^2$
$= 10000 + 200 + 1$
$= 10201$
Answer: $10201$
21.
Use the identity $(x+a)(x+b) = x^2 + (a+b)x + ab$ to find the product:
$(p + 3)(p + 5)$.
(3)
Solution:
Using the given identity: $(x+a)(x+b) = x^2 + (a+b)x + ab$
Here, $x = p$, $a = 3$, and $b = 5$
$= (p)^2 + (3 + 5)p + (3 \times 5)$
$= p^2 + (8)p + 15$
$= p^2 + 8p + 15$
Answer: $p^2 + 8p + 15$
22.
Simplify the expression: $(a + b)^2 - (a - b)^2$.
(3)
Solution:
Expand both terms using identities:
$(a + b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2$
$(a - b)^2 = a^2 - 2ab + b^2$
Now, subtract the second from the first:
$= (a^2 + 2ab + b^2) - (a^2 - 2ab + b^2)$
$= a^2 + 2ab + b^2 - a^2 + 2ab - b^2$
Grouping like terms:
$= (a^2 - a^2) + (b^2 - b^2) + (2ab + 2ab)$
$= 0 + 0 + 4ab$
Answer: $4ab$
23.
Using the identity $a^2 - b^2 = (a-b)(a+b)$, evaluate $52^2 - 48^2$.
(3)
Solution:
Using the given identity: $a^2 - b^2 = (a-b)(a+b)$
Here, $a = 52$ and $b = 48$
$= (52 - 48)(52 + 48)$
$= (4) \times (100)$
$= 400$
Answer: $400$
24.
If $x + y = 10$ and $xy = 21$, find the value of $x^2 + y^2$.
(3)
Solution:
We know the identity: $(x + y)^2 = x^2 + 2xy + y^2$
Substitute the given values ($x + y = 10$ and $xy = 21$):
$(10)^2 = x^2 + 2(21) + y^2$
$100 = x^2 + 42 + y^2$
Rearranging to find $x^2 + y^2$:
$x^2 + y^2 = 100 - 42$
$x^2 + y^2 = 58$
Answer: $58$
