Physics Test Part 1 - Electrostatics | Set - A (Solutions)
1.
SI unit of Electric Charge is Coulomb.
Explanation: Electric charge is defined by the amount of excess or deficit of electrons. By definition the unit is the Coulomb, where $1\ \mathrm{C}$ equals the charge transported by a current of $1\ \mathrm{A}$ in $1\ \mathrm{s}$. Answer: (B)
2.
Two charges +2 μC and +6 μC; after giving -4 μC to each they become -2 μC and +2 μC.
Explanation: Initial charges: $q_1=+2\ \mu\mathrm{C}$, $q_2=+6\ \mu\mathrm{C}$. After adding $-4\ \mu\mathrm{C}$ to each: $q_1'=-2\ \mu\mathrm{C}$, $q_2'=+2\ \mu\mathrm{C}$. Coulomb force magnitude $F\propto|q_1 q_2|$ so new magnitude ratio $\dfrac{|q_1' q_2'|}{|q_1 q_2|}=\dfrac{(2)(2)}{(2)(6)}=\dfrac{1}{3}$. Thus new force $=12\ \mathrm{N} \times \tfrac{1}{3}=4\ \mathrm{N}$. Signs opposite ⇒ attractive. Answer: (A)
3.
Distance doubled and charges doubled.
Explanation: Coulomb's law: $F= k\dfrac{q_1 q_2}{r^2}$. If $q_1,q_2$ each double, numerator multiplies by $4$. If $r$ doubles, denominator multiplies by $4$. Net change: $4/4=1$, so $F$ unchanged. Answer: (B)
4.
SI unit of Electric Field Intensity.
Explanation: Electric field $\mathbf{E}=\mathbf{F}/q$ (force per unit test charge), so units are $\mathrm{N}/\mathrm{C}$. Answer: (B)
5.
Force on 5 μC in E = 2×10^4 N/C.
Explanation: Use $F=qE$. With $q=5\times10^{-6}\ \mathrm{C}$ and $E=2\times10^{4}\ \mathrm{N/C}$, $F=5\times10^{-6}\times2\times10^{4}=0.1\ \mathrm{N}$. Answer: (A)
6.
Two identical spheres +10 μC and -4 μC brought in contact.
Explanation: Total charge $=+10\ \mu\mathrm{C}+(-4\ \mu\mathrm{C})=+6\ \mu\mathrm{C}$. When identical conductors touch, charge shares equally: each gets $+3\ \mu\mathrm{C}$. Answer: (B)
7.
Net force on -q at vertex of equilateral triangle with +q at other vertices.
Explanation: Force magnitude from each +q on -q: $F=k\dfrac{q^2}{L^2}$. Two equal forces at $60^\circ$ apart have resultant $F_R=2F\cos30^\circ=2F(\sqrt{3}/2)=\sqrt{3}F$. Thus $F_R=\sqrt{3}k\dfrac{q^2}{L^2}$. Answer: (A)
8.
Value of constant 1/(4πε₀).
Explanation: The Coulomb constant $k=1/(4\pi\epsilon_0)\approx9\times10^{9}\ \mathrm{N\,m^2\,C^{-2}}$. Answer: (A)
9.
Minimum isolated electric charge (electron charge).
Explanation: The elementary charge (magnitude of electron charge) is $e\approx1.602\times10^{-19}\ \mathrm{C}$, so the minimum isolated charge is approximately $1.6\times10^{-19}\ \mathrm{C}$. Answer: (B)
10.
Electric field inside a hollow charged conducting sphere.
Explanation: In electrostatic equilibrium charges reside on the surface of a conductor and the electric field inside is zero by Gauss's law and conductor properties. Answer: (B)
11.
Ratio of force in air to force in medium of dielectric constant K.
Explanation: In a dielectric medium with constant $K$, Coulomb's law becomes $F=\dfrac{1}{K}F_{\text{air}}$. So the ratio $F_{\text{air}}:F_{\text{medium}}=K:1$. Answer: (B)
12.
Electric field to balance weight of electron (E = mg/e).
Calculation: Balance weight by electric force: $eE=mg\Rightarrow E=\dfrac{mg}{e}$. Substituting $m=9.1\times10^{-31}\ \mathrm{kg}$, $g=10\ \mathrm{m/s^2}$, $e=1.6\times10^{-19}\ \mathrm{C}$ gives $E\approx\dfrac{9.1\times10^{-30}}{1.6\times10^{-19}}\approx5.7\times10^{-11}\ \mathrm{N/C}$. Answer: (A)
13.
Three identical +q at vertices: net field at centroid.
Explanation: Three identical positive charges symmetrically placed produce electric field vectors at the centroid that cancel pairwise due to $120^\circ$ separations; net field = 0. Answer: (A)
14.
Polythene piece has -3.2×10^-7 C; number of electrons transferred?
Calculation: Number of electrons $N=\dfrac{|Q|}{e}=\dfrac{3.2\times10^{-7}}{1.6\times10^{-19}}=2\times10^{12}$. Answer: (A)
15.
Two charges +q and -q at separation 2a; field at midpoint.
Explanation: At midpoint the magnitudes of fields due to +q and -q are equal but directions are towards negative charge (from +q) and towards +q (from -q); net direction is towards the negative charge. Answer: (C)
16.
Linear charge density of wire with total 10 μC over 5 m.
Explanation: Linear charge density $\lambda=Q/L=\dfrac{10\ \mu\mathrm{C}}{5\ \mathrm{m}}=2\ \mu\mathrm{C/m}$. Answer: (A)
17.
Acceleration of electron in field E: a = eE/m.
Explanation: Force on electron $F=eE$, so acceleration $a=F/m=eE/m$. Answer: (A)
18.
Force scaling when medium K = 80.
Explanation: With dielectric constant $K=80$, $F_{\text{water}}=F_{\text{air}}/K=F/80$. Answer: (B)
19.
Equilibrium location for third charge with +9e and +e.
Explanation: For equilibrium between +9e at $x=0$ and +e at $x=a$, place third charge at distance $x$ from +9e so that fields cancel: $\dfrac{9}{x^2}=\dfrac{1}{(a-x)^2}$. Taking square roots: $3/(x) = 1/(a-x)\Rightarrow 3(a-x)=x\Rightarrow x=3a/4$. Answer: (C)
20.
Initial acceleration of particle mass 2×10^-3 kg and charge 1 μC due to 1 mC at 1 m.
Calculation: $F=k\dfrac{q_1 q_2}{r^2}$ with $k=9\times10^9, q_1=1\times10^{-6}, q_2=1\times10^{-3}, r=1$ gives $F=9\ \mathrm{N}$. Acceleration $a=F/m=9/(2\times10^{-3})=4500\ \mathrm{m/s^2}$. Answer: (C)
21.
Electric field of 1 μC at 3 m.
Calculation: $E=k\dfrac{q}{r^2}=9\times10^9\times\dfrac{1\times10^{-6}}{3^2}=1000\ \mathrm{N/C}$. Answer: (A)
22.
Principle of superposition of electric forces.
Explanation: Superposition principle: net force on any charge equals vector sum of forces due to each other charge independently. Answer: (C)
23.
Nature of charges A and B from field lines (lines originate at A and terminate at B).
Explanation: Field lines originate at positive charges and terminate at negatives. If lines originate at A and end at B, then A is positive and B is negative. Answer: (C)
24.
Two spheres +5 μC and -15 μC brought into contact and separated 0.5 m: new force.
Explanation: Total charge $=+5\mu-15\mu=-10\times10^{-6}\ \mathrm{C}$. Shared equally ⇒ each has $-5\times10^{-6}\ \mathrm{C}$. Force magnitude $F=k\dfrac{(5\times10^{-6})^2}{(0.5)^2}=9\times10^9\times\dfrac{25\times10^{-12}}{0.25}=0.9\ \mathrm{N}$; opposite signs ⇒ attractive. Answer: (B)
25.
Graph of E vs r for a conducting sphere.
Explanation: For a conducting sphere, $E=0$ for $rR$ the field equals that of a point charge $E=kQ/r^2$. Graph A matches this. Answer: (A)
