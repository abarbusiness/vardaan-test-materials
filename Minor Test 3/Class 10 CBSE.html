Minor Test 3
Class: 10 CBSE Subject: Science & Social Science Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly to the point.
  • Draw neat and properly labeled diagrams wherever required.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
What is the specific role of saliva in the digestion of food? Name the enzyme present in it.
2.
Define 'Majoritarianism'. Name the country from your syllabus that adopted this policy.
3.
Differentiate between renewable and non-renewable resources with one example of each.
4.
Why do herbivores have a longer small intestine compared to carnivores?
5.
Mention the two main sets of reasons why power sharing is desirable. Give one line explaining each.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
Briefly explain the three complex stages involved in the process of resource planning in India.
7.
Describe the process of nutrition in Amoeba. Mention the role of pseudopodia in this process.
8.
"Power sharing is the very spirit of democracy." Justify this statement with three valid arguments.
9.
What is meant by 'Sustainable Development'? Why is it considered crucial for economic growth and the future of our planet?
10.
Name the specific site of complete digestion in the human body. What are the final end products formed after the complete digestion of carbohydrates, proteins, and fats?
SECTION C (25 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
(a) Draw a neat, well-labeled diagram of the human digestive system.
(b) Briefly explain the role played by the liver and the pancreas in the human digestive system.
12.
Compare and contrast the different approaches taken by the leaders of Belgium and Sri Lanka to deal with the problem of cultural diversity and regional differences in their respective countries.
13.
(a) "Land is a natural resource of utmost importance." Justify this statement with two valid points.
(b) Identify the main causes of land degradation in India. Suggest any two effective measures to control land degradation in agricultural and industrial areas.
14.
(a) Write the balanced chemical equation for the process of photosynthesis.
(b) State the three main events that occur sequentially during this process in plants.
(c) How do desert plants take up carbon dioxide and perform photosynthesis?
15.
Explain the four different forms of power sharing in modern democracies. Provide one relevant example for each form to support your answer.
