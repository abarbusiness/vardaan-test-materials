MINOR TEST 3
Class: 6 ICSE Subject: Physics & Mathematics Time: 1.5 Hours Max. Marks: 60
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Section A contains 10 questions of 2 marks each.
  • Section B contains 5 questions of 3 marks each.
  • Section C contains 5 questions of 5 marks each.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly. Show step-by-step calculations for Mathematics.
SECTION A (20 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Insert commas suitably and write the number name of 87654321 according to the Indian System of Numeration.
2.
Write the numeral for "Seventy-five million, four hundred twenty thousand, eight hundred ten" using the International System.
3.
Find the product of the place value and the face value of the digit 5 in the number 4,56,789.
4.
Round off 54,876 to the nearest thousands and 1,234 to the nearest hundreds.
5.
Find the difference between the successor of 99,999 and the predecessor of 1,00,000.
6.
Define matter. Name the two basic properties that are common to all forms of matter.
7.
Name the three states of matter. Give one example of a substance that exists naturally in all three states.
8.
Briefly explain the meaning of the terms 'Intermolecular space' and 'Intermolecular force of attraction'.
9.
Define sublimation. Give two examples of substances that undergo this process.
10.
State the difference between melting and freezing.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
11.
Make the greatest and the smallest 7-digit numbers using the digits 4, 0, 7, 1, 8, 5 (you may repeat any one digit once). Find the difference between the two numbers formed.
12.
How many 6-digit numbers are there in all? Show your step-by-step working.
13.
Differentiate between Mass and Weight based on their definition, SI unit, and the instrument used to measure them.
14.
Explain why gases do not have a definite shape or a definite volume, using the concept of intermolecular forces and spaces.
15.
What is meant by the expansion of matter? Name the three types of expansion that take place in solid matter on heating.
SECTION C (25 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
16.
Consider the number 45678912:
(i) Rewrite the number by inserting commas according to both the Indian and International systems of numeration. (2 Marks)
(ii) Write their respective number names in both systems. (3 Marks)
17.
A factory produces 15,435 toys every day. How many toys will it produce in a leap year if there are 45 holidays in the year during which no toys are produced? Show all your calculations clearly.
18.
Describe the 'Ball and Ring' experiment to demonstrate that solids expand on heating. Include the state before heating and after heating in your explanation.
19.
Define the following terms related to the physical properties and interconversion of states of matter:
(a) Boiling point
(b) Melting point
(c) Condensation
(d) Latent heat
(e) Vapourisation
20.
Give scientific reasons for the following statements:
(a) We can easily move our hand through air, but to do the same through a solid block of wood we need a karate expert. (2.5 Marks)
(b) A solid has a definite volume and a fixed shape, whereas a liquid has a definite volume but no definite shape. (2.5 Marks)
