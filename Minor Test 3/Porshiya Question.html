MINOR TEST 3 (ACADEMIC SESSION 2024-25)
Class: 7 ICSE Subject: General Mathematics Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory. Calculations must be shown clearly.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Rough work should be done in the right margin of your answer sheet.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Conceptual Recall - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Evaluate the integer expression: $(-15) + (-20) - (-10)$
2.
Convert the fraction $\frac{3}{4}$ into its equivalent percentage form.
3.
In the algebraic expression $5xy^{2} - 7x + 3$, identify the numerical coefficient of the term containing $x$.
4.
Calculate the supplement of an angle whose measure is $115^{\circ}$.
5.
Write the Roman numeral representation for the Hindu-Arabic number $98$.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer & Application - 3 Marks Each)
6.
Simplify the algebraic expression by grouping like terms:
$2(3a - b) + 5(a + 2b)$
7.
A trader purchased a wooden chair for ₹$1200$ and sold it for ₹$1500$. Find his Profit or Loss percentage.
8.
Find the Highest Common Factor (HCF) of $24$, $36$, and $60$ using the Prime Factorization method.
9.
Solve the linear equation to find the value of $x$:
$3x + 5 = 20$
10.
Express the ratio of $500\text{ g}$ to $2.5\text{ kg}$ in its simplest form. (Hint: $1\text{ kg} = 1000\text{ g}$)
SECTION C (25 Marks)(Detailed Analysis - 5 Marks Each)
11.
The perimeter of a rectangular playground is $120\text{ m}$. If the length of the playground is $40\text{ m}$, find:
(i) The breadth of the playground.
(ii) The total area of the playground in square meters.
12.
Construct a triangle $\triangle ABC$ given that $AB = 5\text{ cm}$, $BC = 6\text{ cm}$, and $AC = 7\text{ cm}$. List the steps of construction clearly.
13.
Ramesh deposited ₹$5000$ in a bank at an annual interest rate of $8\%$. Calculate:
(i) The Simple Interest earned after $3$ years.
(ii) The total Amount he will receive at the end of the period.
14.
Evaluate the following expression using the BODMAS rule:
$$ \left( \frac{2}{3} \div \frac{4}{9} \right) \times \frac{5}{6} + \frac{1}{2} $$
15.
In a class of $40$ students, the following data is collected regarding sports preferences:
- $25\%$ of students like Cricket.
- $45\%$ of students like Football.
- The remaining students like Tennis.
Calculate the exact number of students who prefer each of the three sports.
