SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
What is the specific role of saliva in the digestion of food? Name the enzyme present in it.
[2 Marks]
Answer: Saliva acts as a lubricant that moistens the food, making it easier to swallow by forming a bolus.
The enzyme present in saliva is Salivary Amylase, which breaks down complex starch into simpler sugars (maltose). (Role: 1 Mark, Enzyme: 1 Mark)
2.
Define 'Majoritarianism'. Name the country from your syllabus that adopted this policy.
[2 Marks]
Answer: Majoritarianism is a belief that the majority community should be able to rule a country in whichever way it wants, by disregarding the wishes and needs of the minority.
Sri Lanka adopted this policy. (Definition: 1 Mark, Country: 1 Mark)
3.
Differentiate between renewable and non-renewable resources with one example of each.
[2 Marks]
Answer:
Renewable Resources: Resources which can be renewed or reproduced by physical, chemical, or mechanical processes are known as renewable resources. Example: Solar energy, wind energy, water.
Non-renewable Resources: Resources that take millions of years in their formation and cannot be renewed easily once exhausted. Example: Minerals, fossil fuels (coal, petroleum). (1 Mark for each correct diff + example)
4.
Why do herbivores have a longer small intestine compared to carnivores?
[2 Marks]
Answer: Herbivores eat a plant-based diet which is rich in cellulose. Cellulose takes a longer time to digest. Therefore, herbivores need a longer small intestine to allow complete digestion of cellulose. Carnivores eat meat which is easier to digest, hence they have a shorter small intestine. (2 Marks for logical reasoning mentioning cellulose)
5.
Mention the two main sets of reasons why power sharing is desirable. Give one line explaining each.
[2 Marks]
Answer:
1. Prudential reasons: It helps to reduce the possibility of conflict between social groups, ensuring political stability.
2. Moral reasons: Power sharing is the very spirit of democracy; citizens have a right to be consulted on how they are to be governed. (1 Mark for each correct reason + explanation)
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
Briefly explain the three complex stages involved in the process of resource planning in India.
[3 Marks]
Answer: Resource planning in India involves three stages:
1. Identification and inventory of resources across the regions of the country. This involves surveying, mapping, qualitative and quantitative estimation, and measurement of the resources.
2. Evolving a planning structure endowed with appropriate technology, skill, and institutional set-up for implementing resource development plans.
3. Matching the resource development plans with overall national development plans. (1 Mark for each stage)
7.
Describe the process of nutrition in Amoeba. Mention the role of pseudopodia in this process.
[3 Marks]
Answer: Amoeba exhibits holozoic nutrition.
Role of pseudopodia: Amoeba takes in food using temporary finger-like extensions of the cell surface called pseudopodia.
Process: The pseudopodia fuse over the food particle forming a food vacuole. Inside the food vacuole, complex substances are broken down into simpler ones, which then diffuse into the cytoplasm. The remaining undigested material is moved to the surface of the cell and thrown out. (1 Mark for Pseudopodia role, 2 Marks for process)
8.
"Power sharing is the very spirit of democracy." Justify this statement with three valid arguments.
[3 Marks]
Answer: This statement is justified on moral grounds:
1. A democratic rule inherently involves sharing power with those affected by its exercise, and who have to live with its effects.
2. In a democracy, people have a right to be consulted on how they are to be governed.
3. A legitimate government is one where citizens, through participation, acquire a stake in the system, preventing the alienation of any group. (1 Mark for each valid argument)
9.
What is meant by 'Sustainable Development'? Why is it considered crucial for economic growth and the future of our planet?
[3 Marks]
Answer:
Definition: Sustainable economic development means that "development should take place without damaging the environment, and development in the present should not compromise with the needs of the future generations."
Why it is crucial:
1. Natural resources are finite and will get exhausted if overexploited by current generations.
2. Irresponsible development leads to severe environmental degradation (like climate change, pollution), threatening the survival of life on Earth. (1 Mark for definition, 2 Marks for explanation)

Name the specific site of complete digestion in the human body. What are the final end products formed after the complete digestion of carbohydrates, proteins, and fats?
[3 Marks]
Answer:
Site of complete digestion: Small Intestine.
End products:
1. Carbohydrates are converted into glucose.
2. Proteins are converted into amino acids.
3. Fats are converted into fatty acids and glycerol. (1 Mark for Small Intestine, 2 Marks for correct end products)

12.
Compare and contrast the different approaches taken by the leaders of Belgium and Sri Lanka to deal with the problem of cultural diversity and regional differences in their respective countries.
[5 Marks]
Answer:
Belgium's Approach (Accommodation):
1. Belgian leaders recognized the existence of regional differences and cultural diversities.
2. They amended their constitution four times between 1970 and 1993 to work out an arrangement that would enable everyone to live together.
3. The central government has an equal number of Dutch and French-speaking ministers. State governments are not subordinate to the Central Government.
4. They established a 'Community Government' elected by people belonging to one language community, handling cultural, educational, and language-related issues. This ensured peace.

Sri Lanka's Approach (Majoritarianism):
1. Sri Lankan leaders sought to secure dominance over government by virtue of their majority (Sinhala community).
2. Passed an Act in 1956 to recognize Sinhala as the only official language, disregarding Tamil.
3. The governments followed preferential policies that favoured Sinhala applicants for university positions and government jobs.
4. This alienated the Sri Lankan Tamils, leading to strained relations and ultimately a devastating civil war. (2.5 Marks for Belgium points, 2.5 Marks for Sri Lanka points)
13.
(a) "Land is a natural resource of utmost importance." Justify this statement with two valid points.
(b) Identify the main causes of land degradation in India. Suggest any two effective measures to control land degradation in agricultural and industrial areas.
[5 Marks]
Answer:
(a) Importance of Land:
1. It supports natural vegetation, wildlife, and human life.
2. It is the basis for all economic activities (agriculture, mining) and transport/communication systems. (2 Marks)

(b) Causes of Land Degradation: Deforestation, overgrazing, mining and quarrying, and over-irrigation leading to waterlogging (salinity/alkalinity). (1.5 Marks)
Measures to control:
Agricultural areas: Afforestation, proper management of grazing, planting of shelter belts of plants, control on over-irrigation.
Industrial areas: Proper discharge and disposal of industrial effluents and wastes after treatment. (1.5 Marks for any two valid measures) (Part a: 2M, Part b causes: 1.5M, measures: 1.5M)
14.
(a) Write the balanced chemical equation for the process of photosynthesis.
(b) State the three main events that occur sequentially during this process in plants.
(c) How do desert plants take up carbon dioxide and perform photosynthesis?
[5 Marks]
Answer:
(a) Balanced Equation:
6CO₂ + 12H₂O xrightarrow[Chlorophyll]{Sunlight} C₆H₁₂O₆ (Glucose) + 6O₂ + 6H₂O. (1 Mark)

(b) Three Main Events:
1. Absorption of light energy by chlorophyll.
2. Conversion of light energy to chemical energy and splitting of water molecules into hydrogen and oxygen.
3. Reduction of carbon dioxide to carbohydrates. (3 Marks)

(c) Desert Plants: Desert plants keep their stomata closed during the day to prevent water loss. They take up carbon dioxide at night and prepare an intermediate compound. This intermediate is acted upon by the energy absorbed by the chlorophyll during the day to complete photosynthesis. (1 Mark) (Eq: 1M, Events: 3M, Desert Plants: 1M)
15.
Explain the four different forms of power sharing in modern democracies. Provide one relevant example for each form to support your answer.
[5 Marks]
Answer:
1. Power shared among different organs of government (Horizontal Distribution): Power is shared between the legislature, executive, and judiciary. It allows different organs placed at the same level to exercise different powers (Checks and Balances). Example: India (Parliament, Council of Ministers, Supreme Court).
2. Power shared among governments at different levels (Vertical Distribution): A general government for the entire country (Federal/Central) and governments at the provincial or regional level (State/Local). Example: India has Central, State, and Panchayat/Municipal levels.
3. Power shared among different social groups: Power can be shared among religious or linguistic groups. This method is used to give minority communities a fair share in power. Example: 'Community Government' in Belgium.
4. Power shared among political parties, pressure groups, and movements: Such sharing controls or influences those in power. If an alliance of different parties forms a government, it's a coalition government where power is shared. Example: UPA or NDA coalition governments in India. (1.25 Marks for each form + example = 5 Marks)
