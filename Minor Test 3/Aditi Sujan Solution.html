SECTION A (16 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Define 'Density of Population'. What is the average global density of population?
(2)
Solution: Density of Population: It refers to the number of people living in a one square kilometre area of land.
Average Global Density: The average density of population in the world is approximately 61 persons per square km.
2.
Evaluate the following exponent expression:
$3^2 \times 3^{-4} \times 3^0$
(2)
Solution: Using the law of exponents ($a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}$):
$3^2 \times 3^{-4} \times 3^0 = 3^{(2 + (-4) + 0)}$
$= 3^{-2}$
$= \frac{1}{3^2} = \mathbf{\frac{1}{9}}$
3.
State the formula to calculate the 'Natural Growth Rate' of a population.
(2)
Solution: Natural Growth Rate = Birth Rate - Death Rate
(Where Birth rate and Death rate are calculated per thousand persons in a year).
4.
Find the reciprocal of $(-3/4)^{-2}$ and express it in its simplest form.
(2)
Solution: First, simplify $(-3/4)^{-2}$:
$(-3/4)^{-2} = (-4/3)^2 = \frac{16}{9}$
The reciprocal of $\frac{16}{9}$ is $\mathbf{\frac{9}{16}}$.
5.
Differentiate between Emigration and Immigration.
(2)
Solution: Emigration: It is the movement of people out of a country, which results in a decrease in population.
Immigration: It is the movement of people into a country, which results in an increase in population.
6.
Express the following number in Scientific Notation:
$0.000000000452$
(2)
Solution: Move the decimal point 10 places to the right to get 4.52.
The scientific notation is: $\mathbf{4.52 \times 10^{-10}}$
7.
Name two physical factors that lead to sparse population in Equatorial regions.
(2)
Solution: 1. Harsh Climate: Extreme heat and heavy rainfall throughout the year.
2. Rugged/Inaccessible Terrain: Dense, thick forests (like the Amazon Basin) that are difficult to navigate.
8.
Represent $0.\overline{6}$ as a rational number in the form $p/q$.
(2)
Solution: Let $x = 0.666...$ (i)
Multiply by 10: $10x = 6.666...$ (ii)
Subtract (i) from (ii):
$9x = 6 \implies x = \frac{6}{9} = \mathbf{\frac{2}{3}}$
SECTION B (24 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 3 Marks Each)
9.
"Fertile river valleys are highly populated." Explain this statement with reference to the Ganga and Nile rivers.
(3)
Solution: River valleys like the Ganga in India and the Nile in Egypt provide:
1. Fertile Soil: Alluvial deposits make the land excellent for agriculture.
2. Water Supply: Perennial water for domestic, industrial, and irrigation needs.
3. Flat Plains: Ease of building settlements and transport networks compared to mountains.
10.
Simplify using the laws of exponents:
$\frac{25 \times a^{-4}}{5^{-3} \times 10 \times a^{-8}}$ (where $a \neq 0$)
(3)
Solution: $\frac{5^2 \times a^{-4}}{5^{-3} \times (2 \times 5^1) \times a^{-8}}$
$= \frac{5^2 \times 5^3 \times a^8}{2 \times 5^1 \times a^4}$
$= \frac{5^5 \times a^{(8-4)}}{2 \times 5^1} = \frac{5^4 \times a^4}{2} = \mathbf{\frac{625a^4}{2}}$
11.
What are the three main regions of world population based on density? Provide one example for each.
(3)
Solution: 1. Densely Populated: Level land and fertile soil. Example: Eastern Asia or South Asia.
2. Moderately Populated: Growing industries or irrigation. Example: Deccan Plateau (India) or Central USA.
3. Sparsely Populated: Harsh climate/rugged terrain. Example: Sahara Desert or Antarctica.
12.
Find three rational numbers between $2/3$ and $3/4$ using the mean method.
(3)
Solution: Common denominator: $2/3 = 32/48$ and $3/4 = 36/48$.
Numbers in between: $\mathbf{33/48, 34/48, 35/48}$
(Or simplified: $11/16, 17/24, 35/48$)
13.
Discuss three negative impacts of overpopulation on a country's natural resources and economy.
(3)
Solution: 1. Resource Depletion: Rapid consumption of water, minerals, and forests leading to scarcity.
2. Economic Strain: High unemployment and poverty due to excessive labor supply compared to jobs.
3. Social/Infrastructure Pressure: Poor health, sanitation, and high crime rates due to overcrowding.
14.
Find the value of $n$ in the following equation:
$5^{2n-1} \div 25 = 125$
(3)
Solution: $5^{2n-1} \div 5^2 = 5^3$
$5^{(2n-1-2)} = 5^3 \implies 5^{2n-3} = 5^3$
Equating powers: $2n - 3 = 3$
$2n = 6 \implies \mathbf{n = 3}$
15.
Explain the Age Composition of a population. Why is the 15-64 years age group called 'productive'?
(3)
Solution: Age composition divides the population into Children (0-14), Adults (15-64), and Aged (65+).
The 15-64 years group is called productive (or working) because they are economically active, contribute to the country's GDP, and support the dependent population (children and elderly).
16.
Evaluate:
$(1/2)^{-2} + (1/3)^{-2} + (1/4)^{-2}$
(3)
Solution: $(2/1)^2 + (3/1)^2 + (4/1)^2$
$= 4 + 9 + 16 = \mathbf{29}$
SECTION C (20 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 5 Marks Each)
17.
What is a Population Pyramid? Describe the characteristics of a population pyramid with a broad base and a narrow top. What does it indicate about the country's birth and death rates?
(5)
Solution: Population Pyramid: A graphical representation plotting the distribution of age and sex of a population.
Broad Base: Indicates a high birth rate as more children are being born.
Narrow Top: Indicates a high death rate and lower life expectancy among the elderly.
Indication: This structure is typical of developing nations (like Kenya or India), where population growth is rapid but healthcare for the aged is still developing.
18.
(a) Simplify: $[(1/3)^{-1} - (1/4)^{-1}]^{-1}$
(b) If $x = (2/3)^{-2} \times (3/4)^0$, find the value of $x^{-2}$.
(5)
Solution: (a) $[3^1 - 4^1]^{-1} = [-1]^{-1} = \frac{1}{-1} = \mathbf{-1}$
(b) $x = (3/2)^2 \times 1 = \frac{9}{4}$.
Now, $x^{-2} = (\frac{9}{4})^{-2} = (\frac{4}{9})^2 = \mathbf{\frac{16}{81}}$
19.
Compare Overpopulation and Underpopulation. List two causes and two positive impacts for each condition.
(5)
Solution: Overpopulation: Pop. exceeds carrying capacity.
- Causes: High birth rate, improved medical facilities.
- Positive Impacts: Abundance of human labor, supply of skilled/unskilled labor.
Underpopulation: Pop. is too small to utilize resources.
- Causes: Emigration, war/political conflict.
- Positive Impacts: Low pollution, abundance of natural resources per head.
20.
(a) Write $31860000000$ in standard form.
(b) Simplify: $\frac{3^{-5} \times 10^{-5} \times 125}{5^{-7} \times 6^{-5}}$
(5)
Solution: (a) $\mathbf{3.186 \times 10^{10}}$
(b) $\frac{3^{-5} \times (2 \times 5)^{-5} \times 5^3}{5^{-7} \times (2 \times 3)^{-5}}$
$= \frac{3^{-5} \times 2^{-5} \times 5^{-5} \times 5^3}{5^{-7} \times 2^{-5} \times 3^{-5}}$
$= \frac{5^{-2}}{5^{-7}} = 5^{(-2 - (-7))} = 5^5 = \mathbf{3125}$
