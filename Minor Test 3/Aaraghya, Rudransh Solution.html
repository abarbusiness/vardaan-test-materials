IV. Define the following terms: [3 × 2 = 6 Marks]
1.
Metalloids
Ans: Elements that exhibit properties of both metals and nonmetals (e.g., Silicon, Arsenic).
2.
Molecule of a Compound
Ans: The smallest unit of a compound formed when atoms of two or more different elements combine chemically in a fixed proportion.
3.
Atomicity
Ans: The total number of atoms that join together to form one molecule of an element or compound.
V. Short & Long Answer Questions: [3 × 3 = 9 Marks]
1.
Mention any three differences between Compounds and Mixtures.
Ans:
1. Compounds are pure substances; Mixtures are impure substances.
2. In a compound, constituents combine in a fixed ratio; In a mixture, components can be in any ratio.
3. Compounds cannot be separated by physical methods; Mixtures can be separated by physical methods.
2.
State any three characteristics of a compound.
Ans:
1. Cannot be separated into constituent elements by physical methods.
2. The properties of a compound are entirely different from the properties of its constituent elements.
3. Energy is either absorbed or released when a compound is formed.
3.
Differentiate between homogeneous and heterogeneous mixtures with one example for each.
Ans:
Homogeneous: A mixture in which the constituents are distributed uniformly. Example: Sugar and water, or Air.
Heterogeneous: A mixture in which the constituents are not distributed uniformly. Example: Iron and sulphur powder, or Sand and water.
