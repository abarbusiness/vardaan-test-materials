Physics Test Part 1 - Electrostatics | Set - B (Solutions)
1.
Glass rod acquires $+1.6\times10^{-13}\ \mathrm{C}$.
Explanation: Number of electrons transferred $N=\dfrac{Q}{e}=\dfrac{1.6\times10^{-13}}{1.602\times10^{-19}}\approx10^{6}$. Direction: positive on rod means electrons moved from rod to silk. Answer: (C)
2.
+3 μC and +8 μC; add -5 μC to each → -2 μC and +3 μC.
Explanation: Initial forces set $F_0=40\ \mathrm{N}$. After adding $-5\ \mu\mathrm{C}$: charges become $-2\ \mu\mathrm{C}$ and $+3\ \mu\mathrm{C}$ so product magnitude $=6$ vs original $=24$, ratio $6/24=1/4$. Thus $F=40\times\tfrac{1}{4}=10\ \mathrm{N}$ (opposite signs ⇒ attractive). Answer: (A)
3.
Divide Q into q and Q−q to maximize force.
Explanation: Force $F\propto q(Q-q)=qQ-q^2$. Differentiate w.r.t. $q$: $\dfrac{d}{dq}(qQ-q^2)=Q-2q=0\Rightarrow q=Q/2$ giving maximum. Answer: (C)
4.
Small displacement x of third charge in symmetric configuration.
Explanation: For small displacement $x\ll a$, expand forces to first order; net force ~ proportional to $x$ and directed opposite displacement indicating Hooke-like restoring force $ herefore$ proportional to $-x$. Answer: (D)
5.
Three charges +Q (0), q (d/2), +Q (d); net force on +Q at 0 zero ⇒ q = ?
Explanation: Equate magnitude of forces on +Q at origin due to q at $d/2$ and +Q at $d$. Force from q: $F_q=kQ|q|/(d/2)^2=4kQ|q|/d^2$. From +Q at $d$: $F_Q=kQ^2/d^2$. For net zero: $4|q|=Q\Rightarrow q=-Q/4$ (negative sign indicates q opposite sign). Answer: (A)
6.
Electron and proton fall under reversed field.
Explanation: Acceleration magnitude $a=|q|E/m$. Proton mass $m_p\approx1836 m_e$, so $a_e/a_p=(e/m_e)/(e/m_p)=m_p/m_e\approx1836$, hence electron falls much faster (time smaller). Answer: (A)
7.
Ratio electrostatic to gravitational between electron & proton.
Explanation: Ratio $\dfrac{F_{elec}}{F_{grav}}\approx\dfrac{k e^2 / r^2}{G m_e m_p / r^2}=\dfrac{k e^2}{G m_e m_p}\sim10^{39}$ (standard estimate). Answer: (B)
8.
Contact and re-separation problem (charges change by -3 μC each).
Explanation: Original product $=6\times9=54$ (in μC^2 units). After giving -3 μC to each: $3\times6=18$. Ratio $18/54=1/3$ → new force $=F/3$. Answer: (A)
9.
Field lines of isolated positive charge.
Explanation: Positive point charge field lines are radial and outward. Answer: (A)
10.
Force between +Q and -Q after 25% transfer.
Explanation: Original magnitudes: $Q$ and $-Q$. After transferring 25% of $A$ to $B$: $Q_A=0.75Q$, $Q_B=-0.75Q$. Force magnitude scales with product $|Q_AQ_B|=(0.75)^2Q^2=9/16Q^2$ so new force $=\tfrac{9}{16}F$. Answer: (C)
11.
Field lines for metallic sphere in uniform external field.
Explanation: For a conductor in external field, induced charges arrange to make interior field zero; surface field is perpendicular to surface. Answer: (C)
12.
Relation between field lines and charge magnitudes.
Explanation: Field lines are drawn proportional to charge. If $N_1$ emerge and $N_2$ terminate, then $|q_1|/|q_2|=N_1/N_2$. Answer: (A)
13.
+8q at 0 and -2q at L; location where net field = 0.
Explanation: Set $\dfrac{8}{x^2}=\dfrac{2}{(x-L)^2}$. Simplify: $4/(x^2)=1/(x-L)^2\Rightarrow 2/(x)=1/(x-L)\Rightarrow 2(x-L)=x\Rightarrow x=2L$. Answer: (B)
14.
Pendulum bob 30 mg with charge 2.0×10^-8 C in E = 2×10^4 V/m.
Calculation: Weight $mg=30\times10^{-6}\times10=3\times10^{-4}\ \mathrm{N}$. Electric force $qE=2\times10^{-8}\times2\times10^{4}=4\times10^{-4}\ \mathrm{N}$. Tension $T=\sqrt{(mg)^2+(qE)^2}=\sqrt{(3\times10^{-4})^2+(4\times10^{-4})^2}=5\times10^{-4}\ \mathrm{N}$. Answer: (C)
15.
q producing E = 2 N/C at 0.5 m.
Calculation: $E=kq/r^2\Rightarrow q=Er^2/k$. With $E=2$, $r=0.5$, $k=9\times10^9$, $q=2\times0.25/9\times10^9\approx5.56\times10^{-11}\ \mathrm{C}$. Answer: (A)
16.
Four -Q at corners, q at centre for equilibrium.
Explanation: By symmetry and Coulomb balancing, the equilibrium central charge equals $q=\dfrac{Q}{4}(1+2\sqrt{2})$ (positive). Answer: (B)
17.
Semicircular arc of radius a and linear charge density λ: field at centre.
Explanation: For a semicircular arc, integrate contributions $dE$ and resolve: result $E=\dfrac{\lambda}{2\pi\epsilon_0 a}$ at the center along symmetry axis. Answer: (A)
18.
Two spheres +6 μC and +9 μC; after -3 μC to both.
Explanation: Original product $=6\times9=54$ (units). After -3 μC each: $3\times6=18$. Ratio $18/54=1/3$ so new force $=F/3$. Answer: (A)
19.
Electric field lines of isolated positive point charge.
Explanation: Field lines of an isolated positive charge point radially outward in all directions. Answer: (A)
20.
Time period of pendulum with charge q in field E.
Explanation: Small oscillations use effective acceleration $g_{\text{eff}}=g+qE/m$ (if field assists gravity). Period $T=2\pi\sqrt{\dfrac{L}{g_{\text{eff}}}}=2\pi\sqrt{\dfrac{L}{g+qE/m}}$. Answer: (C)
21.
Kinetic energy after moving distance y in field E.
Explanation: Work done by electric force over distance $y$ is $W=qEy$, which becomes kinetic energy: $K=qEy$. Answer: (C)
22.
Max field along axis of charged ring occurs at x = ?
Explanation: For a ring of radius $R$, axial field $E(x)=\dfrac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0}\dfrac{Qx}{(x^2+R^2)^{3/2}}$. Differentiate and set to zero gives maximum at $x=R/\sqrt{2}$. Answer: (B)
23.
Force on q2 due to q1 at given coordinates.
Calculation: Separation: $\Delta x=3-(-1)=4$, $\Delta z=-2-1=-3$, $r=\sqrt{4^2+0^2+(-3)^2}=5\ \mathrm{m}$. $F=k\dfrac{(20\times10^{-6})(25\times10^{-6})}{5^2}=9\times10^9\times\dfrac{500\times10^{-12}}{25}=0.18\ \mathrm{N}$. Answer: (A)
24.
Midway between +λ and -λ separated by 2R.
Explanation: Field from each line at midpoint adds; for +λ and -λ separated by 2R the net magnitude evaluates to $E=\dfrac{\lambda}{\pi\epsilon_0 R}$. Answer: (D)
25.
Two spheres suspended, densities given, angle unchanged in liquid of density 0.8 g/cm^3.
Explanation: Buoyancy reduces effective weight by factor $1-\dfrac{\rho_{liquid}}{\rho_{sphere}}$. To keep same angle the electrostatic force must remain same fraction of effective weight, implying dielectric constant $K=\dfrac{1}{1-\rho_{liquid}/\rho_{sphere}}=\dfrac{1}{1-0.8/1.6}=2$. Answer: (C)
