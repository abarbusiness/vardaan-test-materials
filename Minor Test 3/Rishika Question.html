SECTION C (15 Marks)
Ans 11. [5 Marks]
(a) Combination Reaction: CaO(s) + SiO2(g) → CaSiO3(s) (1 Mark)
Reason: Two or more reactants combine to form a single product. (0.5 Mark)

(b) Decomposition Reaction: BaCO3(s) → BaO(s) + CO2(g) (1 Mark)
Reason: A single reactant breaks down into two or more simpler products on heating. (0.5 Mark)

(c) Neutralisation / Double Displacement Reaction: KOH(aq) + HCl(aq) → KCl(aq) + H2O(l) (1 Mark)
Reason: An acid (HCl) reacts with a base (KOH) to produce salt (KCl) and water. (1 Mark)
Ans 12. [5 Marks]
Interdependence: The physical environment (non-living elements like soil, water, air) provides the essential habitat and nutrients for the biological environment (plants, animals, humans) to survive. Conversely, biological elements modify the physical environment (e.g., plants prevent soil erosion and release oxygen). (2 Marks)
Example: Plants (biological) absorb water from the soil (physical) and carbon dioxide from the atmosphere (physical) to prepare food, maintaining atmospheric balance. (1.5 Marks)
Impact of Volcanoes: Volcanoes are an internal disturbance that erupts and alters the lithosphere rapidly. This sudden physical change destroys local habitats (biological environment), causes air pollution (atmosphere), and forces species to migrate or perish, disrupting the balance. (1.5 Marks)
Ans 13. [5 Marks]
(a) Steps for Calcium Phosphate: (2 Marks)
  1. Write the symbols of the cation and anion side-by-side: Ca and PO4
  2. Write their respective valencies below them: Ca (2) and PO4 (3).
  3. Criss-cross the valencies to become the subscripts of the elements.
  4. Resulting formula: Ca3(PO4)2
(b) Chemical Symbols: (3 Marks, 1 each)
  • (i) Iron: Fe
  • (ii) Silver: Ag
  • (iii) Chlorine: Cl
