SECTION A (20 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Find the product of the following integers: (-15) × (-10) × (-2).
2.
State the Distributive Property of multiplication over addition for integers. Provide one mathematical example to illustrate it.
3.
A submarine is situated at 800m below sea level. If it ascends 250m, what is its new position? Express the final answer using an appropriate integer sign.
4.
Write a pair of negative integers whose difference gives 8.
5.
Evaluate: [(-30) ÷ (-6)] ÷ 5
6.
Name the three parallel ranges that make up the majestic Himalayan mountain system.
7.
What were the early forms of democratic assemblies practiced during the Vedic period? Briefly state who comprised them.
8.
Mention any two key differences between the Western Coastal Plains and the Eastern Coastal Plains of India.
9.
Define the terms 'Janapada' and 'Mahajanapada'.
10.
Which geographic region is known as the 'Granary of India', and why is it called so?
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
11.
In a class test containing 15 questions, 4 marks are awarded for every correct answer and (-2) marks are awarded for every incorrect answer. Gurpreet attempts all questions but only 9 of her answers are correct. What is her total score?
12.
Evaluate the following using suitable properties of integers: 26 × (-48) + (-48) × (-36). Also, name the property used.
13.
What was the impact of iron technology on agriculture and warfare during the period of Second Urbanisation in Ancient India?
14.
The Great Indian Desert (Thar Desert) has a harsh climate, yet people have adapted to survive there. Describe the lifestyle, housing, and main occupations of the people living in this region.
15.
Differentiate between Varna and Jati as per the ancient Indian societal structure. Provide at least three points of distinction.
SECTION C (25 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
16.
A merchant earns a profit of ₹15 per bag of basmati rice sold and a loss of ₹5 per bag of non-basmati rice sold.
(i) He sells 3,000 bags of basmati rice and 5,000 bags of non-basmati rice in a month. What is his net profit or loss? (2 Marks)
(ii) What is the number of basmati rice bags he must sell to have neither profit nor loss, if the number of non-basmati rice bags sold is 6,400? (3 Marks)
17.
The temperature at 12 noon was 10°C above zero. If it decreases at the uniform rate of 2°C per hour until midnight:
(i) At what time would the temperature be exactly 8°C below zero? (3 Marks)
(ii) What would be the temperature at midnight? (2 Marks)
18.
Simplify the following mathematical expressions systematically:
(i) [(-36) ÷ 12] ÷ 3
(ii) (-15) × 8 + (-15) × 2
(iii) (-40) ÷ [(-10) + (-10)]
19.
The physical features of India heavily influence its economy and lifestyle. Justify this statement by explaining the economic importance and distinct geographical characteristics of the Peninsular Plateau. How do its rivers differ from those in the Northern Plains?
20.
Give a detailed account of the "Second Urbanisation" in ancient India. In your answer, include the reasons for the emergence of Mahajanapadas in the Ganga plains, the forms of government they practiced (Monarchy vs. Republic), and the role of punch-marked coins in expanding trade.
