SECTION B: LITERATURE (25 Marks)(Julius Caesar - Act 2 to Act 4)
5.
Read the extract given below and answer the questions that follow:
ANTONY:
"Friends, Romans, countrymen, lend me your ears;
I come to bury Caesar, not to praise him.
The evil that men do lives after them;
The good is oft interred with their bones;
So let it be with Caesar."
(i)
Where does this scene take place? Who spoke to the crowd just before Antony? [2]
(ii)
Why does Antony say, "I come to bury Caesar, not to praise him"? What is his actual motive? [2]
(iii)
How does Antony manipulate the word "honourable" during his speech to turn the crowd against the conspirators? [3]
(iv)
What document does Antony produce later in his speech to further incite the mob? Mention one provision of that document. [3]
6.
Read the extract given below and answer the questions that follow:
CAESAR:
"Cowards die many times before their deaths;
The valiant never taste of death but once.
Of all the wonders that I yet have heard,
It seems to me most strange that men should fear;
Seeing that death, a necessary end,
Will come when it will come."
(i)
To whom are these words spoken? What has just happened to prompt the listener to stop Caesar from going out? [2]
(ii)
Explain the meaning of "Cowards die many times before their deaths". [2]
(iii)
Despite this brave speech, who eventually convinces Caesar to go to the Capitol and how does he reinterpret the frightening omens? [3]
(iv)
What tragic irony is embedded in Caesar's refusal to heed the warnings given to him on the Ides of March? [3]
7.
Answer the following question in about 80-100 words: [5 Marks]

In Act 4, Scene 3 (the tent scene at Sardis), a bitter quarrel breaks out between Brutus and Cassius. What are the primary reasons for this conflict, and how does it reveal the fundamental differences in their characters?
