Q1. Differentiate: \( y = 3x^4 - \frac{2}{x^2} + 5\sin(x) \)
[2 Marks]
Given function: \( y = 3x^4 - 2x^{-2} + 5\sin(x) \)
Differentiating w.r.t \( x \):
\(\displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{d}{dx}(3x^4) - \frac{d}{dx}(2x^{-2}) + \frac{d}{dx}(5\sin(x)) \)
\( \displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx} = 3(4x^3) - 2(-2x^{-3}) + 5(\cos(x)) \)
Ans: \( \displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx} = 12x^3 + \frac{4}{x^3} + 5\cos(x) \)
Q2. Evaluate: \( \displaystyle \int (4x^3 + \cos(x) - e^x) \,dx \)
[2 Marks]
Using the property of linearity in integrals:
\( \displaystyle I = \int 4x^3 \,dx + \int \cos(x) \,dx - \int e^x \,dx \)
\( \displaystyle I = 4 \left( \frac{x^4}{4} \right) + \sin(x) - e^x + C \)
(where C is the constant of integration)
Ans: \( I = x^4 + \sin(x) - e^x + C \)
Q3. Find slope of tangent to \( y = x^2 - 4x + 3 \) at \( x = 2 \).
[2 Marks]
The slope of the tangent is given by the derivative \( \frac{dy}{dx} \).
\( \displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{d}{dx}(x^2 - 4x + 3) = 2x - 4 \)
Evaluating at \( x = 2 \):
Slope \( m = 2(2) - 4 = 4 - 4 = 0 \)
Ans: Slope = \( 0 \) (The tangent is horizontal)
Q4. Given \( s(t) = 2t^2 - 3t + 1 \), find velocity at \( t = 3 \)s.
[2 Marks]
Velocity \( v \) is the rate of change of position with respect to time: \( v = \frac{ds}{dt} \).
\( \displaystyle v(t) = \frac{d}{dt}(2t^2 - 3t + 1) = 4t - 3 \)
Substituting \( t = 3 \):
\( v(3) = 4(3) - 3 = 12 - 3 = 9 \)
Ans: Velocity at \( t=3 \)s is \( 9 \text{ m/s} \).
Q5. Evaluate: \( \displaystyle \int_{0}^{\pi/2} \sin(x) \,dx \)
[2 Marks]
The integral of \( \sin(x) \) is \( -\cos(x) \).
\( \displaystyle I = \left[ -\cos(x) \right]_{0}^{\pi/2} \)
Applying the upper and lower limits:
\( I = (-\cos(\frac{\pi}{2})) - (-\cos(0)) \)
Since \( \cos(\frac{\pi}{2}) = 0 \) and \( \cos(0) = 1 \):
\( I = (-0) - (-1) = 1 \)
Ans: \( 1 \)

Q6. Differentiate \( y = (2x + 3)^5 \) using the chain rule.
[3 Marks]
Let \( u = 2x + 3 \). Then \( y = u^5 \).
By chain rule: \( \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy}{du} \cdot \frac{du}{dx} \)
Step 1: \( \displaystyle \frac{dy}{du} = 5u^4 \)
Step 2: \( \displaystyle \frac{du}{dx} = \frac{d}{dx}(2x + 3) = 2 \)
Multiplying them together:
\( \displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx} = 5(2x + 3)^4 \cdot 2 \)
Ans: \( \displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx} = 10(2x + 3)^4 \)
Q7. \( v(t) = 3t^2 - 2t \). Starts from origin. Find position \( x(t) \) at \( t = 2 \)s.
[3 Marks]
Position \( x(t) \) is the integral of velocity: \( \displaystyle x(t) = \int v(t) \,dt \)
\( \displaystyle x(t) = \int (3t^2 - 2t) \,dt = \frac{3t^3}{3} - \frac{2t^2}{2} + C \)
\( x(t) = t^3 - t^2 + C \)
Initial condition: At \( t=0, x=0 \) (starts from origin).
\( 0 = 0^3 - 0^2 + C \implies C = 0 \)
So, general position equation is \( x(t) = t^3 - t^2 \).
At \( t = 2 \)s:
\( x(2) = (2)^3 - (2)^2 = 8 - 4 = 4 \)
Ans: Position at \( t=2 \)s is \( 4 \text{ m} \).
Q8. Use product rule for \( y = x^2 \sin(x) \).
[3 Marks]
The product rule states: \( \frac{d}{dx}(u \cdot v) = u\frac{dv}{dx} + v\frac{du}{dx} \)
Let \( u = x^2 \) and \( v = \sin(x) \).
\( \frac{du}{dx} = 2x \)
\( \frac{dv}{dx} = \cos(x) \)
Substitute into the formula:
\( \displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx} = x^2 \cdot \cos(x) + \sin(x) \cdot 2x \)
Ans: \( \displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx} = x^2\cos(x) + 2x\sin(x) \)
Q9. Area under \( y = 3x^2 \) from \( x = 1 \) to \( x = 3 \).
[3 Marks]
Area under a curve is given by the definite integral: \( \displaystyle A = \int_{a}^{b} y \,dx \)
\( \displaystyle A = \int_{1}^{3} 3x^2 \,dx \)
Evaluating the integral:
\( \displaystyle A = \left[ 3 \cdot \frac{x^3}{3} \right]_{1}^{3} = \left[ x^3 \right]_{1}^{3} \)
Applying limits:
\( A = (3^3) - (1^3) = 27 - 1 = 26 \)
Ans: Area = \( 26 \text{ sq. units} \)
Q10. Use quotient rule for \( \displaystyle y = \frac{\ln x}{x} \).
[3 Marks]
The quotient rule states: \( \displaystyle \frac{d}{dx}\left(\frac{u}{v}\right) = \frac{v\frac{du}{dx} - u\frac{dv}{dx}}{v^2} \)
Let \( u = \ln(x) \) and \( v = x \).
\( \displaystyle \frac{du}{dx} = \frac{1}{x} \)
\( \displaystyle \frac{dv}{dx} = 1 \)
Substitute into the formula:
\( \displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{x \cdot (\frac{1}{x}) - \ln(x) \cdot 1}{x^2} \)
Simplify the numerator:
\( x \cdot (\frac{1}{x}) = 1 \)
Ans: \( \displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{1 - \ln(x)}{x^2} \)

Q11. \( \displaystyle x(t) = \frac{t^3}{3} - 2t^2 + 3t + 5 \). Find \( v, a \), rest times, and acceleration at rest.
[5 Marks]
(a) Velocity and Acceleration:
\( \displaystyle v(t) = \frac{dx}{dt} = \frac{d}{dt}\left( \frac{t^3}{3} - 2t^2 + 3t + 5 \right) \)
\( \displaystyle v(t) = \frac{3t^2}{3} - 4t + 3 = t^2 - 4t + 3 \)

\( \displaystyle a(t) = \frac{dv}{dt} = \frac{d}{dt}(t^2 - 4t + 3) = 2t - 4 \)
(b) Time(s) when momentarily at rest:
Particle is at rest when \( v(t) = 0 \).
\( t^2 - 4t + 3 = 0 \)
Factorizing the quadratic equation:
\( t^2 - 3t - t + 3 = 0 \)
\( t(t - 3) - 1(t - 3) = 0 \)
\( (t - 1)(t - 3) = 0 \)
Therefore, the particle is at rest at \( t = 1 \)s and \( t = 3 \)s.
(c) Acceleration at these times:
At \( t = 1 \)s: \( a(1) = 2(1) - 4 = -2 \text{ m/s}^2 \)
At \( t = 3 \)s: \( a(3) = 2(3) - 4 = 2 \text{ m/s}^2 \)
Q12. \( a(t) = 6t - 4 \). Initial velocity = 2, Initial pos = 5. Derive \( v(t) \) and \( x(t) \).
[5 Marks]
Step 1: Finding Velocity \( v(t) \)
\( \displaystyle v(t) = \int a(t) \,dt = \int (6t - 4) \,dt \)
\( \displaystyle v(t) = \frac{6t^2}{2} - 4t + C_1 = 3t^2 - 4t + C_1 \)
Using initial condition for velocity: At \( t = 0, v = 2 \)
\( 2 = 3(0)^2 - 4(0) + C_1 \implies C_1 = 2 \)
\( \therefore v(t) = 3t^2 - 4t + 2 \)
Step 2: Finding Position \( x(t) \)
\( \displaystyle x(t) = \int v(t) \,dt = \int (3t^2 - 4t + 2) \,dt \)
\( \displaystyle x(t) = \frac{3t^3}{3} - \frac{4t^2}{2} + 2t + C_2 \)
\( \displaystyle x(t) = t^3 - 2t^2 + 2t + C_2 \)
Using initial condition for position: At \( t = 0, x = 5 \)
\( 5 = (0)^3 - 2(0)^2 + 2(0) + C_2 \implies C_2 = 5 \)
Ans:
\( v(t) = 3t^2 - 4t + 2 \)
\( x(t) = t^3 - 2t^2 + 2t + 5 \)
Q13. Find local maxima and minima for \( y = x^3 - 3x^2 - 9x + 5 \).
[5 Marks]
Step 1: Find First Derivative \( \frac{dy}{dx} \)
\( \displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx} = 3x^2 - 6x - 9 \)
Step 2: Find Critical Points (set \( \frac{dy}{dx} = 0 \))
\( 3(x^2 - 2x - 3) = 0 \)
\( x^2 - 3x + x - 3 = 0 \)
\( (x - 3)(x + 1) = 0 \)
Critical points are \( x = 3 \) and \( x = -1 \).
Step 3: Second Derivative Test
\( \displaystyle \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = \frac{d}{dx}(3x^2 - 6x - 9) = 6x - 6 \)

Check \( x = -1 \):
\( \frac{d^2y}{dx^2}\big|_{x=-1} = 6(-1) - 6 = -12 \) (Negative \( \implies \) Local Maximum)
Maximum Value = \( (-1)^3 - 3(-1)^2 - 9(-1) + 5 = -1 - 3 + 9 + 5 = 10 \)

Check \( x = 3 \):
\( \frac{d^2y}{dx^2}\big|_{x=3} = 6(3) - 6 = 12 \) (Positive \( \implies \) Local Minimum)
Minimum Value = \( (3)^3 - 3(3)^2 - 9(3) + 5 = 27 - 27 - 27 + 5 = -22 \)
Ans:
Local Max at \( x = -1 \), Max Value = \( 10 \)
Local Min at \( x = 3 \), Min Value = \( -22 \)
Q14. Evaluate Integral and Product Rule Differentiation.
[5 Marks]
(a) Evaluate: \( \displaystyle \int_{1}^{4} \left(\sqrt{x} + \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}}\right) \,dx \) [3 Marks]

Rewrite function as powers: \( x^{1/2} + x^{-1/2} \)
Integral = \( \displaystyle \left[ \frac{x^{3/2}}{3/2} + \frac{x^{1/2}}{1/2} \right]_{1}^{4} = \left[ \frac{2}{3}x^{3/2} + 2x^{1/2} \right]_{1}^{4} \)

Substitute Upper Limit (4):
\( \displaystyle \frac{2}{3}(4)^{3/2} + 2(4)^{1/2} = \frac{2}{3}(8) + 2(2) = \frac{16}{3} + 4 = \frac{16 + 12}{3} = \frac{28}{3} \)

Substitute Lower Limit (1):
\( \displaystyle \frac{2}{3}(1)^{3/2} + 2(1)^{1/2} = \frac{2}{3} + 2 = \frac{2 + 6}{3} = \frac{8}{3} \)

Final Value = Upper - Lower = \( \displaystyle \frac{28}{3} - \frac{8}{3} = \frac{20}{3} \)
(b) If \( y = e^{2x} \ln(x) \), find \( \frac{dy}{dx} \) [2 Marks]

Apply Product Rule: \( (uv)' = uv' + vu' \)
\( u = e^{2x} \implies u' = 2e^{2x} \) (Chain Rule applied)
\( v = \ln(x) \implies v' = \frac{1}{x} \)

\( \displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx} = e^{2x}\left(\frac{1}{x}\right) + \ln(x)(2e^{2x}) \)
\( \displaystyle \frac{dy}{dx} = e^{2x} \left( \frac{1}{x} + 2\ln(x) \right) \)
Q15. Vector Resultant and Sketch of \( y = -e^{-x} \).
[5 Marks]
(a) Resultant Unit Vector [3 Marks]

Given vectors:
\( \vec{A} = 3\hat{i} - 2\hat{j} + \hat{k} \)
\( \vec{B} = -2\hat{i} + 5\hat{j} - 3\hat{k} \)

Resultant \( \vec{R} = \vec{A} + \vec{B} \)
\( \vec{R} = (3 - 2)\hat{i} + (-2 + 5)\hat{j} + (1 - 3)\hat{k} \)
\( \vec{R} = \hat{i} + 3\hat{j} - 2\hat{k} \)

Magnitude \( |\vec{R}| = \sqrt{(1)^2 + (3)^2 + (-2)^2} = \sqrt{1 + 9 + 4} = \sqrt{14} \)

Unit Vector \( \hat{R} = \frac{\vec{R}}{|\vec{R}|} \)
\( \hat{R} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{14}} (\hat{i} + 3\hat{j} - 2\hat{k}) \)
(b) Graph Sketch for \( y = -e^{-x}, x \ge 0 \) [2 Marks]

- Standard graph \( e^{-x} \) starts at \( (0, 1) \) and decays to \( 0 \) as \( x \to \infty \).
- The negative sign flips this graph upside down.
- At \( x = 0 \), \( y = -e^0 = -1 \).
- As \( x \to \infty \), \( e^{-x} \to 0 \), so \( y \to 0 \) from the negative side.

Sketch Description:
Axes: Standard X (horizontal) and Y (vertical).
Curve: Starts at the point \( (0, -1) \) on the y-axis.
It curves upwards steadily in the 4th quadrant, getting infinitely closer to the positive x-axis (horizontal asymptote \( y = 0 \)) without ever touching it.
