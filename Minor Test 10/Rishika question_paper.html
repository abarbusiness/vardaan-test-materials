SECTION A: GRAMMAR (15 Marks)(Sentences)
1.
Identify the kind of sentence (Assertive/Declarative, Interrogative, Imperative, or Exclamatory) for each of the following: [5 × 1 = 5]
(i)
Alas! We have lost the final match by one goal.
(ii)
Please wait for me at the bus stop until I arrive.
(iii)
The mighty Himalayas act as a natural barrier for our country.
(iv)
Why did you not complete your assignment on time?
(v)
Never speak ill of others behind their backs.
2.
Separate the Subject and the Predicate in the following sentences: [5 × 1 = 5]
(i)
The old man with a heavy walking stick crossed the busy street.
(ii)
Under the huge banyan tree sat the wise village elder.
(iii)
Barking dogs seldom bite.
(iv)
A fleet of ships was anchored near the harbor.
(v)
On the top of the hill lives a hermit.
3.
Transform the following sentences as directed in the brackets without changing their core meaning: [5 × 1 = 5]
(i)
She is a very talented singer. (Change into Exclamatory)
(ii)
Can a leopard change its spots? (Change into Assertive)
(iii)
What a terrifying nightmare it was! (Change into Assertive)
(iv)
He is an honest boy. (Change into Negative)
(v)
Please open the windows. (Change into Interrogative)
SECTION B: LITERATURE (25 Marks)("A Hero" & "I Dream a World")
4.
Read the extract given below and answer the questions that follow: [5 × 1 = 5]
"Leave alone strength, can you prove you have courage? Let me see if you can sleep alone tonight in my office room."
(i)
Who is the speaker of the above lines and to whom are they addressed?
(ii)
What incident or news sparked this conversation between the two?
(iii)
Where did the listener usually sleep every night?
(iv)
What was the listener's immediate reaction to this challenge?
(v)
Find a word from the extract which means the opposite of 'cowardice'.
5.
Answer ANY FOUR of the following questions in 30-40 words each: [4 × 3 = 12]
(a)
Why did Swami's father insist that he should sleep alone in the office room? What was he trying to teach him?
(b)
Describe the terrifying nightmare that Swami experienced while sleeping under the wooden bench.
(c)
In the poem "I Dream a World", what kind of world does Langston Hughes envision for mankind?
(d)
How does the poet personify 'wretchedness' and 'greed' in the poem? What effect do they have on the world?
(e)
How did Swami's classmates and teachers react the next day? Was Swami genuinely a brave boy?
6.
Answer ANY ONE of the following questions in 80-100 words: [1 × 8 = 8]
(a)
"Courage is everything, strength and age are not important." Do you agree with this statement made by Swami's father? Discuss with reference to the story "A Hero", highlighting the irony in how Swami eventually proved his father right.

OR
(b)
The poem "I Dream a World" is a powerful plea for equality, peace, and universal brotherhood. Elaborate on the poet's vision and how it contrasts with the harsh realities of prejudice and discrimination in our current world.

You are Rohan/Rhea residing at 45, Gulmohar Park, New Delhi.
