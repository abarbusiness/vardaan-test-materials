Q14. Conceptual Questions:
(a) Finite vs Non-finite Verbs:
A Finite verb changes its form according to the tense, person, and number of the subject. It acts as the main verb of a clause.
Example: She plays piano. (Changes to 'played' in past tense).

A Non-finite verb does not change its form according to tense, person, or number. It does not act as the main verb but functions as a noun, adjective, or adverb (Infinitives, Gerunds, Participles).
Example: She likes to play piano. ('to play' remains unchanged regardless of subject/tense).
[2.5 Marks]
(b) Preposition vs Conjunction:
A Preposition is a word placed before a noun or pronoun to show its relationship with other words in the sentence (indicating time, place, direction, etc.).
Example: The book is on the table.

A Conjunction is a joining word used to connect words, phrases, or clauses together.
Example: I brought a pen and a notebook.
[2.5 Marks]
