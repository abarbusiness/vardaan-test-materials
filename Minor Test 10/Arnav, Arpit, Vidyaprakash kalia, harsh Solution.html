SECTION A: GRAMMAR (20 Marks)
Q1. Change into Indirect Speech. Ensure proper tense and pronoun changes.
10 Marks [5 x 2]
(a) The principal said to the students, "The school will remain closed tomorrow due to heavy rain."
Ans:
The principal told the students that the school would remain closed the next day (or the following day) due to heavy rain.
Reasoning: 'said to' changes to 'told'. Conjunction 'that' is added. 'will' changes to 'would' (past form in reported speech). 'tomorrow' changes to 'the next day'.
(b) She asked me, "Have you submitted your final assignment to the coordinator?"
Ans:
She asked me if (or whether) I had submitted my final assignment to the coordinator.
Reasoning: Yes/No question, so 'if/whether' is used. Present perfect ('have submitted') changes to past perfect ('had submitted'). 'you' changes to 'I', and 'your' changes to 'my'.
(c) The doctor advised the patient, "Do not eat oily food and exercise daily."
Ans:
The doctor advised the patient not to eat oily food and to exercise daily.
Reasoning: Imperative sentence. Negative command 'Do not' changes to 'not to + verb'. The reporting verb is already 'advised' which is perfectly suited here.
(d) "What a magnificent performance you gave on stage!" exclaimed the director.
Ans:
The director exclaimed with admiration/joy that he/she had given a very magnificent performance on stage.
Reasoning: Exclamatory sentence changes to assertive. 'gave' (V2 - Simple Past) changes to 'had given' (Past Perfect). The word 'very' or 'truly' is added to show the intensity of 'What a'.
(e) He said, "I was studying for my exams when the lights went out."
Ans:
He said that he had been studying for his exams when the lights went out (or had gone out).
Reasoning: Past Continuous ('was studying') changes to Past Perfect Continuous ('had been studying'). Past simple ('went') can change to 'had gone', though keeping it as 'went' is also grammatically acceptable in time clauses.
Q2. Transform the sentences from Active to Passive Voice or vice versa.
10 Marks [5 x 2]
(a) The management is continuously ignoring the legitimate demands of the workers. (Active)
Ans:
The legitimate demands of the workers are continuously being ignored by the management.
Rule: Present Continuous active (is/are + v-ing) becomes (is/are + being + V3) in passive. Plural object ('demands') takes 'are'.
(b) By whom was this exquisite portrait painted in the 18th century? (Passive)
Ans:
Who painted this exquisite portrait in the 18th century?
Rule: 'By whom' changes back to 'Who'. Past simple passive ('was painted') reverts to past simple active ('painted').
(c) Please maintain absolute silence inside the library premises. (Active)
Ans:
You are requested to maintain absolute silence inside the library premises.
(OR) Absolute silence must be maintained inside the library premises.
Rule: Sentences starting with 'Please' are usually changed using 'You are requested to'. Using modals like 'should be' or 'must be' is also correct.
(d) The government will have completed the new highway project before the next elections. (Active)
Ans:
The new highway project will have been completed by the government before the next elections.
Rule: Future Perfect (will have + V3) becomes (will have been + V3). The object 'the new highway project' becomes the subject.
(e) They expected the scientist to win the Nobel Prize for his groundbreaking discovery. (Active)
Ans:
It was expected by them that the scientist would win the Nobel Prize for his groundbreaking discovery.
(OR) The scientist was expected to win the Nobel Prize for his groundbreaking discovery.
Rule: Complex sentence structure. The second answer is more idiomatic and preferred in modern English grammar.

Q3. Notice Writing: Inter-School Drama Competition
10 Marks
Model Answer (Format & Content):
ST. XAVIER'S HIGH SCHOOL, DELHI
NOTICE
14 October 202X
Selection Trials for Inter-School Drama Competition
This is to inform all students from Classes VIII to XII that our school is participating in the District Level Inter-School Drama Competition scheduled for next month. Selection trials to finalize the school drama troupe will be held as per the details given below:

Date: 20 October 202X
Time: 10:00 AM onwards
Venue: School Auditorium

Interested students with a flair for acting should register their names with the undersigned latest by 18 October 202X. For further queries, contact the undersigned.
Ananya / Aarav
(Cultural Secretary)

SECTION C: LONG WRITING SKILLS (20 Marks)
Q4. Formal Letter (Choice 1): Complaint to Municipal Commissioner regarding dug-up roads.
20 Marks
Model Answer:
45, Green Park
Civil Lines
Kanpur

14 October 202X

The Municipal Commissioner
Kanpur Municipal Corporation
Kanpur

Subject: Request for immediate repair of dug-up road in Green Park locality.

Sir/Madam,

Through this letter, I wish to draw your urgent attention to the deplorable condition of the main road leading to the Green Park locality in Civil Lines.

The road was excavated by the civic authorities approximately two months ago to lay a new sewer line. While the underground pipe-laying work was completed weeks ago, the road has been left unattended and heavily dug up. Mountains of rubble and deep trenches make it nearly impossible for vehicles to pass, resulting in severe daily traffic jams during peak hours.

Moreover, the open trenches pose a massive risk to the safety of pedestrians, especially the elderly and school-going children. We have witnessed multiple minor accidents involving two-wheelers skidding on the loose debris at night due to poor street lighting.

Despite repeated verbal complaints to the local ward officers, no action has been initiated. I earnestly request you to instruct the concerned department to clear the rubble and reconstruct the road immediately to restore normalcy and prevent any fatal accidents.

Thanking you.

Yours faithfully,
Siddharth / Smriti
(Resident, Green Park)
Marking Breakdown (CBSE/ICSE Standard Scaled to 20):
Format (Sender's address, Date, Receiver's address, Subject, Salutation, Closing) : \( 5 \) Marks
Content (Clear statement of issue, elaboration on effects/risks, call to action) : \( 10 \) Marks
Expression (Coherence, Vocabulary, Grammatical accuracy) : \( 5 \) Marks
Total: \( 5 + 10 + 5 = 20 \) Marks
Q4. Formal Letter (Choice 2 - OR): Letter to Editor regarding social media influence on teenagers.
20 Marks
Model Answer Overview (Key Points):
  • Format: Sender's Add -> Date -> The Editor, The Times of India, New Delhi -> Subject -> Sir/Madam -> Body -> Yours truly, Rakesh/Rupali.
  • Introductory Paragraph: Express desire to use the columns of the esteemed newspaper to highlight the alarming impact of unchecked social media usage on teenagers.
  • Main Body (The Problem): Constant scrolling leads to sleep deprivation, lack of physical activity, and severe distraction from academics. Unrealistic standards online trigger anxiety, depression, and self-esteem issues among the youth.
  • Concluding Paragraph (Solutions): Parents should implement 'digital-free zones' and fixed screen times. Schools must conduct counseling sessions on digital detox and cyber hygiene.
Teacher's Tip for Grading: Ensure the student maintains a formal, objective tone. The distinction between a complaint letter (direct action requested) and a letter to the editor (raising awareness) must be evident.
