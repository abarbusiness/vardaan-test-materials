SECTION A SOLUTIONS (10 Marks)
Ans 1.
Change to Indirect: The teacher said to the students, "Have you completed your assignments?"
[2 Marks]
Answer: The teacher asked the students if (or whether) they had completed their assignments.
Explanation: The reporting verb changes to 'asked'. Since it is a Yes/No question, the conjunction 'if' or 'whether' is used. Present perfect tense changes to past perfect.
Ans 2.
Change to Indirect: She said, "Alas! I have failed the entrance test."
[2 Marks]
Answer: She exclaimed with sorrow that she had failed the entrance test.
Explanation: 'Alas' denotes sadness, so reporting verb becomes 'exclaimed with sorrow'. The present perfect tense changes to past perfect ('had failed').
Ans 3.
Change to Indirect: My mother said to me, "Do not waste your time playing video games."
[2 Marks]
Answer: My mother advised (or warned) me not to waste my time playing video games.
Explanation: Imperative negative sentence. "Said to" changes to "advised". "Do not" changes to "not to". Pronoun 'your' changes to 'my'.
Ans 4.
Fill in the blanks with determiners: (a) ___ knowledge... (b) Is there ___ coffee...
[1+1=2 Marks]
Answer:
(a) A little knowledge is a dangerous thing.
(b) Is there any coffee left in the pot?
Explanation: 'A little' means a small amount (positive sense), which fits the proverb. 'Any' is used in interrogative sentences for uncountable nouns.
Ans 5.
Choose the correct determiner: (a) He has ___ experience... (b) ___ the brothers...
[1+1=2 Marks]
Answer:
(a) He has much experience in software development.
(b) Both the brothers are participating in the marathon.
Explanation: 'Experience' is an uncountable noun, so 'much' is used. 'Both' is used for a specific pair of two, followed by 'the'.
SECTION B SOLUTIONS (10 Marks)
Ans 6.
Change to Direct: The manager ordered the clerk to bring the file immediately.
[2 Marks]
Answer: The manager said to the clerk, "Bring the file immediately."
Explanation: The reporting verb 'ordered' indicates an imperative sentence in direct speech.
Ans 7.
Change to Direct: He asked me where I was going for my summer vacation.
[2 Marks]
Answer: He said to me, "Where are you going for your summer vacation?"
Explanation: 'Asked' reverts to 'said to'. The past continuous tense ('was going') reverts to present continuous ('are going').
Ans 8.
Identify and correct the determiner error: (a) I don't have some money... (b) She has read whole books...
[1+1=2 Marks]
Answer:
(a) I don't have any money to lend you right now.
(b) She has read all the books in the public library.
Explanation: 'Any' is used in negative sentences, not 'some'. 'Whole' is used with singular nouns (e.g., the whole book), whereas 'all' is used with plural nouns.
Ans 9.
Insert suitable determiners: (a) ___ boy... (b) I have bought ___ apples.
[1+1=2 Marks]
Answer:
(a) Each (or Every) boy in the class was given a certificate of participation.
(b) I have bought some fresh apples from the market.
Explanation: 'Each/Every' correctly distributes the singular noun 'boy'. 'Some' is used in affirmative sentences for countable plurals.
Ans 10.
Change to Indirect (Focus on demonstrative determiners): John said, "This is the best book I have ever read."
[2 Marks]
Answer: John said that that was the best book he had ever read.
Explanation: The demonstrative determiner 'this' changes to 'that'. The first 'that' is the conjunction, and the second 'that' replaces 'this'. Present perfect ('have ever read') changes to past perfect ('had ever read').

Respected Sir/Madam,

Through the columns of your esteemed newspaper, I wish to draw the attention of the concerned authorities towards the growing nuisance of loudspeakers in our locality.

Applied Grammar: (A) Dialogue Conversion and (B) Cloze Passage - Determiners.
[10 Marks]
(A) Dialogue Conversion [\(5\) Marks]
The doctor asked the patient how he was feeling that day. The patient replied that he had a severe headache. The doctor then inquired if he had taken the medicine he had prescribed the previous day. The patient replied in the affirmative but added that it had not helped much.
Note: Give \(1\frac{1}{4}\) marks for each correct sentence conversion (ensuring time/pronoun/tense shifts).

(B) Cloze Passage - Determiners [\(5 \times 1 = 5\) Marks]
Once upon a time, there lived (i) a king who was highly respected. He had (ii) much (or a lot of) wealth in his treasury, but he never spent (iii) any of it on his own luxuries. (iv) All (or The) people in his kingdom loved him dearly because he patiently listened to (v) every problem they faced and tried to solve it.
