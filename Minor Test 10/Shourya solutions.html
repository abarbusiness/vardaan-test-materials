SECTION B: LITERATURE (20 Marks)
Q4. Read the extract and answer:
"The way a crow / Shook down on me / The dust of snow / From a hemlock tree"
(a) Name the poem and poet. (b) Where was the poet and his state of mind? (c) What is dust of snow and how did it fall? (d) Symbolism of crow and hemlock tree.
Answer: 10 Marks
(a) The poem is "Dust of Snow" and the poet is Robert Frost. (2 Marks)

(b) The poet was sitting (or standing) under a hemlock tree. His state of mind was deeply sorrowful, depressed, and gloomy. He was regretting the day and felt it was ruined. (2 Marks)

(c) "Dust of snow" refers to the fine, powdery particles of snow that had settled on the branches of the tree. It fell on the poet because a crow alighted on, took off from, or simply shook the branch of the hemlock tree, causing the snow to fall. (2 Marks)

(d) The 'crow' and the 'hemlock tree' symbolize sorrow, gloom, and negativity. The hemlock is a poisonous tree, and crows are traditionally associated with bad omens and darkness.
The poet has deliberately chosen them over conventionally 'beautiful' aspects of nature (like a nightingale or a pine tree) to show that even seemingly negative, dark, or ordinary elements of nature possess the power to heal and bring joy. It breaks the stigma around these so-called 'bad omens'. (4 Marks)
Marking: Marks distributed as indicated. For (d), 2 marks for identifying the symbolism (gloom/negativity) and 2 marks for explaining why the poet chose them (to show healing comes from unexpected places).
Q5. Answer the following questions in 40-50 words:
(a) How does the falling of the dust of snow bring about a change in the poet's mood?
(b) Elaborate on the central theme of the poem.
Answer: 10 Marks (4+6)
(a) Change in Mood:
Before the snow fell, the poet was in a state of despair and felt his day was a total waste. However, the sudden, soft, and cold touch of the fine "dust of snow" falling on him shook him out of his gloomy thoughts. It refreshed his senses, gladdened his heart, and uplifted his spirit. He realized that a part of his day was saved, and he could spend the rest of it happily. (4 Marks)

(b) Central Theme:
The central theme of "Dust of Snow" is the healing power of nature and the significance of small, ordinary events in our lives.
The poem teaches us that we do not always need grand gestures to feel happy; even a trivial incident—like snow falling from a poisonous tree shaken by a crow—can completely transform our mindset from sadness to joy. Furthermore, it dismantles the idea of 'bad omens' (crow, hemlock), emphasizing that nature, in all its forms, has the supreme capability to cure human sorrow and bestow a fresh perspective on life. (6 Marks)
Marking: (a) 2 marks for stating the initial mood and 2 marks for explaining how it was uplifted by the sensory experience. (b) 2 marks for 'healing power of nature', 2 marks for 'small events have large impacts', and 2 marks for 'breaking the idea of bad omens'. Deduct marks for poor grammar and exceeding word limits unnecessarily.
