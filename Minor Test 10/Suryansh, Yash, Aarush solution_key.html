Answer Key & Marking Scheme - Minor Test 10 (Class 7)
SECTION A (10 Marks)
Ans 1.
The principal said to the students, "Do not make noise in the library."
Marking: 2 Marks. 0.5 for 'The', 0.5 for comma before quotes, 0.5 for quotes & capital 'D', 0.5 for full stop inside closing quotes.
Ans 2.
Prepositions: into, with, in (Any two)
Marking: 1 mark each for identifying any two correct prepositions.
Ans 3.
Rajat was severely penalized because / as / since he had broken the traffic rules.
(OR: Because / As / Since Rajat had broken the traffic rules, he was severely penalized.)
Marking: 2 marks for a grammatically correct sentence using a valid causal conjunction.
Ans 4.
The science exhibition will begin at 10:00 AM on Monday.
Marking: 1 mark for each correct preposition.
Ans 5.
It's a pity that the dog's tail was severely injured in the accident.
Marking: 0.5 for capital 'I', 0.5 for apostrophe in It's, 0.5 for apostrophe in dog's, 0.5 for full stop.
SECTION B (20 Marks)
Ans 6.
Mr. Sharma, the new science teacher, entered the classroom and said, "Good morning students. Today we will learn about the fascinating solar system."
Marking: 4 marks overall. Key checkpoints: Capital 'M' & 'S', full stop after Mr (optional in British English, accept both), commas around apposition ("the new science teacher"), comma after said, quote marks, capital 'G' and 'T', punctuation inside quotes. Deduct 0.5 for each omission/error up to 4 marks.
Ans 7.
a) with (agree with a person)
b) of (afraid of)
c) upon / onto / on
d) between (used for two people)
Marking: 1 mark for each correct fixed/phrasal preposition.
Ans 8.
a) She can neither read nor write French.
b) He is not only a talented singer but also a phenomenal dancer.
Marking: 2 marks for each correct use of correlative conjunctions.
Ans 9.
a) since (point of time)
b) across / over (over is acceptable if students write it, but from options it is 'across')
c) while
d) because of
Marking: 1 mark for each correct choice.
Ans 10.
a) He prefers drinking black coffee to herbal tea. (Error: than -> to)
b) Though he is extremely poor, he is honest. / Although/Though he is extremely poor, yet he is honest. (Error: remove 'but' or replace with 'yet')
c) I have been waiting here for the bus for two hours. (Error: from -> for)
d) The furious principal entered the classroom. (Error: remove 'into'. 'Enter' does not take a preposition when meaning physical entry.)
Marking: 1 mark for each completely correct and grammatically sound sentence.
SECTION C (20 Marks)
Ans 11.
Father said, "Why are you looking so sad?"
Rita replied, "I lost my new fountain pen."
Father smiled and said, "Don't worry, we will buy another one this evening."
Marking: 5 marks overall. Deduct 0.5 for each error. Essential: Question mark for Father's query, apostrophe in Don't, comma separating dialog, proper caps for Father and Rita.
Ans 12.
(a) at / before
(b) through / in
(c) until / till / before
(d) over / across / via
(e) but / although / though / even though
Marking: 1 mark for each correct blank. Accept contextually valid alternatives.
Ans 13.
a) Although / Though / Even though the man is very old, he can still run incredibly fast.
b) You must apologize to the teacher, otherwise / or else you will be suspended.
c) In spite of / Despite being severely ill, she attended the mandatory meeting. (OR: Although she was severely ill, she attended...)
d) As soon as / After he finished his homework quickly, he went out to play cricket.
e) Work hard right now or / otherwise you will regret it later. (OR: If you do not work hard right now, you will...)
Marking: 1 mark for each correctly joined sentence.
