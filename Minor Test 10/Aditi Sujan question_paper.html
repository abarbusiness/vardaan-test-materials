The 1943 copper penny is worth a lot of money. Copper was hard to get during the war because it was primarily being used for military purposes, such as manufacturing ammunition and communication wires. Therefore, the government minted pennies using zinc-coated steel instead. However, a small batch of pennies was accidentally minted in copper, making them exceedingly rare today. Similarly, the 1955 double die penny is also highly valuable. These pennies were stamped twice by accident during the minting process, resulting in a distinct doubled image on the coin's face. Coin collectors covet these rare errors, and they are willing to pay thousands of dollars to add them to their collections.
