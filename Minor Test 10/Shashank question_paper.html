General Instructions:
  • The question paper comprises three sections: Section A (Grammar Fundamentals), Section B (Applied Grammar), and Section C (Writing Skills).

  • Write neatly and clearly. Number your answers according to the question paper.
SECTION A: GRAMMAR FUNDAMENTALS (20 Marks)
1.
Identify the type of sentence (Declarative, Interrogative, Imperative, or Exclamatory) for each of the following: [5 × 1 = 5]
(i)
How wonderfully the children performed on stage!
(ii)
Please submit your assignments before the deadline.
(iii)
The Earth revolves around the Sun in an elliptical orbit.
(iv)
Where did you keep the library books?
(v)
Do not step on the fresh grass.
2.
Identify the Nouns in the following sentences and state their kinds (Proper, Common, Collective, or Abstract): [5 × 1 = 5]
(i)
Honesty is the best policy.
(ii)
A swarm of bees attacked the bear.
(iii)
Kolkata is situated on the banks of the river Hooghly.
(iv)
The woodcutter lost his axe in the river.
(v)
We must respect our teachers.
3.
Fill in the blanks with suitable Articles (a, an, the). Put a cross (X) where no article is required: [5 × 1 = 5]
(i)
He is _______ honorable man who always keeps his promises.
(ii)
_______ Ganges is considered a holy river in India.
(iii)
I saw _______ one-eyed man walking down the street yesterday.
(iv)
_______ gold is a precious metal.
(v)
She is carrying _______ umbrella because it is raining.
4.
Fill in the blanks with suitable Pronouns as directed in the brackets: [5 × 1 = 5]
(i)
_______ is the boy who won the gold medal. (Demonstrative Pronoun)
(ii)
The dog hurt _______ while jumping over the fence. (Reflexive Pronoun)
(iii)
_______ are you talking to? (Interrogative Pronoun)
(iv)
Please give _______ a glass of water, I am thirsty. (Personal Pronoun)
(v)
This book is mine, and that is _______. (Possessive Pronoun)
SECTION B: APPLIED GRAMMAR (20 Marks)
5.
A. Identify the Adjectives in the following sentences and state their kinds: [5 × 1 = 5]
(i)
The foolish crow tried to sing.
(ii)
I have bought some apples from the market.
(iii)
Which pen do you prefer?
(iv)
He was the first man to step on the moon.
(v)
Those mangoes are very sweet.

B. Fill in the blanks with the correct Degree of Comparison of the adjective given in brackets: [5 × 1 = 5]
(i)
Mount Everest is the _______ peak in the world. (high)
(ii)
Prevention is _______ than cure. (good)
(iii)
Iron is _______ than any other metal. (useful)
(iv)
She is as _______ as her sister. (smart)
(v)
Today is the _______ day of the season. (hot)
6.
A. Separate the Subject and the Predicate in the following sentences: [5 × 1 = 5]
(i)
The early bird catches the worm.
(ii)
Barking dogs seldom bite.
(iii)
Up went the balloon.
(iv)
My mother and my aunt are arriving today.
(v)
A wise owl lived in an oak tree.

B. Transform the following sentences as directed in the brackets: [5 × 1 = 5]
(i)
She is a very intelligent student. (Change into Exclamatory)
(ii)
What a beautiful flower the rose is! (Change into Declarative)
(iii)
Leave the room at once. (Change into Interrogative)
(iv)
He did not finish his homework. (Change into Affirmative/Positive)
(v)
Are you coming to the party tonight? (Change into Declarative)
SECTION C: WRITING SKILLS (10 Marks)
