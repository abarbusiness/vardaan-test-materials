SOLUTIONS & MARKING SCHEME - MINOR TEST (MIT 10)

Class: 9 CBSE | Subject: English Lang. & Lit. | Max Marks: 50

SECTION A: GRAMMAR (20 Marks)
Q1. Fill in the blanks with determiners: (a) ____ (a little/few) more milk (b) ____ (Every/All) student...
Answer: 2 Marks
(a) a little (Used with uncountable nouns like milk).
(b) Every (Followed by a singular noun 'student').
Marking: 1 mark for each correct answer.
Q2. Complete the sentences with determiners: (a) hardly ____ (some/any) space (b) ____ (Neither/Either) of the two roads...
Answer: 2 Marks
(a) any (Used in negative contexts; 'hardly' implies a negative meaning).
(b) Neither (Used to negate both options out of two).
Marking: 1 mark for each correct determiner.
Q3. Fill in the blanks with correct tenses: (a) the movie ____ (start) already. (b) My grandfather usually ____ (go)...
Answer: 2 Marks
(a) had started (Past Perfect is used for the first of two past actions).
(b) goes (Simple Present is used for habitual actions indicated by 'usually').
Marking: 1 mark for each correct tense formulation.
Q4. Fill in the blanks with correct tenses: (a) It ____ (rain) heavily since morning. (b) If you heat ice, it ____ (melt).
Answer: 2 Marks
(a) has been raining (Present Perfect Continuous for an action starting in the past and continuing into the present).
(b) melts (Simple Present for universal truths/scientific facts).
Marking: 1 mark for each correct tense.
Q5. Error Correction: (a) "Much people gathered..." (b) "I have been knowing him..."
Answer: 2 Marks
(a) Error: Much | Correction: Many (People is a countable noun).
(b) Error: knowing (or have been knowing) | Correction: known (or have known) ('Know' is a stative verb and is rarely used in continuous form).
Marking: 1 mark for each fully correct row (identifying error + providing correct correction). Partial marks (0.5) can be given if only the error is identified.
Q6. Choose correct option: (a) We don't have ____ (much/many) time... (b) She is the ____ (only/very) person...
Answer: 2 Marks
(a) much ('Time' is an uncountable noun here).
(b) only (Determiner indicating uniqueness).
Marking: 1 mark for each correct answer.
Q7. Transform sentences: (a) The mechanic repairs the car. (Present Perfect Continuous) (b) I am writing a letter... (Future Continuous)
Answer: 2 Marks
(a) The mechanic has been repairing the car.
(b) I will be writing a letter to my friend.
Marking: 1 mark for each correct transformation.
Q8. Error Correction: (a) "She have completed..." (b) "...a few sugar..."
Answer: 2 Marks
(a) Error: have | Correction: had (Past perfect needed before 'arrived' in past simple).
(b) Error: few | Correction: little (Sugar is uncountable).
Marking: 1 mark for each correct row.
Q9. Fill in the blanks: (a) While I ____ (read) a book... (b) The Earth ____ (revolve)...
Answer: 2 Marks
(a) was reading (Past continuous for an action in progress when interrupted).
(b) revolves (Simple present for a universal truth).
Marking: 1 mark for each correct answer.
Q10. Fill in the blanks: (a) ____ (This/These) mangoes... (b) ...show me ____ (some/few) fresh vegetables?
Answer: 2 Marks
(a) These (Demonstrative determiner for plural noun close by).
(b) some (Used in polite requests).
Marking: 1 mark for each correct answer.
SECTION B: FORMAL LETTER WRITING (15 Marks)
Q11. Attempt ANY ONE:
A. Complaint letter regarding a defective washing machine.
OR
B. Letter to Editor regarding open manholes and bad roads.
Answer: 15 Marks
Option A (Letter of Complaint):

45, Civil Lines
Kanpur

24th October 202X

The Sales Manager
M/s Electronic World
Mall Road, Kanpur

Subject: Complaint regarding defective "Eco-Wash" washing machine.

Sir/Madam,

I am writing to express my dissatisfaction with an "Eco-Wash" automatic washing machine I purchased from your Mall Road showroom on 17th October 202X, against cash memo number 45892.

Within just a week of usage, the machine has developed serious operational faults. The spinning mechanism makes an unbearable, loud rattling noise, and since yesterday, the dryer has stopped functioning completely. It is highly disappointing to experience such poor quality from a reputed brand and store.

As the machine is well within the warranty period of two years, I request you to kindly arrange for an immediate replacement of the defective piece with a new, thoroughly tested one. Alternatively, please send a qualified technician to repair the fault at the earliest. A copy of the invoice and warranty card is attached for your reference.

I expect a prompt and positive response.

Yours sincerely,
Ankit / Ankita


Option B (Letter to Editor):

Sector-4, Dwarka
New Delhi

24th October 202X

The Editor
The National Times
New Delhi

Subject: Grave danger posed by broken roads and open manholes in Sector-4, Dwarka.

Sir/Madam,

Through the columns of your esteemed newspaper, I wish to draw the urgent attention of the civic authorities towards the deplorable condition of roads and the life-threatening hazard of open manholes in Sector-4, Dwarka.

For the past three months, the main connecting roads have been completely dug up and left unrepaired. Potholes are everywhere, making driving a nightmare. To make matters worse, several manholes have been left uncovered. During the monsoons or at night, when visibility is low, these act as death traps. Just last week, a two-wheeler rider suffered severe injuries after falling into an open drain, and a school bus barely avoided a major accident.

Despite repeated complaints to the Municipal Corporation, no action has been initiated. I strongly urge the authorities to wake up from their slumber, immediately cover the open manholes, and undertake urgent road repair work before a fatal tragedy occurs.

Yours truly,
Rohan / Rohini
Marking Scheme (15 Marks Total):
Format (3 Marks): Sender's address, Date, Receiver's address, Subject, Salutation, Closing.
Content (6 Marks): Clear description of the issue, logical progression of thoughts, specific details (invoice no. or locality details), and clear expectations/suggestions.
Expression & Accuracy (6 Marks): Grammatical accuracy, appropriate formal vocabulary, spellings, coherence, and relevance to the topic. Deduct marks for informal tone.
SECTION C: STORY WRITING (15 Marks)
Q12. Attempt ANY ONE:
A. Story based on the prompt "It was a dark and stormy night..."
OR
B. Story based on the prompt "Ravi was walking through the dense forest..."
Answer: 15 Marks
Option A (Sample Story):

The Uninvited Guest

It was a dark and stormy night. I was completely alone at home while my parents were out of town. Suddenly, the lights went out. I fumbled for a flashlight when I heard a heavy knock at the front door. My heart skipped a beat. Who could be visiting in this torrential rain?

I tiptoed to the window and peeked through the curtains, but the darkness was impenetrable. The knocking grew louder, more frantic. Trembling, I grabbed a heavy umbrella for self-defense and slowly unlocked the door, opening it just a crack.

To my absolute shock, a tiny, soaking wet golden retriever puppy tumbled inside, whining pitifully. It looked up at me with big, terrified eyes. Relief washed over me, replacing my fear with overwhelming pity. I scooped up the shivering pup, wrapped him in a warm towel, and fed him some milk.

The storm raged on outside, but inside, I had found a new friend. When my parents returned the next day, they agreed to let me keep him. I named him 'Thunder'.

Moral: Things are not always as terrifying as they seem; sometimes, fear gives way to beautiful surprises.


Option B (Sample Story):

The Secret of the Forest

Ravi was walking through the dense, uncharted forest to reach his grandmother's village before sunset. The path was lonely. Suddenly, he heard a strange rustling in the bushes nearby. He froze in his tracks. To his absolute surprise, he saw a glowing, metallic object trapped under heavy fallen branches.

Curiosity overcoming his fear, Ravi cautiously approached. It wasn't an animal, but a small, futuristic drone that seemed to be malfunctioning. As he carefully lifted the branches, a holographic screen projected from the device, displaying a map of the very forest he was in, with a red blinking dot marked 'Endangered Species Sanctuary'.

Ravi realized this was no ordinary drone; it belonged to forest rangers tracking poachers. He noticed a tracking beacon on the side and immediately pressed it. Within an hour, a ranger patrol arrived at the scene. They thanked Ravi profusely; the drone held vital data they had lost the previous night.

Ravi reached his grandmother's house late, but he had a thrilling adventure to share. He learned that sometimes, straying slightly off the path leads to helping a greater cause.
Marking Scheme (15 Marks Total):
Title & Moral (2 Marks): Catchy and relevant title. Moral required for Option A as per instruction.
Content/Plot (6 Marks): Creative development of the given prompt, logical sequencing of events, clear climax, and resolution.
Expression & Accuracy (7 Marks): Use of descriptive vocabulary, correct tense usage (mostly past tense for narratives), spelling, punctuation, and overall flow of the story.
