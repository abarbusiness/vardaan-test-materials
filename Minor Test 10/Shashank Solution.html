SOLUTIONS & MARKING SCHEME - MINOR TEST

Class: 6 ICSE | Subject: English Grammar | Max Marks: 50 | Time: 1.5 Hours

SECTION A: GRAMMAR FUNDAMENTALS (20 Marks)
Q1. Identify the type of sentence:
(i) How wonderfully the children performed on stage!
(ii) Please submit your assignments before the deadline.
(iii) The Earth revolves around the Sun in an elliptical orbit.
(iv) Where did you keep the library books?
(v) Do not step on the fresh grass.
Answer: 5 Marks
(i) Exclamatory Sentence
(ii) Imperative Sentence
(iii) Declarative / Assertive Sentence
(iv) Interrogative Sentence
(v) Imperative Sentence
Marking: 1 mark for each correct identification. Spelling errors in the name of the sentence type may be penalized by ½ mark at the examiner's discretion.
Q2. Identify the Nouns and state their kinds:
(i) Honesty is the best policy.
(ii) A swarm of bees attacked the bear.
(iii) Kolkata is situated on the banks of the river Hooghly.
(iv) The woodcutter lost his axe in the river.
(v) We must respect our teachers.
Answer: 5 Marks
(i) Honesty - Abstract Noun
(ii) Swarm - Collective Noun
(iii) Kolkata - Proper Noun
(iv) Axe - Common Noun (Concrete Noun is also acceptable)
(v) Teachers - Common Noun
Marking: 1 mark for each correct identification of the kind.
Q3. Fill in the blanks with suitable Articles (a, an, the, or X):
(i) He is _______ honorable man...
(ii) _______ Ganges is considered...
(iii) I saw _______ one-eyed man...
(iv) _______ gold is a precious metal.
(v) She is carrying _______ umbrella...
Answer: 5 Marks
(i) He is an honorable man who always keeps his promises. ('h' is silent)
(ii) The Ganges is considered a holy river in India. (Specific river)
(iii) I saw a one-eyed man walking down the street yesterday. ('o' makes a 'w' sound)
(iv) X gold is a precious metal. (No article before uncountable material nouns used generally)
(v) She is carrying an umbrella because it is raining. (Vowel sound)
Marking: 1 mark for each correct answer. No partial marks.
Q4. Fill in the blanks with suitable Pronouns:
(i) _______ is the boy... (Demonstrative)
(ii) The dog hurt _______... (Reflexive)
(iii) _______ are you talking to? (Interrogative)
(iv) Please give _______ a glass of water... (Personal)
(v) This book is mine, and that is _______. (Possessive)
Answer: 5 Marks
(i) This / That is the boy who won the gold medal.
(ii) The dog hurt itself while jumping over the fence.
(iii) Whom / Who are you talking to?
(iv) Please give me / him / her / us a glass of water, I am thirsty.
(v) This book is mine, and that is yours / his / hers / theirs.
Marking: 1 mark for each correct appropriate pronoun matching the requested type.
SECTION B: APPLIED GRAMMAR (20 Marks)
Q5. A. Identify Adjectives and state their kinds:
(i) The foolish crow... (ii) ...some apples... (iii) Which pen... (iv) ...first man... (v) Those mangoes...
Answer: 5 Marks
(i) foolish - Adjective of Quality
(ii) some - Adjective of Quantity / Number (Accept both contextually)
(iii) Which - Interrogative Adjective
(iv) first - Adjective of Number (Definite Numeral/Ordinal)
(v) Those - Demonstrative Adjective
Marking: 1 mark for each correctly identified kind.
Q5. B. Correct Degree of Comparison:
(i) Mount Everest is the _______ (high) peak.
(ii) Prevention is _______ (good) than cure.
(iii) Iron is _______ (useful) than any other metal.
(iv) She is as _______ (smart) as her sister.
(v) Today is the _______ (hot) day.
Answer: 5 Marks
(i) Mount Everest is the highest peak in the world.
(ii) Prevention is better than cure.
(iii) Iron is more useful than any other metal.
(iv) She is as smart as her sister.
(v) Today is the hottest day of the season.
Marking: 1 mark for each correct word. Spelling must be exact (e.g., double 't' in hottest).
Q6. A. Separate Subject and Predicate:
(i) The early bird catches the worm.
(ii) Barking dogs seldom bite.
(iii) Up went the balloon.
(iv) My mother and my aunt are arriving today.
(v) A wise owl lived in an oak tree.
Answer: 5 Marks
(i) Subject: The early bird | Predicate: catches the worm.
(ii) Subject: Barking dogs | Predicate: seldom bite.
(iii) Subject: the balloon | Predicate: Up went
(iv) Subject: My mother and my aunt | Predicate: are arriving today.
(v) Subject: A wise owl | Predicate: lived in an oak tree.
Marking: 1 mark for each correctly separated subject and predicate. Note carefully that in (iii) the subject comes at the end.
Q6. B. Transform Sentences:
(i) She is a very intelligent student. (To Exclamatory)
(ii) What a beautiful flower the rose is! (To Declarative)
(iii) Leave the room at once. (To Interrogative)
(iv) He did not finish his homework. (To Affirmative)
(v) Are you coming to the party tonight? (To Declarative)
Answer: 5 Marks
(i) What an intelligent student she is! OR How intelligent a student she is!
(ii) The rose is a very beautiful flower.
(iii) Will you leave the room at once? OR Can you leave the room at once?
(iv) He finished his homework.
(v) You are coming to the party tonight.
Marking: 1 mark for each completely correct transformation. Deduct ½ mark if correct punctuation (e.g., !, ?) is missing at the end of the sentence.
SECTION C: WRITING SKILLS (10 Marks)
Q7. Letter to Principal (Leave Application):
Sick leave due to viral fever OR Leave for elder sister's wedding.
Answer / Value Points: 10 Marks
Format Requirements (Formal Letter to Principal):
  • To / Receiver's Designation: The Principal
  • School Name & Address: [Name of School], [City]
  • Date: (e.g., 24 October 202X)
  • Subject: Leave application for three days / Application for leave to attend sister's wedding.
  • Salutation: Respected Sir / Madam,
  • Body of the letter: 2-3 paragraphs.
  • Sign-off: Yours obediently / Yours sincerely,
  • Sender's Name: [Name], Class [X], Section [Y], Roll No. [Z]

Content Value Points (Option 1 - Viral Fever):
  • Introduction stating the student's name and class.
  • Reason for leave: Suffering from severe viral fever since last night.
  • Doctor's advice: Strict bed rest for three days.
  • Specific dates of leave required (e.g., from 24th Oct to 26th Oct).
  • Assurance of completing missed work. Medical certificate attached (optional mention).

Content Value Points (Option 2 - Sister's Wedding):
  • Introduction stating the student's name and class.
  • Reason for leave: Elder sister's wedding ceremony.
  • Location of the wedding (if in another city, necessitating travel).
  • Specific dates of leave required (spanning four days).
  • Assurance that all pending classwork and homework will be completed promptly upon return.
Marking Scheme Breakdown (10 Marks):
- Format: 3 Marks (Receiver's address, Date, Subject, Salutation, Subscription, Name/Class). Deduct ½ mark for each missing/incorrect element.
- Content: 4 Marks (Clear reason, dates specified, polite tone, assurance of completing work).
- Expression: 3 Marks (Grammatical accuracy, spelling, and appropriate vocabulary).
