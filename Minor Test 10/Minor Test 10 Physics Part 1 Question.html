Minor Test 10 (SURPRISE TEST)
Class: 11 Subject: Physics (Basic Mathematics) Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • Section A contains 5 questions of 2 marks each.
  • Section B contains 5 questions of 3 marks each.
  • Section C contains 5 questions of 5 marks each.
  • Use of calculators is strictly prohibited. Write your answers neatly.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Differentiate the following function with respect to \( x \):
\( y = 3x^4 - \frac{2}{x^2} + 5\sin(x) \)
2.
Evaluate the indefinite integral:
\( \displaystyle \int (4x^3 + \cos(x) - e^x) \,dx \)
3.
Find the slope of the tangent to the curve \( y = x^2 - 4x + 3 \) at the point where \( x = 2 \).
4.
If the position of a particle moving along a straight line is given by \( s(t) = 2t^2 - 3t + 1 \) (where \( s \) is in meters and \( t \) is in seconds), find its instantaneous velocity at \( t = 3 \) seconds.
5.
Evaluate the definite integral:
\( \displaystyle \int_{0}^{\pi/2} \sin(x) \,dx \)
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
Using the chain rule, differentiate the function: \( y = (2x + 3)^5 \) with respect to \( x \).
7.
The velocity of a particle moving along the x-axis is given by \( v(t) = 3t^2 - 2t \) m/s. If the particle starts from the origin at \( t = 0 \), determine its position \( x(t) \) at \( t = 2 \) seconds.
8.
Use the product rule to find the derivative of \( y = x^2 \sin(x) \) with respect to \( x \).
9.
Calculate the area enclosed under the curve \( y = 3x^2 \), bounded by the x-axis, between the ordinates \( x = 1 \) and \( x = 3 \).
10.
Use the quotient rule to differentiate \( \displaystyle y = \frac{\ln x}{x} \) with respect to \( x \).
SECTION C (25 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
The position of a particle is given by the equation \( \displaystyle x(t) = \frac{t^3}{3} - 2t^2 + 3t + 5 \) (where \( x \) is in meters and \( t \) is in seconds).
(a) Find the expressions for the velocity \( v(t) \) and acceleration \( a(t) \) of the particle.
(b) Calculate the time(s) when the particle is momentarily at rest.
(c) What is the acceleration of the particle at these times?
12.
A particle's acceleration is given by \( a(t) = (6t - 4) \text{ m/s}^2 \). Its initial velocity at \( t = 0 \) is \( 2 \text{ m/s} \) and its initial position is \( 5 \text{ m} \).

Using the method of integration, rigorously derive the explicit functions for velocity \( v(t) \) and position \( x(t) \) with respect to time.
13.
For the algebraic function \( y = x^3 - 3x^2 - 9x + 5 \):
(a) Find the critical points where the first derivative is zero.
(b) Use the second derivative test to determine whether each critical point is a local maximum or local minimum.
(c) Find the corresponding maximum and minimum values of the function.
14.
Solve the following calculus problems:
(a) Evaluate the definite integral: \( \displaystyle \int_{1}^{4} \left(\sqrt{x} + \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}}\right) \,dx \).
(b) If \( y = e^{2x} \ln(x) \), find \( \frac{dy}{dx} \).
15.
Answer the following questions based on basic vectors and graphs:
(a) Given two vectors \( \vec{A} = 3\hat{i} - 2\hat{j} + \hat{k} \) and \( \vec{B} = -2\hat{i} + 5\hat{j} - 3\hat{k} \), find the unit vector parallel to their resultant vector \( \vec{R} \).
(b) Draw a neat and clearly labeled rough sketch of the graph for the function \( y = -e^{-x} \) for \( x \geq 0 \), showing the asymptotic behaviour.
