Q9. Give scientific reasons for: (a) A gas fills completely the vessel. (b) Smell of hot sizzling food reaches from meters away.
(a) In gases, the intermolecular forces of attraction are negligible, and particles possess high kinetic energy.
[1.0]
Therefore, the particles move freely in all directions and spread out to completely fill the vessel.
[0.5]
(b) Hot food releases aromatic vapors. Due to high temperature, the kinetic energy of these vapor particles is very high.
[1.0]
As a result, they diffuse rapidly into the air and reach us even from several meters away.

Q11. Prove that: \( (4pq + 3q)^2 - 48pq^2 = (4pq - 3q)^2 \)
LHS: \( (4pq + 3q)^2 - 48pq^2 \)
[0.5]
Expand using \( (a + b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2 \):
\( = [(4pq)^2 + 2(4pq)(3q) + (3q)^2] - 48pq^2 \)
[1.5]
\( = 16p^2q^2 + 24pq^2 + 9q^2 - 48pq^2 \)
[1.0]
Group like terms:
\( = 16p^2q^2 - 24pq^2 + 9q^2 \)
[1.0]
Notice this matches the expansion of \( (a - b)^2 = a^2 - 2ab + b^2 \):
\( = (4pq)^2 - 2(4pq)(3q) + (3q)^2 \)

\( = (4pq - 3q)^2 \) = RHS. Hence Proved.

Q13. What is meant by evaporation? Explain any three factors that affect the rate of evaporation.
Evaporation: The phenomenon of change of a liquid into vapors at any temperature below its boiling point is called evaporation.
[2.0]
Factors affecting rate of evaporation (Any 3):
1. Surface Area: An increase in surface area increases the rate of evaporation (e.g., spreading wet clothes to dry).
[1.0]
2. Temperature: With the increase of temperature, more particles get enough kinetic energy to go into the vapor state, increasing evaporation.
[1.0]
3. Humidity: If the amount of water vapor (humidity) in the air is already high, the rate of evaporation decreases.
[1.0]
(Optional 4th factor): 4. Wind Speed: Higher wind speed carries away water vapor particles faster, increasing evaporation.
