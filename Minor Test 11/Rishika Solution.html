Villi: These are finger-like outgrowths that increase the surface area for the rapid absorption of digested food. [1 mark]
Location: They are found in the inner walls of the small intestine. [1 mark]

State three differences between milk teeth and permanent teeth. [3 Marks]
Solution:
Basis Milk Teeth Permanent Teeth

8.
What is 'rainwater harvesting'? Why is this practice important? [3 Marks]
Solution:
Rainwater Harvesting: The process of collecting and storing rainwater from rooftops and open spaces, and directing it into deep pits or underground tanks to recharge groundwater, is called rainwater harvesting. [1.5 marks]
Importance: It is highly important because it helps in raising the declining water table, prevents wastage of rainwater through drains, and provides a reliable source of water during dry seasons. [1.5 marks]
9.
How does an Amoeba capture its food? Explain briefly. [3 Marks]
Solution:
1. When Amoeba senses food, it pushes out finger-like projections called pseudopodia (false feet) around the food particle. [1 mark]
2. The pseudopodia engulf the food completely and fuse together, trapping the food inside a food vacuole. [1 mark]
3. Digestive juices are secreted into the food vacuole to break down the food into simpler substances for absorption. [1 mark]
10.
State the main functions of: (a) Saliva in the mouth. (b) Bile juice in the small intestine. [3 Marks]
Solution:
(a) Function of Saliva: Saliva contains digestive enzymes (salivary amylase) that break down the complex starch present in food into simple sugars. It also moistens the food, making it easy to swallow. [1.5 marks]
(b) Function of Bile juice: Bile juice is secreted by the liver. It plays a very important role in the digestion of fats. It breaks down large fat globules into smaller ones (emulsification) so that they can be easily digested by enzymes. [1.5 marks]

12.
(a) Explain the 'water cycle' in nature. What are the main processes involved in it? [3 Marks]
(b) Water exists in three different forms in nature. Name these three forms with one example of each. [2 Marks]
Solution (a):
The continuous circulation of water from the Earth's surface to the atmosphere and back to the Earth is called the Water Cycle. It maintains the total amount of water on Earth. [1 mark]
The main processes involved are:
1. Evaporation: Water from oceans, lakes, and rivers turns into vapor due to the sun's heat. (Also Transpiration from plants).
2. Condensation: Water vapor cools down in the atmosphere to form clouds.
3. Precipitation: Water falls back to the earth as rain, snow, or hail. [2 marks]

Solution (b):
The three forms of water are:
1. Solid: Ice or snow (e.g., glaciers, polar ice caps).
2. Liquid: Normal water (e.g., oceans, rivers, drinking water).
3. Gas: Water vapor (e.g., moisture in the air/atmosphere).
[0.5 mark for each form + 0.5 for overall examples = 2 marks]
13.
(a) Who are called 'ruminants'? Give two examples. [2 Marks]
(b) Explain the special process of digestion in grass-eating animals. What is 'cud'? [3 Marks]
Solution (a):
Ruminants: Grass-eating animals that have a specialized stomach (with four chambers) to digest cellulose are called ruminants. [1 mark]
Examples: Cows, Buffaloes, Goats, Deer (Any two). [1 mark]

Solution (b):
Digestion process: These animals quickly swallow the grass and store it in a part of the stomach called the rumen. Here, the food gets partially digested. [1.5 marks]
Cud: The partially digested food stored in the rumen is called cud. Later, the cud returns to the mouth in small lumps and the animal chews it thoroughly. This process is called rumination. [1.5 marks]
14.
(a) What is meant by 'groundwater' and 'aquifer'? [2 Marks]
(b) Discuss any three factors that are responsible for the depletion of the water table in cities. [3 Marks]
Solution (a):
Groundwater: The water found below the water table, trapped in the soil and rocks, is called groundwater. [1 mark]
Aquifer: The huge reservoir of groundwater stored between layers of hard rocks below the water table is called an aquifer. [1 mark]

Solution (b):
Factors for Depletion of Water Table (Any three):
1. Increasing population: Higher demand for construction, drinking, and daily needs forces more groundwater extraction. [1 mark]
2. Increasing industries: Factories consume massive amounts of water, mostly pumped directly from the ground. [1 mark]
3. Paved areas and concrete roads: Heavy construction in cities decreases the open soil area, reducing the seepage of rainwater into the ground to recharge the aquifer. [1 mark]
15.
(a) Define the terms 'assimilation' and 'egestion' in the context of animal nutrition. [3 Marks]
(b) Which organ in the human digestive system is primarily responsible for absorbing water from undigested food? What happens to the remaining semi-solid waste? [2 Marks]
Solution (a):
Assimilation: The process by which the absorbed digested food is transported via blood vessels to different organs of the body, where it is used to build complex substances (like proteins) and provide energy. [1.5 marks]
Egestion: The process of removal of undigested, unabsorbed solid waste material (feces) from the body through the anus. [1.5 marks]

Solution (b):
Organ for water absorption: The Large Intestine is primarily responsible for absorbing water and some salts from the undigested food. [1 mark]
Remaining waste: The remaining semi-solid waste passes into the rectum, where it is stored temporarily before being excreted out through the anus. [1 mark]
