Minor Test
Class: 8 ICSE Subject: Maths & Physics Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly. Show rough work logically on the right margin.
  • Calculations and relevant steps must be clearly shown.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Define contact and non-contact forces. Give one example of each.
2.
A solid metallic block has a mass of 120 g and occupies a volume of 30 cm3. Calculate its density in SI units.
3.
Find the area of a rhombus whose diagonals are 12 cm and 16 cm respectively.
4.
The circumference of a circle is 88 cm. Find its area. (Take π = 22/7)
5.
Why does an iron nail sink in water, but a large steel ship floats? Explain briefly using the concept of density and upthrust.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
Define 'Moment of Force' and state its SI unit. What are the two factors on which the turning effect of a force depends?
7.
A rectangular grassy plot is 112 m long and 78 m broad. It has a gravel path 2.5 m wide all around it on the inside. Find the area of the gravel path.
8.
The relative density of silver is 10.8. The density of water is 103 kg/m3. What is the density of silver in SI units? What does this statement imply about the mass of silver compared to an equal volume of water?
9.
Find the area of a trapezium whose parallel sides are 24 cm and 20 cm, and the perpendicular distance between them is 15 cm.
10.
A solid metallic cube of side 5 cm has a mass of 1000 g. Find its density in g/cm3. Will it float or sink in a liquid of density 1.2 g/cm3? Give a reason.
SECTION C (25 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
A room is 8 m long, 6 m wide, and 4 m high. Find the total cost of whitewashing the four walls and the ceiling at the rate of ₹ 50 per square metre.
12.
(a) Differentiate between mass and weight. (Any two points of difference).
(b) A body of volume 100 cm3 weighs 5 kgf in air. It is completely immersed in a liquid of density 1.8 × 103 kg m−3. Find:
(i) The upthrust due to the liquid.
(ii) The apparent weight of the body in the liquid.
13.
A rectangular park is 60 m long and 40 m wide. Two crossroads, each of width 3 m, run at right angles through the centre of the park, parallel to its sides. Find the area of the crossroads and the cost of laying them with concrete at ₹ 150 per m2.
14.
(a) State the Principle of Floatation.
(b) A block of wood of volume 50 cm3 and density 0.6 g/cm3 floats in water. Find the mass of the block and the volume of the block immersed in water. (Density of water = 1 g/cm3).
15.
A circular park is surrounded by a path 7 m wide. If the circumference of the park is 352 m, find the total area of the path. (Take π = 22/7)
