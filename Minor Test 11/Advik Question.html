MINOR TEST 11
Class: 8 ICSE Subject: Maths & Chemistry Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • The question paper comprises three sections: A, B, and C. All questions are compulsory.
  • Section A: Questions 1 to 5 carry 2 marks each.
  • Section B: Questions 6 to 10 carry 3 marks each.
  • Section C: Questions 11 to 15 carry 5 marks each.
  • In each section, Mathematics questions precede Chemistry questions.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly. Show all essential working steps for mathematical problems.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Evaluate \( (3x - 4y)^2 \) using a suitable algebraic identity.
2.
Find the value of \( 78^2 - 22^2 \) without actual multiplication, using standard identities.
3.
Define 'Intermolecular force of attraction'. State briefly how it varies among solids, liquids, and gases.
4.
What is the Kinetic Molecular Theory of matter? State any one of its primary postulates.
5.
Differentiate between sublimation and deposition with one suitable example of each.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
Simplify the following algebraic expression using identities: \( (2p + 3q)^2 + (2p - 3q)^2 \)
7.
If \( a - \frac{1}{a} = 5 \), find the value of \( a^2 + \frac{1}{a^2} \).
8.
Explain the process of melting of a solid into a liquid on the basis of the kinetic theory of matter.
9.
Give scientific reasons for the following statements:
(a) Solids have a fixed shape and fixed volume. (1.5 Marks)
(b) Gases exert pressure on the walls of the container in all directions. (1.5 Marks)
10.
State three significant differences between the process of evaporation and boiling.
SECTION C (25 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
Prove that: \( (5m - 2n)^2 + 40mn = (5m + 2n)^2 \)
12.
Using suitable standard identities, evaluate the following:
(a) \( 104 \times 96 \) (2.5 Marks)
(b) \( 102^2 \) (2.5 Marks)
13.
If \( x + y = 12 \) and \( xy = 14 \), find the value of \( x^2 + y^2 \). Show your working clearly.
14.
State the five main postulates of the kinetic molecular theory of matter.
15.
Tabulate five points of difference between solids, liquids, and gases based on their physical properties. Consider the following parameters for your differences: Shape, Volume, Compressibility, Intermolecular Space, and Intermolecular Force.
