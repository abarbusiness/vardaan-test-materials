PART I: MATHEMATICS (25 Marks)
1.
Express \( 729 \) as a power of \( 3 \). [2 Marks]
Solution:
To express 729 as a power of 3, we find its prime factorization using 3:
\( 729 \div 3 = 243 \)
\( 243 \div 3 = 81 \)
\( 81 \div 3 = 27 \)
\( 27 \div 3 = 9 \)
\( 9 \div 3 = 3 \)
\( 3 \div 3 = 1 \) [1 mark for factorization]
Multiplying them, we get: \( 729 = 3 \times 3 \times 3 \times 3 \times 3 \times 3 \)
\[ 729 = 3^6 \]
Answer: \( 3^6 \)
[1 mark for correct exponent]
2.
Two numbers are in the ratio \( 3 : 5 \). If their sum is \( 96 \), find the two numbers. [2 Marks]
Solution:
Let the common multiple be \( x \).
Therefore, the two numbers are \( 3x \) and \( 5x \).
Given that their sum is 96:
\[ 3x + 5x = 96 \] \[ 8x = 96 \]
[1 mark for setting up equation]
\[ x = \frac{96}{8} = 12 \]
Now, substitute the value of \( x \):
First number = \( 3 \times 12 = 36 \)
Second number = \( 5 \times 12 = 60 \)
Answer: The numbers are 36 and 60.
[1 mark for correct numbers]
3.
Simplify using laws of exponents: \( (2^3 \times 2^4) \div 2^5 \) [2 Marks]
Solution:
According to the product law of exponents: \( a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n} \)
Numerator = \( 2^{3+4} = 2^7 \) [1 mark for product law]
Now, substitute this back into the expression:
\( 2^7 \div 2^5 \)
According to the quotient law of exponents: \( a^m \div a^n = a^{m-n} \)
\[ = 2^{7-5} \] \[ = 2^2 \]
Answer: \( 2^2 \) or 4
[1 mark for quotient law/final answer]
4.
Simplify the given exponential expression completely:
\( \frac{3^5 \times 10^5 \times 25}{5^7 \times 6^5} \) [3 Marks]
Solution:
First, express composite bases (\( 10, 25, 6 \)) in terms of prime factors:
\( 10 = 2 \times 5 \)
\( 25 = 5^2 \)
\( 6 = 2 \times 3 \) [1 mark for prime factorization]
Substitute these into the expression:
\[ \frac{3^5 \times (2 \times 5)^5 \times 5^2}{5^7 \times (2 \times 3)^5} \]
Distribute the powers using the law \( (ab)^m = a^m b^m \):
\[ \frac{3^5 \times 2^5 \times 5^5 \times 5^2}{5^7 \times 2^5 \times 3^5} \]
Group like bases in the numerator:
\[ \frac{2^5 \times 3^5 \times 5^{(5+2)}}{2^5 \times 3^5 \times 5^7} = \frac{2^5 \times 3^5 \times 5^7}{2^5 \times 3^5 \times 5^7} \]
[1 mark for grouping and laws]
Since the numerator is identical to the denominator, all terms cancel out (using \( a^m \div a^m = a^0 = 1 \)):
\[ 2^{(5-5)} \times 3^{(5-5)} \times 5^{(7-7)} = 2^0 \times 3^0 \times 5^0 = 1 \times 1 \times 1 = 1 \]
Answer: 1
[1 mark for correct final simplification]
5.
The angles of a triangle are in the ratio \( 2 : 3 : 4 \). Find the measure of each angle. [3 Marks]
Solution:
Let the angles of the triangle be \( 2x \), \( 3x \), and \( 4x \).
We know that the sum of angles in a triangle is \( 180^\circ \). [1 mark for stating property]
\[ 2x + 3x + 4x = 180^\circ \] \[ 9x = 180^\circ \] \[ x = \frac{180^\circ}{9} = 20^\circ \]
[1 mark for solving for x]
Now, calculate each angle:
First angle = \( 2x = 2 \times 20^\circ = 40^\circ \)
Second angle = \( 3x = 3 \times 20^\circ = 60^\circ \)
Third angle = \( 4x = 4 \times 20^\circ = 80^\circ \)
Answer: 40°, 60°, and 80°
[1 mark for final angles]
6.
If \( x : y = 3 : 4 \) and \( y : z = 8 : 9 \), calculate the combined ratio \( x : y : z \), and thereby find the ratio \( x : z \). [3 Marks]
Solution:
Given ratios:
\( \frac{x}{y} = \frac{3}{4} \) and \( \frac{y}{z} = \frac{8}{9} \)
To combine the ratios, the common term '\( y \)' must have the same value in both ratios.
Currently, \( y = 4 \) in the first ratio and \( y = 8 \) in the second. [1 mark for identifying LCM approach]
LCM of 4 and 8 is 8.
Multiply the first ratio by \( \frac{2}{2} \) to make \( y = 8 \):
\[ x : y = (3 \times 2) : (4 \times 2) = 6 : 8 \]
Now, we have:
\( x : y = 6 : 8 \)
\( y : z = 8 : 9 \)
Combined ratio \( x : y : z = 6 : 8 : 9 \) [1 mark for combined ratio]
From this, the ratio of \( x \) to \( z \) is directly observed as:
\[ x : z = 6 : 9 \]
Simplifying by dividing by 3: \( x : z = 2 : 3 \)
Answer: x : z = 2 : 3
[1 mark for x:z simplified]
7.
(a) Find the value of \( m \) if: \( 5^m \div 5^{-3} = 5^5 \) [2 Marks]
(b) A car travels 228 km in 3 hours. How long will it take to travel 912 km at a uniform speed? [3 Marks]
Solution (a):
Given: \( 5^m \div 5^{-3} = 5^5 \)
Using the quotient law: \( a^x \div a^y = a^{x-y} \)
\[ 5^{m - (-3)} = 5^5 \] \[ 5^{m + 3} = 5^5 \]
[1 mark for quotient law application]
Since bases are the same, equate the powers:
\[ m + 3 = 5 \] \[ m = 5 - 3 = 2 \]
Answer: m = 2
[1 mark for final value]

Solution (b):
Using Unitary Method:
Time taken to travel 228 km = 3 hours [1 mark]
Time taken to travel 1 km = \( \frac{3}{228} \) hours [1 mark]
Time taken to travel 912 km = \( \frac{3}{228} \times 912 \)
\[ = \frac{3 \times 912}{228} = 3 \times 4 = 12 \text{ hours} \]
Answer: 12 hours
[1 mark for final calculation]
8.
(a) Determine if the numbers 4, 6, 8, and 12 are in proportion. [2 Marks]
(b) Simplify: \( \left( \frac{a^3}{b^4} \right)^2 \times \left( \frac{b^2}{a} \right)^3 \) [3 Marks]
Solution (a):
Four numbers \( a, b, c, d \) are in proportion if \( a \times d = b \times c \) (Product of extremes = Product of means).
Here, extremes are 4 and 12. Means are 6 and 8.
Product of extremes = \( 4 \times 12 = 48 \)
Product of means = \( 6 \times 8 = 48 \) [1 mark for calculating both]
Since \( 48 = 48 \), the given numbers are in proportion.
Answer: Yes, they are in proportion.
[1 mark for conclusion]

Solution (b):
Given expression: \( \left( \frac{a^3}{b^4} \right)^2 \times \left( \frac{b^2}{a^1} \right)^3 \)
Apply power of a power rule \( (x^m)^n = x^{mn} \) to numerators and denominators:
\[ = \frac{a^{3 \times 2}}{b^{4 \times 2}} \times \frac{b^{2 \times 3}}{a^{1 \times 3}} \] \[ = \frac{a^6}{b^8} \times \frac{b^6}{a^3} \]
[1.5 marks for expanding powers correctly]
Combine and apply quotient law \( x^m \div x^n = x^{m-n} \):
\[ = \frac{a^6 \times b^6}{a^3 \times b^8} \] \[ = a^{6-3} \times b^{6-8} \] \[ = a^3 \times b^{-2} \]
Express with positive exponents:
\[ = \frac{a^3}{b^2} \]
Answer: \( \frac{a^3}{b^2} \)
[1.5 marks for simplification and positive exponent]

What is meant by the mechanical energy of a body? Name its two primary types. [2 Marks]
Solution:
Mechanical Energy: The total energy possessed by a body due to its state of motion or its state of rest/position is called mechanical energy. [1 mark]
Two Types:
1. Kinetic Energy (KE)
2. Potential Energy (PE) [1 mark for both]
11.
State the Law of Conservation of Energy. [2 Marks]
Solution:
The Law of Conservation of Energy states that energy can neither be created nor be destroyed; it can only be transformed from one form to another. The total energy of an isolated system remains constant. [2 marks for complete statement]

14.
A boulder of mass \( 5 \text{ kg} \) is at a height of \( 10 \text{ m} \). Calculate potential energy. (\( g = 10 \text{ m/s}^2 \)) [3 Marks]
Solution:
Given data:
Mass (\( m \)) = \( 5 \text{ kg} \)
Height (\( h \)) = \( 10 \text{ m} \)
Acceleration due to gravity (\( g \)) = \( 10 \text{ m/s}^2 \) [1 mark for data extraction]
Formula for Gravitational Potential Energy (P.E.) = \( m \times g \times h \) [1 mark for formula]
\[ \text{P.E.} = 5 \times 10 \times 10 \] \[ \text{P.E.} = 500 \text{ Joules (J)} \]
Answer: The potential energy is 500 J.
[1 mark for calculation and unit]
15.
(a) Define 1 Joule of energy. Relation to calorie. [2 Marks]
(b) Energy transformation in a swinging pendulum. [3 Marks]
Solution (a):
1 Joule: 1 Joule is the amount of work done (or energy transferred) when a force of 1 Newton displaces a body by 1 metre in the direction of the force. [1 mark]
Relation: \( 1 \text{ calorie} = 4.184 \text{ Joules} \) (or approx. 4.2 J). [1 mark]

Solution (b):
In a swinging simple pendulum, there is a continuous exchange between potential and kinetic energy:
1. At the extreme positions (highest points of swing), the bob momentarily stops. Its velocity is zero, so Kinetic Energy = 0. All its energy is in the form of Maximum Potential Energy. [1 mark]
2. As the bob swings downwards towards the center, Potential Energy decreases (height decreases) and transforms into Kinetic Energy (velocity increases). [1 mark]
3. At the mean position (lowest point), its height is minimum and velocity is maximum. Here, Potential Energy is zero (relative to the bottom), and all energy is in the form of Maximum Kinetic Energy. The total mechanical energy remains constant throughout. [1 mark]
16.
(a) Energy stored in a compressed spring? [1 Mark]
(b) Example to show sound is a form of energy. [2 Marks]
(c) Calculate kinetic energy for \( m = 0.2 \text{ kg} \), \( v = 10 \text{ m/s} \). [2 Marks]
Solution (a):
Elastic Potential Energy is stored in a compressed spring. [1 mark]

Solution (b):
When a loud sound (like a sonic boom from a jet or a loud explosion) occurs near a house, the glass windowpanes vibrate heavily and sometimes even shatter. This doing of work (moving or breaking the glass) proves that sound possesses energy. [2 marks for appropriate example and reasoning]

Solution (c):
Given: Mass (\( m \)) = \( 0.2 \text{ kg} \), Velocity (\( v \)) = \( 10 \text{ m/s} \)
Formula for Kinetic Energy (K.E.) = \( \frac{1}{2} m v^2 \) [1 mark for formula]
\[ \text{K.E.} = \frac{1}{2} \times 0.2 \times (10)^2 \] \[ \text{K.E.} = \frac{1}{2} \times 0.2 \times 100 \] \[ \text{K.E.} = 0.1 \times 100 = 10 \text{ J} \]
Answer: The kinetic energy is 10 J.
[1 mark for correct calculation with unit]
