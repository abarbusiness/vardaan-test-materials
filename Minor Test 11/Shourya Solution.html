Solution:
Expression: \( (-15) \times [(-7) + 2] \)
Solve inside the bracket first:
\( = (-15) \times [-5] \) [1 mark]
Multiply the integers (negative \(\times\) negative = positive):
\( = 75 \) [1 mark]
2.
Convert \( 3 \frac{2}{5} \) into an improper fraction and find its reciprocal. [2 Marks]
Solution:
Improper fraction: \( 3 \frac{2}{5} = \frac{3 \times 5 + 2}{5} = \frac{17}{5} \) [1 mark]
Reciprocal of \( \frac{17}{5} \) is: \( \frac{5}{17} \) [1 mark]
3.
The temperature at 12:00 noon was \( 12^\circ\text{C} \) above zero. If it decreases at the rate of \( 2^\circ\text{C} \) per hour until midnight, what will be the temperature at 8:00 PM? [2 Marks]
Solution:
Initial temperature at 12 noon = \( 12^\circ\text{C} \)
Total hours from 12 noon to 8 PM = 8 hours.
Total decrease in temperature = \( 8 \text{ hrs} \times (-2^\circ\text{C/hr}) = -16^\circ\text{C} \) [1 mark]
Temperature at 8 PM = \( 12^\circ\text{C} - 16^\circ\text{C} = -4^\circ\text{C} \) [1 mark]
4.
Simplify using BODMAS rule: \( 24 \div (8 - 2 \times 3) \) [2 Marks]
Solution:
Expression: \( 24 \div (8 - 2 \times 3) \)
According to BODMAS, solve multiplication inside the bracket first:
\( = 24 \div (8 - 6) \) [1 mark]
\( = 24 \div 2 \)
\( = 12 \) [1 mark]

Find the sum: \( \frac{2}{7} + \frac{3}{5} \) [2 Marks]

Take LCM of denominators 7 and 5 = 35.
\( \frac{2 \times 5}{35} + \frac{3 \times 7}{35} \) [1 mark]
\( = \frac{10 + 21}{35} \)
\( = \frac{31}{35} \) [1 mark]
SECTION B (15 Marks)
6.
Rahul's scores in five successive rounds were \( 25, -5, -10, 15, \) and \( 10 \). What was his total score? [3 Marks]
Solution:
Total score is the sum of all scores in the five rounds.
Total = \( 25 + (-5) + (-10) + 15 + 10 \) [1 mark]
Group positive and negative integers:
Positive sum = \( 25 + 15 + 10 = 50 \) [1 mark]
Negative sum = \( (-5) + (-10) = -15 \)
Final Total = \( 50 + (-15) = 35 \) [1 mark]
7.
Simplify: \( \frac{4}{9} \times \frac{15}{16} \div \frac{5}{8} \) [3 Marks]
Solution:
First, convert the division sign to multiplication by taking the reciprocal of the divisor:
\( = \frac{4}{9} \times \frac{15}{16} \times \frac{8}{5} \) [1.5 marks]
Simplify by cancelling out common factors:
- \( 4 \) and \( 16 \) (leaves \( 4 \) in denominator)
- \( 5 \) and \( 15 \) (leaves \( 3 \) in numerator)
\( = \frac{1 \times 3 \times 8}{9 \times 4 \times 1} \)
\( = \frac{24}{36} \)
Reducing to lowest terms: \( = \frac{2}{3} \) [1.5 marks]
8.
Subtract the sum of \( -34 \) and \( -15 \) from the sum of \( 12 \) and \( -5 \). [3 Marks]
Solution:
Step 1: Sum of \( -34 \) and \( -15 \)
\( (-34) + (-15) = -49 \) [1 mark]
Step 2: Sum of \( 12 \) and \( -5 \)
\( 12 + (-5) = 7 \) [1 mark]
Step 3: Subtract the first sum from the second sum.
\( 7 - (-49) = 7 + 49 = 56 \) [1 mark]
9.
Simplify: \( 15 - [ 8 + \{ 27 - ( 2 \times 8 ) \} ] \) [3 Marks]
Solution:
Following BODMAS, resolve brackets inwards out (Round -> Curly -> Square):
\( = 15 - [ 8 + \{ 27 - 16 \} ] \) [1 mark]
\( = 15 - [ 8 + \{ 11 \} ] \) [1 mark]
\( = 15 - [ 19 ] \)
\( = 15 - 19 = -4 \) [1 mark]
10.
A storybook contains 120 pages. Aryan read \( \frac{3}{5} \) of the book. How many pages are still left? [3 Marks]
Solution:
Total number of pages = \( 120 \)
Number of pages read = \( \frac{3}{5} \text{ of } 120 \)
\( = \frac{3}{5} \times 120 = 3 \times 24 = 72 \text{ pages} \) [1.5 marks]
Number of pages left to read = Total pages - Pages read
\( = 120 - 72 = 48 \text{ pages} \) [1.5 marks]
SECTION C (25 Marks)
11.
Simplify: \( 5 \frac{1}{2} - \left[ 2 \frac{1}{3} \div \left\{ \frac{3}{4} - \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{2}{3} - \frac{1}{6} \right) \right\} \right] \) [5 Marks]
Solution:
Convert mixed fractions to improper fractions:
\( = \frac{11}{2} - \left[ \frac{7}{3} \div \left\{ \frac{3}{4} - \frac{1}{2} \left( \frac{2}{3} - \frac{1}{6} \right) \right\} \right] \)

Step 1: Solve inner round bracket \( \left( \frac{2}{3} - \frac{1}{6} \right) \)
LCM of 3 and 6 is 6.
\( = \left( \frac{4 - 1}{6} \right) = \frac{3}{6} = \frac{1}{2} \) [1 mark]

Step 2: Multiply by \( \frac{1}{2} \)
Expression becomes: \( \frac{11}{2} - \left[ \frac{7}{3} \div \left\{ \frac{3}{4} - \left( \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{2} \right) \right\} \right] \)
\( = \frac{11}{2} - \left[ \frac{7}{3} \div \left\{ \frac{3}{4} - \frac{1}{4} \right\} \right] \) [1 mark]

Step 3: Solve curly bracket \( \left\{ \frac{3}{4} - \frac{1}{4} \right\} \)
\( = \left\{ \frac{2}{4} \right\} = \frac{1}{2} \) [1 mark]

Step 4: Solve square bracket \( \left[ \frac{7}{3} \div \frac{1}{2} \right] \)
\( = \frac{7}{3} \times \frac{2}{1} = \frac{14}{3} \) [1 mark]

Step 5: Final subtraction
\( = \frac{11}{2} - \frac{14}{3} \) (LCM of 2, 3 is 6)
\( = \frac{33 - 28}{6} = \frac{5}{6} \) [1 mark]
12.
An elevator descends at \( 6 \text{ m/min} \). Descent starts from \( 10 \text{ m} \) above ground to \( -350 \text{ m} \). Find time taken. [5 Marks]
Solution:
Initial position of elevator = \( +10 \text{ m} \) (above ground) [1 mark]
Final position of elevator = \( -350 \text{ m} \) (below ground)
Total vertical distance to be covered = Final position - Initial position (Magnitude)
\( = | -350 - 10 | = | -360 | = 360 \text{ m} \) [2 marks]
Rate of descent = \( 6 \text{ m/min} \)
Time taken = \( \frac{\text{Total Distance}}{\text{Rate}} \)
\( = \frac{360}{6} = 60 \text{ minutes} \) [1.5 marks]
Since \( 60 \text{ minutes} = 1 \text{ hour} \), the elevator takes 1 hour. [0.5 mark]
13.
\( \frac{4}{9} \) of students are boys. Girls = \( 775 \). Find (i) Total students, (ii) Number of boys. [5 Marks]
Solution:
Let the total number of students be \( x \).
Fraction of boys = \( \frac{4}{9} \)
Fraction of girls = \( 1 - \frac{4}{9} = \frac{5}{9} \) [1.5 marks]

(i) Total Students:
According to the question, \( \frac{5}{9} \) of total students = 775
\( \frac{5}{9} \times x = 775 \)
\( x = 775 \times \frac{9}{5} \) [1 mark]
\( x = 155 \times 9 = 1395 \).
Total number of students = 1395. [1 mark]

(ii) Number of Boys:
Number of boys = Total students - Number of girls
\( = 1395 - 775 = 620 \) boys. [1.5 marks]
(Alternate method: \( \frac{4}{9} \times 1395 = 620 \))
14.
Simplify: \( (-20) + (-8) \div (-2) \times 3 - [ 5 \times (-2) - (-3) \times (-4) ] \) [5 Marks]
Solution:
Expression: \( (-20) + (-8) \div (-2) \times 3 - [ 5 \times (-2) - (-3) \times (-4) ] \)
Apply BODMAS:
Step 1: Solve inside the square bracket \( [ 5 \times (-2) - (-3) \times (-4) ] \)
\( [ -10 - (12) ] = [ -22 ] \) [2 marks]

Step 2: Substitute back into expression
\( = (-20) + (-8) \div (-2) \times 3 - [ -22 ] \)

Step 3: Perform division outside bracket \( (-8) \div (-2) \)
\( = (-20) + 4 \times 3 - [ -22 ] \) [1 mark]

Step 4: Perform multiplication
\( = (-20) + 12 - [ -22 ] \) [1 mark]

Step 5: Perform addition/subtraction
\( = -20 + 12 + 22 \)
\( = -8 + 22 = 14 \) [1 mark]
15.
Tin is \( \frac{4}{5} \) full. Take out 6 bottles, pour in 4 bottles, tin is \( \frac{3}{4} \) full. Find total capacity. [5 Marks]
Solution:
Let the total capacity of the tin be \( x \) bottles.
Initial quantity of oil in the tin = \( \frac{4}{5}x \) [1 mark]

Net change in oil = 6 bottles taken out and 4 poured in.
Net removal = \( -6 + 4 = -2 \) bottles. [1 mark]

Quantity after this change = \( \frac{3}{4}x \)
Therefore, equation is: \( \frac{4}{5}x - 2 = \frac{3}{4}x \) [1 mark]

Rearranging terms to solve for \( x \):
\( \frac{4}{5}x - \frac{3}{4}x = 2 \)
Take LCM of 5 and 4 (which is 20):
\( \frac{16x - 15x}{20} = 2 \) [1 mark]
\( \frac{x}{20} = 2 \)
\( x = 20 \times 2 = 40 \)
The tin can contain a total of 40 bottles. [1 mark]
