MINOR TEST 11
Class: 8 CBSE Subject: Maths & Science Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • The question paper comprises three sections: A, B, and C. All questions are compulsory.
  • Section A: Questions 1 to 5 carry 2 marks each.
  • Section B: Questions 6 to 10 carry 3 marks each.
  • Section C: Questions 11 to 15 carry 5 marks each.
  • In each section, Mathematics questions precede Science questions.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly. Show working for mathematical problems.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
A machine in a soft drink factory fills 840 bottles in 6 hours. How many bottles will it fill in 5 hours?
2.
If 15 workers can build a wall in 48 hours, how many workers will be required to do the same work in 30 hours?
3.
Define malleability and ductility. Name one metal that is highly malleable.
4.
Why is sodium metal kept immersed in kerosene? Give a scientific reason.
5.
What happens when a copper vessel is exposed to moist air for a long time? Write the chemical components of the green coating formed.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
A train is moving at a uniform speed of \(75 \text{ km/hr}\).
(i) How far will it travel in 20 minutes?
(ii) Find the time required to cover a distance of 250 km.
7.
A school has 8 periods a day, each of 45 minutes duration. How long would each period be if the school has 9 periods a day, assuming the total number of school hours remains the same?
8.
Suppose 2 kg of sugar contains \(9 \times 10^6\) crystals. How many sugar crystals are there in:
(i) 5 kg of sugar?
(ii) 1.2 kg of sugar?
9.
State three clear points of differentiation between metals and non-metals based on their physical properties.
10.
State reasons for the following statements:
(a) Aluminium foils are used to wrap food items.
(b) Immersion rods for heating liquids are made up of metallic substances.
(c) Copper cannot displace zinc from its salt solution.
SECTION C (25 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
A garrison of 120 men has provisions for 30 days. At the end of 5 days, 5 more men joined them.
(a) How many days can they sustain on the remaining provision? (4 Marks)
(b) Identify the type of proportion used in this problem. (1 Mark)
12.
(a) A farmer has enough food to feed 20 animals in his cattle for 6 days. How long would the food last if there were 10 more animals in his cattle? (3 Marks)
(b) In a model of a ship, the mast is 9 cm high, while the mast of the actual ship is 12 m high. If the length of the ship is 28 m, how long is the model ship? (2 Marks)
13.
(a) What is a displacement reaction? Illustrate with a balanced chemical equation. (3 Marks)
(b) Can you store lemon pickle in an aluminium utensil? Explain your answer with scientific reasoning. (2 Marks)
14.
Write the complete word equations for the chemical reactions when:
(a) Magnesium is reacted with dilute hydrochloric acid. (1.5 Marks)
(b) Copper is reacted with dilute sulphuric acid. (1.5 Marks)
(c) Explain the rule of reactivity series that dictates the outcome of reaction (b). (2 Marks)
15.
(a) What are metalloids? Give two examples. (2 Marks)
(b) Identify and name the specific non-metal used in the following applications: (3 Marks)
  • (i) In the water purification process to kill germs.
  • (ii) In fertilizers to enhance plant growth.
  • (iii) In the purple coloured solution applied on wounds as an antiseptic.
