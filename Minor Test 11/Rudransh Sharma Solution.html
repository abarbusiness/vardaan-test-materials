SECTION A (10 Marks)
1.
Define 'atomicity'. State the atomicity of Ozone and Phosphorus. [2 Marks]
Solution:
Atomicity: The total number of atoms present in one molecule of an element or a compound is known as its atomicity. [1 mark]
Atomicity of Ozone (\(\mathrm{O_3}\)): 3 (Triatomic) [0.5 mark]
Atomicity of Phosphorus (\(\mathrm{P_4}\)): 4 (Tetratomic) [0.5 mark]
2.
Classify into elements, compounds, and mixtures: Air, Pure Water, Iron, Blood. [2 Marks]
Solution:
1. Air: Mixture (of gases like \(N_2\), \(O_2\), \(CO_2\), etc.) [0.5 mark]
2. Pure Water (\(\mathrm{H_2O}\)): Compound [0.5 mark]
3. Iron (\(\mathrm{Fe}\)): Element [0.5 mark]
4. Blood: Mixture (of plasma, RBCs, WBCs, etc.) [0.5 mark]
3.
State any two core characteristics of a physical change. [2 Marks]
Solution:
Characteristics of a physical change (Any two):
1. No new substance is formed. [1 mark]
2. The change is temporary and usually reversible. [1 mark]
(Alternate accepted points: There is no change in the chemical composition of the substance; there is usually no overall absorption or evolution of energy).
4.
Why is the melting of wax considered a physical change, whereas the burning of a candle is considered a chemical change? [2 Marks]
Solution:
Melting of wax: It is a physical change because only the state of wax changes from solid to liquid. No new substance is formed, and solid wax can be obtained back on cooling (reversible). [1 mark]
Burning of a candle: It is a chemical change because the wax combusts with oxygen in the air to form new chemical substances like Carbon Dioxide (\(\mathrm{CO_2}\)) and Water vapour (\(\mathrm{H_2O}\)), along with the release of heat and light energy. It is irreversible. [1 mark]
5.
Write the chemical formulae for: (a) Sodium chloride (b) Calcium carbonate. [2 Marks]
Solution:
(a) Sodium chloride: \(\mathrm{NaCl}\) [1 mark]
(b) Calcium carbonate: \(\mathrm{CaCO_3}\) [1 mark]
SECTION B (15 Marks)
6.
Differentiate between an element and a compound on the basis of any three distinct points. [3 Marks]
Solution:
Basis Element Compound
Definition A pure substance made up of only one kind of atom. A pure substance made up of two or more elements chemically combined in a fixed ratio.
Breakdown Cannot be broken down into simpler substances by ordinary physical or chemical methods. Can be broken down into its constituent elements by chemical methods.
Properties Has its own unique set of properties. Properties of a compound are entirely different from the properties of its constituent elements.
[1 mark for each valid difference point = 3 marks total]
7.
Give scientific reasons for: (a) Rusting of iron is a chemical change. (b) Tearing of paper is a physical change, but burning it is a chemical change. [3 Marks]
Solution:
(a) Rusting of iron is a chemical change because iron reacts with oxygen and moisture in the air to form a completely new substance called iron oxide (rust). The original properties of iron are lost, and the change is permanent and irreversible. [1.5 marks]

(b) Tearing of paper is a physical change because it only changes the size and shape of the paper; no new substance is formed. [0.75 marks]
However, burning of paper is a chemical change because it produces ash, carbon dioxide, and water vapor (new substances) with a completely different chemical composition, which cannot be reversed back to paper. [0.75 marks]

Solution:
(a) Aluminium: Symbol = \(\mathrm{Al}\), Valency = 3 (or +3) [1 mark]
(b) Oxygen: Symbol = \(\mathrm{O}\), Valency = 2 (or -2) [1 mark]
(c) Potassium: Symbol = \(\mathrm{K}\), Valency = 1 (or +1) [1 mark]
9.
What is meant by a chemical equation? State two vital conditions necessary for a chemical change to take place. [3 Marks]
Solution:
Chemical Equation: The short-hand representation of a chemical reaction using the symbols and formulae of the respective elements and compounds involved is called a chemical equation. [1 mark]

Conditions for a chemical change (Any two):
1. Close Contact: Reactants must come into close physical contact or be mixed in a solution state. [1 mark]
2. Energy Supply: Many reactions require the application of heat (e.g., thermal decomposition), light (e.g., photosynthesis), or electricity (e.g., electrolysis) to occur. [1 mark]
10.
Explain 'sublimation' with a suitable example. Is it a physical or a chemical change? [3 Marks]
Solution:
Sublimation: It is the process in which a solid changes directly into its gaseous state on heating, without passing through the intermediate liquid state, and vice versa on cooling. [1.5 marks]

Example: Heating of Iodine crystals, Naphthalene balls, or Camphor. [0.5 mark]

Nature of Change: It is a Physical Change because no new substance is formed, and the solid can be recovered simply by cooling the vapors. [1 mark]
SECTION C (25 Marks)
11.
(a) State three fundamental differences between physical and chemical changes. [3 Marks]
(b) Classify into physical or chemical changes: (i) Curdling of milk (ii) Dissolving sugar in water. [2 Marks]
Solution (a):
Physical Change Chemical Change
No new substance is formed. One or more new substances are formed.
The change is usually temporary and reversible. The change is permanent and generally irreversible.
Chemical composition of the substance remains the same. Chemical composition of the substance completely changes.
[3 marks: 1 for each difference]

Solution (b):
(i) Curdling of milk: Chemical Change (Lactic acid is formed, irreversible). [1 mark]
(ii) Dissolving sugar in water: Physical Change (Sugar can be recovered by evaporation). [1 mark]
12.
(a) Define 'mixture'. [1 Mark]
(b) How does a mixture differ from a compound? (Two points) [2 Marks]
(c) Name the constituent elements present in: (i) Ammonia (ii) Sulphuric acid. [2 Marks]
Solution (a):
Mixture: A material containing two or more elements or compounds physically mixed together in any proportion without undergoing any chemical change. [1 mark]

Solution (b): (Any two valid points)
1. Proportion: In a mixture, constituents can be mixed in any ratio, whereas in a compound, elements combine in a fixed, definite ratio by mass. [1 mark]
2. Separation: Constituents of a mixture can be separated by simple physical methods (e.g., filtration, evaporation). Constituents of a compound can only be separated by chemical or electrochemical methods. [1 mark]

Solution (c):
(i) Ammonia (\(\mathrm{NH_3}\)): Nitrogen and Hydrogen. [1 mark]
(ii) Sulphuric Acid (\(\mathrm{H_2SO_4}\)): Hydrogen, Sulphur, and Oxygen. [1 mark]

14.
(a) What happens when magnesium ribbon is burnt in air? Write word equation and identify type of change. [3 Marks]
(b) Mention two observations that indicate a chemical change. [2 Marks]
Solution (a):
Observation: When a magnesium ribbon is burnt in air, it burns with a dazzling, brilliant white flame and forms a white powdery ash (Magnesium Oxide). [1 mark]
Word Equation:
Magnesium + Oxygen (from air) \(\rightarrow\) Magnesium oxide [1 mark]
Type of change: It is a Chemical Change (as a new substance, magnesium oxide, is formed and heat/light is emitted). [1 mark]

Solution (b):
General observations indicating a chemical change (Any two):
1. Evolution of a gas (effervescence). [1 mark]
2. Change in colour. [1 mark]
(Alternate answers: Formation of a precipitate; Change in temperature/Release or absorption of heat).
15.
(a) Describe an activity to show dissolving copper sulphate is a physical change, but adding iron nails is a chemical change. [3 Marks]
(b) What is meant by 'intermolecular space' and 'intermolecular force'? How do they differ in solids and gases? [2 Marks]
Solution (a):
Physical Change Part: Take some water in a beaker and add a few blue crystals of copper sulphate. Stir it. The water turns blue, forming a copper sulphate solution. If we evaporate the water, we get the blue crystals back. No new substance is formed, making it a physical change. [1.5 marks]
Chemical Change Part: Now, drop a clean iron nail into this blue copper sulphate solution and leave it for a while. You will observe that the blue colour of the solution turns light green (Iron Sulphate is formed) and a brown coating of copper deposits on the iron nail. New substances are formed, making this a chemical change. [1.5 marks]

Solution (b):
Definitions:
1. Intermolecular Space: The empty space between the constituent particles (atoms/molecules) of a matter. [0.5 mark]
2. Intermolecular Force: The force of attraction existing between the constituent particles of a matter. [0.5 mark]
Difference in Solids and Gases:
In solids, the intermolecular space is minimum and the intermolecular force of attraction is maximum (very strong).
In gases, the intermolecular space is maximum and the intermolecular force of attraction is minimum (negligible). [1 mark for both comparisons]
