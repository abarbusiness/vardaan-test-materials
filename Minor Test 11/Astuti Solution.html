Q1. Evaluate using the rules of integers: (a) \( (-28) - (-14) \)   (b) \( |-35| + |-12| \)
(a) \( (-28) - (-14) \)
\( = -28 + 14 \) (Subtracting a negative is adding a positive)
\( = -14 \)

(b) \( |-35| + |-12| \)
Absolute value makes it positive:
\( = 35 + 12 \)
\( = 47 \)

Q2. Convert \( 4 \frac{2}{5} \) into an improper fraction and then find its reciprocal.
Improper fraction \( = \frac{(4 \times 5) + 2}{5} \)
\( = \frac{20 + 2}{5} \)
\( = \frac{22}{5} \)   [1 Mark]
The reciprocal is found by flipping the numerator and denominator.
Reciprocal \( = \frac{5}{22} \)
[2M]
Q3. The temperature of a freezer was \( -6^\circ\text{C} \). If lowered by \( 5^\circ\text{C} \), what is the new temperature?
Initial temperature \( = -6^\circ\text{C} \)
"Lowered by" means we must subtract \( 5^\circ\text{C} \).
New temperature \( = -6 - 5 \)
\( = -11^\circ\text{C} \)
[2M]
Q4. Find the integer which is \( 5 \) less than \( -3 \) and write its additive inverse.
Integer 5 less than -3 implies subtraction:
\( -3 - 5 = -8 \)   [1 Mark]
The additive inverse of a number changes its sign.
Additive inverse of \( -8 \) is:
\( +8 \)
[2M]
Q5. Add the following fractions: \( \frac{5}{12} \) and \( \frac{7}{18} \).
Find LCM of denominators 12 and 18.
LCM(12, 18) \( = 36 \)   [0.5 Mark]
Convert to equivalent fractions:
\( \frac{5 \times 3}{12 \times 3} = \frac{15}{36} \) and \( \frac{7 \times 2}{18 \times 2} = \frac{14}{36} \)   [1 Mark]
Add them: \( \frac{15}{36} + \frac{14}{36} \)
\( = \frac{29}{36} \)
[2M]

Resolve signs first:

Q7. Simplify: \( \frac{4}{5} + \frac{7}{10} - \frac{1}{2} \)
Find LCM of denominators 5, 10, and 2.
LCM(5, 10, 2) \( = 10 \)   [1 Mark]
Convert to common denominator:
\( = \frac{4 \times 2}{10} + \frac{7 \times 1}{10} - \frac{1 \times 5}{10} \)
\( = \frac{8 + 7 - 5}{10} \)   [1 Mark]
\( = \frac{15 - 5}{10} \)
\( = \frac{10}{10} \)
\( = 1 \)
[3M]
Q8. Total 60 students. \( \frac{3}{5} \) like cricket, \( \frac{1}{4} \) like football, rest like tennis. Find number of tennis players.
Number of students who like cricket \( = \frac{3}{5} \text{ of } 60 = \frac{3}{5} \times 60 = 3 \times 12 = 36 \)   [1 Mark]
Number of students who like football \( = \frac{1}{4} \text{ of } 60 = \frac{1}{4} \times 60 = 15 \)   [1 Mark]
Total students liking cricket and football \( = 36 + 15 = 51 \)
Students liking tennis = Total - (Cricket + Football)
\( = 60 - 51 \)
\( = 9 \text{ students} \)
[3M]
Q9. Subtract the sum of \( -250 \) and \( 138 \) from the sum of \( 136 \) and \( -272 \).
Step 1: Find the sum of -250 and 138
\( (-250) + 138 = -112 \)   [1 Mark]

Step 2: Find the sum of 136 and -272
\( 136 + (-272) = -136 \)   [1 Mark]

Step 3: Subtract Step 1 from Step 2
\( (-136) - (-112) \)
\( = -136 + 112 \)
\( = -24 \)
[3M]
Q10. Elevator descends at \( 6 \text{ m/min} \). Starts from \( +10 \text{ m} \) to reach \( -350 \text{ m} \). Find time taken.
Initial position \( = +10 \text{ m} \)
Final position \( = -350 \text{ m} \)
Total distance to be covered \( = \) Difference in positions
\( = 10 \text{ m} - (-350 \text{ m}) \)
\( = 10 + 350 = 360 \text{ m} \)   [1.5 Marks]

Speed of descent \( = 6 \text{ m/min} \)
Time taken \( = \frac{\text{Total Distance}}{\text{Speed}} \)
\( = \frac{360}{6} \)
\( = 60 \text{ minutes} \text{ (or 1 Hour)} \)
[3M]

Q11. Simplify using BODMAS: \( 3 \frac{1}{5} - \left[ 2 \frac{1}{2} - \left\{ 1 \frac{1}{3} - \left( \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{6} \right) \right\} \right] \)
Convert all mixed fractions to improper fractions:
\( \frac{16}{5} - \left[ \frac{5}{2} - \left\{ \frac{4}{3} - \left( \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{6} \right) \right\} \right] \)   [1 Mark]

Solve inner parenthesis \( ( \dots ) \):
\( \frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{6} = \frac{3 + 1}{6} = \frac{4}{6} = \frac{2}{3} \)   [1 Mark]

Substitute back and solve curly braces \( \{ \dots \} \):
\( \frac{16}{5} - \left[ \frac{5}{2} - \left\{ \frac{4}{3} - \frac{2}{3} \right\} \right] \)
\( = \frac{16}{5} - \left[ \frac{5}{2} - \frac{2}{3} \right] \)   [1 Mark]

Solve square brackets \( [ \dots ] \):
\( \frac{5}{2} - \frac{2}{3} = \frac{15 - 4}{6} = \frac{11}{6} \)   [1 Mark]

Final subtraction:
\( \frac{16}{5} - \frac{11}{6} \)
LCM of 5 and 6 is 30.
\( = \frac{96 - 55}{30} \)
\( = \frac{41}{30} \text{ or } 1 \frac{11}{30} \)
[5M]
Q12. Earns ₹45,000/month. Spends \( \frac{1}{3} \) on rent, \( \frac{1}{5} \) on food, \( \frac{1}{6} \) on education. Calculate: (a) Total spent (b) Saved (c) Fraction saved.
Total Income \( = \text{₹}45,000 \)

(a) Calculating Expenditures:
Rent \( = \frac{1}{3} \times 45000 = \text{₹}15,000 \)
Food \( = \frac{1}{5} \times 45000 = \text{₹}9,000 \)
Education \( = \frac{1}{6} \times 45000 = \text{₹}7,500 \)
Total Spent \( = 15000 + 9000 + 7500 \)
Total Spent \( = \text{₹}31,500 \)
[2 Marks]

(b) Calculating Savings:
Savings \( = \text{Total Income} - \text{Total Spent} \)
\( = 45000 - 31500 \)
Savings \( = \text{₹}13,500 \)
[2 Marks]

(c) Fraction of Income Saved:
Fraction \( = \frac{\text{Savings}}{\text{Total Income}} \)
\( = \frac{13500}{45000} \)
Divide by 100: \( \frac{135}{450} \)
Divide by 45: \( \frac{3}{10} \)
Fraction Saved \( = \frac{3}{10} \)

Q13. (a) Arrange in ascending order: \( -15, 23, -8, 0, -2, 12 \). (b) Evaluate using number line concept: \( (-4) + 6 + (-3) \).
(a) Ascending Order (Smallest to Largest):
Identify the negatives first, the most negative is smallest.
\( -15, -8, -2, 0, 12, 23 \)
[2 Marks]

(b) Number Line Evaluation for \( (-4) + 6 + (-3) \):
Step 1: Start at \( 0 \), move \( 4 \) units left to reach \( -4 \).
Step 2: From \( -4 \), add \( 6 \). Move \( 6 \) units to the right.
\( -4 + 6 = +2 \). Current position is at \( +2 \).
Step 3: From \( +2 \), add \( -3 \). Move \( 3 \) units left.
\( 2 - 3 = -1 \).
Final Answer \( = -1 \)
[3 Marks - 1 mark for each logical step]

Q14. Reads \( \frac{1}{5} \) book in 1 hr. (a) Part read in \( 2 \frac{1}{5} \) hrs? (b) If book is 250 pages, pages read in this time?
(a) Part read in given time:
Time given \( = 2 \frac{1}{5} \text{ hours} = \frac{11}{5} \text{ hours} \)
Part read in 1 hour \( = \frac{1}{5} \)
Part read in \( \frac{11}{5} \text{ hours} = \frac{1}{5} \times \frac{11}{5} \)
\( = \frac{11}{25} \text{ part of the book} \)
[2 Marks]

(b) Pages read:
Total pages in book \( = 250 \)
Number of pages read \( = \text{Part read} \times \text{Total pages} \)
\( = \frac{11}{25} \times 250 \)
\( = 11 \times 10 \)
\( = 110 \text{ pages} \)
[2+3=5M]
Q15. Diver is \( -20 \text{m} \), goes down \( 15 \text{m} \), rises \( 8 \text{m} \). (a) Final position? (b) Vertical distance from bird at \( +35 \text{m} \)?
(a) Final Position:
Let sea level be \( 0 \). Below sea level is negative.
Initial position \( = -20 \text{ m} \)
Goes down by 15m \( \rightarrow \) Add \( -15 \)
Rises by 8m \( \rightarrow \) Add \( +8 \)
Final Position \( = -20 - 15 + 8 \)
\( = -35 + 8 \)
\( = -27 \text{ m} \) (or 27m below sea level)
[2 Marks]

(b) Vertical distance to bird:
Position of bird \( = +35 \text{ m} \)
Position of diver \( = -27 \text{ m} \)
Vertical distance \( = \text{Top position} - \text{Bottom position} \)
\( = 35 - (-27) \)
\( = 35 + 27 \)
\( = 62 \text{ m} \)
[3 Marks]
