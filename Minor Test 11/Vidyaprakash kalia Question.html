MINOR TEST 11
Class: 8 CBSE Subject: Maths & Science Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • The question paper comprises three sections: A, B, and C. All questions are compulsory.
  • Section A: Questions 1 to 5 carry 2 marks each.
  • Section B: Questions 6 to 10 carry 3 marks each.
  • Section C: Questions 11 to 15 carry 5 marks each.
  • In each section, Mathematics questions (Algebraic Identities) precede Science questions (Matter).
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly. Show working for mathematical problems.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Evaluate \( (2x + 3y)^2 \) using a suitable algebraic identity.
2.
Find the value of \( 52^2 - 48^2 \) without actual multiplication, using a standard identity.
3.
Define the melting point and boiling point of a substance.
4.
Give a scientific reason: Why does a desert cooler cool better on a hot, dry day?
5.
State two main points of difference between solids and gases based on their physical properties.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
Simplify the following algebraic expression using identities: \( (3a - 4b)^2 - (3a + 4b)^2 \)
7.
If \( x + \frac{1}{x} = 4 \), find the value of \( x^2 + \frac{1}{x^2} \).
8.
Evaluate \( 105 \times 95 \) using a suitable algebraic identity. Show the steps clearly.
9.
Give scientific reasons for the following statements:
(a) A gas fills completely the vessel in which it is kept.
(b) We get the smell of hot sizzling food from several meters away.
10.
What is sublimation? Name any two chemical substances that undergo sublimation.
SECTION C (25 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
Prove that: \( (4pq + 3q)^2 - 48pq^2 = (4pq - 3q)^2 \)
12.
Using suitable standard identities, evaluate the following:
(a) \( 103 \times 104 \) (2.5 Marks)
(b) \( 99^2 \) (2.5 Marks)
13.
What is meant by evaporation? Explain any three factors that affect the rate of evaporation of a liquid.
14.
Tabulate the differences between the three states of matter (Solid, Liquid, and Gas) based on the following parameters:
(i) Shape and volume
(ii) Intermolecular forces
(iii) Compressibility
15.
Answer the following questions:
(a) Convert 300 K to the Celsius scale. (1 Mark)
(b) Why does ice float on water despite being a solid? (2 Marks)
(c) Name the state of matter that is highly compressible and give one practical application of its high compressibility in our daily lives. (2 Marks)
