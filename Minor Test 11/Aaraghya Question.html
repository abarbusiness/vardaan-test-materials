PART I: MATHEMATICS (25 Marks)
SECTION A (6 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Express \( 729 \) as a power of \( 3 \). [2]
2.
Two numbers are in the ratio \( 3 : 5 \). If their sum is \( 96 \), find the two numbers. [2]
3.
Simplify the following expression using laws of exponents: \[ (2^3 \times 2^4) \div 2^5 \] [2]
SECTION B (9 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
4.
Simplify the given exponential expression completely: \[ \frac{3^5 \times 10^5 \times 25}{5^7 \times 6^5} \] [3]
5.
The angles of a triangle are in the ratio \( 2 : 3 : 4 \). Find the measure of each angle of the triangle. [3]
6.
If \( x : y = 3 : 4 \) and \( y : z = 8 : 9 \), calculate the combined ratio \( x : y : z \), and thereby find the ratio \( x : z \). [3]
SECTION C (10 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
7.
(a)
Find the value of \( m \) if:   \( 5^m \div 5^{-3} = 5^5 \) [2]
(b)
A car travels \( 228 \text{ km} \) in \( 3 \text{ hours} \). Using the unitary method (or proportion), how long will it take the same car to travel \( 912 \text{ km} \) at a uniform speed? [3]
8.
(a)
Determine if the numbers \( 4, 6, 8, \) and \( 12 \) are in proportion. Justify your answer mathematically. [2]
(b)
Simplify using the laws of exponents: \[ \left( \frac{a^3}{b^4} \right)^2 \times \left( \frac{b^2}{a} \right)^3 \] [3]
PART II: PHYSICS (25 Marks)

10.
What is meant by the mechanical energy of a body? Name its two primary types. [2]
11.
State the Law of Conservation of Energy. [2]
SECTION B (9 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
12.
State the primary energy transformations occurring in the following cases:
(a)
A glowing electric bulb
(b)
A microphone being used during a speech
(c)
Photosynthesis in green plants
[3]

Differentiate between kinetic energy and potential energy. Provide one practical example of each. [3]

SECTION C (10 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
15.
(a)
Define 1 Joule of energy. State the mathematical relationship between one Joule and one calorie. [2]
(b)
Describe in detail the transformation of energy that takes place when a simple pendulum swings from one extreme position to another. (You may assume no air resistance). [3]
16.
(a)
What specific form of energy is stored in a compressed spring? [1]
(b)
Briefly describe a common example or a simple observation which proves that sound is a form of energy. [2]
(c)
