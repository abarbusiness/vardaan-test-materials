(b) Conversions:
(i) \( 1 \text{ g/cm}^3 = 1000 \text{ kg/m}^3 \).
Therefore, \( 8.5 \text{ g/cm}^3 = 8.5 \times 1000 = \) \( 8500 \text{ kg/m}^3 \)
(ii) To convert \( \text{km/h} \) to \( \text{m/s} \), multiply by \( \frac{5}{18} \).
\( 18 \times \frac{5}{18} = \) \( 5 \text{ m/s} \)

Q4. Differentiate: (a) Homogeneous vs Heterogeneous mixture. (b) Element vs Compound.
(a) Mixtures:
Homogeneous: Has a uniform composition throughout. Example: Salt dissolved in water.
Heterogeneous: Has a non-uniform composition where components can be distinctly seen. Example: Sand in water.

(b) Microscopic Composition:
Element: Made entirely of only one type of atom (cannot be broken down into simpler substances).
Compound: Made up of two or more different types of atoms chemically bonded in a fixed ratio.

Q6. Principle behind: (a) Separating funnel (b) Fractional distillation (c) Magnetic separation.
(a) Separating Funnel: Based on the principle of separating immiscible liquids having different densities. The heavier liquid settles at the bottom.
(b) Fractional Distillation: Based on the principle of separating miscible liquids that have different boiling points. The liquid with lower boiling point vaporizes first.
(c) Magnetic Separation: Based on the principle of magnetism. Iron is strongly attracted to a magnet, while sulfur is non-magnetic, allowing easy mechanical separation.

Q8. (a) Formula and SI Unit of density. (b) Relative density definition and lack of units.
(a) Standard Density:
Formula: \( \text{Density} (\rho) = \frac{\text{Mass } (m)}{\text{Volume } (V)} \)
SI Unit: \( \text{kg/m}^3 \) (kilogram per cubic meter)

(b) Relative Density:
Definition: It is the ratio of the density of a substance to the density of water at \( 4^\circ\text{C} \).
Reason for no units: Because it is a ratio of two identical physical quantities (density divided by density), their units cancel out perfectly, making it a dimensionless pure number.

Q9. (a) Burning wax as dual process. (b) Compounds formed and word equation.
(a) Dual Process Description:
When a candle burns, the heat first melts the solid wax into liquid wax. This is a Physical Change because the state changes, but it is still wax, and the process is reversible upon cooling.
The liquid wax then travels up the wick, vaporizes, and reacts with oxygen in the air to burn. This combustion is a Chemical Change because new, irreversible products (gases) are formed along with the release of heat and light.

(b) Compounds and Equation:
Main compounds formed: Carbon Dioxide and Water (vapor).
Word Equation:
Wax + Oxygen \( \rightarrow \) Carbon Dioxide + Water + Heat + Light

Q10. Separate Sand, Salt, and Ammonium Chloride.
Experimental Plan:
Step 1: Sublimation (To remove Ammonium Chloride)
- Place the mixture in a china dish covered with an inverted glass funnel (plugged with cotton).
- Heat gently. Ammonium chloride sublimes (solid to gas directly) and solidifies on the cool inner walls of the funnel. Scrape off the pure solid \( \text{NH}_4\text{Cl} \).

Step 2: Dissolution and Filtration (To separate Sand)
- Transfer the remaining mixture (sand + salt) into a beaker. Add pure water and stir well.
- The common salt dissolves completely, while sand is insoluble.
- Pour the mixture through filter paper in a funnel. Sand remains on the filter paper as residue. Dry it to get pure solid sand.

Step 3: Evaporation (To recover Common Salt)
- The clear liquid that passed through the filter is salt water (filtrate).
- Heat the filtrate in an evaporating dish until all the water boils off.
- Pure, dry solid sodium chloride is left behind in the dish.

Q11. Empty bottle=25g, w/ water=50g, w/ liquid=45g. Find (a) Capacity (b) Density of Y (c) RD of Y (d) Mass of 100cm³ of Y.
Given Data: Mass of empty bottle (\( M_0 \)) = \( 25 \text{ g} \).
Mass with water (\( M_1 \)) = \( 50 \text{ g} \). Mass with liquid (\( M_2 \)) = \( 45 \text{ g} \).

(a) Volume Capacity:
Mass of water = \( M_1 - M_0 = 50 - 25 = 25 \text{ g} \).
Since density of water is \( 1 \text{ g/cm}^3 \), Volume = Mass / Density = \( 25 / 1 \).
Capacity of bottle = \( 25 \text{ cm}^3 \)

(b) Density of Liquid 'Y':
Mass of liquid 'Y' = \( M_2 - M_0 = 45 - 25 = 20 \text{ g} \).
Volume of liquid = Capacity of bottle = \( 25 \text{ cm}^3 \).
Density of Y in CGS = \( \frac{20}{25} = \) \( 0.8 \text{ g/cm}^3 \)
Density of Y in SI = \( 0.8 \times 1000 = \) \( 800 \text{ kg/m}^3 \)

(c) Relative Density:
\( \text{RD} = \frac{\text{Mass of liquid}}{\text{Mass of equal volume of water}} = \frac{20}{25} = \) \( 0.8 \)

(d) Mass of \( 100 \text{ cm}^3 \) of 'Y':
\( \text{Mass} = \text{Volume} \times \text{Density} = 100 \times 0.8 = \) \( 80 \text{ g} \)

Q12. (a) Three conditions for chemical change with examples. (b) Rusting vs Burning of iron.
(a) Conditions for Chemical Change:
1. Close Contact (Mixing): E.g., Adding sodium metal directly into water causes a violent reaction.
2. Light Energy: E.g., Photosynthesis in plants requires sunlight to convert \( \text{CO}_2 \) and water into glucose.
3. Electrical Energy: E.g., Passing electric current through water (Electrolysis) breaks it down into hydrogen and oxygen gases.

(b) Differences (Rusting vs Burning):
1. Speed: Rusting is a very slow, continuous oxidation process taking days/months. Burning is a rapid oxidation process happening in seconds.
2. Energy Release: Burning releases large amounts of sensible heat and light energy. Rusting produces no light and only unnoticeable trace amounts of heat.
