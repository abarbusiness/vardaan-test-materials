SECTION A (10 Marks)
Q1. A machine in a soft drink factory fills 840 bottles in 6 hours. How many bottles will it fill in 5 hours?
Let the number of bottles filled in 5 hours be \( x \).
Since time and the number of bottles filled are in Direct Proportion:
[0.5]
\(\frac{840}{6} = \frac{x}{5}\)
[0.5]
\(x = \frac{840 \times 5}{6}\)
\(x = 140 \times 5 = 700\)
[0.5]
Answer: The machine will fill 700 bottles in 5 hours.
[0.5]
Q2. If 15 workers can build a wall in 48 hours, how many workers will be required to do the same work in 30 hours?
Let the number of workers required be \( y \).
More workers will take less time, hence this is a case of Inverse Proportion.
[0.5]
\(x_1 y_1 = x_2 y_2\)
\(15 \times 48 = y \times 30\)
[0.5]
\(y = \frac{15 \times 48}{30}\)
\(y = \frac{48}{2} = 24\)
[0.5]
Answer: 24 workers are required.
[0.5]
Q3. Define malleability and ductility. Name one metal that is highly malleable.
Malleability: The property of metals by which they can be beaten into thin sheets without breaking.
[1.0]
Ductility: The property of metals by which they can be drawn into thin wires.
[0.5]
Example: Gold or Silver.
[0.5]
Q4. Why is sodium metal kept immersed in kerosene?
Sodium is a highly reactive metal. It reacts vigorously with oxygen and moisture present in the air.
[1.0]
This reaction generates a lot of heat and easily catches fire. To prevent this accidental fire and cut off its contact with air and moisture, it is kept immersed in kerosene.
[1.0]
Q5. What happens when a copper vessel is exposed to moist air for a long time? Write the chemical components.
When a copper vessel is exposed to moist air for a long time, it acquires a dull green coating. This process is called corrosion of copper.
[1.0]
The green material is a mixture of copper hydroxide [Cu(OH)₂] and copper carbonate [CuCO₃].

SECTION B (15 Marks)
Q6. A train is moving at a uniform speed of 75 km/hr. (i) How far will it travel in 20 minutes? (ii) Find time for 250 km.
Speed = 75 km in 60 minutes. This is a direct proportion between Distance and Time.
[0.5]
(i) Distance in 20 mins:
Let distance be \( d_1 \). \(\frac{75}{60} = \frac{d_1}{20}\)
[0.5]
\(d_1 = \frac{75 \times 20}{60} = 25 \text{ km}\).
[0.5]
(ii) Time for 250 km:
Let time be \( t_2 \). \(\frac{75}{60} = \frac{250}{t_2}\)
[0.5]
\(t_2 = \frac{250 \times 60}{75} = \frac{10 \times 60}{3} = 200 \text{ minutes}\).
(or 3 hours 20 minutes)
[1.0]
Q7. 8 periods a day of 45 minutes. Find period duration if 9 periods a day.
Let the new duration of each period be \( x \) minutes.
If the number of periods increases, the duration of each period will decrease. Therefore, this is an Inverse Proportion.
[1.0]
\(8 \times 45 = 9 \times x\)
[1.0]
\(x = \frac{8 \times 45}{9}\)
\(x = 8 \times 5 = 40\) minutes.
[1.0]

Q10. State reasons for: (a) Al foil for food (b) Immersion rods metallic (c) Cu cannot displace Zn.
(a) Aluminium is highly malleable and can be easily beaten into thin foils. It is also non-reactive with most dry foods.
[1.0]

SECTION C (25 Marks)
Q11. Garrison of 120 men, provisions for 30 days. After 5 days, 5 more men joined. How many days will remaining provision last? Type of proportion?
(a) Total provision initially = for 120 men for 30 days.
After 5 days, the remaining food would be sufficient for the original 120 men for \(30 - 5 = 25\) days.
[1.0]
New number of men = \(120 + 5 = 125\) men.
Let the number of days the remaining food lasts for 125 men be \( D \).
[1.0]
Number of men and number of days are in inverse proportion.
\(120 \times 25 = 125 \times D\)
[1.0]
\(D = \frac{120 \times 25}{125} = \frac{120}{5} = 24\) days.
The remaining provision will last for 24 days.
[1.0]
(b) This is a case of Inverse Proportion because as the number of men increases, the food lasts for fewer days.
[1.0]
Q12. (a) 20 animals for 6 days. If 10 more animals join? (b) Ship model mast 9cm, actual 12m. Length 28m, find model length.
(a) Let food last for \( y \) days. New total animals = \(20 + 10 = 30\).
Inverse proportion: \(20 \times 6 = 30 \times y\)
[1.5]
\(y = \frac{120}{30} = 4\) days. The food will last for 4 days.
[1.5]
(b) Let the length of the model ship be \( x \) cm.
Direct proportion between model and actual lengths: \(\frac{\text{Model Mast}}{\text{Actual Mast}} = \frac{\text{Model Length}}{\text{Actual Length}}\)
[1.0]
\(\frac{9 \text{ cm}}{12 \text{ m}} = \frac{x \text{ cm}}{28 \text{ m}}\) &implies; \(x = \frac{9 \times 28}{12} = \frac{3 \times 28}{4} = 3 \times 7 = 21\).
Length of model ship = 21 cm.
[1.0]
Q13. (a) Displacement reaction definition & example. (b) Store lemon pickle in aluminium?
(a) A reaction in which a more reactive metal displaces a less reactive metal from its aqueous salt solution is called a displacement reaction.
[1.5]
Example: When Iron nails are placed in copper sulphate solution, iron displaces copper.
\(\text{Fe} + \text{CuSO}_4 \rightarrow \text{FeSO}_4 + \text{Cu}\)
[1.5]
(b) No. Lemon pickle contains citric acid. Aluminium is a reactive metal that reacts with acids to produce hydrogen gas and toxic aluminium salts.
[1.0]
This reaction would spoil the pickle and make it unfit (and poisonous) for consumption.
[1.0]
Q14. Word equations: (a) Mg + dil HCl (b) Cu + dil H2SO4 (c) Reactivity series rule applied to (b).
(a) Magnesium + Hydrochloric acid (dilute) → Magnesium chloride + Hydrogen gas
[1.5]
(b) Copper + Sulphuric acid (dilute) → No Reaction
[1.5]
(c) In the reactivity series, Copper is placed below Hydrogen. This means copper is less reactive than hydrogen.
[1.0]
Because it is less reactive, copper cannot displace hydrogen from dilute acids (like dilute sulphuric acid or dilute hydrochloric acid). Hence, no reaction takes place.
[1.0]
