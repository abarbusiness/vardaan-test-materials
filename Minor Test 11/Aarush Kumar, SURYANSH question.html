Minor Test 11
Class: 7 CBSE Subject: Science & Mathematics Time: 1.5 Hours Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly. Break down mathematical steps clearly.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Find the value of \( 2^3 \times 3^2 \).
2.
Express \( 343 \) in exponential form.
3.
Name two metals which are exceptionally soft and can be easily cut with a knife.
4.
What happens when a magnesium ribbon is burnt in air? Write the corresponding word equation.
5.
Define the term malleability. Name the most malleable metal known to us.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
Simplify and express the result in power notation:
\( (5^2)^3 \times 5^4 \div 5^7 \)
7.
Write the number \( 279404 \) in its expanded form using exponents.
8.
Simplify the following expression:
\( \displaystyle \frac{2^5 \times 3^4 \times 4}{3^2 \times 32} \)
9.
State three clear differences between metals and non-metals based purely on their physical properties.
10.
Why is sodium metal kept immersed under kerosene oil? Explain the scientific reason briefly.
SECTION C (25 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
Solve the following:
(a) Express \( 729 \times 64 \) as a product of prime factors only in exponential form. (3 Marks)
(b) Evaluate the expression: \( (-1)^{20} + (-1)^{21} + (-1)^{22} \). (2 Marks)
12.
Simplify the given exponential expression completely showing all steps clearly:
\( \displaystyle \frac{3^5 \times 10^5 \times 25}{5^7 \times 6^5} \)
13.
Answer the following based on chemical reactivity:
(a) What do you understand by "displacement reactions"? (2 Marks)
(b) Detail the observation when an iron nail is placed in a copper sulphate solution. Write the word equation for this reaction. (3 Marks)
14.
Give strict scientific reasons for the following statements (1 mark each):
(a) Aluminum foils are widely used to wrap food items.
(b) Immersion rods used for heating liquids are made of metallic substances.
(c) Copper cannot displace zinc from its salt solution.
(d) Temple bells are made of metals and not wood.
(e) Gold and silver are highly preferred for making jewelry.
15.
Answer the following based on properties of non-metals:
(a) Distinguish between a metal and a non-metal strictly on the basis of their chemical properties (provide two distinct points). (2 Marks)
(b) What happens when non-metals react with oxygen? Explain in detail with the help of an example (e.g., Sulphur) and mention the nature of the product formed. (3 Marks)
