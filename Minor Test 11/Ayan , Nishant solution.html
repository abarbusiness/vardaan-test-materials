SECTION B (15 Marks) - Short Answer Type
Q16. State the three main characteristics that a standard unit of a physical quantity must possess to be globally acceptable.
[3]
Answer:
  1. It should be of a convenient size.
  2. Its value (magnitude) should not change with respect to place or time.
  3. It should be easily reproducible and internationally accepted.
Q17. While measuring the length of a wooden block with a metre scale, an error can occur due to the thickness of the scale. (a) How does this error occur? (b) State two ways to avoid or reduce this error for more accurate reading.
[3]
Answer:
(a) Because the plastic metre scale has some thickness, looking at the scale from an angle (parallax) causes the reading to be higher or lower than the actual value.
(b) Ways to avoid:
  • Hold the eye vertically above the end of the object being measured (correct line of sight).
  • Use a metre scale with a bevelled (tapered) edge, placing the tapered edge directly touching the object.
Q18. (a) Define the term 'Mass'. (b) Differentiate between the tools used by a jeweller and a grocer to measure mass, giving a reason for the difference.
[3]
Answer:
(a) Mass is defined as the amount of matter contained in an object.
(b) A jeweller uses a physical balance because it is highly sensitive and can accurately measure very small differences in the mass of costly substances like gold or silver. A grocer uses a simplified beam balance or electronic balance as they need to measure larger, bulk quantities where milligram-level precision is not critical.
Q19. Briefly outline the step-by-step method you would use to measure the length of a curved line drawn on a piece of paper using a thin cotton thread and common pins.
[3]
Answer:
  1. Fix common pins at small intervals along the curved line drawn on the paper.
  2. Tie a knot at one end of the cotton thread and place it at the starting pin.
  3. Carefully guide the thread around each pin along the curve, ensuring it is neither too tight nor too loose.
  4. Mark the thread or cut it exactly at the end point of the curved line.
  5. Remove the thread, stretch it straight along a metre scale, and measure the distance from the knot to the mark/cut to get the length of the curve.
Q20. A student needs to find the diameter of a thin copper wire. He winds the wire over a smooth pencil to form a tight coil of 30 turns. If the total length of the coil on a centimetre scale is measured as 1.5 cm, calculate the diameter of the wire in millimetres (mm).
[3]
Answer:
Number of turns ($N$) $= 30$
Total length of the coil ($L$) $= 1.5 \text{ cm}$ $$ \text{Diameter of wire} = \frac{\text{Total Length}}{\text{Number of turns}} $$ $$ \text{Diameter} = \frac{1.5 \text{ cm}}{30} = 0.05 \text{ cm} $$ Since $1 \text{ cm} = 10 \text{ mm}$: $$ 0.05 \text{ cm} \times 10 = 0.5 \text{ mm} $$ Result: The diameter of the wire is $0.5 \text{ mm}$.
SECTION C (20 Marks) - Long Answer Type
Q21. (a) Define 'time' and 'mean solar day'. (2 Marks)
(b) Establish the mathematical relation between the SI unit of time (second) and a mean solar day. Show the complete calculation. (3 Marks)
[5]
Answer:
(a) Time: It is defined as an interval or gap between two events.
Mean Solar Day: It is the average of all solar days in which the Earth completes one revolution around the Sun.

(b) Mathematical Relation:
We know that:
$$ 1 \text{ mean solar day} = 24 \text{ hours} $$ Since $1 \text{ hour} = 60 \text{ minutes}$, $$ 1 \text{ mean solar day} = 24 \times 60 \text{ minutes} = 1440 \text{ minutes} $$ Since $1 \text{ minute} = 60 \text{ seconds}$, $$ 1 \text{ mean solar day} = 1440 \times 60 \text{ seconds} = 86,400 \text{ seconds} $$ Therefore, the SI unit of time (1 second) is defined as: $$ 1 \text{ second} = \frac{1}{86400} \text{ part of a mean solar day.} $$
Q22. (a) What is a simple pendulum? Define the terms 'oscillation' and 'time period'. (3 Marks)
(b) During an event, a seconds pendulum swings (oscillates) 20 times. Calculate the total time taken by the event in seconds. (2 Marks)
[5]
Answer:
(a) Simple Pendulum: A small heavy weight (bob) suspended freely by a light thread such that it can swing back and forth freely is called a simple pendulum.
Oscillation: One complete to-and-fro motion of the pendulum about its mean position is called one oscillation.
Time Period: The total time taken by the pendulum to complete exactly one oscillation is called its time period.

(b) Calculation:
A "seconds pendulum" is defined as a pendulum having a time period of exactly $2 \text{ seconds}$.
$$ \text{Time taken for 1 oscillation} = 2 \text{ s} $$ $$ \text{Total oscillations} = 20 $$ $$ \text{Total time taken} = 20 \times 2 \text{ s} = 40 \text{ seconds} $$ Result: The total time taken by the event is $40 \text{ seconds}$.
Q23. (a) Describe a practical method to measure the thickness of a single 5-rupee coin accurately using a centimetre scale. (3 Marks)
(b) 24 identical coins placed one upon another have a total thickness of 4.8 cm. Mathematically calculate the thickness of a single coin in mm. (2 Marks)
[5]
Answer:
(a) Method: Measuring a single coin directly leads to large errors due to the scale's resolution. Instead, stack a known number of identical coins (e.g., 20 or 25 coins) perfectly one above the other. Measure the total thickness of this entire stack using the centimetre scale. Then, divide the total measured thickness by the number of coins to get the highly accurate thickness of a single coin.

(b) Calculation:
Total thickness of the stack $= 4.8 \text{ cm}$
Number of coins $= 24$
$$ \text{Thickness of one coin} = \frac{\text{Total thickness}}{\text{Number of coins}} $$ $$ \text{Thickness of one coin} = \frac{4.8 \text{ cm}}{24} = 0.2 \text{ cm} $$ Converting to millimetres ($1 \text{ cm} = 10 \text{ mm}$): $$ 0.2 \text{ cm} \times 10 = 2 \text{ mm} $$ Result: The thickness of each coin is $2 \text{ mm}$.
Q24. (a) List three advantages of using the 24-hour clock. (3 Marks)
(b) Perform the interconversions: (i) Convert 11:30 a.m. to 24-hour clock time. (ii) Convert 15:45 (24-hour clock) to 12-hour clock time. (2 Marks)
[5]
Answer:
(a) Advantages of the 24-hour clock:
  1. It prevents confusion as the terms a.m., p.m., morning, or evening are not needed.
  2. The same time is never repeated twice in one day, eliminating ambiguity (e.g., distinguishing midnight from noon).
  3. It is easily represented by a simple four-digit format, where the first two digits signify hours and the next two signify minutes.
(b) Interconversions:
(i) 11:30 a.m. to 24-hour clock:
Since it is an a.m. time less than 12, it translates directly without changes to the digits.
$$ \text{Answer: } 11:30 $$
(ii) 15:45 to 12-hour clock:
Since the hour is greater than 12, we subtract 12 and add "p.m.".
$$ 15:45 - 12\text{ h} = 3:45 \text{ p.m.} $$ $$ \text{Answer: } 3:45 \text{ p.m.} $$
