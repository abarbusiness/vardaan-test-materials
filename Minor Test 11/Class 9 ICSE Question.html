SECTION A: MATHEMATICS (Logarithms) - 25 Marks
1.
(a) Convert the given exponential equation into logarithmic form: \( 5^3 = 125 \)
(b) Convert the given logarithmic equation into exponential form: \( \log_{10} 0.01 = -2 \)
[2]
2.
Evaluate the following expression without using log tables:
\[ \log_2 16 + \log_3 27 \]
[2]
3.
If \( \log_x 64 = \frac{3}{2} \), determine the value of the base \( x \).
[2]
4.
Express the following as a single logarithm:
\[ 2 \log 3 - \frac{1}{2} \log 16 + \log 12 \]
[2]
5.
Solve for \( x \):
\[ \log_{10} (x^2 - 21) = 2 \]
[3]
6.
Prove the following identity using the laws of logarithms:
\[ \log \left( \frac{81}{8} \right) - 2 \log \left( \frac{3}{2} \right) + 3 \log \left( \frac{2}{3} \right) + \log \left( \frac{3}{4} \right) = 0 \]
[3]
7.
If \( a^2 + b^2 = 7ab \), prove that:
\[ 2 \log (a + b) = 2 \log 3 + \log a + \log b \]
[3]
8.
Given that \( \log_{10} x = a \) and \( \log_{10} y = b \), express the value of \( 10^{2a-b} \) in terms of \( x \) and \( y \).
[3]
9.
Solve the following simultaneous equations for \( x \) and \( y \):
\[ \log x + \log y = 2 \]
\[ x - y = 15 \]
[5]
SECTION B: SCIENCE (Physics) - 25 Marks
10.
A body is moving with a uniform velocity of \( 10 \text{ m/s} \). What will be its acceleration? Justify your answer.
[2]
11.
Define inertia. On what physical quantity does the inertia of a body depend?
[2]
12.
A stone is dropped freely from a cliff. What will be its velocity after \( 3 \text{ s} \)? (Take \( g = 10 \text{ m/s}^2 \))
[2]
13.
State any two differences between distance and displacement.
[2]
14.
Derive the third equation of motion algebraically: \( v^2 = u^2 + 2as \).
[3]
15.
A bullet of mass \( 20 \text{ g} \) moving with a speed of \( 150 \text{ m/s} \) strikes a wooden plank and comes to rest in \( 0.03 \text{ s} \). Calculate the magnitude of force exerted by the wooden plank on the bullet.
[3]
16.
A car accelerates uniformly from \( 18 \text{ km/h} \) to \( 36 \text{ km/h} \) in \( 5 \text{ s} \). Calculate:
(i) the acceleration in \( \text{m/s}^2 \).
(ii) the distance covered by the car in that time.
[3]
17.
State Newton's Third Law of Motion. Explain briefly how it applies to a person swimming in a pool.
[3]
18.
(a) State the law of conservation of momentum. (2 marks)
(b) A boy of mass \( 40 \text{ kg} \) running at a velocity of \( 5 \text{ m/s} \) jumps onto a stationary cart of mass \( 10 \text{ kg} \). Find the common velocity of the cart with the boy on it. (3 marks)
