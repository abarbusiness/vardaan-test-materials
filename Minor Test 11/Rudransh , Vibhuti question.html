MINOR TEST 11
Class: 8 CBSE Subject: Science & Maths Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Section A: 5 questions of 2 marks each (10 Marks).
  • Section B: 5 questions of 3 marks each (15 Marks).
  • Section C: 5 questions of 5 marks each (25 Marks).
SECTION A (10 Marks) (Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
An object experiences a net non-zero force. Is it possible for the object to move with a constant speed along a straight line? Give a reason.
2.
When we throw a ball upwards, what happens to its speed while it is going up? Give the scientific reason.
3.
Expand using algebraic identities: \( (x + 5)^2 \).
4.
Factorise the expression: \( a^2 - 16 \).
5.
Is it necessary for two bodies to be in direct contact for a force to exist? Give one example of a non-contact force.
SECTION B (15 Marks) (Attempt all questions - 3 Marks Each)
6.
Distinguish between contact and non-contact forces. Give one example of each.
7.
Explain why a sharp knife cuts better than a blunt knife using the concept of pressure.
8.
Evaluate \( (99)^2 \) using the identity \( (a - b)^2 = a^2 - 2ab + b^2 \).
9.
Simplify: \( (2x + 3y)^2 - (2x - 3y)^2 \).
10.
Show that \( (a - b)^2 + 2ab = a^2 + b^2 \).
SECTION C (25 Marks) (Attempt all questions - 5 Marks Each)
11.
Describe an activity using a plastic bottle with two holes to conclude that a liquid exerts equal pressure at the same depth.
12.
In the following situations, identify the agent exerting the force and state the effect:
(a) Squeezing a lemon. (b) A car coming to rest. (c) An athlete high jumping.
13.
Verify the identity \( (x + a)(x + b) = x^2 + (a + b)x + ab \) by taking \( x = 3, a = 2, b = 4 \).
14.
Evaluate using identities: (a) \( 104 \times 96 \) (b) \( 51^2 - 49^2 \).
15.
Why do porters place a large round piece of cloth on their head while carrying heavy loads? Explain using the concept of pressure.
