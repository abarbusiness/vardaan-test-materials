[2 Marks]

\[ = 8ab + (-5ab) + 3ab + (-ab) \] \[ = (8 - 5 + 3 - 1)ab \] \[ = (11 - 6)ab \] \[ = \mathbf{5ab} \]

Section B Solutions (4 Marks Each)
6. (Science) Why does water flow, but ice doesn't?
[4 Marks]
Solution:
  • Ice (Solid): In ice, water molecules are held tightly together by strong intermolecular forces of attraction. The intermolecular space is minimal. The particles cannot leave their positions and only vibrate. Therefore, ice maintains a rigid shape and does not flow.
  • Water (Liquid): In liquid water, the intermolecular forces are weaker, and the intermolecular spaces are larger compared to ice. This allows the molecules to slide past one another. Consequently, water lacks a fixed shape, takes the shape of its container, and has the property of fluidity (it flows).
7. (Maths) Add the following algebraic expressions: \( 3a - 4b + 4c, \quad 2a + 3b - 8c, \quad \text{and} \quad a - 6b + c \)
[4 Marks]
Solution: Writing the expressions and collecting like terms together:
\begin{align*} &\text{Sum} = (3a - 4b + 4c) + (2a + 3b - 8c) + (a - 6b + c) \\ &\text{Grouping like terms:} \\ &= (3a + 2a + a) + (-4b + 3b - 6b) + (4c - 8c + c) \\ &= (6a) + (-7b) + (-3c) \\ &= \mathbf{6a - 7b - 3c} \end{align*}
*(Alternatively, this can be solved perfectly using the column method aligning \( a \), \( b \), and \( c \))*
8. (Science) What is called diffusion? Give an example from real life.
[4 Marks]
Solution: Diffusion: The natural phenomenon of intermixing of particles of two different types of matter on their own is called diffusion. It happens due to the constant random motion of particles.
Example: When a bottle of perfume is opened in one corner of a room, its fragrance quickly spreads and can be smelled across the entire room. (Another example: The smell of hot sizzling food reaching you from a distance).
9. (Maths) Subtract \( 2a - 5b + 2c - 9 \) from \( 3a - 4b - c + 6 \).
[4 Marks]
Solution: We write the expression to be subtracted inside parentheses and reverse the signs:
\begin{align*} &\text{Required Difference} = (3a - 4b - c + 6) - (2a - 5b + 2c - 9) \\ &\text{Opening brackets and changing signs:} \\ &= 3a - 4b - c + 6 - 2a + 5b - 2c + 9 \\ &\text{Collecting like terms together:} \\ &= (3a - 2a) + (-4b + 5b) + (-c - 2c) + (6 + 9) \\ &= \mathbf{a + b - 3c + 15} \end{align*}
10. (Science) Explain any two characteristics of particles of matter.
[4 Marks]
Solution: Two fundamental characteristics of particles of matter are:
  1. Particles of matter have space between them: These spaces are called intermolecular spaces. The space is minimum in solids, moderate in liquids, and maximum in gases. (e.g., sugar dissolving in water occupies these spaces).
  2. Particles of matter are continuously moving: They possess kinetic energy. This motion is minimal (vibrational) in solids and highly random and rapid in gases. As temperature rises, the kinetic energy and movement of particles increase.

12. (Maths) The two adjacent sides of a rectangle are \( 5x^2 - 3y^2 \) and \( x^2 + 2xy \). Find the perimeter of the rectangle.
[5 Marks]
Solution: Let the Length (\(L\)) \(= 5x^2 - 3y^2\)
Let the Breadth (\(B\)) \(= x^2 + 2xy\)

The formula for the perimeter of a rectangle is:
\begin{align*} \text{Perimeter} &= 2 \times (L + B) \\ \text{Substituting the given expressions:} \\ \text{Perimeter} &= 2 \times [(5x^2 - 3y^2) + (x^2 + 2xy)] \\ \text{Combining like terms inside the bracket (none to combine in this step):} \\ &= 2 \times (5x^2 - 3y^2 + x^2 + 2xy) \\ \text{Grouping } x^2 \text{ terms:} \\ &= 2 \times (6x^2 + 2xy - 3y^2) \\ \text{Multiplying each term by 2:} \\ &= \mathbf{12x^2 + 4xy - 6y^2} \end{align*}
Answer: The perimeter of the rectangle is \( 12x^2 + 4xy - 6y^2 \).
13. (Maths) What must be subtracted from \( 3a^2 - 6ab - 3b^2 - 1 \) to get \( 4a^2 - 7ab - 4b^2 + 1 \)?
[5 Marks]
Solution: Let the required algebraic expression to be subtracted be \( X \).
According to the question:
\begin{align*} &(3a^2 - 6ab - 3b^2 - 1) - X = 4a^2 - 7ab - 4b^2 + 1 \\ &\text{Rearranging the equation to solve for } X: \\ &X = (3a^2 - 6ab - 3b^2 - 1) - (4a^2 - 7ab - 4b^2 + 1) \\ &\text{Opening the second bracket and reversing signs:} \\ &X = 3a^2 - 6ab - 3b^2 - 1 - 4a^2 + 7ab + 4b^2 - 1 \\ &\text{Collecting like terms:} \\ &X = (3a^2 - 4a^2) + (-6ab + 7ab) + (-3b^2 + 4b^2) + (-1 - 1) \\ &X = -a^2 + ab + b^2 - 2 \\ &X = \mathbf{-a^2 + b^2 + ab - 2} \quad \text{(Standard Form)} \end{align*}
Answer: The expression to be subtracted is \( -a^2 + ab + b^2 - 2 \).
14. (Maths) The perimeter of a triangle is \( 6p^2 - 4p + 9 \) and two of its sides are \( p^2 - 2p + 1 \) and \( 3p^2 - 5p + 3 \). Find the third side of the triangle.
[5 Marks]
Solution: The perimeter of a triangle is the sum of all its three sides.
Therefore, \(\text{Third Side} = \text{Perimeter} - (\text{Sum of given two sides})\).

Step 1: Find the sum of the two given sides.
\begin{align*} \text{Sum of two sides} &= (p^2 - 2p + 1) + (3p^2 - 5p + 3) \\ &= (1p^2 + 3p^2) + (-2p - 5p) + (1 + 3) \\ &= 4p^2 - 7p + 4 \end{align*}
Step 2: Subtract this sum from the Perimeter.
\begin{align*} \text{Third Side} &= (6p^2 - 4p + 9) - (4p^2 - 7p + 4) \\ \text{Change signs for subtraction:} \\ &= 6p^2 - 4p + 9 - 4p^2 + 7p - 4 \\ \text{Group like terms:} \\ &= (6p^2 - 4p^2) + (-4p + 7p) + (9 - 4) \\ &= \mathbf{2p^2 + 3p + 5} \end{align*}
Answer: The third side of the triangle is \( 2p^2 + 3p + 5 \).
