Detailed Solutions & Answer Key - Minor Test (Class 8 ICSE)

SECTION A (10 Marks)
1. Define contact and non-contact forces. Give one example of each.
2 Marks
Contact Forces: Forces that act on a body only when objects are in physical contact with each other.
Example: Frictional force, Muscular force. (1 Mark)
Non-Contact Forces: Forces that can act on an object from a distance without any physical contact.
Example: Gravitational force, Magnetic force. (1 Mark)
2. A solid metallic block has a mass of 120 g and occupies a volume of 30 cm³. Calculate its density in SI units.
2 Marks
Mass ($m$) = $120 \text{ g}$
Volume ($V$) = $30 \text{ cm}^3$
Density ($d$) = $\frac{\text{Mass}}{\text{Volume}} = \frac{120}{30} = 4 \text{ g/cm}^3$ (1 Mark)
To convert to SI units ($\text{kg/m}^3$), we multiply by 1000.
Density in SI units = $4 \times 1000 = 4000 \text{ kg/m}^3$ (1 Mark)
3. Find the area of a rhombus whose diagonals are 12 cm and 16 cm respectively.
2 Marks
Let diagonals be $d_1 = 12 \text{ cm}$ and $d_2 = 16 \text{ cm}$.
Area of a rhombus = $\frac{1}{2} \times d_1 \times d_2$
$$ \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times 12 \times 16 $$ $$ \text{Area} = 6 \times 16 $$
Area = $96 \text{ cm}^2$
4. The circumference of a circle is 88 cm. Find its area. (Take π = 22/7)
2 Marks
Circumference ($C$) = $2 \pi r = 88 \text{ cm}$
$$ 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times r = 88 $$ $$ \frac{44}{7} \times r = 88 $$ $$ r = \frac{88 \times 7}{44} = 2 \times 7 = 14 \text{ cm} $$
Area = $\pi r^2$
$$ \text{Area} = \frac{22}{7} \times 14 \times 14 = 22 \times 2 \times 14 $$
Area = $616 \text{ cm}^2$
5. Why does an iron nail sink in water, but a large steel ship floats? Explain briefly.
2 Marks
The density of a solid iron nail is greater than the density of water. Therefore, the upthrust (buoyant force) acting on it is less than its weight, causing it to sink. (1 Mark)
A ship, although made of steel, is hollow and traps a large volume of air. This makes its average density less than that of water. It displaces a volume of water whose weight is equal to the total weight of the ship, allowing it to float (Principle of Floatation). (1 Mark)
SECTION B (15 Marks)
6. Define 'Moment of Force' and state its SI unit. What are the two factors on which the turning effect of a force depends?
3 Marks
Definition: The turning effect produced by a force on a rigid body about a fixed point or axis is called the moment of force. It is equal to the product of the magnitude of the force and the perpendicular distance of the line of action of the force from the axis of rotation. (1 Mark)
SI Unit: Newton-metre (N m). (1 Mark)
Factors:
1. The magnitude of the force applied.
2. The perpendicular distance of the force from the pivoted point (axis of rotation). (1 Mark)
7. A rectangular grassy plot is 112 m long and 78 m broad. It has a gravel path 2.5 m wide all around it on the inside. Find the area of the gravel path.
3 Marks
Outer dimensions of the plot: Length ($L$) = 112 m, Breadth ($B$) = 78 m.
Outer Area = $112 \times 78 = 8736 \text{ m}^2$. (1 Mark)
The path is inside. Therefore, inner dimensions:
$$ \text{Inner Length } (l) = 112 - (2.5 + 2.5) = 112 - 5 = 107 \text{ m} $$ $$ \text{Inner Breadth } (b) = 78 - (2.5 + 2.5) = 78 - 5 = 73 \text{ m} $$
Inner Area = $107 \times 73 = 7811 \text{ m}^2$. (1 Mark)
Area of Path = Outer Area - Inner Area
$$ \text{Area} = 8736 - 7811 $$
Area of the path = $925 \text{ m}^2$ (1 Mark)
8. The relative density of silver is 10.8. The density of water is 10³ kg/m³. What is the density of silver in SI units? What does this imply?
3 Marks
Relative Density (RD) = $\frac{\text{Density of substance}}{\text{Density of water}}$
$$ 10.8 = \frac{\text{Density of silver}}{10^3 \text{ kg/m}^3} $$
Density of silver = $10.8 \times 10^3 \text{ kg/m}^3$ or $10800 \text{ kg/m}^3$ (1.5 Marks)
Implication: This statement implies that silver is 10.8 times heavier than an equal volume of water. (1.5 Marks)
9. Find the area of a trapezium whose parallel sides are 24 cm and 20 cm, and the perpendicular distance between them is 15 cm.
3 Marks
Parallel sides: $a = 24 \text{ cm}$, $b = 20 \text{ cm}$
Height (distance between them), $h = 15 \text{ cm}$
Formula: Area = $\frac{1}{2} \times (\text{Sum of parallel sides}) \times \text{height}$ (1 Mark)
$$ \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times (24 + 20) \times 15 $$ $$ \text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times 44 \times 15 $$ $$ \text{Area} = 22 \times 15 $$
Area = $330 \text{ cm}^2$ (2 Marks)
10. A solid metallic cube of side 5 cm has a mass of 1000 g. Find its density in g/cm³. Will it float or sink in a liquid of density 1.2 g/cm³? Give a reason.
3 Marks
Side of cube = $5 \text{ cm}$
Volume of cube = $5 \times 5 \times 5 = 125 \text{ cm}^3$ (1 Mark)
Mass = $1000 \text{ g}$
Density = $\frac{\text{Mass}}{\text{Volume}} = \frac{1000}{125} = 8 \text{ g/cm}^3$ (1 Mark)
The cube will sink in the liquid.
Reason: The density of the cube ($8 \text{ g/cm}^3$) is greater than the density of the liquid ($1.2 \text{ g/cm}^3$). (1 Mark)
SECTION C (25 Marks)
11. A room is 8 m long, 6 m wide, and 4 m high. Find the total cost of whitewashing the four walls and the ceiling at the rate of ₹ 50 per square metre.
5 Marks
Dimensions: $L = 8 \text{ m}$, $B = 6 \text{ m}$, $H = 4 \text{ m}$
Area of 4 walls (Lateral Surface Area) = $2(L + B) \times H$
$$ \text{Area}_{\text{walls}} = 2(8 + 6) \times 4 = 2(14) \times 4 = 112 \text{ m}^2 \quad \text{(2 Marks)} $$
Area of ceiling = $L \times B$
$$ \text{Area}_{\text{ceiling}} = 8 \times 6 = 48 \text{ m}^2 \quad \text{(1 Mark)} $$
Total area to be whitewashed = $112 + 48 = 160 \text{ m}^2$ (1 Mark)
Cost of whitewashing = Total Area $\times$ Rate
$$ \text{Cost} = 160 \times 50 $$
Total Cost = ₹ 8,000 (1 Mark)
12. (a) Differentiate between mass and weight.
(b) A body of volume 100 cm³ weighs 5 kgf in air. Immersed in liquid of density 1.8 × 10³ kg/m³. Find (i) Upthrust (ii) Apparent weight.
5 Marks
(a) Differences (Any two - 2 Marks):
1. Mass is the quantity of matter in a body, Weight is the gravitational force acting on it.
2. Mass is scalar; Weight is a vector quantity.
3. Mass remains constant everywhere; Weight varies from place to place.
4. SI unit of Mass is kg; SI unit of weight is Newton (N).
(b) Numerical (3 Marks):
Volume of body ($V$) = $100 \text{ cm}^3 = 100 \times 10^{-6} \text{ m}^3 = 10^{-4} \text{ m}^3$
Density of liquid ($\rho$) = $1.8 \times 10^3 \text{ kg/m}^3 = 1800 \text{ kg/m}^3$
Weight in air ($W_1$) = $5 \text{ kgf}$
(i) Upthrust ($F_B$): Formula = $V \rho g$
$$ F_B = 10^{-4} \times 1800 \times g = 0.18 \text{ kgf} \quad \text{(1.5 Marks)} $$
(ii) Apparent weight in liquid: = Weight in Air - Upthrust
$$ W_{\text{app}} = 5 - 0.18 = 4.82 \text{ kgf} \quad \text{(1.5 Marks)} $$
13. A rectangular park 60m × 40m. Two crossroads of width 3m run at right angles through centre. Find area of crossroads and cost of concreting at ₹ 150/m².
5 Marks
Area of the road parallel to the length = $60 \text{ m} \times 3 \text{ m} = 180 \text{ m}^2$ (1 Mark)
Area of the road parallel to the breadth = $40 \text{ m} \times 3 \text{ m} = 120 \text{ m}^2$ (1 Mark)
Area of the common central intersection (counted twice) = $3 \text{ m} \times 3 \text{ m} = 9 \text{ m}^2$ (1 Mark)
Total area of crossroads = (Area of road 1) + (Area of road 2) - (Common Area)
$$ \text{Total Area} = 180 + 120 - 9 = 300 - 9 = 291 \text{ m}^2 \quad \text{(1 Mark)} $$
Cost of concreting = Area $\times$ Rate
$$ \text{Cost} = 291 \times 150 $$
Total Cost = ₹ 43,650 (1 Mark)
14. (a) State the Principle of Floatation. (b) A block of wood of volume 50 cm³ and density 0.6 g/cm³ floats in water. Find its mass and volume immersed.
5 Marks
(a) Principle of Floatation (2 Marks):
It states that when a body floats in a liquid, the weight of the liquid displaced by its immersed part is equal to the total weight of the body.
(b) Numerical (3 Marks):
Volume of block ($V$) = $50 \text{ cm}^3$, Density of block ($d$) = $0.6 \text{ g/cm}^3$
Mass of the block: $m = V \times d = 50 \times 0.6$
Mass = $30 \text{ g}$ (1 Mark)
Volume immersed: By principle of floatation, Weight of block = Weight of water displaced.
Mass of block = Mass of water displaced $\Rightarrow 30 \text{ g}$ of water is displaced.
Since density of water is $1 \text{ g/cm}^3$, Volume of $30 \text{ g}$ water = $30 \text{ cm}^3$.
Therefore, Volume of water displaced = Volume of block immersed.
Volume immersed in water = $30 \text{ cm}^3$ (2 Marks)
Alternative formula: $v = \frac{d}{d_w} \times V = \frac{0.6}{1} \times 50 = 30 \text{ cm}^3$
15. A circular park is surrounded by a path 7 m wide. Circumference of the park is 352 m. Find total area of the path.
5 Marks
Let the inner radius of the park be $r$.
Circumference = $2 \pi r = 352 \text{ m}$
$$ 2 \times \frac{22}{7} \times r = 352 $$ $$ r = \frac{352 \times 7}{44} = 8 \times 7 = 56 \text{ m} \quad \text{(1.5 Marks)} $$
Width of the path = $7 \text{ m}$. Outer radius ($R$) = $56 + 7 = 63 \text{ m}$ (1 Mark)
Area of the path = Area of outer circle - Area of inner circle = $\pi(R^2 - r^2)$
$$ \text{Area} = \frac{22}{7} \times (63^2 - 56^2) $$ $$ \text{Area} = \frac{22}{7} \times (63 - 56) \times (63 + 56) \quad \text{(using } a^2-b^2 \text{ identity)} $$ $$ \text{Area} = \frac{22}{7} \times 7 \times 119 \quad \text{(1.5 Marks)} $$ $$ \text{Area} = 22 \times 119 $$
Area of the path = $2618 \text{ m}^2$ (1 Mark)
