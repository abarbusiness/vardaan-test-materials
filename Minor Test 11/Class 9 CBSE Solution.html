SECTION A: MATHEMATICS (Algebraic Identities)
1. Evaluate $(99)^2$ using a suitable algebraic identity.
[2 Marks]

Solution:

We can write $99$ as $(100 - 1)$.

Using the identity: $(a - b)^2 = a^2 - 2ab + b^2$

$$\begin{aligned} (99)^2 &= (100 - 1)^2 \\ &= (100)^2 - 2(100)(1) + (1)^2 \\ &= 10000 - 200 + 1 \\ &= 9800 + 1 \\ &= 9801 \end{aligned}$$
9801
2. Expand using a suitable identity: $(2x - y + z)^2$.
[2 Marks]

Solution:

Using the identity: $(a + b + c)^2 = a^2 + b^2 + c^2 + 2ab + 2bc + 2ca$

Here, $a = 2x, b = -y, c = z$

$$\begin{aligned} (2x - y + z)^2 &= (2x)^2 + (-y)^2 + (z)^2 + 2(2x)(-y) + 2(-y)(z) + 2(z)(2x) \\ &= 4x^2 + y^2 + z^2 - 4xy - 2yz + 4zx \end{aligned}$$
$4x^2 + y^2 + z^2 - 4xy - 2yz + 4zx$
3. Factorise: $\displaystyle 4x^2 - \frac{y^2}{100}$.
[2 Marks]

Solution:

We can rewrite the expression as perfect squares:

$$4x^2 - \frac{y^2}{100} = (2x)^2 - \left(\frac{y}{10}\right)^2$$

Using the identity: $a^2 - b^2 = (a - b)(a + b)$

$$\text{Here, } a = 2x, \text{ and } b = \frac{y}{10}$$ $$= \left(2x - \frac{y}{10}\right)\left(2x + \frac{y}{10}\right)$$
$\left(2x - \frac{y}{10}\right)\left(2x + \frac{y}{10}\right)$
4. If $x + y + z = 0$, show that $x^3 + y^3 + z^3 = 3xyz$.
[3 Marks]

Solution:

Given: $x + y + z = 0$

$\implies x + y = -z$

Cubing both sides:

$$\begin{aligned} (x + y)^3 &= (-z)^3 \\ x^3 + y^3 + 3xy(x + y) &= -z^3 \end{aligned}$$

Since we know $x + y = -z$, substitute it back into the equation:

$$\begin{aligned} x^3 + y^3 + 3xy(-z) &= -z^3 \\ x^3 + y^3 - 3xyz &= -z^3 \\ x^3 + y^3 + z^3 &= 3xyz \end{aligned}$$
Hence Proved.

Here, $x = 2a$ and $y = b$.

Using the identity:

$$\begin{aligned} &= (2a + b)^3 \\ &= (2a + b)(2a + b)(2a + b) \end{aligned}$$
$(2a + b)^3$
6. Evaluate $103 \times 107$ using an appropriate algebraic identity.
[3 Marks]

Solution:

We can rewrite the numbers as:

$$103 \times 107 = (100 + 3) \times (100 + 7)$$

Using the identity: $(x + a)(x + b) = x^2 + (a + b)x + ab$

Here, $x = 100$, $a = 3$, $b = 7$

$$\begin{aligned} (100 + 3)(100 + 7) &= (100)^2 + (3 + 7) \times 100 + (3 \times 7) \\ &= 10000 + (10) \times 100 + 21 \\ &= 10000 + 1000 + 21 \\ &= 11021 \end{aligned}$$
11021
7. (i) If $\displaystyle x^2 + \frac{1}{x^2} = 51$, find the value of $\displaystyle \left(x - \frac{1}{x}\right)$.
(ii) Expand: $(3a - 7b - c)^2$.
[5 Marks]

Solution:

Part (i): [2.5 Marks]

We know the identity: $\displaystyle \left(x - \frac{1}{x}\right)^2 = x^2 + \frac{1}{x^2} - 2\left(x\right)\left(\frac{1}{x}\right)$

$$\begin{aligned} \left(x - \frac{1}{x}\right)^2 &= x^2 + \frac{1}{x^2} - 2 \end{aligned}$$

Substitute $\displaystyle x^2 + \frac{1}{x^2} = 51$:

$$\begin{aligned} \left(x - \frac{1}{x}\right)^2 &= 51 - 2 \\ \left(x - \frac{1}{x}\right)^2 &= 49 \end{aligned}$$

Taking square root on both sides:

$$x - \frac{1}{x} = \pm 7$$

Part (ii): [2.5 Marks]

Using the identity: $(x + y + z)^2 = x^2 + y^2 + z^2 + 2xy + 2yz + 2zx$

Here, $x = 3a, y = -7b, z = -c$

$$\begin{aligned} (3a - 7b - c)^2 &= (3a)^2 + (-7b)^2 + (-c)^2 + 2(3a)(-7b) + 2(-7b)(-c) + 2(-c)(3a) \\ &= 9a^2 + 49b^2 + c^2 - 42ab + 14bc - 6ac \end{aligned}$$
8. (i) Verify that: $x^3 + y^3 = (x + y)(x^2 - xy + y^2)$.
(ii) Factorise using the above identity: $64m^3 - 343n^3$.
[5 Marks]

Solution:

Part (i): [2.5 Marks]

We take the Right Hand Side (RHS) and expand it:

$$\begin{aligned} \text{RHS} &= (x + y)(x^2 - xy + y^2) \\ &= x(x^2 - xy + y^2) + y(x^2 - xy + y^2) \\ &= x^3 - x^2y + xy^2 + yx^2 - xy^2 + y^3 \end{aligned}$$

Since $-x^2y$ and $+yx^2$ cancel out, and $+xy^2$ and $-xy^2$ cancel out:

$$\begin{aligned} \text{RHS} &= x^3 + y^3 = \text{LHS} \end{aligned}$$

Hence Verified.

Part (ii): [2.5 Marks]

We are given: $64m^3 - 343n^3$

This can be written as difference of cubes: $(4m)^3 - (7n)^3$

Using the identity for difference of cubes: $a^3 - b^3 = (a - b)(a^2 + ab + b^2)$

Here, $a = 4m$ and $b = 7n$

$$\begin{aligned} (4m)^3 - (7n)^3 &= (4m - 7n)[(4m)^2 + (4m)(7n) + (7n)^2] \\ &= (4m - 7n)(16m^2 + 28mn + 49n^2) \end{aligned}$$
$(4m - 7n)(16m^2 + 28mn + 49n^2)$
SECTION B: SCIENCE (Force and Laws of Motion)
9. Define inertia. How is the inertia of an object related to its mass?
[2 Marks]

Solution:

Inertia: It is the natural tendency of an object to resist any change in its state of rest or of uniform motion.

Relation with Mass: Mass is the quantitative measure of inertia. Heavier objects have more inertia than lighter objects. Therefore, the inertia of an object is directly proportional to its mass.

10. Why is it advised to tie any luggage kept on the roof of a bus with a rope?
[2 Marks]

Solution:

When a bus starts suddenly, the lower part of the luggage comes in motion along with the bus, but the upper part tends to remain at rest due to the inertia of rest, and it may fall backward.

Similarly, when a moving bus stops suddenly, the bus comes to rest but the luggage tends to remain in motion due to the inertia of motion, and it may fall forward. To prevent the luggage from falling, it is advised to tie it with a rope.

11. A body of mass 2 kg is moving with a constant velocity of 5 m/s. What is the net force acting on the body?
[2 Marks]

Solution:

Given: Velocity ($v$) = 5 m/s, which is constant.

Since velocity is constant, there is no change in velocity, meaning Acceleration ($a$) = 0 m/s$^2$.

According to Newton's Second Law of Motion:

$$F = m \times a$$ $$F = 2 \text{ kg} \times 0 \text{ m/s}^2 = 0 \text{ N}$$
Net force = 0 N (Zero)
12. State Newton's Second Law of Motion. Derive its mathematical formulation ($F = ma$).
[3 Marks]

Solution:

Newton's Second Law of Motion: The rate of change of momentum of an object is directly proportional to the applied unbalanced force in the direction of the force.

Derivation:

Let an object of mass $m$ move with an initial velocity $u$. A constant force $F$ acts on it for time $t$, accelerating it to a final velocity $v$.

Initial momentum, $p_1 = mu$

Final momentum, $p_2 = mv$

Change in momentum = $p_2 - p_1 = mv - mu = m(v - u)$

Rate of change of momentum = $\frac{m(v - u)}{t}$

According to the law:

$$F \propto \frac{m(v - u)}{t}$$ $$F = k \cdot m\left(\frac{v - u}{t}\right)$$

Since acceleration $a = \frac{v - u}{t}$:

$$F = kma$$

We choose the unit of force such that $k = 1$. Therefore,

$F = ma$
13. Explain why a cricketer pulls his hands backwards while catching a fast-moving cricket ball.
[3 Marks]

Solution:

According to Newton's second law of motion, the force required to stop a moving body is directly proportional to the rate of change of momentum ($F \propto \frac{\Delta p}{t}$).

While catching a fast-moving ball, a cricketer pulls his hands backwards to increase the time ($t$) taken to bring the ball to rest (i.e., to reduce its momentum to zero).

By increasing the time duration, the rate of change of momentum decreases. As a result, a much smaller impact force ($F$) is exerted on his hands, preventing severe injuries.

14. A truck starts from rest and rolls down a hill with a constant acceleration. It travels a distance of 400 m in 20 s. Find its acceleration. Also, calculate the force acting on it if its mass is 7 tonnes. (Hint: 1 tonne = 1000 kg).
[3 Marks]

Solution:

Given parameters:

  • Initial velocity ($u$) = 0 m/s (starts from rest)
  • Distance ($s$) = 400 m
  • Time ($t$) = 20 s
  • Mass ($m$) = 7 tonnes = $7 \times 1000 = 7000$ kg

Using the second equation of motion: $s = ut + \frac{1}{2}at^2$

$$\begin{aligned} 400 &= (0 \times 20) + \frac{1}{2} \times a \times (20)^2 \\ 400 &= 0 + \frac{1}{2} \times a \times 400 \\ 400 &= 200a \\ a &= \frac{400}{200} = 2 \text{ m/s}^2 \end{aligned}$$

Now, Force ($F$) = $ma$

$$F = 7000 \text{ kg} \times 2 \text{ m/s}^2 = 14000 \text{ N}$$
Acceleration = 2 m/s$^2$ and Force = 14000 N.
15. (a) State the Law of Conservation of Momentum.
(b) A bullet of mass 20 g is horizontally fired with a velocity of 150 m/s from a pistol of mass 2 kg. What is the recoil velocity of the pistol?
[5 Marks]

Solution:

(a) Law of Conservation of Momentum: [2 Marks]
It states that when two or more bodies interact with each other, their total momentum remains constant, provided no external unbalanced force acts upon them.

(b) Numerical: [3 Marks]

  • Mass of bullet ($m_1$) = 20 g = 0.02 kg
  • Velocity of bullet ($v_1$) = 150 m/s
  • Mass of pistol ($m_2$) = 2 kg
  • Initial velocities of bullet ($u_1$) and pistol ($u_2$) are 0 (both are at rest).
  • Let the recoil velocity of the pistol be $v_2$.

According to the law of conservation of momentum:

$$\begin{aligned} m_1u_1 + m_2u_2 &= m_1v_1 + m_2v_2 \\ (0.02 \times 0) + (2 \times 0) &= (0.02 \times 150) + (2 \times v_2) \\ 0 &= 3 + 2v_2 \\ 2v_2 &= -3 \\ v_2 &= -1.5 \text{ m/s} \end{aligned}$$

The negative sign indicates that the direction in which the pistol recoils is opposite to that of the bullet.

Recoil velocity of pistol = 1.5 m/s (backwards).
16. (a) State Newton's Third Law of Motion and give one example from daily life.
(b) Two objects of masses 100 g and 200 g are moving along the same line and direction with velocities of 2 m/s and 1 m/s, respectively. They collide, and after the collision, the first object moves at a velocity of 1.67 m/s. Determine the velocity of the second object.
[5 Marks]

Solution:

(a) Newton's Third Law: [2 Marks]
Statement: To every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction, and they act on two different bodies.
Example: Walking. When we walk, we push the ground backward (action). In response, the ground pushes our foot forward with an equal force (reaction), allowing us to move.

(b) Numerical: [3 Marks]

Given Data:

  • Mass of object 1 ($m_1$) = 100 g = 0.1 kg
  • Mass of object 2 ($m_2$) = 200 g = 0.2 kg
  • Initial velocity of object 1 ($u_1$) = 2 m/s
  • Initial velocity of object 2 ($u_2$) = 1 m/s
  • Final velocity of object 1 ($v_1$) = 1.67 m/s
  • Let the final velocity of object 2 be $v_2$.

By the law of conservation of momentum:

$$\begin{aligned} m_1u_1 + m_2u_2 &= m_1v_1 + m_2v_2 \\ (0.1 \times 2) + (0.2 \times 1) &= (0.1 \times 1.67) + (0.2 \times v_2) \\ 0.2 + 0.2 &= 0.167 + 0.2v_2 \\ 0.4 &= 0.167 + 0.2v_2 \\ 0.2v_2 &= 0.4 - 0.167 \\ 0.2v_2 &= 0.233 \\ v_2 &= \frac{0.233}{0.2} \\ v_2 &= 1.165 \text{ m/s} \end{aligned}$$
Velocity of the second object is 1.165 m/s.
