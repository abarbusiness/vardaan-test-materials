PART III: GENERAL MATHEMATICS
31. (B) -b/a: For any quadratic polynomial \( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \), the sum of the roots (\( \alpha + \beta \)) is given by the formula \( -\frac{b}{a} \). The product of the roots (\( \alpha\beta \)) is \( \frac{c}{a} \).
32. (C) 1: According to the fundamental trigonometric identities, for any angle \( \theta \), the sum of the square of sine and square of cosine is always equal to 1 (\( \sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta = 1 \)).
33. (B) 47: The given A.P. is 2, 7, 12, 17... Here, the first term \( a = 2 \) and the common difference \( d = 7 - 2 = 5 \). The formula for the \( n \)-th term is \( T_n = a + (n - 1)d \).
For the 10th term: \( T_{10} = 2 + (10 - 1)5 = 2 + (9 \times 5) = 2 + 45 = 47 \).
34. (C) 90°: A fundamental theorem in circle geometry states that the angle subtended by an arc at the center is double the angle subtended by it at any remaining part of the circle. Since a diameter forms a 180° straight angle at the center, the angle in the semicircle is half of that, which is 90° (a right angle).
35. (B) πr²h: The volume of a cylinder is found by multiplying the area of its circular base (\( \pi r^2 \)) by its height (\( h \)). Therefore, Volume = \( \pi r^2 h \).
36. (C) Non-terminating non-repeating: Rational numbers have decimal expansions that either terminate or are non-terminating but repeating. Irrational numbers (like \( \sqrt{2}, \pi \)) have decimal expansions that never end and never form a repeating pattern.
37. (C) 1: The probability of any event \( P(E) \) always lies between 0 and 1 inclusive (\( 0 \le P(E) \le 1 \)). An impossible event has a probability of 0, and a sure or certain event has a probability of exactly 1 (or 100%).
38. (B) A unique solution: When the ratio of the coefficients of \( x \) (\( \frac{a_1}{a_2} \)) is not equal to the ratio of the coefficients of \( y \) (\( \frac{b_1}{b_2} \)), the two lines intersect at exactly one point, meaning the system has a unique solution.
39. (B) 5 units: Use the distance formula: \( d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2} \).
\( d = \sqrt{(4 - 0)^2 + (0 - 3)^2} = \sqrt{(4)^2 + (-3)^2} = \sqrt{16 + 9} = \sqrt{25} = 5 \) units.
40. (B) Mode = 3Median - 2Mean: In a moderately skewed distribution, Karl Pearson's empirical relationship between the three measures of central tendency is given precisely by this formula.
PART IV: APTITUDE & REASONING
41. (C) 35: Analyze the differences between consecutive terms: \( 8 - 3 = 5 \); \( 15 - 8 = 7 \); \( 24 - 15 = 9 \). The differences are odd numbers increasing by 2. The next difference should be 11. Therefore, \( 24 + 11 = 35 \). (Alternatively, the series follows the pattern \( n^2 - 1 \): \( 2^2-1, 3^2-1, 4^2-1, 5^2-1, 6^2-1=35 \)).
42. (A) HKTG: Each letter in the word is shifted exactly 2 positions forward in the English alphabet.
W(+2)=Y, A(+2)=C, T(+2)=V, E(+2)=G, R(+2)=T.
Applying the same logic to FIRE: F(+2)=H, I(+2)=K, R(+2)=T, E(+2)=G. Answer is HKTG.
43. (C) His son's: Break down the statement. "My father's son" refers to the speaker himself, because the speaker explicitly states, "I have no brother or sister." Therefore, the statement "that man's father is [me]" means the speaker is the father of the person in the photograph. Thus, the photograph is of his son.
44. (B) 13 km: The boy walks 5 km North, and a right turn means he walks 12 km East. His path forms a right-angled triangle. The shortest distance from the start is the hypotenuse. Using Pythagoras theorem: \( \text{Distance} = \sqrt{5^2 + 12^2} = \sqrt{25 + 144} = \sqrt{169} = 13 \) km.
45. (C) 75°: Use the clock angle formula: \( \text{Angle} = |30H - 5.5M| \), where H is hours and M is minutes.
Here H = 3 and M = 30.
\( \text{Angle} = |30(3) - 5.5(30)| = |90 - 165| = |-75| = 75^\circ \).
46. (A) Monday: A normal year has 365 days, which results in 1 odd day (52 weeks and 1 day). 2011 is not a leap year. Therefore, the day of the week advances by exactly 1 day. If it was Sunday in 2010, it will be Monday in 2011.
47. (A) 5: Use the formula for sets: \( n(A \cup B) = n(A) + n(B) - n(A \cap B) \).
Total who like at least one beverage = \( 30 \text{ (Tea)} + 25 \text{ (Coffee)} - 10 \text{ (Both)} = 45 \) students.
Total students in class = 50. Therefore, students who like neither = \( 50 - 45 = 5 \).
48. (B) 12 years: Let son's present age = \( x \). Father's present age = \( 3x \).
After 12 years: Son's age = \( x + 12 \), Father's age = \( 3x + 12 \).
According to the condition: \( 3x + 12 = 2(x + 12) \)
\( 3x + 12 = 2x + 24 \)
\( x = 12 \). The son is 12 years old.
49. (B) 34: The formula to find the total number of items in a row when the position of one item is known from both ends is:
\( \text{Total} = (\text{Position from Top} + \text{Position from Bottom}) - 1 \).
Total = \( 17 + 18 - 1 = 35 - 1 = 34 \).
50. (C) Pressure: This is an instrument-measurement analogy. An odometer is an instrument mounted on vehicles used to measure distance/mileage. Similarly, a barometer is an instrument used specifically to measure atmospheric pressure.
