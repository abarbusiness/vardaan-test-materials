PART III: GENERAL MATHEMATICS

31. (A) 12: The square root of 144 is 12, because 12 × 12 = 144.
32. (B) 5: Given the linear equation 3x - 5 = 10. Add 5 to both sides: 3x = 15. Divide by 3: x = 5.
33. (C) 64: Cube of 4 means 4³ = 4 × 4 × 4 = 64.
34. (C) -4/3: The multiplicative inverse (reciprocal) of a number a/b is b/a. So, for -3/4, it is -4/3.
35. (C) 360°: The sum of the interior angles of any quadrilateral is always 360 degrees.
36. (A) 40 cm²: Area of a rhombus = ½ × d1 × d2. Area = ½ × 10 × 8 = ½ × 80 = 40 cm².
37. (C) 128: Using laws of exponents, a^m × a^n = a^(m+n). So, 2³ × 2⁴ = 2⁷. 2⁷ = 128.
38. (B) 15%: Marked Price = 800, Selling Price = 680. Discount = 800 - 680 = 120. Discount % = (Discount / MP) × 100 = (120 / 800) × 100 = 15%.
39. (A) 15: If x² = 225, then x is the square root of 225. Since x is positive, x = 15.
40. (B) 6.4 × 10⁻⁵: To write 0.000064 in standard form, move the decimal 5 places to the right to get 6.4. The exponent becomes -5.

PART IV: APTITUDE & REASONING

41. (C) 37: Calculate the difference between consecutive terms: 5-2 = 3; 10-5 = 5; 17-10 = 7; 26-17 = 9. The differences are odd numbers (3, 5, 7, 9). The next difference is 11. Therefore, 26 + 11 = 37. (Alternatively: sequence is n² + 1).
42. (A) UVWFGPV: Each letter in TEACHER is shifted forward by +2 in the alphabetical order. (T+2=V, E+2=G, A+2=C, etc.). Applying the same to STUDENT: S+2=U, T+2=V, U+2=W, D+2=F, E+2=G, N+2=P, T+2=V.
43. (B) 39: The code is the sum of the numerical values of the letters. C(3) + A(1) + T(20) = 24. D(4) + O(15) + G(7) = 26. Therefore, R(18) + A(1) + T(20) = 39.
44. (B) Grandson/Granddaughter: C is the mother of A. Since A and B are siblings (brother/sister), C is also the mother of B. D is the father of C (mother's father). Therefore, B is the grandchild (Grandson or Granddaughter) of D.
45. (A) 15m: Boy walks 10m North. Turns right (faces East) and walks 10m. Turns left (faces North) and walks 5m. Turns left (faces West) and walks 10m. He is exactly directly North of his starting point. Distance = 10m + 5m = 15m.
46. (C) 35: 9(3²), 16(4²), 25(5²), and 49(7²) are all perfect squares. 35 is not a perfect square.
47. (A) 2, 4, 1, 3: The logical hiring sequence is: Application (2) → Written Test (4) → Interview (1) → Job Offer (3).
48. (B) V: The sequence consists of pairs of letters from opposite ends of the alphabet. A (1st from start) and Z (1st from end). C (3rd from start) and X (3rd from end). E (5th from start), so next is 5th from end, which is V.
49. (B) Wednesday: The year 2024 is a leap year, which has 366 days. Therefore, there are 2 odd days. If 1st Jan 2024 is Monday, adding 2 days gives Wednesday for 1st Jan 2025.
50. (C) 12: Let the number of cows be 'c' and hens be 'h'. Total heads: c + h = 20 (Equation 1). Total legs: Cows have 4, hens have 2. So, 4c + 2h = 64 (Equation 2). Multiply Eq 1 by 2: 2c + 2h = 40. Subtract this from Eq 2: 2c = 24. Therefore, c = 12. There are 12 cows.
