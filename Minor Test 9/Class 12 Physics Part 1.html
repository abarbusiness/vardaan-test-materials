Surprise Test - MiT 09 - Physics Part 1 (Electric Potential & Capacitance)
Class: 12 Subject: Physics Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 40
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Section A contains 5 questions of 2 marks each.
  • Section B contains 5 questions of 3 marks each.
  • Section C contains 3 questions of 5 marks each.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly. Use properly labeled diagrams wherever necessary.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Two equipotential surfaces can never intersect each other. Justify this statement with a valid scientific reason.
2.
Write the expression for the potential energy of an electric dipole of dipole moment \( \vec{p} \) placed in a uniform electric field \( \vec{E} \). Identify the conditions for stable and unstable equilibrium.
3.
A charge of \( 5\mu C \) is moved from a point \( A \) to a point \( B \) on an equipotential surface. What is the work done by the electrostatic force in this process? Explain briefly.
4.
Draw the equipotential surfaces for a single positive point charge. How does the spacing between the surfaces change as we move away from the charge?
5.
Establish the relationship between electric field intensity (\( E \)) and electric potential (\( V \)) at a point.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
Derive an expression for the electrostatic potential energy of a system of two point charges \( q_1 \) and \( q_2 \) separated by a distance \( r \) in the absence of any external electric field.
7.
Two capacitors of capacitances \( 3 \mu F \) and \( 6 \mu F \) are connected in series across a \( 12V \) battery. Calculate:
(i) The equivalent capacitance of the combination.
(ii) The potential difference across the \( 3 \mu F \) capacitor.
8.
Derive the expression for the energy stored in a parallel plate capacitor of capacitance \( C \) charged to a potential \( V \).
9.
A parallel plate capacitor is charged to a certain potential difference by a battery. The battery is then disconnected, and a dielectric slab of dielectric constant \( K \) is inserted, completely filling the space between the plates. State the effect (with reasoning) on:
(i) The capacitance of the capacitor.
(ii) The electric field between the plates.
(iii) The energy stored in the capacitor.
10.
Three point charges \( +q \), \( -2q \), and \( +3q \) are placed at the vertices \( A \), \( B \), and \( C \) respectively of an equilateral triangle of side \( a \). Find the total electrostatic potential energy of the system.
SECTION C (15 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
(a) Derive an expression for the capacitance of a parallel plate capacitor of plate area \( A \) and plate separation \( d \) when the space between the plates is completely filled with a vacuum. (2 Marks)
(b) A dielectric slab of thickness \( t \) (\( t < d \)) and dielectric constant \( K \) is introduced between the plates of the above capacitor. Obtain the new expression for its capacitance. (3 Marks)
12.
(a) Define an electric dipole. Find the expression for the electric potential at a point on the axial line of an electric dipole. (3 Marks)
(b) Show that the electric potential at any point on the equatorial line of an electric dipole is zero. (2 Marks)
13.
A network of four capacitors \( C_1 = 10 \mu F \), \( C_2 = 20 \mu F \), \( C_3 = 30 \mu F \), and \( C_4 = 40 \mu F \) are connected to a \( 100V \) DC supply. \( C_1 \), \( C_2 \), and \( C_3 \) are connected in parallel, and this combination is connected in series with \( C_4 \).
(a) Draw the circuit diagram for the given arrangement. (1 Mark)
(b) Calculate the equivalent capacitance of the network. (2 Marks)
(c) Calculate the total charge supplied by the battery. (1 Mark)
(d) Calculate the energy stored in the capacitor \( C_4 \). (1 Mark)
