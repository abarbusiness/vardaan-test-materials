SECTION A: MATHEMATICS (20 Marks) (Syllabus: HCF and LCM)
Q1.
Answer the following short questions: (5 Marks)
(a) Find the Highest Common Factor (HCF) of 36 and 84 using the prime factorization method. [2]
(b) The product of two numbers is 2160 and their HCF is 12. Find their Lowest Common Multiple (LCM). [2]
(c) State True or False: The LCM of two co-prime numbers is always equal to their product. [1]
Q2.
Solve the following: (6 Marks)
(a) Find the LCM of 24, 36, and 40 using the common division method. [3]
(b) Find the greatest number which divides 2011 and 2623, leaving remainders 9 and 5 respectively. [3]
Q3.
Word Problems (Show all necessary statements): (9 Marks)
(a) Three bells toll at intervals of 12, 15, and 18 minutes respectively. If they all start tolling together at 9:00 AM, at what time will they next toll together? [3]
(b) Find the least number of 4 digits which is exactly divisible by 12, 15, 20, and 35. [3]
(c) Three measuring rods are 64 cm, 80 cm, and 96 cm in length. Find the least length of cloth that can be measured an exact number of times, using any of the three rods. [3]
SECTION B: SCIENCE (20 Marks) (Syllabus: Chapter 1 - Shifting Shapes: Exploring States of Matter)
Q4.
Objective Type Questions: (5 Marks)
(a) Fill in the blank: Anything that has mass and occupies space is called _____________. [1]
(b) Fill in the blank: The force of attraction exerted by molecules of different substances on each other is known as the force of _____________. [1]
(c) Fill in the blank: The process of continuous random motion of molecules spreading in a given space is called _____________. [1]
(d) True or False: In gases, the intermolecular force of attraction is very strong and molecules are closely packed. [1]
(e) Name the process: A substance changes from its gaseous state to its liquid state on cooling. [1]
Q5.
Short Answer Type Questions: (6 Marks)
(a) What is the difference between the force of cohesion and the force of adhesion? Give one example of the force of adhesion. [3]
(b) Give Reason: When we spray perfume at one corner of the room, we can smell its fragrance in the entire room. Why? [2]
(c) Define the term 'element'. [1]
Q6.
Long Answer Type / Analytical Questions: (9 Marks)
(a) Compare Solids, Liquids, and Gases on the basis of the following three parameters:
(i) Intermolecular space
(ii) Intermolecular force of attraction
(iii) Rigidity [3]
(b) Describe an activity/experiment to prove that particles of matter have spaces between them (intermolecular spaces). Hint: You may use the example of water and powdered sugar. [3]
(c) Explain what happens to the movement of molecules when a solid is heated and eventually turns into a liquid (melting). [3]
