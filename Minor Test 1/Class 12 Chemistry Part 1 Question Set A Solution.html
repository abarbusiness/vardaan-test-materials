SECTION A: Conceptual Fundamentals
1.
An amalgam of mercury with sodium is a solid solution. Identify the physical states of the solute and the solvent respectively:
[4 Marks]
(A) Solid, Liquid
(B) Liquid, Solid
(C) Solid, Solid
(D) Liquid, Liquid
Correct Answer: (B) Liquid, Solid
Solution: An amalgam is an alloy of mercury with another metal. Here, the final physical state of the mixture is solid. The component determining the physical state is the solvent (Sodium, Solid). The component dissolved in it is the solute (Mercury, Liquid).
2.
Which of the following concentration expressions is highly preferred in precise scientific calculations due to its temperature independence?
[4 Marks]
(A) Molarity
(B) Volume Percentage (v/v)
(C) Mass by Volume Percentage (w/v)
(D) Molality
Correct Answer: (D) Molality
Solution: Molarity, v/v%, and w/v% all depend on the volume of the solution, which expands or contracts with changes in temperature. Molality is defined as moles of solute per kg of solvent. Since it depends strictly on mass (which is invariant with temperature), it is preferred for precise calculations.
3.
For a solution formed by completely mixing two ideal volatile liquids, which of the following thermodynamic conditions holds true?
[4 Marks]
(A) ΔHmix > 0, ΔVmix = 0
(B) ΔHmix = 0, ΔVmix = 0
(C) ΔHmix < 0, ΔVmix < 0
(D) ΔHmix = 0, ΔVmix > 0
Correct Answer: (B) ΔHmix = 0, ΔVmix = 0
Solution: By definition, an ideal solution obeys Raoult's Law at all concentrations. Because the A-B intermolecular forces are identical in strength to the pure A-A and B-B forces, no heat is evolved or absorbed (ΔHmix = 0) and no volume expansion or contraction occurs (ΔVmix = 0).
4.
Aquatic species are generally more comfortable in cold water than in warm water. According to Henry's Law, this is primarily because the value of the Henry's Law constant (KH) for oxygen:
[4 Marks]
(A) Increases with an increase in temperature, decreasing solubility.
(B) Decreases with an increase in temperature, decreasing solubility.
(C) Increases with an increase in temperature, increasing solubility.
(D) Remains unaffected by temperature.
Correct Answer: (A) Increases with an increase in temperature, decreasing solubility.
Solution: According to Henry's Law, $p = K_H \cdot x$. For a constant pressure of oxygen ($p$), the solubility ($x$) is inversely proportional to $K_H$. As temperature increases, the value of $K_H$ increases, which implies that the dissolved mole fraction ($x$) decreases. Thus, cold water holds more dissolved oxygen.
5.
To prevent "The Bends" in scuba divers deep underwater, the air in scuba tanks is typically diluted with which of the following gases, owing to its exceptionally low solubility in blood?
[4 Marks]
(A) Argon
(B) Helium
(C) Nitrogen
(D) Xenon
Correct Answer: (B) Helium
Solution: Under high pressure, nitrogen dissolves heavily in blood. When a diver ascends quickly, nitrogen bubbles out, blocking capillaries (The Bends). Helium has an extremely high $K_H$ value, meaning its solubility is negligible even at high underwater pressures.
6.
A mixture of Chloroform (CHCl3) and Acetone (CH3COCH3) exhibits a specific deviation from Raoult's Law. This is characterized by:
[4 Marks]
(A) Positive deviation due to the breaking of hydrogen bonds.
(B) Negative deviation due to the formation of new hydrogen bonds.
(C) Ideal behavior as both are polar aprotic solvents.
(D) Positive deviation as they form a minimum boiling azeotrope.
Correct Answer: (B) Negative deviation due to the formation of new hydrogen bonds.
Solution: Pure chloroform and pure acetone lack intermolecular hydrogen bonding. When mixed, the hydrogen of chloroform forms a strong hydrogen bond with the oxygen of acetone. These strong new A-B interactions prevent molecules from escaping into the vapour phase easily, lowering vapour pressure (Negative deviation).
7.
A non-ideal solution that shows a large positive deviation from Raoult's law will form a:
[4 Marks]
(A) Maximum boiling azeotrope at a specific composition.
(B) Minimum boiling azeotrope at a specific composition.
(C) Mixture that can be perfectly separated by fractional distillation.
(D) Sublimating mixture.
Correct Answer: (B) Minimum boiling azeotrope at a specific composition.
Solution: Large positive deviations mean the solution has a higher vapour pressure than expected. Since vapour pressure and boiling point are inversely related, a higher vapour pressure causes the mixture to boil at a temperature lower than either of its pure components (e.g., Ethanol-Water mixture).
8.
According to Raoult's Law as a special case of Henry's Law, when the solute is highly volatile, the constant KH is mathematically replaced by:
[4 Marks]
(A) Total pressure (Ptotal)
(B) Mole fraction in vapour phase (yA)
(C) Vapour pressure of the pure component (p°)
(D) Universal Gas Constant (R)
Correct Answer: (C) Vapour pressure of the pure component (p°)
Solution: Henry's Law: $p_A = K_H \cdot x_A$. Raoult's Law: $p_A = p_A^\circ \cdot x_A$. For volatile liquids, Raoult's law becomes a special case of Henry's Law where the proportionality constant $K_H$ becomes strictly equal to the vapour pressure of the pure component ($p_A^\circ$).
9.
At a given temperature, Nitrogen gas has a higher KH value than Oxygen gas in water. Which of the following statements is correct regarding their solubilities at a constant pressure?
[4 Marks]
(A) Nitrogen is more soluble than Oxygen.
(B) Oxygen is more soluble than Nitrogen.
(C) Both have identical solubility.
(D) Solubilities depend only on the volume of the container.
Correct Answer: (B) Oxygen is more soluble than Nitrogen.
Solution: From Henry's Law, $x = p / K_H$. At constant pressure, the gas with the higher $K_H$ (Nitrogen) will have a lower mole fraction dissolved in the liquid. Therefore, Oxygen (with a lower $K_H$) is more soluble in water.
10.
When 50 mL of liquid A is mixed with 50 mL of liquid B, the total volume of the resulting solution is observed to be exactly 99 mL. What can be concluded about the A-B intermolecular forces?
[4 Marks]
(A) A-B interactions are weaker than A-A and B-B interactions.
(B) A-B interactions are equal to A-A and B-B interactions.
(C) A-B interactions are stronger than A-A and B-B interactions.
(D) Liquids A and B are completely immiscible.
Correct Answer: (C) A-B interactions are stronger than A-A and B-B interactions.
Solution: $\Delta V_{mix} = 99 - (50+50) = -1$ mL. Since $\Delta V_{mix} < 0$, a volume contraction has occurred. This implies the newly formed A-B interactions pull the molecules closer together tightly, hence they must be stronger than the pure interactions. This represents a negative deviation from Raoult's law.
SECTION B: Moderate Numericals & Application
11.
What is the mole fraction of the solute in a 1.00 m (molal) aqueous solution?
[4 Marks]
(A) 0.0177
(B) 0.0344
(C) 0.0555
(D) 0.100
Correct Answer: (A) 0.0177
Solution: 1.00 m (molal) means 1 mole of solute is dissolved in 1 kg (1000 g) of solvent (Water).
Moles of Solute ($n_2$) = 1 mol.
Moles of Water ($n_1$) = $1000 \text{ g} / 18 \text{ g/mol} = 55.55 \text{ mol}$.
Total Moles = $1 + 55.55 = 56.55 \text{ mol}$.
Mole fraction of solute ($x_2$) = $1 / 56.55 \approx 0.01768 \approx 0.0177$.
12.
The correct mathematical relationship interconverting Molarity (M) and molality (m) for a solution with density 'd' (in g/mL) and solute molar mass 'M2' (in g/mol) is:
[4 Marks]
(A) m = 1000×M / [ (1000×d) + (M×M2) ]
(B) m = 1000×d / [ (1000×M) - (M×M2) ]
(C) m = 1000×M / [ (1000×d) - (M×M2) ]
(D) M = 1000×m / [ (1000×d) - (m×M2) ]
Correct Answer: (C) m = 1000×M / [ (1000×d) - (M×M2) ]
Solution: Let's assume 1 L (1000 mL) of solution. It contains 'M' moles of solute.
Mass of 1L solution = $1000 \cdot d$ grams.
Mass of solute = $M \cdot M_2$ grams.
Mass of solvent ($W_1$) = Total Mass - Solute Mass = $(1000d - M \cdot M_2)$ grams.
Molality ($m$) = $\frac{\text{Moles of solute} \times 1000}{W_1(\text{in g})} = \frac{1000 \cdot M}{(1000d - M \cdot M_2)}$.
13.
A 3 M solution of NaCl in water has a density of 1.25 g mL-1. What is the molality of the solution? (Molar mass of NaCl = 58.5 g/mol)
[4 Marks]
(A) 2.79 m
(B) 3.15 m
(C) 2.40 m
(D) 3.00 m
Correct Answer: (A) 2.79 m
Solution: Let V = 1 Litre (1000 mL).
Moles of NaCl ($n$) = 3. Mass of NaCl = $3 \times 58.5 = 175.5 \text{ g}$.
Mass of solution = $1000 \text{ mL} \times 1.25 \text{ g/mL} = 1250 \text{ g}$.
Mass of solvent = $1250 - 175.5 = 1074.5 \text{ g} = 1.0745 \text{ kg}$.
Molality ($m$) = $\frac{3 \text{ moles}}{1.0745 \text{ kg}} \approx 2.79 \text{ m}$.
14.
Calculate the mole fraction of ethylene glycol (C2H6O2) in an aqueous solution containing 20% of C2H6O2 by mass. (Molar mass: C2H6O2 = 62 g/mol, H2O = 18 g/mol)
[4 Marks]
(A) 0.050
(B) 0.068
(C) 0.124
(D) 0.932
Correct Answer: (B) 0.068
Solution: Let Total Mass = 100 g.
Mass of Ethylene Glycol = 20 g $\implies$ Moles = $20 / 62 \approx 0.322 \text{ mol}$.
Mass of Water = $100 - 20 = 80 \text{ g} \implies$ Moles = $80 / 18 \approx 4.444 \text{ mol}$.
Total Moles = $0.322 + 4.444 = 4.766 \text{ mol}$.
Mole fraction ($x_2$) = $\frac{0.322}{4.766} \approx 0.0675 \approx 0.068$.
15.
The Henry's law constant (KH) for N2 gas at 293 K is 76.48 kbar. The mole fraction of N2 gas dissolved in water if it exerts a partial pressure of 0.987 bar is approximately:
[4 Marks]
(A) 1.29 × 10-5
(B) 1.29 × 10-3
(C) 2.58 × 10-5
(D) 7.54 × 104
Correct Answer: (A) 1.29 × 10-5
Solution: Ensure units match. $K_H = 76.48 \text{ kbar} = 76480 \text{ bar}$.
$p = K_H \cdot x \implies x = p / K_H$
$x = 0.987 \text{ bar} / 76480 \text{ bar}$
$x \approx 1.29 \times 10^{-5}$.
16.
For a binary ideal solution of two volatile liquids A and B, the vapour pressures of pure A and pure B are 100 mm Hg and 200 mm Hg respectively. The total vapour pressure of a solution containing 1 mole of A and 3 moles of B is:
[4 Marks]
(A) 125 mm Hg
(B) 150 mm Hg
(C) 175 mm Hg
(D) 200 mm Hg
Correct Answer: (C) 175 mm Hg
Solution: Total moles = $1 + 3 = 4$.
$x_A = 1/4 = 0.25$, $x_B = 3/4 = 0.75$.
$P_{total} = p_A^\circ x_A + p_B^\circ x_B$
$P_{total} = (100 \times 0.25) + (200 \times 0.75) = 25 + 150 = 175 \text{ mm Hg}$.
17.
Which of the following aqueous solutions will have the highest molarity if they all have exactly 10% (w/w) concentration? (Assume densities are roughly equal to 1 g/mL)
[4 Marks]
(A) NaOH (Molar mass = 40)
(B) Urea (Molar mass = 60)
(C) Glucose (Molar mass = 180)
(D) Sucrose (Molar mass = 342)
Correct Answer: (A) NaOH (Molar mass = 40)
Solution: $Molarity = \frac{Mass\% \times 10 \times density}{Molar Mass}$.
Since the mass %, constant 10, and density ($\approx 1$) are the same for all options, Molarity is inversely proportional to the Molar Mass ($M \propto \frac{1}{M_2}$). NaOH has the smallest molar mass (40), so it yields the most number of moles and thus the highest molarity.
18.
The vapour pressure of pure liquid A is 70 torr and pure liquid B is 50 torr. What will be the mole fraction of component A in the vapour phase (yA) for an ideal solution where the mole fraction of A in the liquid phase (xA) is 0.2?
[4 Marks]
(A) 0.200
(B) 0.259
(C) 0.583
(D) 0.740
Correct Answer: (B) 0.259
Solution: Liquid phase: $x_A = 0.2$, so $x_B = 0.8$.
Partial pressures: $p_A = 70 \times 0.2 = 14 \text{ torr}$. $p_B = 50 \times 0.8 = 40 \text{ torr}$.
Total Pressure: $P_T = 14 + 40 = 54 \text{ torr}$.
Vapour Phase fraction (Dalton's Law): $y_A = p_A / P_T = 14 / 54 \approx 0.259$.
19.
A gas has a Henry's Law constant KH = 1.0 × 105 atm in water. The mole fraction of this gas in the solution when it is subjected to a pressure of 10 atm over the liquid is:
[4 Marks]
(A) 10-3
(B) 10-4
(C) 10-5
(D) 10-6
Correct Answer: (B) 10-4
Solution: By Henry's Law: $p = K_H \cdot x$
$10 \text{ atm} = (1.0 \times 10^5 \text{ atm}) \cdot x$
$x = 10 / 10^5 = 1 / 10^4 = 10^{-4}$.
20.
An azeotropic mixture of two liquids behaves like a pure substance because its:
[4 Marks]
(A) Composition in the liquid phase and vapour phase are completely different.
(B) Boiling point changes continuously during fractional distillation.
(C) Vapour phase composition is identical to its liquid phase composition.
(D) Components follow Raoult's Law perfectly at all concentrations.
Correct Answer: (C) Vapour phase composition is identical to its liquid phase composition.
Solution: An azeotrope is a constant boiling mixture. This happens specifically when the mole fraction of each component in the liquid phase exactly equals its mole fraction in the vapour phase ($x_A = y_A$ and $x_B = y_B$). Because they boil off at the same ratio, they cannot be separated by fractional distillation.
SECTION C: Advanced Numericals / JEE Mains Level
21.
Two completely miscible liquids X and Y form an ideal solution. At 300K, the vapour pressure of a solution containing 1 mole of X and 3 moles of Y is 550 mm Hg. At the same temperature, if 1 additional mole of Y is added to this solution, the vapour pressure of the solution increases to 560 mm Hg. The vapour pressures of pure X and pure Y respectively are:
[4 Marks]
(A) 200 mm Hg, 400 mm Hg
(B) 300 mm Hg, 500 mm Hg
(C) 400 mm Hg, 600 mm Hg
(D) 600 mm Hg, 400 mm Hg
Correct Answer: (C) 400 mm Hg, 600 mm Hg
Solution:
Case 1: 1 mol X, 3 mol Y (Total 4 mol). $x_X = 1/4$, $x_Y = 3/4$.
$P_1 = p_X^\circ(1/4) + p_Y^\circ(3/4) = 550 \implies p_X^\circ + 3p_Y^\circ = 2200$ --- (Eq 1)

Case 2: Add 1 mol Y $\implies$ 1 mol X, 4 mol Y (Total 5 mol). $x_X = 1/5$, $x_Y = 4/5$.
$P_2 = p_X^\circ(1/5) + p_Y^\circ(4/5) = 560 \implies p_X^\circ + 4p_Y^\circ = 2800$ --- (Eq 2)

Subtracting Eq 1 from Eq 2:
$(p_X^\circ + 4p_Y^\circ) - (p_X^\circ + 3p_Y^\circ) = 2800 - 2200 \implies p_Y^\circ = 600 \text{ mm Hg}$.
Substitute $p_Y^\circ$ into Eq 1: $p_X^\circ + 3(600) = 2200 \implies p_X^\circ = 2200 - 1800 = 400 \text{ mm Hg}$.
22.
A mixture of two volatile liquid components A and B forms an ideal solution. The total vapour pressure (P) of the mixture (in torr) at a certain temperature follows the relation: P = 254 − 119 xA, where xA is the mole fraction of A in the liquid phase. The vapour pressures of pure A (P°A) and pure B (P°B) respectively are:
[4 Marks]
(A) 254 torr and 119 torr
(B) 119 torr and 254 torr
(C) 135 torr and 254 torr
(D) 254 torr and 135 torr
Correct Answer: (C) 135 torr and 254 torr
Solution: The standard formula is $P_{total} = p_B^\circ + (p_A^\circ - p_B^\circ)x_A$.
Given equation: $P = 254 - 119 x_A$.
By direct comparison:
Intercept ($x_A = 0$): $p_B^\circ = 254 \text{ torr}$.
Slope: $p_A^\circ - p_B^\circ = -119$
$p_A^\circ - 254 = -119 \implies p_A^\circ = 254 - 119 = 135 \text{ torr}$.
So, pure A = 135 torr, pure B = 254 torr.
23.
An aqueous solution of ethanol (C2H5OH) is 2.0 M. If the density of the solution is given as 1.025 g/mL, what is the exact molality of this solution? (Molar mass of ethanol = 46 g/mol)
[4 Marks]
(A) 1.95 m
(B) 2.14 m
(C) 1.79 m
(D) 2.50 m
Correct Answer: (B) 2.14 m
Solution: 2.0 M means 2.0 moles of ethanol in 1000 mL of solution.
Mass of 1000 mL solution = $1000 \text{ mL} \times 1.025 \text{ g/mL} = 1025 \text{ g}$.
Mass of ethanol solute = $2.0 \text{ mol} \times 46 \text{ g/mol} = 92 \text{ g}$.
Mass of water solvent = $1025 \text{ g} - 92 \text{ g} = 933 \text{ g} = 0.933 \text{ kg}$.
Molality ($m$) = Moles / mass of solvent (kg) = $2.0 / 0.933 \approx 2.143 \text{ m}$.
24.
The mole fraction of liquid A in an ideal binary mixture with liquid B is 0.4. The vapour pressures of pure A and B are 200 mm Hg and 400 mm Hg respectively. The solution is allowed to vaporize. What is the mole fraction of A in the vapour phase?
[4 Marks]
(A) 0.25
(B) 0.40
(C) 0.50
(D) 0.75
Correct Answer: (A) 0.25
Solution: Liquid phase: $x_A = 0.4$, $x_B = 1 - 0.4 = 0.6$.
$p_A = p_A^\circ \cdot x_A = 200 \times 0.4 = 80 \text{ mm Hg}$.
$p_B = p_B^\circ \cdot x_B = 400 \times 0.6 = 240 \text{ mm Hg}$.
$P_{total} = 80 + 240 = 320 \text{ mm Hg}$.
Vapour phase fraction $y_A = p_A / P_{total} = 80 / 320 = 1/4 = 0.25$.
25.
A solution of nitric acid and water forms a maximum boiling azeotrope. Which of the following thermodynamic statements correctly describes this mixture?
[4 Marks]
(A) ΔHmix > 0, representing a positive deviation from Raoult's law.
(B) ΔHmix < 0, representing a negative deviation from Raoult's law.
(C) ΔHmix = 0, representing an ideal solution.
(D) The intermolecular forces in the mixture are weaker than in the pure liquids.
Correct Answer: (B) ΔHmix < 0, representing a negative deviation from Raoult's law.
Solution: A maximum boiling azeotrope means the mixture boils at a temperature higher than both pure components. This requires a lower-than-expected vapour pressure, meaning the solution shows a severe negative deviation from Raoult's law. In negative deviations, new bonds formed are stronger, making the mixing process exothermic ($\Delta H_{mix} < 0$).
