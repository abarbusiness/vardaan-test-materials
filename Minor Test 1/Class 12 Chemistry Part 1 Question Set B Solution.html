SECTION A: Direct Recall & Definitions
1.
An amalgam of mercury with sodium is an example of which type of solution?
[4 Marks]
(A) Solid in liquid
(B) Liquid in solid
(C) Solid in solid
(D) Gas in solid
Correct Answer: (B) Liquid in solid
Solution: In an amalgam, mercury is the solute (which is a liquid at room temperature) and sodium is the solvent (which is a solid). Since the solvent determines the final state, it is a liquid dissolved in a solid.
2.
Which of the following concentration terms is independent of temperature?
[4 Marks]
(A) Molarity
(B) Molality
(C) Volume percentage (v/v)
(D) Mass by volume percentage (w/v)
Correct Answer: (B) Molality
Solution: Any concentration term that involves volume (like Molarity, v/v%, and w/v%) will change with temperature due to the thermal expansion/contraction of liquids. Molality ($m$) involves only mass (moles of solute per kg of solvent), making it perfectly independent of temperature.
3.
The sum of the mole fractions of all the components in a solution is always equal to:
[4 Marks]
(A) $0$
(B) $1$
(C) $100$
(D) Depends on the temperature
Correct Answer: (B) $1$
Solution: Mole fraction is a ratio of parts to the whole. For a binary solution with components A and B, $x_A + x_B = \frac{n_A}{n_A + n_B} + \frac{n_B}{n_A + n_B} = \frac{n_A + n_B}{n_A + n_B} = 1$.
4.
A $10\%$ (w/v) solution of $NaCl$ indicates that:
[4 Marks]
(A) $10\text{ g}$ of $NaCl$ is present in $100\text{ g}$ of solution
(B) $10\text{ g}$ of $NaCl$ is present in $100\text{ mL}$ of solvent
(C) $10\text{ g}$ of $NaCl$ is present in $100\text{ mL}$ of solution
(D) $10\text{ mL}$ of $NaCl$ is present in $100\text{ mL}$ of solution
Correct Answer: (C) $10\text{ g}$ of $NaCl$ is present in $100\text{ mL}$ of solution
Solution: The term w/v means "weight per volume". It specifically defines the mass of the solute in grams present in exactly $100\text{ mL}$ of the total solution. This term is heavily used in the pharmaceutical industry.
5.
To appropriately express the concentration of a pollutant in water that is present in trace (very minute) quantities, the preferred unit is:
[4 Marks]
(A) Molarity
(B) Parts per million (ppm)
(C) Mass percentage
(D) Mole fraction
Correct Answer: (B) Parts per million (ppm)
Solution: When solutes are present in extremely small amounts (trace quantities), expressing them in percentage or molarity yields very inconvenient decimal numbers. PPM (parts per million) is the standard unit for such solutions (e.g., measuring hardness or pollutants in water).
6.
Which law states that "at a constant temperature, the partial pressure of a gas in the vapour phase is directly proportional to its mole fraction in the solution"?
[4 Marks]
(A) Raoult's Law
(B) Dalton's Law
(C) Henry's Law
(D) Le Chatelier's Principle
Correct Answer: (C) Henry's Law
Solution: This is the exact definition of Henry's Law concerning the solubility of a gas in a liquid. Mathematically, it is expressed as $p = K_H \cdot x$.
7.
Aquatic animals are more comfortable in cold water rather than warm water because:
[4 Marks]
(A) $K_H$ decreases with a decrease in temperature, decreasing solubility.
(B) $K_H$ decreases with a decrease in temperature, increasing solubility.
(C) $K_H$ increases with a decrease in temperature, increasing solubility.
(D) Temperature has absolutely no effect on oxygen solubility.
Correct Answer: (B) $K_H$ decreases with a decrease in temperature, increasing solubility.
Solution: The value of Henry's Law constant ($K_H$) is a function of the nature of the gas and temperature. $K_H$ generally increases with temperature. Thus, as water gets colder, $K_H$ decreases. Since solubility $x = p / K_H$, a lower $K_H$ means higher solubility of oxygen, making cold water more oxygen-rich.
8.
For a liquid-liquid solution to be considered perfectly ideal, the correct thermodynamic conditions are:
[4 Marks]
(A) $\Delta H_{mix} > 0, \Delta V_{mix} = 0$
(B) $\Delta H_{mix} < 0, \Delta V_{mix} < 0$
(C) $\Delta H_{mix} = 0, \Delta V_{mix} > 0$
(D) $\Delta H_{mix} = 0, \Delta V_{mix} = 0$
Correct Answer: (D) $\Delta H_{mix} = 0, \Delta V_{mix} = 0$
Solution: Ideal solutions obey Raoult's law perfectly because the new A-B interactions are exactly identical to the pure A-A and B-B interactions. Because no bonds are effectively strengthened or weakened, no heat is evolved or absorbed ($\Delta H = 0$) and the volume simply adds up without contracting or expanding ($\Delta V = 0$).
9.
What is the effect of increased external pressure on the solubility of a solid in a liquid?
[4 Marks]
(A) Solubility increases significantly
(B) Solubility decreases significantly
(C) It has practically no effect
(D) It first increases then decreases
Correct Answer: (C) It has practically no effect
Solution: Solids and liquids are virtually incompressible. Because their volume doesn't change significantly with pressure, varying the pressure has practically no effect on the solubility of a solid dissolved in a liquid.
10.
A non-ideal solution exhibiting a large positive deviation from Raoult's law forms a:
[4 Marks]
(A) Maximum boiling azeotrope
(B) Minimum boiling azeotrope
(C) Ideal mixture
(D) Saturated solution
Correct Answer: (B) Minimum boiling azeotrope
Solution: A positive deviation means the vapour pressure is higher than expected. Because the liquid forms vapours easily, less heat is required to make its vapour pressure equal to atmospheric pressure. Therefore, it boils at a lower temperature, forming a minimum boiling azeotrope.
SECTION B: Reasoning & Standard Applications
11.
Scuba divers use tanks diluted with Helium to avoid the medical condition called "the bends". Helium is chosen primarily because of its:
[4 Marks]
(A) High reactivity with blood
(B) High solubility in blood at high pressure
(C) Low solubility in blood at high pressure
(D) Extremely low density compared to oxygen
Correct Answer: (C) Low solubility in blood at high pressure
Solution: The bends are caused by nitrogen dissolving in the blood under high underwater pressure and bubbling out upon rapid ascent. Helium is relatively insoluble in blood even under high pressure (it has a very high $K_H$ value), preventing these dangerous bubbles from forming.
12.
The medical condition known as Anoxia (weakness and inability to think clearly), experienced by climbers at high altitudes, is primarily due to:
[4 Marks]
(A) High partial pressure of oxygen in the air
(B) Low partial pressure of oxygen in the air
(C) High temperature at high altitudes
(D) Excessive carbon dioxide in the air
Correct Answer: (B) Low partial pressure of oxygen in the air
Solution: At high altitudes, the atmospheric pressure is lower than at ground level. According to Henry's law, a lower partial pressure of oxygen means less oxygen dissolves in the blood and tissues, leading to hypoxia/anoxia.
13.
A mixture of chloroform ($CHCl_3$) and acetone ($CH_3COCH_3$) shows a negative deviation from Raoult's law. What is the molecular reason behind this behavior?
[4 Marks]
(A) Formation of new hydrogen bonds between them
(B) Breaking of existing hydrogen bonds
(C) Both liquids are non-polar in nature
(D) Due to volume expansion upon mixing
Correct Answer: (A) Formation of new hydrogen bonds between them
Solution: Chloroform and acetone on their own do not have hydrogen bonding. When mixed, the hydrogen atom of chloroform forms a strong hydrogen bond with the carbonyl oxygen of acetone. This strong attraction reduces the escaping tendency of the molecules, lowering the vapour pressure.
14.
An azeotropic mixture of ethanol and water cannot be completely separated into its pure components by fractional distillation because:
[4 Marks]
(A) They have very different boiling points
(B) The liquid and vapour phases have the exact same composition
(C) They are completely immiscible liquids
(D) Ethanol is highly volatile
Correct Answer: (B) The liquid and vapour phases have the exact same composition
Solution: Azeotropes are constant boiling mixtures. At the azeotropic composition, the ratio of components boiling off into the vapour phase is perfectly identical to their ratio in the liquid phase. Since there is no enrichment of one component over the other in the vapour, fractional distillation fails.
15.
Which of the following binary liquid pairs is known to form a maximum boiling azeotrope?
[4 Marks]
(A) Ethanol + Water
(B) Nitric Acid + Water
(C) Benzene + Toluene
(D) Hexane + Heptane
Correct Answer: (B) Nitric Acid + Water
Solution: Nitric acid and water show a strong negative deviation from Raoult's law (strong acid-water interactions). Negative deviations lead to a lower vapour pressure and, consequently, a higher boiling point, resulting in a maximum boiling azeotrope.
16.
According to Raoult's law, the partial vapour pressure of a volatile component in a solution is directly proportional to its:
[4 Marks]
(A) Molarity in the solution
(B) Molality in the solution
(C) Mole fraction in the solution
(D) Mass percentage in the solution
Correct Answer: (C) Mole fraction in the solution
Solution: Raoult's law states that $p_1 = p_1^\circ \cdot x_1$. Therefore, the partial vapour pressure is directly proportional to the mole fraction ($x$) of the component in the solution.
17.
If a solution formed by mixing two liquids shows a positive deviation from Raoult's law, the sign of $\Delta V_{mix}$ will be:
[4 Marks]
(A) Positive (Volume expansion)
(B) Negative (Volume contraction)
(C) Zero (No volume change)
(D) Variable depending on the atmospheric pressure
Correct Answer: (A) Positive (Volume expansion)
Solution: In a positive deviation, the new A-B interactions are weaker than pure A-A and B-B forces. Because the molecules attract each other less tightly, they take up more space. This results in an overall expansion in volume, making $\Delta V_{mix} > 0$.
18.
If the dissolution of a solid in a liquid is an endothermic process ($\Delta H_{sol} > 0$), what will happen to its solubility if the temperature is increased?
[4 Marks]
(A) Solubility will decrease
(B) Solubility will increase
(C) Solubility remains constant
(D) The solid will immediately precipitate out
Correct Answer: (B) Solubility will increase
Solution: According to Le Chatelier's Principle, increasing the temperature of a system at equilibrium favors the endothermic reaction (which consumes the added heat). Therefore, the forward dissolution process is favored, and solubility increases.
19.
For a binary solution of volatile liquids A and B, the mole fraction of component A specifically in the vapour phase ($y_A$) is correctly calculated using the formula:
[4 Marks]
(A) $y_A = p_A^\circ / P_{total}$
(B) $y_A = p_A / P_{total}$
(C) $y_A = x_A / P_{total}$
(D) $y_A = p_A \cdot P_{total}$
Correct Answer: (B) $y_A = p_A / P_{total}$
Solution: This is derived from Dalton's law of partial pressures. The partial pressure of a gas in a mixture ($p_A$) is equal to its mole fraction in that gas phase ($y_A$) multiplied by the total pressure ($P_{total}$). Rearranging gives $y_A = p_A / P_{total}$.
20.
According to Henry's Law, at a given constant pressure, a higher value of the constant $K_H$ for a gas implies:
[4 Marks]
(A) Higher solubility of the gas in the liquid
(B) Lower solubility of the gas in the liquid
(C) No change in the solubility of the gas
(D) The gas behaves ideally
Correct Answer: (B) Lower solubility of the gas in the liquid
Solution: From the equation $p = K_H \cdot x$, we can rearrange it to find solubility: $x = p / K_H$. At a constant pressure ($p$), the solubility ($x$) is inversely proportional to $K_H$. A higher $K_H$ means less gas dissolves.
SECTION C: Calculations & Numericals
21.
$4.0\text{ g}$ of $NaOH$ (Molar mass = $40\text{ g/mol}$) is dissolved in enough water to make exactly $250\text{ mL}$ of solution. The molarity of this solution is:
[4 Marks]
(A) $0.1\text{ M}$
(B) $0.2\text{ M}$
(C) $0.4\text{ M}$
(D) $1.0\text{ M}$
Correct Answer: (C) $0.4\text{ M}$
Solution: Moles of NaOH ($n$) = $\frac{\text{Given Mass}}{\text{Molar Mass}} = \frac{4.0\text{ g}}{40\text{ g/mol}} = 0.1\text{ mol}$.
Volume of solution ($V$) = $250\text{ mL} = 0.250\text{ L}$.
Molarity ($M$) = $\frac{\text{Moles}}{\text{Volume in L}} = \frac{0.1}{0.250} = 0.4\text{ M}$.
22.
What is the mass percentage of benzene ($C_6H_6$) if $22\text{ g}$ of benzene is dissolved in $122\text{ g}$ of carbon tetrachloride ($CCl_4$)?
[4 Marks]
(A) $15.28\%$
(B) $22.0\%$
(C) $18.03\%$
(D) $84.72\%$
Correct Answer: (A) $15.28\%$
Solution: Mass of Solute (Benzene) = $22\text{ g}$.
Mass of Solvent ($CCl_4$) = $122\text{ g}$.
Total Mass of Solution = $22 + 122 = 144\text{ g}$.
Mass % of Benzene = $\frac{\text{Mass of Benzene}}{\text{Total Mass}} \times 100 = \frac{22}{144} \times 100 = 15.277...\%\ \approx\ 15.28\%$.
23.
The vapour pressures of pure liquids A and B are $100\text{ mm Hg}$ and $200\text{ mm Hg}$ respectively. In an ideal solution containing equal moles of A and B, what is the total vapour pressure?
[4 Marks]
(A) $100\text{ mm Hg}$
(B) $150\text{ mm Hg}$
(C) $200\text{ mm Hg}$
(D) $300\text{ mm Hg}$
Correct Answer: (B) $150\text{ mm Hg}$
Solution: Since the solution contains equal moles of A and B, their mole fractions are equal: $x_A = 0.5$ and $x_B = 0.5$.
According to Raoult's Law:
$P_{total} = p_A^\circ x_A + p_B^\circ x_B$
$P_{total} = (100 \times 0.5) + (200 \times 0.5) = 50 + 100 = 150\text{ mm Hg}$.
24.
$15\text{ g}$ of $NaCl$ is present in $85\text{ g}$ of water. What is the mass percentage of the solute ($NaCl$)?
[4 Marks]
(A) $15\%$
(B) $85\%$
(C) $17.6\%$
(D) $10\%$
Correct Answer: (A) $15\%$
Solution: Mass of Solute ($NaCl$) = $15\text{ g}$.
Mass of Solvent (Water) = $85\text{ g}$.
Total Mass of Solution = $15\text{ g} + 85\text{ g} = 100\text{ g}$.
Mass % of Solute = $\frac{15}{100} \times 100 = 15\%$.
25.
Which of the following pairs of liquids is most likely to form a nearly ideal solution?
[4 Marks]
(A) Chloroform + Acetone
(B) Ethanol + Water
(C) Benzene + Toluene
(D) Nitric acid + Water
Correct Answer: (C) Benzene + Toluene
Solution: Ideal solutions are generally formed by liquids that have very similar chemical structures and polarities (so that A-B interactions $\approx$ A-A interactions). Benzene and Toluene are both non-polar aromatic hydrocarbons of similar size, making them practically ideal. The other options form strong non-ideal solutions with deviations.
