MINOR TEST 01
Class: 7 ICSE Subject: Mathematics & Science Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 40
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Show all working and steps clearly for numericals.
  • Write proper S.I. units for Science numericals.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
Q1.
Fill in the blanks with the correct mathematical values:
a) The additive inverse of -(-25) is _____________. [1]
b) Evaluate: (-18) ÷ [(-3) + 2] = _____________. [1]
Q2.
Evaluate the following decimal expressions:
a) 0.07 × 0.009 = _____________ [1]
b) 3.5 ÷ 1000 = _____________ [1]
Q3.
Convert the following physical quantities:
a) 1 g/cm3 = _____________ kg/m3. [1]
b) 1 hectare = _____________ m2. [1]
Q4.
Answer the following in one word or number:
a) At what temperature (in °C) is the density of water maximum? [1]
b) Which device in a vehicle shows its speed at a particular instant? [1]
Q5.
Define the term 'density' of a substance. State its standard S.I. unit. [2]
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
Q6.
Using the rules of BODMAS, simplify the following integer expression:
15 - [ (-5) × { 2 - (-4) × 3 } ] ÷ (-5) [3]
Q7.
Simplify the following decimal expression strictly showing all intermediate steps:
3.75 × 2.4 - 1.5 ÷ 0.5 + 4.2 [3]
Q8.
A submarine is situated at exactly 250 m below sea level (-250 m). It first descends 120 m, then ascends 85 m, and finally descends another 45 m. Determine its final position relative to the sea level using integer addition. [3]
Q9.
Aditi measures the mass of an iron cube as 78 g and an identical aluminium cube as 27 g. Which physical property explains this difference? State the general relationship between equal volumes of different substances and their masses. [3]
Q10.
A train travels from Station A to Station B at a constant speed of 60 km/h and takes 3 hours to complete the journey. It returns from Station B to Station A at a constant speed of 40 km/h. Calculate the average speed of the train for the entire two-way journey. [3]
SECTION C (15 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
Q11.
Advik went to the supermarket and bought 4.5 kg of apples at ₹ 85.50 per kg, and 2.25 kg of grapes at ₹ 120.40 per kg. He handed a ₹ 1000 note to the shopkeeper.
a) Calculate the total amount spent on the fruits. [3]
b) How much money did he receive back from the shopkeeper? [2]
Q12.
A measuring cylinder contains water up to the 25 mL mark. When an irregular piece of stone is immersed completely into it, the water level rises to the 45 mL mark. If the mass of the stone is 50 g:
a) Explain the principle/method used to find the volume of this irregular stone and state its exact volume. [2]
b) Calculate the density of the stone in g/cm3. [1.5]
c) Convert the calculated density from part (b) into kg/m3. [1.5]
Q13.
Solve the following application-based questions:
a) A car covers 43.2 km using 2.4 litres of petrol. Calculate the distance it will cover in 1 litre of petrol, and determine the total distance it can cover using 15.5 litres of petrol. [2.5]
b) The length, breadth, and height of a rectangular room are 6 m, 4 m, and 3 m respectively. Calculate the volume of the room. If the density of air is 1.3 kg/m3, find the total mass of the air present inside the room. [2.5]
