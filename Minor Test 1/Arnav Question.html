MINOR TEST 01
Class: 8 CBSE Subjects: Mathematics & Social Science Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 40
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • The question paper consists of equal marks distribution for Mathematics (20 Marks) and Social Science (20 Marks).
  • Show proper steps and workings for all Mathematics questions.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly.
SECTION A (8 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
[Mathematics] Find the smallest number by which 2592 must be multiplied so that the product becomes a perfect cube.
2.
[Mathematics] Find the square root of 17.64 by using the long division method.
3.
[Social Science] Utility and value of a resource vary from time to time and place to place. Give a suitable example contrasting early human civilisations and modern human beings to support this statement.
4.
[Social Science] Differentiate between Actual resources and Potential resources based on their development. Provide one example of each.
SECTION B (12 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
5.
[Mathematics] The area of a square playground is 213.16 m2. Find the length of its side. If an athlete runs around the boundary of this playground 3 times, calculate the total distance covered by him.
6.
[Mathematics] Evaluate the following expression, showing proper prime factorization/division for each root:
√4356 + ∛3375 − √729
7.
[Social Science] "Though resources are essential for development, their mere presence does not guarantee development."
Justify this statement by explaining the role of other crucial factors required for the utilisation of resources. Cite the contrasting examples of the USA and African nations given in your text.
8.
[Social Science] Over-use of valuable resources has led to their degradation. Mention three essential steps humans must take to ensure sustainable development and conservation of natural resources for future generations.
SECTION C (20 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
9.
[Mathematics] Find the greatest 6-digit number which is a perfect square. Also, find the square root of this number. (Show detailed long division steps).
10.
[Mathematics] Three numbers are in the ratio of 2 : 3 : 4. The sum of their cubes is 33957. Find all the three numbers.
11.
[Social Science] Classify resources comprehensively on the basis of their renewability. Explain each category with suitable examples. Why is there a growing concern about the over-exploitation of one of these categories, and what could be the potential consequences?
12.
[Social Science] Mahatma Gandhi correctly stated, "There is enough for everybody's need and not for anybody's greed."
Analyze this statement in the context of the present scenario of resource depletion caused by technological advancement and human lifestyle changes. As a young student, what sustainable consumption patterns (5 R's) and lifestyle changes can you adopt to reverse this adverse impact on our habitable planet?
