SURPRISE TEST - Minor Test 01
Class: 6 Subject: Basic Mathematics Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 40
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly. Show all working steps clearly.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Find the sum of the fractions: 2⁄5 and 1⁄4.
2.
Find the Highest Common Factor (HCF) of 24 and 36 using prime factorization.
3.
Find the Lowest Common Multiple (LCM) of 12 and 15.
4.
Write the number 4,50,672 in words (Indian Number System) and give its expanded form.
5.
Subtract 2,876 from 5,004.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
Rahul ate 1⁄3 of a pizza and his sister Riya ate 1⁄4 of the same pizza.
(i) What total fraction of the pizza did they eat together?
(ii) What fraction of the pizza is left over?
7.
Three bells toll at intervals of 10, 15, and 20 minutes respectively. If they ring together at 8:00 AM, at what time will they ring together again?
8.
Solve the following mixed fraction: 5 1⁄2 − 2 1⁄4. Convert your final answer to a mixed fraction.
9.
Write down:
(i) All the factors of 48.
(ii) The first five multiples of 12.
10.
Find the perimeter of a rectangular park whose length is 45 meters and breadth is 30 meters.
SECTION C (15 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
A school library has 3,450 books in total. If 125 books are distributed to each of the 12 classes in the school:
(a) How many books were distributed in total? (2 Marks)
(b) How many books are left in the library? (3 Marks)
12.
Anil received Rs. 500 as pocket money for the month. He spent 2⁄5 of his pocket money on buying books and 1⁄4 on snacks.
(a) What total fraction of his pocket money did he spend? (2 Marks)
(b) Find the exact amount of money he spent on books and snacks combined. (2 Marks)
(c) How much money is left with Anil now? (1 Mark)
13.
Answer the following:
(a) Two ropes of lengths 16 m and 24 m are to be cut into small pieces of equal lengths. What will be the maximum possible length of each piece without wasting any rope? (3 Marks)
(b) Test the divisibility of the number 56,430 by 3 and 5. State the rules for both. (2 Marks)
