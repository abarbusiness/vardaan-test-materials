MINOR TEST 01
Class: 8 ICSE Subject: Maths & Geography Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 40
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • The question paper contains 20 marks for Mathematics and 20 marks for Geography.
  • Show necessary working/steps clearly for Mathematics questions.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
[Maths] Evaluate: $\left( \frac{-3}{4} \right)^{-2} \times \left( \frac{5}{6} \right)^{0} \times \left( \frac{-2}{3} \right)^{2}$
2.
[Maths] Find any two rational numbers lying exactly between $\frac{-1}{3}$ and $\frac{1}{2}$.
3.
[Maths] Simplify and express the result as a rational number: $\left( \frac{2}{3} \right)^3 \div \left( \frac{2}{3} \right)^5 \times \left( \frac{2}{3} \right)^4$.
4.
[Geography] Define the term 'Natural Growth Rate'. How is it expressed differently from the annual growth rate?
5.
[Geography] Mention any two physical reasons why the Equatorial regions such as the Amazon and Zaire basins are sparsely populated.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
[Maths] Simplify the following expression using the laws of exponents (assume $t \neq 0$): $$ \frac{25 \times t^{-4}}{5^{-3} \times 10 \times t^{-8}} $$
7.
[Maths] The product of two rational numbers is $\frac{-16}{9}$. If one of the numbers is $\frac{-4}{3}$, find the other number. Show your steps clearly.
8.
[Maths] Evaluate the following expression: $$ \left( 3^{-1} + 4^{-1} + 5^{-1} \right)^0 + \left( \frac{1}{2} \right)^{-2} + \left( \frac{1}{3} \right)^{-2} + \left( \frac{1}{4} \right)^{-2} $$
9.
[Geography] Overpopulation is a condition where the human population exceeds the carrying capacity of a region. State any three major disadvantages or socio-economic impacts of overpopulation.
10.
[Geography] "A population pyramid tells us about the condition of the population of a country." Briefly explain what a broad base and a narrow, tapering top of a population pyramid indicate. Give one example of a country for each characteristic based on the syllabus.
SECTION C (15 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
[Maths] Solve the following:
(a) By what rational number should $\left( \frac{-2}{3} \right)^{-3}$ be divided so that the quotient becomes $\left( \frac{4}{9} \right)^{-2}$? (3 Marks)
(b) Find the value of $x$ in the given exponential equation: $$ \left( \frac{5}{3} \right)^{-5} \times \left( \frac{5}{3} \right)^{11} = \left( \frac{5}{3} \right)^{3x} $$ (2 Marks)
12.
[Geography] Answer the following questions based on population dynamics:
(a) Clearly differentiate between Immigration and Emigration. (2 Marks)
(b) "The population of a region is heavily influenced by physical factors." Explain the specific roles of Topography, Climatic Conditions, and Availability of Water in determining where people settle. (3 Marks)
13.
[Geography] Answer the following questions concerning population composition and distribution:
(a) What is meant by the 'Dependent Population'? Mention the specific age groups that constitute this demographic. (2 Marks)
(b) According to the density of population, the world can be broadly divided into three main regions. Describe these three regions and provide at least one geographical example for each. (3 Marks)
