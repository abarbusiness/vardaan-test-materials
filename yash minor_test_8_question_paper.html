SECTION A (15 Marks) (Objective / Very Short Answer Type - Attempt All Questions)
1.
Classify the following changes as either a Physical Change or a Chemical Change, and provide a single-sentence scientific justification for each: [5]
(a) Melting of solid candle wax when heated.
(b) Explosion of fireworks during a festival.
(c) Dissolution of sugar in water.
(d) Photosynthesis in green plants.
(e) Growth of a seedling into a tree.
2.
Provide scientific representations for the following:
(a) Write the chemical formulas of: (i) Calcium Carbonate, (ii) Zinc Chloride. [2]
(b) Identify the constituent elements and calculate the total molecular atomicity for:
(i) Sulphuric Acid ($H_2SO_4$)
(ii) Ammonium Chloride ($NH_4Cl$). [3]
3.
Answer the following questions regarding physical measurements and conversions:
(a) Define the term 'Least Count' of a measuring vessel. A measuring cylinder has $10$ equal graduation marks between the $40\text{ mL}$ and $50\text{ mL}$ markings. Calculate its least count. [2]
(b) Perform the following unit conversions, displaying the steps clearly:
(i) Convert a density of $1.25\text{ g/cm}^3$ into the SI unit of density ($\text{kg/m}^3$).
(ii) Convert a speed of $90\text{ km/h}$ into $\text{m/s}$. [3]
SECTION B (15 Marks) (Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
4.
Differentiate between the following terms with clear, scientific comparison points:
(a) A homogeneous mixture and a heterogeneous mixture (give one example of each). [1.5]
(b) An element and a compound based on their microscopic composition. [1.5]
5.
A piece of copper of mass $267\text{ g}$ is fully immersed into a graduated measuring cylinder filled with water. The initial water level reads $45\text{ mL}$ and rises to $75\text{ mL}$ upon complete immersion.
(a) Calculate the volume of the copper piece in $\text{cm}^3$. [1]
(b) Determine the density of copper in standard SI units ($\text{kg/m}^3$). [2]
6.
Explain the fundamental physics or chemistry principle behind each of the following separation processes: [3]
(a) Separation of kerosene oil and water using a separating funnel.
(b) Separation of nitrogen and oxygen from liquid air using fractional distillation.
(c) Separation of iron filings from sulfur powder.
7.
Identify if the following processes are Exothermic or Endothermic changes. Justify your selection: [3]
(a) Dissolving Ammonium Chloride ($NH_4Cl$) crystals in water, which results in the test tube turning very cold.
(b) Burning of a magnesium ribbon in oxygen.
(c) Water boiling and turning into steam.
8.
Define standard density and relative density:
(a) Write down the mathematical relationship between Density, Mass, and Volume. State its SI unit. [1.5]
(b) What is relative density? Why does relative density not possess any physical units? Explain. [1.5]
SECTION C (20 Marks) (Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
9.
(Physical vs. Chemical Changes)
(a) The burning of a wax candle is considered a unique process where both a physical change and a chemical change occur simultaneously. Describe this dual process clearly. [3]
(b) State the two main chemical compounds formed during the combustion of wax. Write down a simple word equation for this combustion process. [2]
10.
(Separation Methodologies)
You are provided with a solid mixture containing: Sand, Common Salt (Sodium Chloride), and Ammonium Chloride.
Design a step-by-step experimental plan to separate all three components in their pure, dry, and solid states. Mention the specific apparatus and purification techniques utilized in each phase. [5]
11.
(Measurement & Density Bottle)
An empty relative density bottle has a mass of $25\text{ g}$. When completely filled with pure water, its total mass is $75\text{ g}$. When emptied, dried, and completely refilled with an unknown organic liquid 'Y', the total recorded mass is $65\text{ g}$. (Assume density of water = $1\text{ g/cm}^3$).
Calculate:
(a) The volume capacity of the density bottle. [1.5]
(b) The absolute density of organic liquid 'Y' in $\text{g/cm}^3$ and in standard SI units ($\text{kg/m}^3$). [2]
(c) The relative density of liquid 'Y'. [0.5]
(d) The mass of liquid 'Y' that would completely occupy a volume of $250\text{ cm}^3$. [1]
12.
(Factors Affecting Changes & Rusting)
(a) State three conditions other than heating that are necessary for a chemical change to occur. Provide one relevant chemical example or reaction for each. [3]
(b) Rusting of iron and burning of iron are both oxidation processes. State two chemical differences between them. [2]
