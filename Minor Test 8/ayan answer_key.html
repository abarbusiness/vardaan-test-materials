8.
State any three fundamental differences between a compound and a mixture.
Ans: [1 Mark for each valid difference = 3 Marks]
Compound Mixture
Constituents are combined chemically. Constituents are mixed physically.
Constituents are always present in a fixed ratio by mass. Constituents can be present in any ratio.
Properties are entirely different from its constituents. Retains the properties of its individual constituents.
Components cannot be separated by simple physical methods. Components can easily be separated by physical methods.

11.
(a) Separation techniques. (b) Why water is a compound.
Ans (a):
(i) Sand and water: Filtration / Decantation [1 Mark]
(ii) Iron filings and sulphur powder: Magnetic Separation [1 Mark]
(iii) Salt from seawater: Evaporation [1 Mark]

Ans (b): Water is a compound because: [1 Mark each = 2 Marks]
1. Its constituents (Hydrogen and Oxygen) are chemically combined in a fixed ratio by mass (1:8).
2. It possesses totally different properties from its constituents (Hydrogen is combustible, Oxygen supports combustion, but water extinguishes fire).

13.
(a) What is a chemical formula? (b) Constituent elements and atomicity.
Ans (a): A chemical formula is the symbolic representation of the composition of a molecule of a substance. [1 Mark]
It signifies: (1) The types of elements present. (2) The exact number of atoms of each element in one molecule. [1 Mark]

Ans (b):
(i) Sulphuric acid (H2SO4): Hydrogen = 2 atoms, Sulphur = 1 atom, Oxygen = 4 atoms. [1.5 Marks]
(ii) Table Sugar (C12H22O11): Carbon = 12 atoms, Hydrogen = 22 atoms, Oxygen = 11 atoms. [1.5 Marks]

15.
(a) Homogeneous vs Heterogeneous. (b) Total pages and ink mass in kg.
Ans (a):
Homogeneous Mixture: A mixture in which the components are uniformly distributed throughout and there are no visible boundaries of separation. [1 Mark] Example: Salt in water / Air. [0.5 Marks]
Heterogeneous Mixture: A mixture in which the composition is not uniform and visible boundaries of separation exist between its components. [0.5 Marks] Example: Sand and iron filings / Oil and water. [0.5 Marks]

Ans (b):
Pages printed per day = 11,980 copies × 12 pages/copy = 1,43,760 pages. [1 Mark]
Mass of ink in grams = 1,43,760 × 2 g = 2,87,520 grams. [0.5 Marks]
Convert to kilograms = 2,87,520 ÷ 1000 = 287.52 kg. [1 Mark]
