Minor Test 8
Class: 6 ICSE Subject: Science (Physics & Chemistry) Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly. Use scientific notations where required.
  • Answers should be concise and strictly to the point.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Define 'Matter'. State its two fundamental characteristics.
2.
What is meant by the 'atomicity' of an element? Give one example of a diatomic element.
3.
Differentiate between evaporation and boiling (give any two distinct points of difference).
4.
Write the correct chemical symbols for the following elements:
(a) Sodium     (b) Iron     (c) Potassium     (d) Gold
5.
Why are gases highly compressible while solids are almost incompressible? Explain briefly on the basis of intermolecular space.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
Explain the process of 'Sublimation'. Name any two substances that undergo sublimation.
7.
Classify the following substances into elements and compounds:
Water, Oxygen, Carbon dioxide, Iron, Calcium carbonate, Nitrogen.
8.
Define 'Intermolecular force of attraction'. How does the magnitude of this force vary across solid, liquid, and gaseous states?
9.
Write the chemical formulae for the following compounds:
(a) Water
(b) Carbon dioxide
(c) Sodium chloride
10.
State any three key differences between an element and a compound. Construct a clear table for comparison.
SECTION C (25 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
(a) Describe the arrangement of molecules in a solid, a liquid, and a gas with the help of a theoretical explanation. (3 Marks)
(b) Based on molecular arrangement, give a scientific reason why solids have a definite shape but liquids take the shape of the container they are poured into. (2 Marks)
12.
What information does a chemical formula convey about a substance? Identify the constituent elements and state the total number of atoms of each element present in one molecule of the following:
(a) Sulphuric acid (H2SO4) (2.5 Marks)
(b) Glucose (C6H12O6) (2.5 Marks)
13.
Explain the inter-conversion of the three states of matter. Define the terms: Melting, Freezing, Vaporization, and Condensation.
14.
(a) What is meant by a 'pure substance' in Chemistry? (1 Mark)
(b) A student argues that "Air is a compound because it contains gases like oxygen and nitrogen." Refute this statement by providing four valid reasons to prove that air is a mixture and not a compound. (4 Marks)
15.
Analyze the given scenario and answer the questions that follow:
A physical substance 'X' has no fixed shape but possesses a fixed volume.
(a) Identify the state of matter of substance 'X'. (1 Mark)
(b) Name the physical process by which 'X' will change into a gaseous state upon continuous heating. (1 Mark)
(c) If we study elements, they are broadly classified into metals, non-metals, and metalloids. Mention any three general physical properties of metals and give one example. (3 Marks)
