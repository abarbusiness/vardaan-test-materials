(Students must sketch/plot the graph correctly on their answer sheet).
(a) Analyzing the coordinates: P(-2, 1) and Q(2, 1) lie on a horizontal line y = 1. Length PQ = 2 - (-2) = 4 units.
S(-3, -2) and R(3, -2) lie on a horizontal line y = -2. Length SR = 3 - (-3) = 6 units.
Since PQ is parallel to SR, and PQ ≠ SR, the figure is a Trapezium.
(b) Area of Trapezium: Area = ½ × (Sum of parallel sides) × (Perpendicular height).
Parallel sides: a = PQ = 4 units, b = SR = 6 units.
Height (h) = distance between lines y = 1 and y = -2 = 1 - (-2) = 3 units.
Area = ½ × (4 + 6) × 3 = ½ × 10 × 3 = 15 square units.
Since 1 unit = 1 cm, Area = 15 cm².
Ans 18.
[3 + 2 = 5 Marks]
(a) Acceleration (a) = -6 m/s² (since it is opposite to motion/brakes applied).
Time (t) = 2 s. Final velocity (v) = 0 m/s (car stops).
First, find initial velocity (u) using v = u + at:
0 = u + (-6)(2) ⇒ 0 = u - 12 ⇒ u = 12 m/s.
Now, calculate distance (S) using S = ut + ½at²:
S = (12)(2) + ½(-6)(2)² = 24 - 3(4) = 24 - 12 = 12 m.
(b) Derivation (Mathematical): Average velocity = (Initial velocity + Final velocity) / 2 = (u + v) / 2.
Distance (S) = Average Velocity × Time = [(u + v) / 2] × t.
From 1st equation, substitute v = u + at:
S = [(u + u + at) / 2] × t = [(2u + at) / 2] × t.
S = (2ut + at²) / 2
S = ut + ½at² (Hence Derived). (Graphical method is also fully accepted).
