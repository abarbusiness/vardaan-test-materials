Minor Test 08
Class: 9 ICSE Subject: Science & Mathematics Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Syllabus: Motion in One Dimension (Physics) | Indices (Mathematics).
  • Write your answers neatly. Show necessary working steps for numericals.
  • Take g = 10 m/s² where necessary, unless specified otherwise.
SECTION A (20 Marks)(Objective / Very Short Answer - Attempt all 10 questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Differentiate between a scalar quantity and a vector quantity. Give one example of each from the chapter of motion.
2.
Simplify the following expression:
(xa / xb)c × (xb / xc)a × (xc / xa)b
3.
A car starts from rest and acquires a velocity of 54 km/h in 5 seconds. Calculate its acceleration in m/s².
4.
Evaluate without using tables:
(81 / 16)-3/4
5.
State what the slope of the following graphs represents:
(i) Displacement-time graph
(ii) Velocity-time graph
6.
Solve the exponential equation for x:
3x + 1 = 27
7.
Define uniform circular motion. Is it an accelerated motion? Give a reason for your answer.
8.
Find the value of:
(20 + 30 + 40)2
9.
Write down the three equations of uniformly accelerated motion for a body moving along a straight line.
10.
Simplify the given expression leaving your answer in index form:
(52 × 54) ÷ 53
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - Attempt all 5 questions - 3 Marks Each)
11.
A train travels the first 15 km at a uniform speed of 30 km/h and the next 75 km at a uniform speed of 50 km/h. Calculate the average speed of the train for the entire journey.
12.
Prove the following relation using the laws of indices:

(xa / xb)a + b × (xb / xc)b + c × (xc / xa)c + a = 1
13.
Draw rough velocity-time (v-t) graphs to represent the following states of motion:
(i) A body moving with a uniform velocity.
(ii) A body starting from rest and moving with uniform acceleration.
(iii) A body moving with a constant initial velocity and coming to rest with uniform retardation.
14.
Solve the following equation for x:
22x + 1 − 9 × 2x + 4 = 0
15.
A stone is dropped freely from a height of 20 m. Calculate:
(i) The velocity with which it hits the ground.
(ii) The time taken by the stone to reach the ground.
SECTION C (15 Marks)(Long Answer Type - Attempt all 3 questions - 5 Marks Each)
16.
Derive the third equation of motion, v2 − u2 = 2aS, using the graphical method. Clearly state the meaning of the symbols used and draw the necessary velocity-time graph for a uniformly accelerating body.
17.
If ax = by = cz and b2 = ac, then prove mathematically that:

y = (2xz) / (x + z)
18.
Attempt both parts based on the laws of indices:
(a) Simplify the following expression:

    [3 × 2n − 4 × 2n - 2] / [2n − 2n - 1]    (3 Marks)

(b) If 3x - 1 × 52y - 3 = 225, find the values of x and y.    (2 Marks)
