(a) Pascal's Law: It states that pressure applied to an enclosed static fluid is transmitted equally and undiminished in all directions. (1 Mark) (b) Device: Hydraulic press / Hydraulic lift / Hydraulic brakes. (1 Mark) (c) Calculation: Mass (m) = 10 kg ⇒ Force (W) = mg = 10 × 10 = 100 N. Dimensions = 0.5 m × 0.2 m × 0.1 m Maximum Area (for min pressure) = 0.5 × 0.2 = 0.1 m² Minimum Area (for max pressure) = 0.2 × 0.1 = 0.02 m² (1 Mark for areas) Minimum Pressure = F / Max Area = 100 / 0.1 = 1000 Pa (1 Mark) Maximum Pressure = F / Min Area = 100 / 0.02 = 5000 Pa (1 Mark)
Ans 12.
(a) Relative Density is the ratio of the density of a substance to the density of water at 4°C. It has no unit because it is a ratio of two identical physical quantities (densities), so the units cancel out. (2 Marks) (b) Calculation: Mass (M) = 450 g, Volume (V) = 50 cm³ (i) Density = M / V = 450 / 50 = 9 g/cm³ (1 Mark) (ii) Density in kg/m³ = 9 × 1000 = 9000 kg/m³ (1 Mark) (iii) Relative Density = Density of substance / Density of water = 9 g/cm³ / 1 g/cm³ = 9 (1 Mark)
Ans 13.
(a) Change of state refers to the physical transition of matter from one state (solid, liquid, or gas) to another by the absorption or release of heat. (1 Mark) (b) Melting: When a solid is heated, its temperature increases, and particles gain kinetic energy. They vibrate vigorously until the kinetic energy overcomes the strong intermolecular forces holding them in rigid positions. The solid structure breaks down, forming a liquid. (2 Marks) Evaporation: In liquids, particles have varying kinetic energies. Particles at the surface with higher kinetic energy overcome the attractive forces of neighboring particles and escape into the air as vapor.
Effect of Temperature: As temperature increases, the average kinetic energy of the molecules increases, making melting and evaporation happen faster. (2 Marks)
