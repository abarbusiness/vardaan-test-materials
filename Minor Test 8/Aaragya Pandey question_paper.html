Minor Test 8
Class: 7 ICSE Subject: Mathematics Time: 45 Minutes Max. Marks: 30
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Working/calculations must be shown clearly alongside the answers.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Objective / Short Answer Type - Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Evaluate the following expression using the rules of integers (BODMAS):
\( (-15) \times (-3) + (-40) \div 8 \)
2.
Convert the mixed fraction \( 3\frac{2}{5} \) into an improper fraction and write its reciprocal.
3.
Find any two rational numbers lying exactly between \( \frac{-1}{3} \) and \( \frac{1}{2} \).
4.
Simplify the following rational expression:
\( \frac{-5}{8} + \frac{3}{4} - \frac{1}{2} \)
5.
Express the rational number \( \frac{-36}{48} \) in its standard (simplest) form.
SECTION B (12 Marks)(Short Answer Type - Attempt all questions - 3 Marks Each)
6.
An elevator descends into a mine shaft at the rate of \( 6 \text{ m/min} \). If the descent starts from \( 10 \text{ m} \) above ground level, how long will it take the elevator to reach \( -350 \text{ m} \)?
7.
Arrange the following rational numbers in descending order (show the LCM method):

\( \frac{-2}{5}, \quad \frac{1}{-3}, \quad \frac{-4}{15}, \quad \frac{7}{-10} \)
8.
A boy reads \( \frac{1}{4} \) of a book on the first day and \( \frac{1}{3} \) of the remaining book on the second day. If \( 100 \) pages are still left unread, how many total pages are there in the book?
9.
Simplify the following expression involving rational fractions:

\( \left( \frac{-2}{3} \times \frac{4}{5} \right) - \left( \frac{1}{2} \div \frac{-3}{4} \right) \)
SECTION C (8 Marks)(Long Answer Type - Attempt all questions - 4 Marks Each)
10.
(a) Draw a neat number line and represent the rational number \( \frac{-7}{4} \) on it. (2 Marks)
(b) What rational number should be added to \( \frac{-5}{8} \) to get \( \frac{3}{2} \)? (2 Marks)
11.
In a competitive mathematics test containing 20 questions, \( 4 \) marks are awarded for every correct answer, \( -2 \) marks are given for every incorrect answer, and \( 0 \) marks for questions not attempted.
(a) Rohan attempts all questions but only \( 12 \) of his answers are correct. What is his total score? (2 Marks)
(b) If another student, Priya, scores \( 20 \) marks in the test and got \( 8 \) correct answers, how many questions did she answer incorrectly? (2 Marks)
