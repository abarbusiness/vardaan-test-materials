Equation: 22x + 1 − 9 × 2x + 4 = 0
Rewrite as: 22x × 21 − 9 × 2x + 4 = 0
2 × (2x)2 − 9 × 2x + 4 = 0
Let 2x = y. The equation becomes: 2y² - 9y + 4 = 0
2y² - 8y - y + 4 = 0 ⇒ 2y(y - 4) - 1(y - 4) = 0
(2y - 1)(y - 4) = 0 ⇒ y = ½ or y = 4
Case 1: 2x = ½ ⇒ 2x = 2-1 ⇒ x = -1
Case 2: 2x = 4 ⇒ 2x = 22 ⇒ x = 2
x = -1, 2

Ans 16.
[5 Marks]
Meaning of symbols: u = initial velocity, v = final velocity, a = uniform acceleration, S = displacement, t = time. (1 Mark)
Graph: Student must draw a v-t graph showing a straight line AB starting from point A on the y-axis (where y-intercept = u) to point B. Draw perpendiculars to axes. Let t be the time on x-axis. (1 Mark)
Derivation: (3 Marks)
Displacement (S) = Area under v-t graph (Area of trapezium OABC).
S = ½ × (Sum of parallel sides) × (Perpendicular distance between them)
S = ½ × (u + v) × t     ...(Equation 1)
From the first equation of motion, v = u + at ⇒ t = (v - u) / a.
Substitute 't' in Equation 1:
S = ½ × (v + u) × [(v - u) / a]
S = (v² - u²) / 2a    (Using mathematical identity (a+b)(a-b) = a²-b²)
v² - u² = 2aS (Hence Derived)
Ans 17.
[5 Marks]
Given: ax = by = cz
Let ax = by = cz = k (a constant).
Then, a = k1/x,   b = k1/y,   c = k1/z
We are given the relation: b² = ac
Substitute the values of a, b, and c in terms of k:
(k1/y)2 = (k1/x) × (k1/z)
k2/y = k1/x + 1/z
Since the bases are the same (k) and k ≠ 0,1, we can equate the exponents:
2 / y = 1/x + 1/z
2 / y = (z + x) / (xz)
Taking reciprocal on both sides:
y / 2 = (xz) / (x + z)
y = (2xz) / (x + z)   (Hence Proved)
