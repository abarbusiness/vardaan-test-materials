Sol 4.
Given: \( 2 \begin{bmatrix} x & 5 \\ 7 & y-3 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -4 \\ 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 7 & 6 \\ 15 & 14 \end{bmatrix} \)
Multiply the scalar into the first matrix:
\( \begin{bmatrix} 2x & 10 \\ 14 & 2y-6 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -4 \\ 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 7 & 6 \\ 15 & 14 \end{bmatrix} \)

Add the matrices on the LHS:
\( \begin{bmatrix} 2x + 3 & 10 - 4 \\ 14 + 1 & 2y - 6 + 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 7 & 6 \\ 15 & 14 \end{bmatrix} \)
\( \begin{bmatrix} 2x + 3 & 6 \\ 15 & 2y - 4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 7 & 6 \\ 15 & 14 \end{bmatrix} \)

By equating corresponding elements:
\( 2x + 3 = 7 \implies 2x = 4 \implies \mathbf{x = 2} \)
\( 2y - 4 = 14 \implies 2y = 18 \implies \mathbf{y = 9} \)
Answer: \( x = 2 \), \( y = 9 \)
Sol 5.

\( AB = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 3 & 0 \end{bmatrix}_{2 \times 2} \times \begin{bmatrix} -3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix}_{2 \times 1} \)

\( AB = \begin{bmatrix} (2 \times -3) + (-1 \times 4) \\ (3 \times -3) + (0 \times 4) \end{bmatrix} \)

\( AB = \begin{bmatrix} -6 - 4 \\ -9 + 0 \end{bmatrix} \)

\( AB = \begin{bmatrix} -10 \\ -9 \end{bmatrix} \)

Sol 8.
Given \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ -2 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \). Find \( M = A^2 - 4A + 5I \).
Step 1: Find \( A^2 \)
\( A^2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ -2 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ -2 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \)
\( A^2 = \begin{bmatrix} (1)(1)+(2)(-2) & (1)(2)+(2)(3) \\ (-2)(1)+(3)(-2) & (-2)(2)+(3)(3) \end{bmatrix} \)
\( A^2 = \begin{bmatrix} 1-4 & 2+6 \\ -2-6 & -4+9 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -3 & 8 \\ -8 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \)

Step 2: Find \( -4A \) and \( 5I \)
\( -4A = -4 \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ -2 & 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} -4 & -8 \\ 8 & -12 \end{bmatrix} \)
\( 5I = 5 \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 0 \\ 0 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \)

Step 3: Calculate \( M \)
\( M = \begin{bmatrix} -3 & 8 \\ -8 & 5 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} -4 & -8 \\ 8 & -12 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 0 \\ 0 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \)
\( M = \begin{bmatrix} -3 - 4 + 5 & 8 - 8 + 0 \\ -8 + 8 + 0 & 5 - 12 + 5 \end{bmatrix} \)
\( M = \begin{bmatrix} -2 & 0 \\ 0 & -2 \end{bmatrix} \)
Answer: \( M = \begin{bmatrix} -2 & 0 \\ 0 & -2 \end{bmatrix} \)
Sol 9.
\( \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -3 & 4 \end{bmatrix} X = \begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} \)
Let matrix \( X \) be \( \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} \) since order must be \( 2 \times 1 \).

\( \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -3 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} \)

\( \begin{bmatrix} 2x + y \\ -3x + 4y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} \)

Equating the elements yields simultaneous equations:
1) \( 2x + y = 7 \implies y = 7 - 2x \)
2) \( -3x + 4y = 6 \)

Substitute (1) into (2):
\( -3x + 4(7 - 2x) = 6 \)
\( -3x + 28 - 8x = 6 \)
\( -11x = 6 - 28 \)
\( -11x = -22 \implies \mathbf{x = 2} \)

Substitute \( x = 2 \) in (1):
\( y = 7 - 2(2) = 7 - 4 \implies \mathbf{y = 3} \)
Answer: Matrix \( X = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \)
Sol 10.
Given \( P = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 2 \\ -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( Q = \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \). Evaluate \( P^2 Q \).
Method: Find \( P^2 \) first, then multiply by \( Q \).
\( P^2 = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 2 \\ -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 2 \\ -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \)
\( P^2 = \begin{bmatrix} 16 - 2 & 8 + 2 \\ -4 - 1 & -2 + 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 10 \\ -5 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \)

Now evaluate \( P^2 Q \):
\( P^2 Q = \begin{bmatrix} 14 & 10 \\ -5 & -1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \)
\( P^2 Q = \begin{bmatrix} 14(-2) + 10(3) \\ -5(-2) + (-1)(3) \end{bmatrix} \)
\( P^2 Q = \begin{bmatrix} -28 + 30 \\ 10 - 3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \)
Answer: \( \begin{bmatrix} 2 \\ 7 \end{bmatrix} \)

Sol 13.
Given: \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( B = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 4 \\ -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \).
(i) Find matrix \( C \), if \( C = 2A - B \). (2 Marks)
\( 2A = 2 \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 2 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \)

\( C = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & 2 \\ -2 & 4 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 4 \\ -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \)
\( C = \begin{bmatrix} 6 - 0 & 2 - 4 \\ -2 - (-2) & 4 - 1 \end{bmatrix} \)
\( C = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & -2 \\ 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \)
(ii) Prove that \( A^2 - 5A + 7I = O \). (3 Marks)
Find \( A^2 \):
\( A^2 = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \)
\( A^2 = \begin{bmatrix} 9-1 & 3+2 \\ -3-2 & -1+4 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 8 & 5 \\ -5 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \)

Find \( 5A \) and \( 7I \):
\( 5A = 5 \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 15 & 5 \\ -5 & 10 \end{bmatrix} \)
\( 7I = 7 \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 7 & 0 \\ 0 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \)

Substitute into expression:
LHS = \( \begin{bmatrix} 8 & 5 \\ -5 & 3 \end{bmatrix} - \begin{bmatrix} 15 & 5 \\ -5 & 10 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 7 & 0 \\ 0 & 7 \end{bmatrix} \)
LHS = \( \begin{bmatrix} 8 - 15 + 7 & 5 - 5 + 0 \\ -5 - (-5) + 0 & 3 - 10 + 7 \end{bmatrix} \)
LHS = \( \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 \end{bmatrix} = O \)

Hence Proved. (LHS = RHS)
Answer: (i) \( C = \begin{bmatrix} 6 & -2 \\ 0 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \)   (ii) Proof shown above.
