MIT 8
Class: 8 CBSE Subject: Mathematics Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly. Show clear steps of calculation.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Define direct proportion. Support your definition with one suitable real-life example.
2.
Identify whether the following pairs of variables are in direct proportion or inverse proportion:
(a) The number of workers working on a construction job and the time taken to complete the job.
(b) The quantity of uniform fabric bought and the total cost of the fabric.
3.
If variables x and y vary directly with each other, and x = 10 when y = 15, find the exact value of x when y = 30.
4.
A heavy-duty machine in a soft drink factory fills 840 bottles in 6 hours. Calculate how many bottles it will fill in 5 hours operating at the same rate.
5.
Observe the table given below carefully. State whether x and y vary directly or inversely. Justify your answer mathematically.
x 2 4 8
y 16 8 4
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
If 15 workers can construct a boundary wall in 48 hours, determine how many workers will be required to do the exact same work in 30 hours.
7.
A fully loaded transport truck travels 14 km in 25 minutes. Assuming its speed remains uniform, calculate the distance it can travel in 5 hours.
8.
In an architectural model of a ship, the mast is 9 cm high, while the mast of the actual real-world ship is 12 m high. If the length of the actual ship is 28 m, logically deduce how long the model ship is.
9.
Suppose 2 kg of fine sugar contains 9 × 106 crystals. Find out how many sugar crystals are there in 5 kg of the same sugar. Express your final answer in standard scientific notation.
10.
A large batch of glass bottles were packed in 25 boxes, with 12 bottles securely placed in each box. If the same batch of bottles is repacked using 20 bottles in each box, calculate how many boxes would be filled.
SECTION C (25 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
A military garrison consisting of 1200 men had sufficient food provisions for 28 days. After exactly 4 days, 300 men are transferred out of the garrison. How long will the remaining food last for the men left behind? Show step-by-step reasoning.
12.
An express train is moving forward at a constant uniform speed of 75 km/h.
(i) Calculate the exact distance it will travel in 20 minutes. (2 Marks)
(ii) Find the total time required for this train to cover a distance of 250 km. (3 Marks)
13.
A set of uniform pipes is connected to a large water reservoir.
(i) Six pipes working together can fill the tank in 1 hour and 20 minutes. Calculate how long it will take to fill the tank if only five pipes of the same type are functional. (3 Marks)
(ii) State explicitly whether the variables in this scenario are in direct or inverse proportion, and write down the fundamental equation connecting them. (2 Marks)
14.
During a festival, a large box of assorted sweets is divided equally among 24 children, resulting in each child receiving 5 sweets.

Calculate how many sweets each child would receive if the number of children is suddenly reduced by 4. Furthermore, formally explain the core mathematical concept governing this relationship.
15.
A local high school operates with 8 periods a day, each having a duration of 45 minutes. The administration decides to restructure the day to have 9 periods a day. Assuming the total number of school hours remains completely unchanged, how long would each new period be?

You must set up a proportion table and demonstrate your detailed working to arrive at the solution.
