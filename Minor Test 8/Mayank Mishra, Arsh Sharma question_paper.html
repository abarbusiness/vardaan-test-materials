Minor Test 8
Surprise Test
Class: 10 ICSE Subjects: History & Mathematics Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 40
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Questions are categorized subject-wise within each section.
  • For Mathematics, all working/calculations must be shown clearly alongside the answers.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Objective / Short Answer Type - Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
SUBJECT: HISTORY
1.
Europe was divided into two major armed camps prior to the First World War. Name the two camps and list the original member nations of the Triple Entente.
2.
Mention any two specific terms of the Treaty of Versailles that severely crippled the German military.
3.
State the immediate cause of the First World War. In your answer, name the assassin and the secret society to which he belonged.
SUBJECT: MATHEMATICS (MATRICES)
4.
Find the values of \( x \) and \( y \) if:

\( 2 \begin{bmatrix} x & 5 \\ 7 & y-3 \end{bmatrix} + \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -4 \\ 1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 7 & 6 \\ 15 & 14 \end{bmatrix} \)
5.
If \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ 3 & 0 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( B = \begin{bmatrix} -3 \\ 4 \end{bmatrix} \), find the matrix product \( AB \). State the order of the resulting matrix.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - Attempt all questions - 3 Marks Each)
SUBJECT: HISTORY
6.
How did "Intense Nationalism" (Competitive Patriotism) and the "Armament Race" act as underlying causes for the outbreak of the First World War? Explain briefly.
7.
State any three primary objectives behind the establishment of the League of Nations following the First World War.
SUBJECT: MATHEMATICS (MATRICES)
8.
Let \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ -2 & 3 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( I \) be the identity matrix of order 2. Find the matrix \( M \) such that \( M = A^2 - 4A + 5I \).
9.
Solve the matrix equation for matrix \( X \):

\( \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ -3 & 4 \end{bmatrix} X = \begin{bmatrix} 7 \\ 6 \end{bmatrix} \)
10.
Given \( P = \begin{bmatrix} 4 & 2 \\ -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( Q = \begin{bmatrix} -2 \\ 3 \end{bmatrix} \). Evaluate \( P^2 Q \).
SECTION C (15 Marks)(Long Answer Type - Attempt all questions - 5 Marks Each)
SUBJECT: HISTORY
11.
The Treaty of Versailles is heavily criticized as a "dictated peace" based on the principle "To the Victors belong the Spoils." Justify this statement by explaining how the treaty dealt with:
(a) The Rhine Valley and Alsace-Lorraine. (2 Marks)
(b) The Independence of Poland (including the city of Danzig). (1.5 Marks)
(c) Germany's overseas colonies. (1.5 Marks)
12.
Discuss the significant results and consequences of the First World War under the following headings:
(a) The emergence of New Ideologies and Dictatorships. (2 Marks)
(b) National Self-Determination and the creation of new nations. (1.5 Marks)
(c) The USA emerging as a World Power. (1.5 Marks)
SUBJECT: MATHEMATICS (MATRICES)
13.
If \( A = \begin{bmatrix} 3 & 1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{bmatrix} \) and \( B = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & 4 \\ -2 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \):
(i) Find matrix \( C \), if \( C = 2A - B \). (2 Marks)
(ii) Prove that \( A^2 - 5A + 7I = O \), where \( I \) is the unit matrix and \( O \) is the null matrix of order 2. (3 Marks)
