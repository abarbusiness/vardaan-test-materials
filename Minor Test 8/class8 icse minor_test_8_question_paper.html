General Instructions:

SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Define atmospheric pressure. Give one practical reason why deep-sea divers must wear specially designed pressurized suits.
2.
The density of a solid metal block is 7.8 g/cm³. Express this density in SI units (kg/m³). Show the conversion steps clearly.
3.
Differentiate between solids and liquids strictly on the basis of:
(i) Intermolecular space
(ii) Intermolecular force of attraction
4.
A stationery shop owner buys a set of notebooks for ₹450 and sells them for ₹540. Calculate his profit percentage.
5.
Calculate the Selling Price (S.P.) of an article if its Cost Price (C.P.) is ₹800 and it is sold at a loss of 15%.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
A force of 150 N is applied uniformly over an area of 3 m².
(a) Calculate the pressure exerted in Pascals.
(b) What will happen to the pressure if the area is reduced to half while keeping the applied force constant?
7.
Describe briefly the procedure to find the volume of an irregular solid (like a piece of stone) using a measuring cylinder and water. State the formula you would use.
8.
State the Kinetic Theory of Matter. Write any two postulates of this theory that explain the behavior of particles in matter.
9.
By selling a watch for ₹1140, a man loses 5%. At what price should the watch be sold to earn a gain of 5%?
10.
Give scientific reasons for the following:
(a) A sharp knife cuts vegetables much easier than a blunt knife.
(b) The walls of a dam are made thicker at the bottom than at the top.
SECTION C (25 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
(a) State Pascal's Law of transmission of fluid pressure. (1)
(b) Name one device that works on the principle of Pascal's Law. (1)
(c) A solid wooden block of mass 10 kg has dimensions 50 cm × 20 cm × 10 cm. It is placed on a table top. Calculate the maximum pressure and the minimum pressure exerted by the wooden block on the table. (Take g = 10 m/s²) (3)
12.
(a) Define Relative Density. Why does it not have any unit? (2)
(b) A metallic piece has a mass of 450 g and occupies a volume of 50 cm³. Find its:
     (i) Density in g/cm³
     (ii) Density in kg/m³
     (iii) Relative density (Given density of water = 1 g/cm³) (3)
13.
(a) What do you understand by a 'change of state' of matter? (1)
(b) Explain the processes of Melting and Evaporation on the basis of the Kinetic Theory of Matter. How does temperature affect the kinetic energy of the molecules in these processes? (4)
14.
A retailer marks up his goods 25% above the cost price. To attract customers, he allows a discount of 12% on the marked price.
(a) Calculate his overall profit percentage. (3)
(b) If the final selling price of an article after the discount is ₹880, what was its original cost price? (2)
15.
(a) A man bought two bicycles for ₹2500 each. He sold one at a profit of 15% and the other at a loss of 15%. Calculate his total selling price and find out his overall gain or loss percentage in the whole transaction. (3)
(b) Explain the relationship between Force, Area, and Pressure with a relevant real-life example not mentioned previously in the paper. (2)
