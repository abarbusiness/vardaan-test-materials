SECTION A (10 Marks)
Sol 1.
Evaluate: \( (-15) \times (-3) + (-40) \div 8 \)
Apply BODMAS (Division first, then Multiplication):
Step 1: Divide: \( (-40) \div 8 = -5 \)
Expression becomes: \( (-15) \times (-3) + (-5) \)
Step 2: Multiply: \( (-15) \times (-3) = 45 \) (product of two negative integers is positive)
Expression becomes: \( 45 + (-5) \)
Step 3: Add: \( 45 - 5 = 40 \)
Answer: \( 40 \)
Sol 2.
Mixed fraction: \( 3\frac{2}{5} \)
Improper fraction = \( \frac{3 \times 5 + 2}{5} = \frac{15 + 2}{5} = \frac{17}{5} \)
Reciprocal of \( \frac{17}{5} \) is formed by inverting the numerator and denominator.
Answer: Improper Fraction: \( \frac{17}{5} \), Reciprocal: \( \frac{5}{17} \)
Sol 3.
Find rational numbers between \( \frac{-1}{3} \) and \( \frac{1}{2} \).
LCM of denominators 3 and 2 is 6.
Convert to equivalent fractions with denominator 6:
\( \frac{-1 \times 2}{3 \times 2} = \frac{-2}{6} \)
\( \frac{1 \times 3}{2 \times 3} = \frac{3}{6} \)
Rational numbers between \( \frac{-2}{6} \) and \( \frac{3}{6} \) are \( \frac{-1}{6}, 0, \frac{1}{6}, \frac{2}{6} \).
Answer: Any two of \( \frac{-1}{6}, 0, \frac{1}{6}, \frac{1}{3} \) (Accept any other valid method/answer).
Sol 4.
Simplify: \( \frac{-5}{8} + \frac{3}{4} - \frac{1}{2} \)
LCM of denominators 8, 4, 2 is 8.
Make equivalent fractions:
\( \frac{-5}{8} = \frac{-5}{8} \)
\( \frac{3 \times 2}{4 \times 2} = \frac{6}{8} \)
\( \frac{1 \times 4}{2 \times 4} = \frac{4}{8} \)
Combine numerators: \( \frac{-5 + 6 - 4}{8} \)
\( = \frac{1 - 4}{8} = \frac{-3}{8} \)
Answer: \( \frac{-3}{8} \)
Sol 5.
Express \( \frac{-36}{48} \) in standard form.
Find the HCF (Highest Common Factor) of 36 and 48, which is 12.
Divide both numerator and denominator by 12:
\( \frac{-36 \div 12}{48 \div 12} \)
\( = \frac{-3}{4} \)
Answer: \( \frac{-3}{4} \)
SECTION B (12 Marks)
Sol 6.
Initial position = \( +10 \text{ m} \) (above ground)
Final position = \( -350 \text{ m} \) (below ground)
Total distance to be covered = \( 10 - (-350) = 10 + 350 = 360 \text{ m} \)
Rate of descent = \( 6 \text{ m/min} \)
Time taken = \( \frac{\text{Total Distance}}{\text{Speed}} \)
Time = \( \frac{360}{6} = 60 \text{ minutes} \)
Answer: \( 60 \text{ minutes} \) (or 1 hour).
Sol 7.
Arrange in descending order: \( \frac{-2}{5}, \frac{1}{-3}, \frac{-4}{15}, \frac{7}{-10} \)
First, write all fractions in standard form (negative sign in numerator):
\( \frac{-2}{5}, \frac{-1}{3}, \frac{-4}{15}, \frac{-7}{10} \)
Find LCM of denominators (5, 3, 15, 10). LCM = 30.
Convert to equivalent fractions with denominator 30:
\( \frac{-2 \times 6}{5 \times 6} = \frac{-12}{30} \)
\( \frac{-1 \times 10}{3 \times 10} = \frac{-10}{30} \)
\( \frac{-4 \times 2}{15 \times 2} = \frac{-8}{30} \)
\( \frac{-7 \times 3}{10 \times 3} = \frac{-21}{30} \)
Compare numerators: \( -8 > -10 > -12 > -21 \)
Therefore: \( \frac{-8}{30} > \frac{-10}{30} > \frac{-12}{30} > \frac{-21}{30} \)
Answer: \( \frac{-4}{15} > \frac{1}{-3} > \frac{-2}{5} > \frac{7}{-10} \)
Sol 8.
Let the total number of pages be \( x \).
Pages read on Day 1 = \( \frac{1}{4}x \)
Remaining pages = \( x - \frac{1}{4}x = \frac{3}{4}x \)
Pages read on Day 2 = \( \frac{1}{3} \text{ of remaining} = \frac{1}{3} \times \frac{3}{4}x = \frac{1}{4}x \)
Total pages read = \( \frac{1}{4}x + \frac{1}{4}x = \frac{2}{4}x = \frac{1}{2}x \)
Unread pages = Total pages - Read pages = \( x - \frac{1}{2}x = \frac{1}{2}x \)
Given unread pages = 100.
So, \( \frac{1}{2}x = 100 \implies x = 200 \).
Answer: The book has \( 200 \) total pages.
Sol 9.
Simplify: \( \left( \frac{-2}{3} \times \frac{4}{5} \right) - \left( \frac{1}{2} \div \frac{-3}{4} \right) \)
Solve the first bracket (Multiplication):
\( \frac{-2 \times 4}{3 \times 5} = \frac{-8}{15} \)
Solve the second bracket (Division):
\( \frac{1}{2} \div \frac{-3}{4} = \frac{1}{2} \times \frac{-4}{3} = \frac{-4}{6} = \frac{-2}{3} \)
Now subtract the results:
\( \frac{-8}{15} - \left( \frac{-2}{3} \right) = \frac{-8}{15} + \frac{2}{3} \)
LCM of 15 and 3 is 15.
\( = \frac{-8}{15} + \frac{2 \times 5}{3 \times 5} = \frac{-8}{15} + \frac{10}{15} \)
\( = \frac{2}{15} \)
Answer: \( \frac{2}{15} \)
