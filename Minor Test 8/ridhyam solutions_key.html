Ans 11.
(a) Kinetic Theory of Matter: It states that all matter is made up of tiny, invisible particles (atoms/molecules) which are in a continuous state of random motion, thus possessing kinetic energy. (2 Marks) (b) Process of Melting: When a solid is heated, the kinetic energy of its particles increases, causing them to vibrate more vigorously. (1 Mark)
This vigorous vibration increases the intermolecular space between the particles. (1 Mark)
Consequently, the strong intermolecular force of attraction weakens. Eventually, the particles overcome their fixed rigid positions and the solid turns into a liquid. (1 Mark)
