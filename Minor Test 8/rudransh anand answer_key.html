6.
(Mathematics) Simplify using exponent laws.
Ans: $$ \frac{8^{-1} \times 5^3}{2^{-4}} $$ Convert base $8$ to $2$: $8 = 2^3$, so $8^{-1} = (2^3)^{-1} = 2^{-3}$ [1 Mark]
Expression becomes: $$ \frac{2^{-3} \times 5^3}{2^{-4}} $$ Using law $\frac{a^m}{a^n} = a^{m-n}$: [1 Mark]
$ = 2^{-3 - (-4)} \times 5^3 $
$ = 2^{-3 + 4} \times 5^3 $
$ = 2^1 \times 5^3 $
$ = 2 \times 125 = \mathbf{250} $ [1 Mark]
7.
(Science) Electromagnet definition & strength.
Ans:
Electromagnet: A coil of insulated wire wound around a piece of magnetic material (like soft iron) which acts as a magnet only as long as electric current is flowing through it. [1 Mark]
Ways to increase strength: [2 Marks]
1. By increasing the number of turns in the coil.
2. By increasing the magnitude of electric current flowing through the coil.

9.
(Science) Electric fuse working & reason.
Ans:
Working Principle: It works on the heating effect of electric current. A fuse contains a special wire with a low melting point. If a current larger than the safe limit flows, the wire heats up rapidly and melts, breaking the circuit and preventing damage. [1.5 Marks]
Reason: An ordinary copper wire has a very high melting point. During a power surge or short circuit, it will not melt to break the circuit. This failure will allow excessive current to flow, potentially damaging appliances or causing an electrical fire. [1.5 Marks]

12.
(Science - Heating Effect & Magnetism)
Ans (a): Oersted's Experiment:
1. Take a magnetic compass and place it near a straight conducting wire.
2. Connect the wire to a battery and a switch to form a closed circuit.
3. When the switch is turned ON (current flows), the compass needle shows a noticeable deflection. [2 Marks]
4. When switched OFF, the needle returns to its original North-South position. This proves that a current-carrying wire produces a magnetic field around it. [1 Mark]

Ans (b): Factors for Heating Effect: [1 Mark each = 2 Marks]
1. The material of the wire (its resistance).
2. The length and thickness of the wire (or the magnitude/duration of current flowing through it).

14.
(Science - Electric Bell)
Ans (a): Working Mechanism:
The electric bell utilizes the magnetic effect of electric current. [0.5 Marks]
When the switch is pressed, current flows through the coil, turning the soft iron core into an electromagnet. This electromagnet attracts the iron armature. [1 Mark]
As the armature moves towards the electromagnet, the hammer strikes the gong, producing sound. Simultaneously, the circuit breaks at the contact screw, cutting off the current. The electromagnet loses its magnetism, and the spring pulls the armature back, remaking the contact. This cycle repeats rapidly, creating a continuous ringing. [1.5 Marks]

Ans (b): Differences: [1 Mark each = 2 Marks]
Electromagnet Permanent Magnet
Temporary; magnetism lasts only as long as current flows. Permanent; retains magnetism continuously.
Strength can be altered easily (by changing current/turns). Strength is fixed and cannot be easily changed.
