2.
Measurement of motion.
Ans (a):
Uniform Motion: An object moving along a straight line with a constant speed (covers equal distances in equal intervals of time). Example: A car moving on a straight highway at exactly $60\text{ km/h}$. [1 Mark]
Non-Uniform Motion: An object moving with a changing speed (covers unequal distances in equal intervals of time). Example: A train entering or leaving a station. [1 Mark]

Ans (b):
Distance = $120\text{ km}$, Time = $2.5\text{ hours}$. $$\text{Average Speed} = \frac{\text{Total Distance}}{\text{Total Time}} = \frac{120}{2.5} = \mathbf{48\text{ km/h}}$$ [1.5 Marks]
Convert to $\text{m/s}$: $$\text{Speed} = 48 \times \frac{5}{18} = \frac{240}{18} = \mathbf{13.33\text{ m/s}}$$ [1.5 Marks]

5.
Miniature Circuit Breakers (MCBs).
Ans:
(a) Miniature Circuit Breaker. [1 Mark]
(b) It works on the magnetic effect of electric current. [1 Mark]
(c) MCBs automatically turn off the switch when current exceeds the safe limit. They are preferred because they do not "blow/melt" permanently; they can simply be switched back 'ON' manually once the fault is fixed, unlike a fuse which must be replaced. [1 Mark]
6.
Odometer numerical.
Ans:
Initial reading = $1048\text{ km}$, Final reading = $1096\text{ km}$.
Distance covered = $1096 - 1048 = \mathbf{48\text{ km}}$. [1 Mark]
Time taken = $8:40\text{ AM} - 8:00\text{ AM} = \mathbf{40\text{ minutes}}$.
Speed in $\text{km/min} = \frac{48}{40} = \mathbf{1.2\text{ km/min}}$. [1 Mark]
Convert time to hours: $40\text{ minutes} = \frac{40}{60}\text{ hours} = \frac{2}{3}\text{ hours}$.
Speed in $\text{km/h} = \frac{48}{\frac{2}{3}} = 48 \times \frac{3}{2} = \mathbf{72\text{ km/h}}$. [1 Mark]
7.
Electric bell.
Ans:
(a) Electromagnet (U-shaped soft iron core wound with wire). [1 Mark]
(b) When the circuit breaks, the current stops flowing. The electromagnet loses its magnetism immediately, and the spring pulls the armature back to its original position. [1 Mark]
(c) Application: Used in heavy cranes to lift magnetic scrap, electric motors, or loudspeakers (any one). [1 Mark]
8.
Comparing speeds.
Ans:
For Friend A: Distance = $3\text{ km}$, Time = $15\text{ mins}$. $$\text{Speed of A} = \frac{3}{15} = \mathbf{0.2\text{ km/min}}$$ [1 Mark]
For Friend B: Distance = $4.5\text{ km}$, Time = $20\text{ mins}$. $$\text{Speed of B} = \frac{4.5}{20} = \mathbf{0.225\text{ km/min}}$$ [1 Mark]
Conclusion: Since $0.225 > 0.2$, Friend B ran faster. [1 Mark]

10.
Kinematics & Averages.
Ans (a): $$\text{Distance before break (D1)} = \text{Speed}_1 \times \text{Time}_1$$ $$D1 = 60\text{ km/h} \times 2\text{ hours} = \mathbf{120\text{ km}}$$ [1.5 Marks]

Ans (b): $$\text{Distance after break (D2)} = \text{Speed}_2 \times \text{Time}_2$$ $$D2 = 80\text{ km/h} \times 1.5\text{ hours} = \mathbf{120\text{ km}}$$ [1.5 Marks]

Ans (c):
Total Distance = $120\text{ km} + 120\text{ km} = 240\text{ km}$.
Total Time = $2\text{ hrs} (\text{initial}) + 1\text{ hr} (\text{break}) + 1.5\text{ hrs} (\text{final}) = 4.5\text{ hours}$. [1 Mark] $$\text{Average Speed} = \frac{\text{Total Distance}}{\text{Total Time}} = \frac{240}{4.5} = \mathbf{53.33\text{ km/h}}$$ [1 Mark]
11.
Electromagnetism.
Ans (a): An electromagnet is a temporary magnet consisting of a coil of insulated wire wrapped around a piece of magnetic material (like soft iron), which behaves like a magnet only when electric current flows through it. [1 Mark]
Difference: A permanent magnet retains its magnetism without current, whereas an electromagnet loses its magnetism the moment current is switched off. [1 Mark]

Ans (b): Magnetic strength can be increased by:
1. Increasing the number of turns of wire in the coil.
2. Increasing the magnitude of electric current flowing through the coil. [2 Marks]

Ans (c): Soft iron is preferred because it gains magnetism very quickly when current flows and loses it completely and instantly when the current is stopped (does not retain residual magnetism). [1 Mark]
