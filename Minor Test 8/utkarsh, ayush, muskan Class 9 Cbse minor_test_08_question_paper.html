Minor Test 08
Class: 9 CBSE Subject: Science & Mathematics Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Syllabus: Describing Motion Around Us (Science) | Coordinate Geometry (Mathematics).
  • Write your answers neatly. Show necessary working steps for mathematical problems.
SECTION A (20 Marks)(Objective / Very Short Answer - Attempt all 10 questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Define displacement. Can displacement be zero even if the distance travelled is not zero? Give a brief example.
2.
Write the coordinates of a point which lies on the x-axis and is at a distance of 4 units to the left of the origin. Also, name the axis on which the point (0, -7) lies.
3.
A car travels 30 km at a uniform speed of 40 km/h and the next 30 km at a uniform speed of 20 km/h. Calculate its average speed.
4.
In which quadrant or on which axis do each of the following points lie?
(i) (-2, 4)      (ii) (3, -1)      (iii) (-1, 0)      (iv) (-3, -5)
5.
What does the odometer of an automobile measure? Which of the following is moving faster: a scooter moving at 300 m per minute or a car moving at 36 km per hour?
6.
The perpendicular distance of a point from the x-axis is 2 units and its perpendicular distance from the y-axis is 3 units. Write the coordinates of the point if it lies in the III Quadrant.
7.
A racing car has a uniform acceleration of 4 m/s². What distance will it cover in 10 s after the start?
8.
Write the name of the point where the horizontal and vertical axes intersect. What are its coordinates?
9.
Under what condition is the magnitude of average velocity of an object equal to its average speed?
10.
If the coordinates of two points are P(-2, 3) and Q(-3, 5), then find the value of:
(abscissa of P) − (ordinate of Q).
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - Attempt all 5 questions - 3 Marks Each)
11.
Draw a neat distance-time graph for a body representing:
(i) A body at rest.
(ii) A body moving with uniform motion.
(iii) A body moving with non-uniform motion.
12.
Plot the points A(2, 3), B(-2, 3), C(-2, -3) and D(2, -3) on a Cartesian plane (or graph paper). Join them in the order A → B → C → D → A. Name the geometrical figure so formed.
13.
A stone is thrown in a vertically upward direction with a velocity of 5 m/s. If the acceleration of the stone during its motion is 10 m/s² in the downward direction, what will be the height attained by the stone and how much time will it take to reach there?
14.
Without plotting the points, indicate the quadrant in which they will lie, if:
(i) Ordinate is 5 and abscissa is -3.
(ii) Abscissa is -5 and ordinate is -3.
(iii) Abscissa is 4 and ordinate is -2.
15.
Derive the first equation of motion, v = u + at, using a velocity-time graph for a uniformly accelerated body.
SECTION C (15 Marks)(Long Answer Type - Attempt all 3 questions - 5 Marks Each)
16.
An artificial satellite is moving in a circular orbit of radius 42250 km.
(a) Calculate its speed if it takes 24 hours to revolve around the earth. (Take π = 22/7) (2 Marks)
(b) Define uniform circular motion. (1 Mark)
(c) Is uniform circular motion an accelerated motion? Explain why. (2 Marks)
17.
Plot the points P(-2, 1), Q(2, 1), R(3, -2) and S(-3, -2) on a graph paper. Join them in order to form a quadrilateral.
(a) Identify the specific type of quadrilateral PQRS. (2 Marks)
(b) Find the area of the quadrilateral PQRS, assuming 1 unit on the graph equals 1 cm. (3 Marks)
18.
Attempt both parts:
(a) The brakes applied to a car produce an acceleration of 6 m/s² in the opposite direction to the motion. If the car takes 2 s to stop after the application of brakes, calculate the distance it travels during this time. (3 Marks)
(b) Derive the position-time relation (S = ut + ½at²) mathematically or graphically. (2 Marks)
