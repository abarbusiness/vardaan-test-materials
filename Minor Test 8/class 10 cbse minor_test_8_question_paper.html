SECTION A (20 Marks)(Objective / Very Short Answer - Attempt all 10 questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Name the acid present in an ant sting. Suggest a common chemical substance that can be rubbed on the stung area to get relief.
2.
Check mathematically whether the following is a quadratic equation:
(x + 1)2 = 2(x - 3)
3.
Why should curd and other sour substances not be kept in brass and copper vessels? Provide a scientific reason.
4.
Find the discriminant of the quadratic equation 2x2 - 4x + 3 = 0, and hence state the nature of its roots.
5.
What happens when an acid reacts with a metal oxide? Give one balanced chemical equation to illustrate your answer.
6.
Find the roots of the quadratic equation x2 - 3x - 10 = 0 by the method of factorization.
7.
Define olfactory indicators. Give two common examples of olfactory indicators.
8.
Write the standard form of a quadratic equation whose roots are 2 and -5.
9.
What is meant by 'water of crystallization'? Give the chemical name and formula of washing soda.
10.
Find the value(s) of 'k' for which the quadratic equation 2x2 + kx + 3 = 0 has two real and equal roots.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - Attempt all 5 questions - 3 Marks Each)
11.
A dry pellet of a common base 'B', widely used in soap making, absorbs moisture and turns sticky when kept open in air. This compound is a major byproduct of the chlor-alkali process.
(i) Identify compound 'B'.
(ii) What happens when 'B' is treated with dilute hydrochloric acid? Write the balanced chemical equation for the reaction.
12.
The sum of the reciprocals of Rehman's age (in years) 3 years ago and 5 years from now is 1/3. Find his present age.
13.
Explain the preparation of Plaster of Paris from Gypsum with a balanced chemical equation. Why should Plaster of Paris strictly be stored in a moisture-proof container?
14.
Solve the following quadratic equation for x using an appropriate mathematical method:
3x2 - 2√6x + 2 = 0
15.
Five solutions A, B, C, D and E when tested with universal indicator showed pH values as 4, 1, 11, 7 and 9, respectively.
(a) Which solution is: (i) neutral, (ii) strongly alkaline, (iii) strongly acidic?
(b) Arrange the pH values in increasing order of hydrogen-ion (H+) concentration.
SECTION C (15 Marks)(Long Answer Type - Attempt all 3 questions - 5 Marks Each)
16.
Identify the compound of calcium which is a yellowish-white powder and is heavily used for disinfecting drinking water to make it free of germs.
(a) Write its chemical name and chemical formula. (2 Marks)
(b) How is this compound manufactured? Write the balanced chemical equation for the reaction involved. (2 Marks)
(c) State one major industrial use of this compound other than water purification. (1 Mark)
17.
A motor boat whose speed is 18 km/h in still water takes 1 hour more to go 24 km upstream than to return downstream to the same spot. Formulate the quadratic equation and find the speed of the stream.
18.
Attempt both parts:
(a) Find two consecutive odd positive integers, sum of whose squares is 290. (3 Marks)
(b) Find the roots of the equation:
1⁄x − 1⁄(x - 2) = 3, where x ≠ 0, 2. (2 Marks)
