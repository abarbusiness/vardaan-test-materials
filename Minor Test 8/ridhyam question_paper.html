SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Define sublimation. Give two examples of substances that undergo sublimation.
2.
Define relative density. Why does relative density not have any physical unit?
3.
Add the following algebraic expressions:
(3x² - 2xy) and (-x² + 5xy)
4.
State any two differences between evaporation and boiling.
5.
Multiply the monomial (4a) with the binomial (2a - 3b).
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
Give scientific reasons for the following:
(a) Solids have a fixed shape but liquids do not.
(b) The smell of perfume spreads quickly across a large room.
7.
The mass of a solid lead block is 226 g and its volume is 20 cm³. Calculate its density in:
(a) g/cm³
(b) kg/m³
8.
Subtract the expression (4p² - 5pq + 2q²) from (7p² + 2pq - 3q²).
9.
Explain briefly the procedure to find the density of an irregular solid (e.g., a stone) using a measuring cylinder, assuming the mass of the stone is already known using a beam balance.
10.
Simplify the following algebraic expression:
3x(x - 2y) - 2y(x + 3y) + xy
SECTION C (25 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
(a) State the Kinetic Theory of Matter. (2)
(b) Based on this theory, explain the process of melting of a solid in terms of kinetic energy, intermolecular space, and intermolecular force of attraction. (3)
12.
An empty density bottle weighs 30 g. When filled completely with water, it weighs 80 g. When filled completely with an unknown liquid 'Y', it weighs 75 g. Calculate:
(a) The volume of the density bottle. (2)
(b) The relative density of liquid 'Y'. (2)
(c) The density of liquid 'Y' in kg/m³. (1)
13.
(a) Multiply the binomials (3x - 4y) and (2x + 5y). (3)
(b) Verify your resulting expression by substituting x = 2 and y = -1 in both the original product form and your final expanded answer. (2)
14.
(a) Divide the polynomial (15a³b² - 10a²b³ + 5ab) by the monomial (5ab). (3)
(b) Evaluate the algebraic expression 2x²y - 3xy² + 4 when x = -2 and y = 3. (2)
15.
(a) A solid rectangular block has dimensions 20 cm × 10 cm × 5 cm. If it has a mass of 8 kg, calculate its density strictly in SI units (kg/m³). (3)
(b) How does an increase in temperature affect the intermolecular space and kinetic energy of gas molecules? (2)
