SECTION A (10 Marks)(2 Marks Each)
Q1.
Define direct proportion. Support your definition with one suitable real-life example.
Solution:
Two quantities x and y are said to be in direct proportion if they increase or decrease together in such a way that the ratio of their corresponding values remains constant.
Mathematically, x / y = k (where k is a positive constant). (1 Mark)

Example: The total cost of notebooks and the number of notebooks purchased. As the number of notebooks increases, the total cost increases in the exact same ratio. (1 Mark)
Q2.
Identify whether the following pairs of variables are in direct proportion or inverse proportion:
Solution:
(a) The number of workers and the time taken: Inverse Proportion (More workers = less time). (1 Mark)
(b) The quantity of fabric and the total cost: Direct Proportion (More fabric = higher cost). (1 Mark)
Q3.
If variables x and y vary directly, and x = 10 when y = 15, find x when y = 30.
Solution:
Since x and y vary directly, x1 / y1 = x2 / y2
⇒ 10 / 15 = x / 30 (1 Mark)
⇒ x = (10 × 30) / 15
⇒ x = 20 (1 Mark)
Q4.
Machine fills 840 bottles in 6 hours. Bottles in 5 hours?
Solution:
Let the number of bottles filled in 5 hours be x. Time and number of bottles are in direct proportion.
840 / 6 = x / 5 (1 Mark)
140 = x / 5
x = 140 × 5 = 700 bottles. (1 Mark)
Q5.
State whether x and y vary directly or inversely. Justify mathematically.
Solution:
Checking the product of x and y for each column:
2 × 16 = 32
4 × 8 = 32
8 × 4 = 32
Since x × y = 32 (a constant) for all pairs, x and y vary inversely. (2 Marks)
SECTION B (15 Marks)(3 Marks Each)
Q6.
15 workers in 48 hours. Workers for 30 hours?
Solution:
Let the required number of workers be x.
Number of workers and time taken are in inverse proportion. (1 Mark)
x1 × y1 = x2 × y2
15 × 48 = x × 30 (1 Mark)
x = (15 × 48) / 30
x = 48 / 2 = 24
Answer: 24 workers are required. (1 Mark)
Q7.
14 km in 25 minutes. Distance in 5 hours?
Solution:
First, convert 5 hours to minutes: 5 × 60 = 300 minutes. (1 Mark)
Let the distance covered be x km. Time and distance are in direct proportion.
14 / 25 = x / 300 (1 Mark)
x = (14 × 300) / 25
x = 14 × 12
Answer: The truck travels 168 km. (1 Mark)
Q8.
Model mast = 9 cm, Actual mast = 12 m. Actual length = 28 m. Model length?
Solution:
Let the length of the model ship be x cm.
Note: We can keep the ratio of Model (cm) to Actual (m) directly as long as we are consistent.
Model Mast / Actual Mast = Model Length / Actual Length (1 Mark)
9 / 12 = x / 28 (1 Mark)
x = (9 × 28) / 12 = (3 × 28) / 4 = 3 × 7 = 21
Answer: The model ship is 21 cm long. (1 Mark)
Q9.
2 kg sugar contains 9 × 106 crystals. Crystals in 5 kg?
Solution:
Let the number of crystals in 5 kg be x. Weight and crystals are in direct proportion.
(9 × 106) / 2 = x / 5 (1 Mark)
x = (5 × 9 × 106) / 2
x = 22.5 × 106 (1 Mark)
Standard Scientific Notation: 2.25 × 107 crystals. (1 Mark)
Q10.
25 boxes with 12 bottles each. Boxes needed for 20 bottles each?
Solution:
Let the number of boxes be x.
Number of bottles per box and number of boxes are in inverse proportion (total bottles remain constant). (1 Mark)
25 × 12 = x × 20 (1 Mark)
300 = 20x
x = 300 / 20 = 15
Answer: 15 boxes would be filled. (1 Mark)
SECTION C (25 Marks)(5 Marks Each)
Q11.
Garrison of 1200 men, food for 28 days. 300 leave after 4 days. How long will food last?
Solution:
Step 1: Determine the remaining provisions.
After 4 days, the remaining food is sufficient for the original 1200 men for (28 - 4) = 24 days. (2 Marks)

Step 2: Determine the new number of men.
Men remaining = 1200 - 300 = 900 men. (1 Mark)

Step 3: Apply inverse proportion.
Let the food last for d days for the remaining 900 men.
Number of men × Number of days = Constant
1200 × 24 = 900 × d (1 Mark)
d = (1200 × 24) / 900
d = (12 × 24) / 9 = (4 × 24) / 3 = 4 × 8 = 32
Answer: The remaining food will last for 32 days. (1 Mark)
Q12.
Train speed = 75 km/h. Find distance in 20 min and time for 250 km.
Solution:
(i) Distance in 20 minutes: (2 Marks)
20 minutes = 20 / 60 hours = 1 / 3 hour.
Distance = Speed × Time = 75 × (1 / 3) = 25 km.
(Alternatively, via Direct Proportion: 75 / 60 = x / 20 ⇒ x = 25 km)

(ii) Time to cover 250 km: (3 Marks)
Distance and Time are in direct proportion.
75 / 1 = 250 / t (where t is in hours)
t = 250 / 75 = 10 / 3 hours.
10 / 3 hours = 3 hours and (1/3 × 60) minutes = 3 hours 20 minutes.
Q13.
Pipes and reservoir problem (6 pipes take 1 hr 20 mins, find time for 5 pipes).
Solution:
(i) Time for 5 pipes: (3 Marks)
6 pipes take 1 hr 20 mins = 80 minutes.
Let 5 pipes take y minutes. This is an inverse proportion.
6 × 80 = 5 × y
y = 480 / 5 = 96 minutes.
96 minutes = 1 hour 36 minutes.

(ii) Nature of proportion and equation: (2 Marks)
The variables (number of pipes and time taken) are in Inverse Proportion.
The fundamental equation is xy = k (or x1y1 = x2y2), meaning the product of the two variables remains constant.
Q14.
Sweets distributed among 24 children (5 sweets each). Find share for 20 children.
Solution:
Total number of children initially = 24. Sweets per child = 5.
New number of children = 24 - 4 = 20. (1 Mark)
Let the new number of sweets per child be x.
This is a case of inverse proportion (as children decrease, sweets per child increase, total sweets being constant). (1 Mark)
24 × 5 = 20 × x (1 Mark)
120 = 20x
x = 120 / 20 = 6 sweets. (1 Mark)
Core Mathematical Concept: Inverse variation/proportion. It dictates that if the total quantity to be distributed is fixed, the share per individual is inversely proportional to the total number of individuals receiving the share. (1 Mark)
Q15.
8 periods of 45 mins each. Find duration if periods are increased to 9.
Solution:
Let the duration of the new period be x minutes.
Since the total school hours remain unchanged, the number of periods and the duration of each period are in inverse proportion. (1 Mark)
Number of Periods 8 9
Duration (in mins) 45 x
(1 Mark for table)
Calculation:
8 × 45 = 9 × x (1.5 Marks)
360 = 9x
x = 360 / 9 = 40 minutes. (1.5 Marks)
Answer: Each new period will be 40 minutes long.
