VARDAAN LEARNING INSTITUTE

Minor Test 1
Class: 6 ICSE Subject: Computer & History Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Marks for each section are indicated in the section header.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly, providing examples wherever necessary.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
What is the fundamental difference between 'Prehistory' and 'History'?
2.
Define 'Assembly Language'. Mention the specific type of codes it uses instead of binary numbers.
3.
What do the abbreviations BCE and CE stand for in historical timelines?
4.
Give two practical applications where Supercomputers are primarily used.
5.
Define the term 'Artifacts' in the context of archaeological sources.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
State three distinct differences between a Compiler and an Interpreter.
7.
Why are coins considered a highly reliable archaeological source? Provide three reasons.
8.
Describe the main characteristics of Minicomputers. How do they differ from Microcomputers in terms of usage?
9.
Differentiate between religious literature and secular literature. Provide one appropriate example for each.
10.
What are Fourth Generation Languages (4GL)? Mention any two key features that make them highly user-friendly.
SECTION C (25 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
Explain the four main categories of computers based on their size, speed, and processing power. Provide relevant examples for each category.
12.
Archaeological sources act as silent witnesses to our past. Elaborate on the various types of archaeological sources (like monuments, inscriptions, etc.) and explain their significance in historical reconstruction.
13.
Trace the evolution of Computer Languages from First Generation to Third Generation. Discuss the major advantages of High-Level Languages over Low-Level Languages.
14.
"Literary sources provide a vivid picture of the past." Discuss this statement by explaining the importance of literary sources, and the challenges historians face while interpreting ancient manuscripts.
15.
Answer the following regarding computer translators:
(a)
What is a Language Processor (Translator), and why is it absolutely necessary for a computer to function? (2 Marks)
(b)
How does an Assembler function differently from other language translators? Explain its specific role. (3 Marks)
