Minor Test 04: History & Mathematics
Class: 7 ICSE Subject: History & Maths Time: 1.5 Hours Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly. Show proper working for mathematical problems.
  • History questions are based exclusively on Chapter 1: Rise and Spread of Christianity.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
What is the "Sermon on the Mount"? In which book(s) of the Bible is it recorded?
2.
Simplify the following:   2⅓ × &frac314; ÷ ½
3.
Briefly explain why the Jewish Pharisees and Sadducees opposed Jesus Christ and decided to get rid of him.
4.
Arrange the following fractions in descending order:   ¾, ⅝, &frac712;, ⅔
5.
State the historical significance of the Edict of Thessalonica passed in 380 CE.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
Describe the sequence of events and trials that led to the crucifixion of Jesus Christ under the Roman Governor, Pontius Pilate.
7.
A water drum is ⅔ full. If 50 litres more are required to fill it completely, calculate the total capacity of the drum.
8.
Highlight any three major contributions of Emperor Constantine-I that helped in the spread and establishment of Christianity in the Roman Empire.
9.
After spending ⅗ of his monthly income on household expenses and ⅙ of his income on education, a man is left with ₹2800. What is his total monthly income?
10.
Define the following terms in the context of the early Christian era:
(a) Messiah
(b) Resurrection
(c) Blasphemy
SECTION C (25 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
Analyze the core principles of Jesus Christ's teachings based on the Gospels. Why did his message have such a strong appeal to the common masses during the turbulent Roman period?
12.
Evaluate the following mathematical expression using BODMAS rules:

5½ − [ 2⅓ + { 1¼ − ( ½ × ⅘ ÷ ⅖ ) } ]
13.
The Medieval Period in Europe brought significant socio-cultural changes. Outline the timeline and defining events that mark the beginning and end of this period. Furthermore, explain how the actions of the Apostles, particularly St. Paul and St. Peter, shaped the religious landscape of this era.
14.
In a co-educational school, &frac49; of the total students are boys. If there are 150 more girls than boys, find:
(a) The total number of students in the school.
(b) The exact number of boys and girls separately.
15.
A superfast train covers a distance of 120¾ km in 3½ hours.
(a) Find the uniform speed of the train in km/hr.
(b) If it maintains the exact same speed, how much time will it take to cover a distance of 172½ km?
