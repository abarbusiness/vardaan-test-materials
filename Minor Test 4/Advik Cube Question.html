Minor Test 04
Class: 8 ICSE Topic: Cubes & Cube Roots Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 40
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Section A contains 5 questions of 2 marks each.
  • Section B contains 5 questions of 3 marks each.
  • Section C contains 3 questions of 5 marks each.
  • Show all working steps clearly, especially for prime factorization and estimation.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Find the cube of the following numbers:
(i) $1.2$
(ii) $\frac{2}{5}$
2.
Is $243$ a perfect cube? Give a clear mathematical reason to support your answer.
3.
Find the value of: $\sqrt[3]{216 \times (-343)}$
4.
What is the smallest number by which $256$ must be multiplied to make it a perfect cube?
5.
Evaluate: $\sqrt[3]{0.008} + \sqrt[3]{0.027}$
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
Find the smallest number by which $8788$ must be divided so that the quotient becomes a perfect cube. Also, find the cube root of the quotient.
7.
Find the cube root of $42875$ using the prime factorization method.
8.
The volume of a cubical box is $32.768\text{ m}^3$. Find the length of each edge of the box.
9.
Evaluate and simplify the following expression:
$\sqrt[3]{ \frac{-512}{343} } \times \sqrt[3]{ \frac{125}{729} }$
10.
Three numbers are in the ratio $2 : 3 : 4$. The sum of their cubes is $33957$. Find the three numbers.
SECTION C (15 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
(a) Find the cube root of $175616$ using the estimation method. Explain your steps clearly. (3 Marks)
(b) Evaluate: $\sqrt[3]{27} + \sqrt[3]{0.008} + \sqrt[3]{0.064}$ (2 Marks)
12.
(a) Find the smallest perfect cube which is exactly divisible by $4, 6,$ and $9$. (3 Marks)
(b) If $\sqrt[3]{x} = 2.5$, find the exact value of $x$. (2 Marks)
13.
A solid rectangular metallic block has dimensions $36\text{ cm} \times 24\text{ cm} \times 16\text{ cm}$. It is melted and recast into small cubes, each having an edge of $6\text{ cm}$.
(a) Find the volume of the original rectangular metallic block. (2 Marks)
(b) Find the number of small cubes that can be formed from the block. (3 Marks)
