Is 64 a perfect cube? State the reason and write its cube root if it is. [2 Marks]
Solution:
Yes, 64 is a perfect cube. [1 Mark]

5.
Find the square root of 144 using the prime factorization method. [2 Marks]
Solution:
Prime factorization of 144 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 × 3 [1 Mark]

7.
Express 82 ÷ 23 in exponential form with base 2. [3 Marks]
Solution:
Convert base 8 to base 2: 8 = 23. Therefore, 82 = (23)2 = 26 [1.5 Marks]
Now perform division: 26 ÷ 23 = 26-3 [1 Mark]
= 23 [0.5 Marks]
8.
Find the smallest number by which 48 must be multiplied to make it a perfect square. [3 Marks]
Solution:
Prime factorization of 48 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 [1 Mark]
Grouping in pairs: (2 × 2) × (2 × 2) × 3. Here, 3 is left without a pair. [1 Mark]
Therefore, the smallest number to multiply is 3 to complete the pair. [1 Mark]
9.
Find the cube root of 216 using the prime factorization method. [3 Marks]
Solution:
Prime factorization of 216 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 × 3 × 3 [1.5 Marks]
Making groups of 3 (triplets): (2 × 2 × 2) × (3 × 3 × 3) [0.5 Marks]
Cube root = 2 × 3 = 6 [1 Mark]
10.
Find the value of m if: (-3)m+1 × (-3)5 = (-3)7 [3 Marks]
Solution:
Using ax × ay = ax+y on the LHS:
(-3)(m+1) + 5 = (-3)7 [1 Mark]
(-3)m+6 = (-3)7 [1 Mark]
Since bases are equal, equating the powers:
m + 6 = 7 ⇒ m = 1 [1 Mark]
SECTION C (15 Marks)
11.
Find the square root of 1024 by using the long division method. [5 Marks]
Solution:
Step 1: Group the digits in pairs from right to left: 10 24 [1 Mark]
Step 2: Find largest square ≤ 10. That is 32 = 9. Quotient = 3, Remainder = 10 - 9 = 1. [2 Marks]
Step 3: Bring down the next pair (24). New dividend is 124. Double the divisor: 3 × 2 = 6. [1 Mark]
Step 4: Find a digit 'x' such that 6x × x ≤ 124. Let x = 2. Then 62 × 2 = 124. Remainder is 0. [1 Mark]
Therefore, √1024 = 32.

Apply law am / an = am-n:
= 52 - (-2) × (1/2) × t-4 - (-8) [1 Mark]
= 54 × (1/2) × t4

13.
A rectangular field has an area of 3.6 m2. If its length is 2.4 m:
(i) Find its breadth in decimal form.
(ii) Express the breadth as a fraction in its simplest form.
(iii) Find the square of the breadth. [5 Marks]
Solution:
Area = Length × Breadth
3.6 = 2.4 × Breadth [1 Mark]

(i) Breadth in decimal form:
Breadth = 3.6 / 2.4 = 36 / 24 = 1.5 m [1.5 Marks]

(ii) Breadth as a simple fraction:
1.5 = 15 / 10 = 3 / 2 m [1.5 Marks]

(iii) Square of the breadth:
(1.5)2 = 2.25 OR (3/2)2 = 9/4 m2 [1 Mark]
