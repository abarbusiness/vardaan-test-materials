SECTION A: Electric Dipole & Equilibrium
1.
In vector notation, the electric field E at a point on the equatorial line of a short dipole of dipole moment p at a distance r from its center is given by: (Let k = 1 / 4πε0)
(A) E = 2kp / r3
(B) E = -kp / r3
(C) E = kp / r3
(D) E = -2kp / r3
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (B)
The electric field on the equatorial line of a dipole is antiparallel to the dipole moment vector p. The magnitude is kp / r3. Since the direction is opposite to p, we add a negative sign in vector form: E = -kp / r3.
2.
Three charges +q, +q, and -2q are placed at the vertices of an equilateral triangle of side a. What is the magnitude of the net electric dipole moment of the system?
(A) qa
(B) 2qa
(C) √3 qa
(D) Zero
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (C)
The system can be treated as two distinct dipoles acting simultaneously. The -2q charge is split into two -q charges. One -q is paired with the +q at the second vertex, and the other -q is paired with the +q at the third vertex.
We now have two dipoles, each of magnitude p = qa, separated by an angle of 60° (the interior angle of the equilateral triangle).
Net dipole moment pnet = √(p2 + p2 + 2p·p·cos(60°)) = √(2p2 + 2p2(1/2)) = √(3p2) = √3 p = √3 qa.
3.
An electric dipole is placed parallel to a non-uniform electric field. It will experience:
(A) A net translational force but no torque
(B) A net torque but no translational force
(C) Both a net force and a net torque
(D) Neither force nor torque
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (A)
Because the dipole is parallel to the electric field (θ = 0°), the torque τ = pE sin(0°) = 0. However, since the field is non-uniform, the electric field strength is different at the locations of the +q and -q charges. Consequently, the force F+ = +qE1 will not be cancelled by F- = -qE2, resulting in a net translational force.
4.
What is the work done in rotating a dipole of dipole moment p from an angle of 60° to 180° relative to a uniform electric field E?
(A) pE
(B) 1.5 pE
(C) 2 pE
(D) 0.5 pE
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (B)
The work done by an external agent is equal to the change in potential energy: W = Ufinal - Uinitial.
Potential energy formula: U = -pE cosθ
Uinitial (at 60°) = -pE cos(60°) = -pE(1/2) = -0.5 pE.
Ufinal (at 180°) = -pE cos(180°) = -pE(-1) = +pE.
W = Ufinal - Uinitial = pE - (-0.5 pE) = 1.5 pE.
5.
The exact formula for the magnitude of the electric field on the axis of an electric dipole (charges +q and -q separated by distance 2a) at a distance r from the center is:
(A) E = k(2pr) / (r2 - a2)2
(B) E = kp / (r2 + a2)3/2
(C) E = k(2p) / (r2 + a2)2
(D) E = k(pr) / (r2 - a2)2
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (A)
This is the standard, un-approximated formula for the electric field on the axial line of a dipole. It is derived by taking the sum of the fields from the +q and -q charges: E = E+ - E- = k[q/(r-a)2 - q/(r+a)2]. Simplifying this gives E = k(p·2r) / (r2 - a2)2, where p = q(2a).
6.
If the magnitude of maximum torque acting on a dipole in a uniform electric field E is τmax, then the dipole moment p is:
(A) τmax E
(B) τmax / E
(C) E / τmax
(D) 2τmax / E
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (B)
Torque is defined as τ = pE sinθ. Maximum torque occurs when the dipole is perpendicular to the electric field (θ = 90°). Therefore, τmax = pE sin(90°) = pE. Rearranging the formula yields p = τmax / E.
7.
An electric dipole of moment p is placed in a uniform electric field E. The ratio of its maximum potential energy to its minimum potential energy is:
(A) 1 : 1
(B) -1 : 1
(C) 1 : 2
(D) 2 : 1
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (B)
Potential energy U = -pE cosθ.
Maximum PE occurs at θ = 180° (unstable equilibrium): Umax = -pE(-1) = +pE.
Minimum PE occurs at θ = 0° (stable equilibrium): Umin = -pE(1) = -pE.
Ratio = Umax / Umin = +pE / -pE = -1 : 1.
8.
A graph is plotted showing the variation of electric field magnitude E produced by a short dipole versus the distance r from its center. The shape of the curve will be:
(A) A straight line passing through the origin
(B) A parabola
(C) An inverse square curve (E ∝ 1/r2)
(D) A rapidly decaying curve (E ∝ 1/r3)
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (D)
For a short dipole (r >> a), the electric field magnitude drops off according to an inverse cube law, E ∝ 1/r3. This produces a curve that decays significantly more rapidly than the 1/r2 curve characteristic of an isolated point charge.
9.
Two point charges +8q and -2q are located at x=0 and x=L respectively. The location of a point on the x-axis at which the net electric field is zero due to these two charges is:
(A) 2L
(B) L / 4
(C) 4L
(D) L / 2
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (A)
The null point for opposite charges must lie outside the region between them and closer to the charge with the smaller magnitude (the -2q charge). Let the null point be at distance d to the right of -2q. Its distance from +8q is (L + d).
For net E = 0, magnitudes of fields must be equal: k(8q) / (L + d)2 = k(2q) / d2.
Simplifying: 4 / (L + d)2 = 1 / d2.
Taking the square root: 2 / (L + d) = 1 / d ⇒ 2d = L + d ⇒ d = L.
The position on the x-axis is L + d = L + L = 2L.
10.
A small dipole is undergoing angular simple harmonic motion in a uniform electric field E. If the field strength is increased by a factor of 4, its frequency of oscillation will:
(A) Increase by a factor of 4
(B) Increase by a factor of 2
(C) Decrease by a factor of 2
(D) Remain unchanged
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (B)
The frequency of angular SHM for a dipole is given by f = (1 / 2π) √(pE / I).
This implies that frequency is directly proportional to the square root of the electric field (f ∝ √E).
If E' = 4E, then f' = √(4E) ∝ 2√E = 2f. Thus, the frequency doubles.
SECTION B: Continuous Charge & Gauss's Law
11.
A point charge q is placed precisely at the center of one of the faces of a cube. What is the total electric flux passing through the cube?
(A) q / ε0
(B) q / (2ε0)
(C) q / (6ε0)
(D) q / (8ε0)
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (B)
By placing an identical imaginary cube directly adjacent to the face holding the charge, the charge becomes perfectly enclosed at the center of the newly formed "double-cube" system.
Total flux through the complete Gaussian system = q / ε0.
By symmetry, exactly half of this flux passes through the original single cube. Hence, flux = q / (2ε0).
12.
A closed Gaussian sphere contains 3 identical electric dipoles and one alpha particle. What is the net outward electric flux through the sphere? (Let e be the elementary charge).
(A) 3e / ε0
(B) 2e / ε0
(C) e / ε0
(D) Zero
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (B)
Gauss's law calculates flux based on net enclosed charge. The net charge of any electric dipole (+q and -q) is exactly zero. Three dipoles contribute 0 net charge.
An alpha particle is a helium nucleus, which carries a charge of +2e.
Total enclosed charge = 0 + 0 + 0 + 2e = 2e.
Net outward flux Φ = Qenclosed / ε0 = 2e / ε0.
13.
For a uniformly charged solid non-conducting sphere of radius R, the magnitude of the electric field E at a distance r from the center (where r < R) is proportional to:
(A) r2
(B) 1 / r2
(C) r
(D) Constant (independent of r)
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (C)
For points inside a uniformly charged solid non-conducting sphere (r < R), applying Gauss's Law yields the formula E = [ρ / (3ε0)] · r, or in terms of total charge, E = (kQ/R3) · r.
This shows that the electric field grows linearly from zero at the center, meaning E is directly proportional to r.
14.
A thin circular ring of radius R carries a uniform total charge Q. At what distance x along the axis from the center of the ring is the magnitude of the electric field maximum?
(A) x = R
(B) x = R / 2
(C) x = R / √2
(D) x = 0
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (C)
The electric field along the axis of a charged ring is given by E = kQx / (x2 + R2)3/2.
To find the maximum, we take the derivative of E with respect to x and set it to zero (dE/dx = 0).
Applying the quotient rule and solving yields the critical points at x = ± R / √2. Thus, magnitude is max at x = R / √2.
15.
An infinitely long line charge having a uniform linear charge density λ lies along the axis of a right circular cylinder of length L and radius R. The flux passing through the curved surface of the cylinder is:
(A) λL / ε0
(B) λR / ε0
(C) λL / (2ε0)
(D) Zero
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (A)
By Gauss's Law, the total flux through the entire closed cylinder is Qenclosed / ε0. The charge enclosed inside length L of the cylinder is λL. So, Φtotal = λL / ε0.
The electric field lines of an infinite line charge point radially outward. Therefore, no field lines cross the flat circular end caps (Φcaps = 0). All the flux passes through the curved surface. Hence, Φcurved = λL / ε0.
16.
The dimensional formula for Electric Flux (ΦE) is:
(A) [M L3 T-3 A-1]
(B) [M L2 T-3 A-1]
(C) [M L3 T-2 A-2]
(D) [M L-1 T-2 A1]
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (A)
Electric flux ΦE = E · A.
Electric field E = Force / Charge. Dimensions of Force = [M L T-2], Charge = [A T]. So, E = [M L T-3 A-1].
Area A = [L2].
Multiplying them: ΦE = [M L T-3 A-1] · [L2] = [M L3 T-3 A-1].
17.
A spherical region of radius R has a volume charge density which varies as ρ = ρ0r2, where r is the distance from the center. The total charge enclosed in the sphere is:
(A) πρ0R4
(B) 4πρ0R5 / 5
(C) 4πρ0R3 / 3
(D) πρ0R5
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (B)
We find total charge Q by integrating the volume charge density over the sphere's volume. Using a spherical shell element of radius r and thickness dr, volume dV = 4πr2 dr. Charge in this shell is dQ = ρ dV = (ρ0r2)(4πr2 dr) = 4πρ0r4 dr.
Total Q = ∫0R 4πρ0r4 dr = 4πρ0 [r5 / 5]0R = 4πρ0R5 / 5.
18.
A closed surface encloses a point charge q. If the volume bounded by the surface is reduced to half its original value, the total electric flux coming out of the surface will:
(A) Double
(B) Halve
(C) Increase by 4 times
(D) Remain unchanged
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (D)
According to Gauss's Law, the total electric flux (Φ = qenclosed / ε0) depends entirely and strictly upon the net amount of charge enclosed within the boundary. It is independent of the size, shape, or volume of the bounding surface, provided the enclosed charge remains inside. Thus, flux remains unchanged.
19.
Gauss's law is mathematically valid for ANY closed surface, but it is practically useful for calculating electric fields only when the charge distribution possesses:
(A) Asymmetry
(B) A high degree of symmetry
(C) Non-uniform charge density
(D) Point charges only
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (B)
While Gauss's Law is a universal truth in electromagnetism, pulling the electric field 'E' out of the flux integral (∮ E • dA = E · A) to solve for it requires the electric field magnitude to be constant over the chosen Gaussian surface, and the angle between E and dA to be constant. This is only possible if the charge setup has high symmetry (spherical, cylindrical, or planar).
20.
A metallic shell of radius R is given a total charge Q. The electric field inside the shell is:
(A) Zero everywhere inside
(B) kQ / r2
(C) kQr / R3
(D) Infinite at the center
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (A)
Under electrostatic conditions, any net charge provided to a metallic (conducting) conductor resides entirely on its outer outer surface. Applying Gauss's law with any spherical Gaussian surface drawn inside the shell (r < R) shows that Qenclosed = 0. Therefore, the electric field is zero everywhere inside.
SECTION C: Applications of Gauss's Law
21.
Two infinite parallel plane sheets carry identical uniform positive surface charge densities +σ. What is the magnitude of the electric field in the region strictly between the two sheets?
(A) σ / ε0
(B) 2σ / ε0
(C) σ / (2ε0)
(D) Zero
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (D)
The electric field produced by a single infinite sheet is uniform and has magnitude E = σ / (2ε0), pointing away from a positively charged sheet.
In the region between the sheets, the field from the left sheet points to the right, and the field from the right sheet points to the left. Since they have equal magnitudes and opposite directions, they cancel each other out entirely: Enet = σ / (2ε0) - σ / (2ε0) = 0.
22.
For an isolated, infinite non-conducting plane sheet with a uniform surface charge density σ, the electric field E at a distance x from the sheet is:
(A) Inversely proportional to x
(B) Directly proportional to x
(C) Independent of the distance x
(D) Inversely proportional to x2
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (C)
Applying Gauss's Law to a pillbox-shaped Gaussian surface traversing the sheet gives E · 2A = (σA) / ε0.
Solving for E gives E = σ / (2ε0). Note that the distance parameter 'x' completely cancels out of the equation. This indicates that the electric field produced by an infinite sheet is perfectly uniform and independent of distance.
23.
A hollow conducting sphere of inner radius R1 and outer radius R2 has a net charge of +Q. A point charge +q is placed precisely at its center. What is the surface charge density on the OUTSIDE surface (radius R2)?
(A) Q / (4πR22)
(B) (Q + q) / (4πR22)
(C) q / (4πR22)
(D) (Q - q) / (4πR22)
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (B)
Due to electrostatic induction, the point charge +q at the center induces a charge of -q uniformly distributed over the inner surface of the conducting shell (to ensure E=0 inside the conductor volume).
By conservation of charge, the total charge on the shell must remain +Q. If the inner surface holds -q, the outer surface must hold a charge Qouter such that Qouter + (-q) = +Q.
Therefore, Qouter = Q + q. The surface charge density on the outer surface is σouter = Qouter / Area = (Q + q) / (4πR22).
24.
If a soap bubble is given a negative electric charge, what happens to its radius?
(A) The radius decreases
(B) The radius increases
(C) The radius remains the same
(D) It bursts immediately regardless of charge magnitude
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (B)
When the bubble is given a negative charge, the excess electrons distribute themselves evenly over the spherical surface. Because like charges repel, every part of the surface experiences an outward electrostatic repulsive force (electrostatic pressure) from all other parts. This outward pressure pushes against the surface tension, causing the bubble to expand and its radius to increase. (This is true for both positive and negative charges).
25.
The electric field intensity due to a solid non-conducting uniformly charged sphere of radius R is maximum at:
(A) The center of the sphere (r = 0)
(B) A point outside the sphere (r > R)
(C) The surface of the sphere (r = R)
(D) A point halfway between center and surface (r = R/2)
[4 Marks]
Solution: Correct Option (C)
For a solid non-conducting sphere:
Inside (r < R): E = (kQ/R3)r. The field increases linearly as you move from the center outward.
Outside (r > R): E = kQ/r2. The field decreases as an inverse square as you move away.
The peak occurs precisely at the boundary where these two functional dependencies meet: at the surface of the sphere (r = R).
