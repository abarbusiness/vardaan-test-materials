[2 Marks] Q1. Given that HCF (306, 657) = 9, find the LCM (306, 657).
We know that for any two positive integers a and b:
HCF(a, b) × LCM(a, b) = a × b (1 Mark)
9 × LCM = 306 × 657
LCM = (306 × 657) / 9
LCM = 34 × 657 = 22338 (1 Mark)
Answer: 22338

[2 Marks] Q3. Find the zeros of the quadratic polynomial p(x) = x2 - 2x - 8.
To find zeros, set p(x) = 0.
x2 - 2x - 8 = 0
Splitting the middle term: x2 - 4x + 2x - 8 = 0 (1 Mark)
x(x - 4) + 2(x - 4) = 0
(x - 4)(x + 2) = 0
x = 4 or x = -2
Zeros are: 4 and -2 (1 Mark)

Let the zeros be α and β.
Given: α + β = -3 and αβ = 2 (½ Mark)
The quadratic polynomial is given by: k[x2 - (α + β)x + αβ] (1 Mark)
= k[x2 - (-3)x + 2]
= k[x2 + 3x + 2]
Required Polynomial (for k=1): x2 + 3x + 2 (½ Mark)

[3 Marks] Q6. Prove that 3 + 2√5 is an irrational number, given that √5 is an irrational number.
Let us assume, to the contrary, that 3 + 2√5 is rational.
Then we can find coprime integers 'a' and 'b' (b ≠ 0) such that:
3 + 2√5 = a/b (1 Mark)
Rearranging the equation:
2√5 = (a/b) - 3
2√5 = (a - 3b) / b
√5 = (a - 3b) / 2b (1 Mark)
Since 'a' and 'b' are integers, (a - 3b)/2b is a rational number, which means √5 is rational.
But this contradicts the given fact that √5 is irrational. (½ Mark)
This contradiction arose due to our incorrect assumption.
Hence, 3 + 2√5 is an irrational number. (½ Mark)

[3 Marks] Q9. Solve by substitution method: x + y = 14, x - y = 4
Let x + y = 14 --- (Eq 1)
Let x - y = 4 --- (Eq 2)
From Eq 2, x = 4 + y (1 Mark)
Substitute the value of x in Eq 1:
(4 + y) + y = 14
4 + 2y = 14
2y = 10 &implies; y = 5 (1 Mark)
Substitute y = 5 in x = 4 + y:
x = 4 + 5 = 9
x = 9, y = 5 (1 Mark)
[3 Marks] Q10. Solve by elimination method: 3x + 4y = 10, 2x - 2y = 2
3x + 4y = 10 --- (Eq 1)
2x - 2y = 2 --- (Eq 2)
Multiply Eq 2 by 2 to equate the coefficients of y:
4x - 4y = 4 --- (Eq 3) (1 Mark)
Add Eq 1 and Eq 3:
(3x + 4x) + (4y - 4y) = 10 + 4
7x = 14 &implies; x = 2 (1 Mark)
Substitute x = 2 in Eq 2:
2(2) - 2y = 2 &implies; 4 - 2y = 2 &implies; 2y = 2 &implies; y = 1
x = 2, y = 1 (1 Mark)

[5 Marks] Q11. Prove that √3 is an irrational number.
Let us assume, to the contrary, that √3 is rational.
Then, √3 = a/b, where 'a' and 'b' are coprime integers and b ≠ 0. (1 Mark)
Squaring both sides:
3 = a2 / b2
3b2 = a2 --- (Eq 1) (1 Mark)
This shows that a2 is divisible by 3.
According to the fundamental theorem of arithmetic, if a prime number 'p' divides a2, then 'p' also divides 'a'. Therefore, 'a' is divisible by 3. (1 Mark)
Let a = 3c for some integer c.
Substitute a = 3c in Eq 1:
3b2 = (3c)2
3b2 = 9c2
b2 = 3c2 (1 Mark)
This means b2 is divisible by 3, and hence 'b' is divisible by 3.
Therefore, 'a' and 'b' have at least 3 as a common factor.
But this contradicts the fact that 'a' and 'b' are coprime.
This contradiction has arisen because of our incorrect assumption.
So, we conclude that √3 is irrational. (1 Mark)
[5 Marks] Q12. Five years hence, the age of Jacob will be three times that of his son. Five years ago, Jacob's age was seven times that of his son. What are their present ages?
Let the present age of Jacob be x years and his son be y years. (1 Mark)
Case 1: Five years hence (future)
Jacob's age = (x + 5), Son's age = (y + 5)
x + 5 = 3(y + 5)
x + 5 = 3y + 15 &implies; x - 3y = 10 --- (Eq 1) (1 Mark)
Case 2: Five years ago (past)
Jacob's age = (x - 5), Son's age = (y - 5)
x - 5 = 7(y - 5)
x - 5 = 7y - 35 &implies; x - 7y = -30 --- (Eq 2) (1 Mark)
Subtract Eq 2 from Eq 1:
(x - 3y) - (x - 7y) = 10 - (-30)
4y = 40 &implies; y = 10 (1 Mark)
Substitute y = 10 in Eq 1:
x - 3(10) = 10 &implies; x - 30 = 10 &implies; x = 40
Jacob's present age = 40 years, Son's present age = 10 years. (1 Mark)

For Equation 2: x - y = 2

Points to plot: If x = 0, y = -2 &implies; (0, -2). If y = 0, x = 2 &implies; (2, 0). (1.5 Marks)
Graph Plotting: Plot the points on a graph paper and draw two straight lines. (1 Mark for drawing lines correctly)
The two lines intersect at a specific point on the graph.
From the graph (or by solving mentally: x+y=4, x-y=2 &implies; 2x=6 &implies; x=3, y=1), the intersection point is (3, 1).
The solution is x = 3, y = 1. (1 Mark)
