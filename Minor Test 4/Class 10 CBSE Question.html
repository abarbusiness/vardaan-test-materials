Minor Test 04
Class: 10 CBSE Subject: Mathematics Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 40
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Section A comprises 5 questions of 2 marks each.
  • Section B comprises 5 questions of 3 marks each.
  • Section C comprises 3 questions of 5 marks each.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly. Show rough work clearly.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Given that HCF (306, 657) = 9, find the LCM (306, 657).
2.
Express 156 as a product of its prime factors.
3.
Find the zeros of the quadratic polynomial p(x) = x2 - 2x - 8.
4.
Find a quadratic polynomial whose sum and product of zeros are -3 and 2, respectively.
5.
Find the value of 'k' for which the following pair of linear equations has infinitely many solutions:
2x + 3y = 7
4x + ky = 14
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
Prove that 3 + 2√5 is an irrational number, given that √5 is an irrational number.
7.
There is a circular path around a sports field. Sonia takes 18 minutes to drive one round of the field, while Ravi takes 12 minutes for the same. Suppose they both start at the same point and at the same time, and go in the same direction. After how many minutes will they meet again at the starting point?
8.
Find the zeros of the quadratic polynomial 4s2 - 4s + 1 and verify the relationship between the zeros and the coefficients.
9.
Solve the following pair of linear equations using the substitution method:
x + y = 14
x - y = 4
10.
Solve the following pair of linear equations using the elimination method:
3x + 4y = 10
2x - 2y = 2
SECTION C (15 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
Prove that √3 is an irrational number.
12.
Five years hence, the age of Jacob will be three times that of his son. Five years ago, Jacob's age was seven times that of his son. What are their present ages?
13.
Solve the following system of linear equations graphically:
x + y = 4
x - y = 2
Also, find the coordinates of the point where the lines intersect.
