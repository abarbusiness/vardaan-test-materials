Minor Test 04
Class: 8 ICSE Topic: Squares & Square Roots Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 40
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Section A contains 5 questions of 2 marks each.
  • Section B contains 5 questions of 3 marks each.
  • Section C contains 3 questions of 5 marks each.
  • Show all working steps clearly, especially for long division and prime factorization.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Find the square of the following numbers:
(i) $1.5$
(ii) $\frac{3}{7}$
2.
Using the properties of squares, evaluate the value of $35^2 - 34^2$ without actual multiplication.
3.
Find the square root of $441$ using the prime factorization method.
4.
Is $1000$ a perfect square? Give a clear reason to support your answer.
5.
Evaluate: $\sqrt{1.44} \times \sqrt{0.81}$
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
Find the smallest number by which $2940$ must be multiplied to make it a perfect square.
7.
Find the square root of $1444$ using the long division method.
8.
Find the least number that must be subtracted from $4000$ to make it a perfect square.
9.
A General wishes to arrange his $3650$ soldiers in the form of a solid square. After arranging them, he found that some soldiers were left out. Find the number of soldiers left out and the number of soldiers in the front row.
10.
Simplify and find the value of: $\sqrt{45} \times \sqrt{20}$
SECTION C (15 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
(a) Find the least number that must be added to $1750$ so as to get a perfect square. Also, find the square root of the obtained number. (3 Marks)
(b) Write a Pythagorean triplet whose smallest member is $8$. (2 Marks)
12.
Find the square root of $2$ correct to two decimal places using the long division method.
13.
The area of a rectangular field is $2450\text{ m}^2$. If the length of the field is twice its breadth, find the perimeter of the field.
