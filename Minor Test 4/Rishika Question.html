Minor Test 04
Class: 7 CBSE Subject: Science (Acids, Bases & Salts) Time: 1.5 Hours Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
What are organic acids? Give any one example of an organic acid.
2.
Name any two naturally occurring indicators used to test whether a substance is acidic or basic.
3.
What happens when an acid reacts with a base? What is this chemical reaction called?
4.
State any two general properties of salts.
5.
Define alkalies. Give one example of an alkali.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
Differentiate between mineral acids and organic acids. Give one example of each.
7.
Classify the following salts as neutral, acidic, or basic salts:
(a) Sodium chloride (NaCl)
(b) Aluminium chloride (AlCl3)
(c) Sodium acetate (CH3COONa)
8.
Identify the organic acid present in the following items:
(a) Tamarind
(b) Vinegar
(c) Sour milk
9.
Write the complete chemical equations for the following neutralisation reactions:
(a) Sodium hydroxide reacts with hydrochloric acid.
(b) Magnesium hydroxide reacts with nitric acid.
10.
What are antacids? How do they help in curing uneasiness and pain in the stomach?
SECTION C (25 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
(a) Define an indicator.
(b) How do the following indicators change colour in an acidic medium and a basic medium?
(i) Litmus paper
(ii) Phenolphthalein
12.
Give scientific reasons for the following observations:
(a) We are advised never to taste any unknown substance at home or in school.
(b) A turmeric stain on a white shirt turns red when washed with soap.
(c) Dilute acids are formed by adding water to highly concentrated acids.
13.
Write the general names given to the salts formed by the following acids:
(a) Sulphuric acid (H2SO4)
(b) Hydrochloric acid (HCl)
(c) Nitric acid (HNO3)
(d) Carbonic acid (H2CO3)
(e) Acetic acid (CH3COOH)
14.
Describe an activity to show that solutions of salts in water can conduct electricity. Include the materials required and the observation.
15.
(a) "All alkalies are bases but all bases are not alkalies." Justify this statement.
(b) Give two examples of strong bases and two examples of weak bases.
