SECTION B (15 Marks)
Q6. Describe the sequence of events and trials that led to the crucifixion of Jesus Christ under Pontius Pilate. [3 Marks]
Answer:
  • The Pharisees and Sadducees arrested Jesus and brought him before the Chief Priest.
  • When asked if he was the Christ (Messiah), Jesus affirmed it, leading the Chief Priest to charge him with blasphemy and demand a death sentence.
  • He was brought before Pontius Pilate, the Roman Governor. Pilate believed Jesus should be set free, but the crowds, stirred up by the religious leaders, demanded his crucifixion.
  • Pilate eventually gave in to the crowd's demand. Jesus was mocked, flogged, and nailed to a cross until he died of suffocation and heart failure.
Q7. A water drum is ⅔ full. If 50 litres more are required to fill it completely, calculate the total capacity of the drum. [3 Marks]
Solution:
Let the total capacity of the drum be 'x' litres.
Volume currently filled = (2/3) of x = 2x/3
Volume remaining to be filled = x - (2x/3) = x/3
According to the question, the remaining volume is 50 litres.
Therefore, x/3 = 50
x = 50 × 3 = 150
Ans: The total capacity of the drum is 150 litres.

Q9. After spending ⅗ of his monthly income on household expenses and ⅙ of his income on education, a man is left with ₹2800. What is his total monthly income? [3 Marks]
Solution:
Let total income be ₹ 'x'.
Total fraction of income spent = 3/5 + 1/6
LCM of 5 and 6 is 30.
Fraction spent = 18/30 + 5/30 = 23/30
Fraction of income left = 1 - 23/30 = 7/30
We are given that the left amount is ₹2800.
Therefore, (7/30) of x = 2800
x = (2800 × 30) / 7
x = 400 × 30 = 12000
Ans: His total monthly income is ₹12,000.
Q10. Define the following terms in the context of the early Christian era: (a) Messiah (b) Resurrection (c) Blasphemy [3 Marks]
Answer:
  • Messiah: A person who saves someone or something from danger or difficulty; the chosen one prophesied in the Old Testament to save the Jews.
  • Resurrection: The rising from the dead of a divine or human being who still retains his own personhood (celebrated as Easter).
  • Blasphemy: The action or offence of speaking sacrilegiously about God or sacred things (the charge laid against Jesus).
SECTION C (25 Marks)
Q11. Analyze the core principles of Jesus Christ's teachings based on the Gospels. Why did his message have such a strong appeal to the common masses during the turbulent Roman period? [5 Marks]
Answer:
Core Principles: His teachings, found in the Gospels (Matthew, Mark, Luke, John), were based on two main principles: to love God with all one's heart, soul, and mind, and to love one's neighbor as oneself. In his Sermon on the Mount, he preached that God is one supreme power, treated all as equal, and renounced hatred. He urged people to forgive and love their enemies, be humble, merciful, and help the poor and sick.

Appeal to the Masses: His message was deeply appealing during the challenging and conflicted Roman times because it offered peace and hope. Unlike other religious leaders of the era, Jesus welcomed everyone equally—from the wealthy to the poor, and the healthy to the sick. His acts of healing and kindness (miracles), coupled with his message of brotherhood, provided comfort and a sense of community to people suffering under rigid societal rules and Roman authority.

Q13. Outline the timeline of the Medieval Period in Europe. Explain how the actions of the Apostles, particularly St. Paul and St. Peter, shaped the religious landscape of this era. [5 Marks]
Answer:
Timeline: The Medieval Period (or Middle Ages) in Europe was a long period of history spanning from 500 CE to 1500 CE. It began around 476 CE with the fall of Romulus Augustus, the last emperor of the Western Roman Empire, and continued until the rise of the Ottoman Empire (the fall of the Eastern Roman Empire).

Role of Apostles: After Jesus' crucifixion and resurrection, his followers were convinced he was the Messiah. The Apostles carried forward his legacy:
  • St. Paul was the most influential person in spreading Christianity. He preached that only with the help of Christ could one live a life of kindness, peace, and joy to reach heaven.
  • St. Peter played a significant role by converting Jews to Christians and spreading the message of love and brotherhood around Jerusalem and Palestine, eventually being executed by Emperor Nero for his faith. Their tireless efforts laid the deep religious foundation for medieval European society.
Q14. In a school, &frac49; of the total students are boys. If there are 150 more girls than boys, find: (a) The total number of students. (b) The number of boys and girls separately. [5 Marks]
Solution:
Let the total number of students in the school be 'x'.
Fraction of boys = 4/9 of x = 4x/9
Fraction of girls = Total - Boys = 1 - 4/9 = 5/9
Number of girls = 5x/9

According to the condition given:
Number of girls − Number of boys = 150
(5x/9) − (4x/9) = 150
x/9 = 150
x = 150 × 9 = 1350

(a) Total number of students = 1350
(b) Number of boys = (4/9) × 1350 = 4 × 150 = 600
(b) Number of girls = (5/9) × 1350 = 5 × 150 = 750 (Verify: 750 - 600 = 150)
Ans: (a) Total Students = 1350; (b) Boys = 600, Girls = 750
Q15. A train covers a distance of 120¾ km in 3½ hours. (a) Find its speed. (b) How much time will it take to cover 172½ km at the same speed? [5 Marks]
Solution:
Part (a): Find Speed
Distance (D) = 120¾ km = 483/4 km
Time (T) = 3½ hours = 7/2 hours
Speed = Distance ÷ Time
Speed = (483/4) ÷ (7/2)
Speed = (483/4) × (2/7)
Speed = (483 × 2) / (4 × 7)
Simplify: 483 / 7 = 69, and 2 / 4 = 1/2.
Speed = 69 / 2 = 34½ km/hr

Part (b): Find New Time
New Distance = 172½ km = 345/2 km
Speed = 69/2 km/hr
New Time = Distance ÷ Speed
New Time = (345/2) ÷ (69/2)
New Time = (345/2) × (2/69)
New Time = 345 / 69 = 5
Ans: (a) Speed = 34½ km/hr (b) Time taken = 5 hours
