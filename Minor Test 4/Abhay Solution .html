SECTION A
[2 Marks] Q1. Simplify: x5 × x-3 × x2
Using the product law of indices (am × an = am+n):
= x5 + (-3) + 2 (1 Mark)
= x5 - 3 + 2
= x4
Answer: x4 (1 Mark)
[2 Marks] Q2. Evaluate: (81)1/4
Expressing 81 as a power of its prime factors: 81 = 34 (1 Mark)
= (34)1/4
Using the power law (am)n = amn:
= 34 × (1/4) = 31
Answer: 3 (1 Mark)
[2 Marks] Q3. Evaluate: (2x2y3)0 + 30
Using the zero index law (a0 = 1 for a ≠ 0):
(2x2y3)0 = 1 (½ Mark)
30 = 1 (½ Mark)
Therefore, 1 + 1 = 2 (1 Mark)
Answer: 2

So, 2x = 24

Answer: x = 4 (1 Mark)

[3 Marks] Q7. Solve for x: 32x + 1 = 27
Expressing 27 as a power of 3: 27 = 33 (1 Mark)
32x + 1 = 33
Equating the exponents since the bases are equal:
2x + 1 = 3 (1 Mark)
2x = 3 - 1
2x = 2
x = 1
Answer: x = 1 (1 Mark)
[3 Marks] Q8. Simplify: (xa / xb)a+b
Using quotient law inside the bracket: xa / xb = xa - b (1 Mark)
The expression becomes: (xa - b)a + b
Using the power law: x(a - b)(a + b) (1 Mark)
Applying the algebraic identity (a - b)(a + b) = a2 - b2:
Answer: xa2 - b2 (1 Mark)
[3 Marks] Q9. Evaluate: (64/25)-3/2
First, handle the negative sign in the exponent by taking the reciprocal of the base:
= (25/64)3/2 (1 Mark)
Expressing 25 and 64 as squares: 25 = 52, 64 = 82
= ((5/8)2)3/2 (1 Mark)
Multiply exponents: (5/8)2 × (3/2) = (5/8)3
= 53 / 83 = 125 / 512
Answer: 125/512 (1 Mark)
[3 Marks] Q10. Simplify: (3n × 9n+1) / 27n
Express all bases as powers of 3: 9 = 32 and 27 = 33 (1 Mark)
= [3n × (32)n+1] / (33)n
= [3n × 32n+2] / 33n (1 Mark)
Apply product law in numerator: 3n + 2n + 2 / 33n
= 33n+2 / 33n
Apply quotient law: 3(3n + 2) - 3n
= 32 = 9
Answer: 9 (1 Mark)
SECTION C
[5 Marks] Q11. Evaluate: (81/16)-3/4 × (25/9)-3/2 ÷ (5/2)-3
Step 1: Reciprocate bases to remove negative exponents.
= (16/81)3/4 × (9/25)3/2 ÷ (2/5)3 (1.5 Marks)
Step 2: Express bases as powers.
16/81 = (2/3)4
9/25 = (3/5)2
= ((2/3)4)3/4 × ((3/5)2)3/2 ÷ (2/5)3 (1.5 Marks)
Step 3: Simplify exponents.
= (2/3)3 × (3/5)3 ÷ (2/5)3 (1 Mark)
= (8/27) × (27/125) ÷ (8/125)
Step 4: Multiply and divide fractions.
= (8/27) × (27/125) × (125/8)
All terms cancel out cleanly.
Answer: 1 (1 Mark)
[5 Marks] Q12. Solve for x: (a) 52x - 1 = 125   (b) 2x - 5 = 1
Part (a): 52x - 1 = 125 [2.5 Marks total for a]
Express 125 as a power of 5: 125 = 53 (1 Mark)
52x - 1 = 53
Equating exponents: 2x - 1 = 3
2x = 4 &implies; x = 2 (1.5 Marks)

Part (b): 2x - 5 = 1 [2.5 Marks total for b]
Any non-zero number to the power of 0 is 1. Therefore, 1 = 20 (1 Mark)
2x - 5 = 20
Equating exponents: x - 5 = 0
x = 5 (1.5 Marks)
Final Answers: (a) x = 2, (b) x = 5
[5 Marks] Q13. Simplify: (2n+1 + 2n) / (2n+1 - 2n)
Expand the terms using the product law backwards: am+n = am × an
2n+1 = 2n × 21 (1 Mark)
Substitute this back into the expression:
= [ (2n × 2) + 2n ] / [ (2n × 2) - 2n ] (1 Mark)
Take 2n common from both numerator and denominator:
= [ 2n(2 + 1) ] / [ 2n(2 - 1) ] (2 Marks)
Cancel out 2n from numerator and denominator:
= (2 + 1) / (2 - 1)
= 3 / 1
Answer: 3 (1 Mark)
