Ans 11.
(a) Estimation Method:
Step 1: Group the number in 3s from right to left: $175$ and $616$. (1 Mark)
Step 2: Take the first group $616$. It ends in $6$. Since $6^3 = 216$ (ends in 6), the unit digit of the cube root must be $6$. (1 Mark)
Step 3: Take the second group $175$. We know $5^3 = 125$ and $6^3 = 216$. Since $125 < 175 < 216$, the ten's digit must be $5$.
Therefore, $\sqrt[3]{175616} = 56$. (1 Mark)

(b) Evaluate Expression:
$\sqrt[3]{27} = 3$
$\sqrt[3]{0.008} = 0.2$
$\sqrt[3]{0.064} = 0.4$ (1 Mark)
Sum $= 3 + 0.2 + 0.4 = 3.6$ (1 Mark)
Ans 12.
(a) Smallest Perfect Cube Divisible by 4, 6, 9:
First, find the LCM of $4, 6,$ and $9$.
LCM $= 36$. (1 Mark)
Prime factorization of $36 = 2^2 \times 3^2$.
To make this a perfect cube, we need to complete the triplets for each prime factor. We must multiply by $2^1 \times 3^1 = 6$. (1 Mark)
Smallest perfect cube $= 36 \times 6 = 216$. (1 Mark)

(b) Finding $x$:
Given $\sqrt[3]{x} = 2.5$
Cubing both sides: $x = (2.5)^3$ (1 Mark)
$x = 2.5 \times 2.5 \times 2.5 = 15.625$. (1 Mark)
