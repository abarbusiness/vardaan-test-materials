SECTION A: Electric Dipole & Equilibrium (40 Marks) (Questions 1 to 10 - 4 Marks Each)
1.
In vector notation, the electric field E at a point on the equatorial line of a short dipole of dipole moment p at a distance r from its center is given by: (Let k = 1 / 4πε0)
(A) E = 2kp / r3
(B) E = -kp / r3
(C) E = kp / r3
(D) E = -2kp / r3
2.
Three charges +q, +q, and -2q are placed at the vertices of an equilateral triangle of side a. What is the magnitude of the net electric dipole moment of the system?
(A) qa
(B) 2qa
(C) √3 qa
(D) Zero
3.
An electric dipole is placed parallel to a non-uniform electric field. It will experience:
(A) A net translational force but no torque
(B) A net torque but no translational force
(C) Both a net force and a net torque
(D) Neither force nor torque
4.
What is the work done in rotating a dipole of dipole moment p from an angle of 60° to 180° relative to a uniform electric field E?
(A) pE
(B) 1.5 pE
(C) 2 pE
(D) 0.5 pE
5.
The exact formula for the magnitude of the electric field on the axis of an electric dipole (charges +q and -q separated by distance 2a) at a distance r from the center is:
(A) E = k(2pr) / (r2 - a2)2
(B) E = kp / (r2 + a2)3/2
(C) E = k(2p) / (r2 + a2)2
(D) E = k(pr) / (r2 - a2)2
6.
If the magnitude of maximum torque acting on a dipole in a uniform electric field E is τmax, then the dipole moment p is:
(A) τmax E
(B) τmax / E
(C) E / τmax
(D) 2τmax / E
7.
An electric dipole of moment p is placed in a uniform electric field E. The ratio of its maximum potential energy to its minimum potential energy is:
(A) 1 : 1
(B) -1 : 1
(C) 1 : 2
(D) 2 : 1
8.
A graph is plotted showing the variation of electric field magnitude E produced by a short dipole versus the distance r from its center. The shape of the curve will be:
(A) A straight line passing through the origin
(B) A parabola
(C) An inverse square curve (E ∝ 1/r2)
(D) A rapidly decaying curve (E ∝ 1/r3)
9.
Two point charges +8q and -2q are located at x=0 and x=L respectively. The location of a point on the x-axis at which the net electric field is zero due to these two charges is:
(A) 2L
(B) L / 4
(C) 4L
(D) L / 2
10.
A small dipole is undergoing angular simple harmonic motion in a uniform electric field E. If the field strength is increased by a factor of 4, its frequency of oscillation will:
(A) Increase by a factor of 4
(B) Increase by a factor of 2
(C) Decrease by a factor of 2

SECTION B: Continuous Charge & Gauss's Law (40 Marks) (Questions 11 to 20 - 4 Marks Each)
11.
A point charge q is placed precisely at the center of one of the faces of a cube. What is the total electric flux passing through the cube?
(A) q / ε0
(B) q / (2ε0)
(C) q / (6ε0)
(D) q / (8ε0)
12.
A closed Gaussian sphere contains 3 identical electric dipoles and one alpha particle. What is the net outward electric flux through the sphere? (Let e be the elementary charge).
(A) 3e / ε0
(B) 2e / ε0
(C) e / ε0
(D) Zero
13.
For a uniformly charged solid non-conducting sphere of radius R, the magnitude of the electric field E at a distance r from the center (where r < R) is proportional to:
(A) r2
(B) 1 / r2
(C) r
(D) Constant (independent of r)
14.
A thin circular ring of radius R carries a uniform total charge Q. At what distance x along the axis from the center of the ring is the magnitude of the electric field maximum?
(A) x = R
(B) x = R / 2
(C) x = R / √2
(D) x = 0
15.
An infinitely long line charge having a uniform linear charge density λ lies along the axis of a right circular cylinder of length L and radius R. The flux passing through the curved surface of the cylinder is:
(A) λL / ε0
(B) λR / ε0
(C) λL / (2ε0)
(D) Zero
16.
The dimensional formula for Electric Flux (ΦE) is:
(A) [M L3 T-3 A-1]
(B) [M L2 T-3 A-1]
(C) [M L3 T-2 A-2]
(D) [M L-1 T-2 A1]
17.
A spherical region of radius R has a volume charge density which varies as ρ = ρ0r2, where r is the distance from the center. The total charge enclosed in the sphere is:
(A) πρ0R4
(B) 4πρ0R5 / 5
(C) 4πρ0R3 / 3
(D) πρ0R5
18.
A closed surface encloses a point charge q. If the volume bounded by the surface is reduced to half its original value, the total electric flux coming out of the surface will:
(A) Double
(B) Halve
(C) Increase by 4 times
(D) Remain unchanged
19.

(A) Asymmetry

20.

(A) Zero everywhere inside

SECTION C: Applications of Gauss's Law (20 Marks) (Questions 21 to 25 - 4 Marks Each)
21.

(A) σ / ε0
(B) 2σ / ε0
(C) σ / (2ε0)
(D) Zero
22.
For an isolated, infinite non-conducting plane sheet with a uniform surface charge density σ, the electric field E at a distance x from the sheet is:
(A) Inversely proportional to x
(B) Directly proportional to x
(C) Independent of the distance x
(D) Inversely proportional to x2
23.
A hollow conducting sphere of inner radius R1 and outer radius R2 has a net charge of +Q. A point charge +q is placed precisely at its center. What is the surface charge density on the OUTSIDE surface (radius R2)?
(A) Q / (4πR22)
(B) (Q + q) / (4πR22)
(C) q / (4πR22)
(D) (Q - q) / (4πR22)
24.
If a soap bubble is given a negative electric charge, what happens to its radius?
(A) The radius decreases
(B) The radius increases
(C) The radius remains the same
(D) It bursts immediately regardless of charge magnitude
25.

(A) The center of the sphere (r = 0)
(B) A point outside the sphere (r > R)
(C) The surface of the sphere (r = R)
(D) A point halfway between center and surface (r = R/2)
