Ans 12.
Fuel: Materials that produce heat energy on burning in air are called fuels. (1 Mark)

Reasons gaseous fuels are better (Any 4 points - 1 Mark each):
1. They can be easily supplied in cylinders or through pipelines, lowering transportation costs.
2. They release a large amount of energy (high calorific value) and leave no ash or solid residue behind.
3. They produce the least amount of harmful gases compared to solid and liquid fuels.
4. They can be easily ignited even with a small spark.
5. Their rate of combustion can be easily controlled using a control valve.
Ans 13.
Health Hazards: Burning of fuels causes health hazards because it releases pollutants that contaminate the air we breathe and harms the environment. (1 Mark)

Harmful Effects (Any 4 points - 1 Mark each):
1. Release of unburnt carbon particles (from wood, coal, etc.) causes severe breathing problems like asthma and forms smog in winter.
2. Incomplete combustion produces carbon monoxide, a highly poisonous gas that can prove fatal if inhaled.
3. Combustion releases carbon dioxide. Excess CO2 traps heat and causes Global Warming, leading to melting glaciers and floods.
4. Burning coal and diesel releases suffocating sulphur dioxide gas, which dissolves in rain to form 'acid rain', damaging buildings, plants, and trees.
