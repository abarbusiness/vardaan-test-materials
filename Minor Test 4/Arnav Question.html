Minor Test 04
Class: 8 CBSE Subject: Science Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 40
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Section A contains 5 questions of 2 marks each.
  • Section B contains 5 questions of 3 marks each.
  • Section C contains 3 questions of 5 marks each.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Define the term 'combustible material'? Give two examples of combustible materials.
2.
State the condition under which 'slow combustion' of a material takes place. Give one example.
3.
Define 'calorific value of a fuel'.
4.
State any two characteristics of an ideal fuel.
5.
Why is water not used in controlling electrical fires?
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
State the three conditions that are needed for combustion to take place.
7.
Fill in the blanks:
(a) A good fuel should have its ignition temperature ____________ the room temperature.
(b) The ____________ zone of a candle flame is the coldest.
(c) During incomplete combustion of a fuel, ____________ gas is formed.
8.
State True or False for the following statements:
(a) The outermost part of a candle flame contains unburnt carbon particles.
(b) All solid fuels have a higher calorific value than liquid and gaseous fuels.
(c) Sulphur dioxide gas, produced during combustion of fossil fuels, causes acid rain.
9.
Calorific value of wood is 18,000 kJ/kg. How much of wood is required to produce 360,000 kJ of heat energy? Show your calculation.
10.
Select the correct option for the following:
(a) An example of a non-combustible substance is: (petrol / coal / wood / iron)
(b) A fuel starts burning only when it is heated to: (its boiling point / its ignition temperature / its melting point)
(c) The highest temperature zone of a candle flame is its: (red zone / yellow zone / blue zone)
SECTION C (15 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
Draw a diagram showing the different zones of a candle flame. Label the zones which contain:
(a) unburnt vapours of wax.
(b) unburnt carbon particles.
12.
What is a fuel? Why are gaseous fuels considered better than liquid or solid fuels? Give any four reasons to support your answer.
13.
Why do we say that 'burning of fuels' can cause 'health hazards'? Describe any four harmful effects of burning fuels on the environment and human health.
