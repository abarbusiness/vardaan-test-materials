SECTION A (15 Marks) (Objective / Very Short Answer Type)
1.
Draw the standard electrical circuit symbols for the following components: [5]
(a) Battery of two cells
(b) Electric Bulb
(c) Switch in the 'OFF' position
(d) Connecting wire
(e) Electric Fuse
2.
Answer the following questions based on the measurement of motion:
(a) Differentiate between Uniform motion and Non-uniform motion with one example of each. [2]
(b) A train covers a distance of $120\text{ km}$ in $2.5\text{ hours}$. Calculate the average speed of the train in $\text{km/h}$ and convert it into $\text{m/s}$. [3]
3.
Provide concise answers for the following:
(a) Name the metal used for making the filament of an electric bulb and give one reason for its use. [2]
(b) Define the 'Time Period' of a simple pendulum. [1]
(c) A simple pendulum takes $36\text{ s}$ to complete $30$ oscillations. What is the time period of the pendulum? [2]
SECTION B (15 Marks) (Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
4.
Sketch the shape of the distance-time graph for the following cases:
(i) A car moving with a constant (uniform) speed.
(ii) A car parked on a side road (at rest).
(iii) A car moving with a non-uniform speed. [3]
5.
Miniature Circuit Breakers (MCBs) are increasingly being used in modern households.
(a) What does MCB stand for?
(b) State the principle on which it works.
(c) Why is it preferred over traditional electric fuses? [3]
6.
The odometer of a car reads $1048\text{ km}$ when the clock shows $8:00\text{ AM}$. What is the distance moved by the car, if at $8:40\text{ AM}$, the odometer reading has changed to $1096\text{ km}$? Calculate the speed of the car in $\text{km/min}$ and $\text{km/h}$. [3]
7.
Regarding an electric bell:
(a) Name the central component of an electric bell that possesses magnetic properties when current flows through it.
(b) What happens to this component when the circuit is broken at the contact screw?
(c) State one everyday application of electromagnets other than an electric bell. [3]
8.
Two friends, A and B, started running from a starting line. A took $15\text{ minutes}$ to cover $3\text{ km}$, while B took $20\text{ minutes}$ to cover $4.5\text{ km}$. Who ran faster? Support your answer with proper calculations. [3]
SECTION C (20 Marks) (Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
9.
(Heating Effect of Electric Current)
(a) State two specific factors on which the amount of heat produced in a wire depends. [2]
(b) An electrician is carrying out some repairs in your house. He wants to replace a blown fuse with a thick piece of ordinary copper wire. Is it safe? Explain giving scientific reasons. [2]
(c) Name the alloy generally used to make the heating elements of appliances like room heaters and geysers. [1]
10.
(Kinematics & Averages)
A bus starts its journey from Delhi to Chandigarh. It travels at an average speed of $60\text{ km/h}$ for the first $2\text{ hours}$. Then, it stops at a Dhaba for a $1\text{ hour}$ lunch break. After the break, it resumes its journey and travels at a speed of $80\text{ km/h}$ for the next $1.5\text{ hours}$ to reach Chandigarh.
(a) Calculate the total distance covered by the bus before the lunch break. [1.5]
(b) Calculate the total distance covered by the bus after the lunch break. [1.5]
(c) Calculate the total time taken for the entire journey (including the break) and the overall average speed of the bus. [2]
11.
(Electromagnetism)
(a) What is an electromagnet? How does it differ from a permanent bar magnet? [2]
(b) State two ways by which the magnetic strength of an electromagnet can be increased. [2]
(c) Why is soft iron highly preferred as the core material inside an electromagnet? [1]
12.
(Electric Circuits)
(a) Draw a neat and labeled circuit diagram consisting of a battery of three cells, an electric bulb, and a switch in the 'ON' position. Indicate the direction of conventional current flow in the circuit with arrows. [3]
(b) If you place a magnetic compass needle near a current-carrying wire in your drawn circuit, what will you observe? Who was the first scientist to notice this effect? [2]
