Geoid & Oblate Spheroid:
'Geoid' simply means "earth-shaped". (1 Mark)
The Earth is an Oblate Spheroid because the high speed of its rotation creates a centrifugal force that causes a bulge at the Equator and compression at the Poles. (1 Mark)

Harappan Script:
Characteristics (Any two for 1 Mark): It is a pictographic script (using signs/pictures instead of letters) with about 400 signs. It was written from right to left (or boustrophedon style).
Undeciphered: It has not yet been deciphered by historians. If deciphered, it could unlock new information about their political system and exact religious beliefs. (1 Mark)

SECTION B
Ans 6.
Indirect Evidence of Earth's Shape:
Bedford Canal Experiment: Three poles of equal height were placed at 5 km intervals in a British canal. When viewed through a telescope, the middle pole appeared higher, proving the curvature of the Earth. (1.5 Marks)
Lunar Eclipse: During a lunar eclipse, the shadow the Earth casts upon the Moon is always circular. Only a sphere casts a circular shadow in all positions. (1.5 Marks)
Ans 7.
Harappan Residential Houses:
1. Houses were built on high mounds to protect against floods and were primarily constructed using standard-sized baked bricks.
2. A typical house had a central courtyard surrounded by rooms, a private well, and a bathroom.
3. Privacy: Entrances were from narrow side lanes, not the main street, and there were no windows facing the main streets. (1 Mark each = 3 Marks)
Ans 8.
Size of the Earth:
Eratosthenes measured the Earth's size by comparing the length of shadows cast by the mid-day sun at two different places in Egypt (Syene and Alexandria) during the summer solstice. Using basic geometry and the distance between them (5000 stadia), he calculated the circumference. (1.5 Marks)
Equatorial Diameter: 12,742 km (0.75 Mark)
Polar Diameter: 12,714 km (0.75 Mark)
Ans 9.
Harappan Seals:
Information provided: Seals provide valuable information about the script, trade (used for stamping goods), religion, and the artistic skills of the Harappans. (1.5 Marks)
Pashupati Seal: It shows a three-faced deity wearing a horned headdress, seated cross-legged, surrounded by an elephant, tiger, rhino, and a buffalo, with two deer at his feet. This deity is identified as Pashupati (Lord Shiva). (1.5 Marks)
Ans 10.
Autotrophs, Heterotrophs, and Food Chain:
Autotrophs (Producers): Green plants that synthesize their own food using sunlight through photosynthesis.
Heterotrophs (Consumers): Organisms that depend on plants or other animals for energy (e.g., Herbivores, Carnivores). (1.5 Marks for both definitions)
Food Chain: The sequential transfer of energy in the form of food from plants to herbivores, and then to carnivores. (1.5 Marks)
SECTION C
Ans 11.
Harappan Architecture (Great Bath & Granaries):
(a) The Great Bath: Found at Mohenjo-daro in the Citadel area. It is a large rectangular tank resembling a modern swimming pool, made of standard baked bricks with flights of steps on the north and south. Significance: Highlights brilliant urban planning (water supply/sewage disposal). It was likely used for ritual bathing or religious purposes, showing the importance of water purification in their society. (2.5 Marks)
(b) The Granaries: Found at Harappa, Mohenjo-daro, and Lothal. Built on raised platforms to protect from floods and had proper ventilation. Significance: Used for storing surplus food grains (wheat/barley). Built near rivers for easy boat transport. It points to a well-organised agricultural tax system or emergency food reserves. (2.5 Marks)
Ans 12.
Earth as a Living Planet:
Earth is known as a living planet due to the following conditions (Any 4 detailed points or 5 brief points for 5 Marks):
1. Right Distance from Sun: At 150 million km, it maintains a moderate average temperature (~15°C), avoiding the extremes of Mercury or Jupiter.
2. Presence of Atmosphere: Contains Oxygen (respiration), Nitrogen (nutrient/cools air), and CO2 (photosynthesis). It acts as a blanket retaining heat and protecting from UV rays.
3. Presence of Water: 71% of the surface is covered in water. It exists in all three states (liquid, solid, gas) and is fundamental to all biological processes.
4. Solid Crust (Lithosphere): Provides a rocky crust which weathers to produce soil, anchoring plants and providing minerals for agriculture.
5. Biosphere: The interaction of land, air, and water creates a unique zone supported by continuous solar energy, sustaining food chains.
Ans 13.
Decline of the Harappan Civilisation:
Historians suggest multiple causes for the decline (Explain any three for 5 Marks):
1. Floods and Earthquakes: Evidence shows Mohenjo-daro flooded multiple times. Earthquakes may have raised flood plains, blocking the Indus river and inundating cities.
2. Increased Aridity & Drying of Rivers: Climate change caused aridity. The river Ghaggar (Saraswati) dried up due to tectonic shifts changing tributary courses, leaving the region waterless.
3. Deforestation: The need for massive amounts of wood to bake millions of bricks and smelt metals led to over-exploitation of forests, causing soil erosion and drastic climate change.
4. Aryan Attack (Theory): Historian Mortimer Wheeler believed invading Aryans destroyed settlements, citing unburied skeletons in Mohenjo-daro as evidence of a massacre (though disputed by modern historians).
