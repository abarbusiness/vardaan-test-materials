Minor Test 6
Class: 9 ICSE Subject: History & Geography Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 40
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Answers must be written strictly based on the syllabus context provided.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
What is meant by the Bronze Age? Name any two major Bronze Age civilisations other than the Harappan Civilisation.
2.
Give the exact meaning of the term 'Geoid'. Why is the true shape of the Earth described as an Oblate Spheroid?
3.
State any two distinct characteristics of the Harappan script. Why is it considered an undeciphered mystery?
4.
Define 'Biosphere'. Name the two main categories of organisms based on their source of energy in an ecosystem.
5.
Mention the northernmost and westernmost geographical boundaries of the Harappan Civilisation.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
Briefly explain the 'Bedford Canal Experiment' and the 'Lunar Eclipse' observation as indirect evidence proving the Earth's spherical shape.
7.
The Harappan cities are known for their spectacular city planning. Mention any three architectural features of their residential houses.
8.
Based on the provided text, how did the Greek philosopher Eratosthenes calculate the size of the Earth? Mention the Earth's Equatorial and Polar Diameters.
9.
What valuable information do the seals provide about the Harappan Civilisation? Describe the physical depictions on the famous Pashupati Seal.
10.
Differentiate between Autotrophs and Heterotrophs based on energy flow. What is a food chain?
SECTION C (15 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
With reference to the archaeological sources of the Harappan Civilisation, explain the structure and historical significance of the following:
(a) The Great Bath (2.5 Marks)
(b) The Granaries (2.5 Marks)
12.
"The Earth is uniquely known as a living planet."
Justify this statement by explaining in detail the critical factors that provide the exact environmental conditions necessary to sustain life.
13.
The Harappan Civilisation began to decline around 1800 BCE. Historians suggest multiple causes rather than a single event for its collapse. Explain any three of these major theories/causes in detail.
