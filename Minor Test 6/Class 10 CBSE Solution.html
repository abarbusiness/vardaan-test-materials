Ans 1.
Laws of Reflection:
1. The angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection (∠i = ∠r).
2. The incident ray, the normal to the mirror at the point of incidence, and the reflected ray, all lie in the same plane.
Yes, these laws apply to all types of reflecting surfaces, including spherical mirrors. (1+1 = 2 Marks)
Ans 2.
Absolute Refractive Index:
The ratio of the speed of light in a vacuum (c) to the speed of light in a given medium (v) is called the absolute refractive index of that medium (n = c/v).
Diamond has a high refractive index (2.42) which implies that the optical density of diamond is very high, and the speed of light in diamond is very low compared to the speed of light in vacuum. (1+1 = 2 Marks)

Ans 5.
Refraction Reason:
When light travels from a rarer to a denser medium, its speed decreases. To compensate for the change in speed while maintaining the frequency, the wavelength decreases, causing the ray to bend towards the normal. (2 Marks)

Ans 6.
Convex Mirror Numerical:
Given: h = +5 cm, u = -10 cm, R = +30 cm (Convex mirror).
Focal length, f = R/2 = +15 cm.
Mirror Formula: 1/v + 1/u = 1/f ⇒ 1/v = 1/f - 1/u
1/v = 1/15 - (1/-10) = 1/15 + 1/10 = (2 + 3)/30 = 5/30 = 1/6
v = +6 cm (Image is formed 6 cm behind the mirror).
Magnification, m = -v/u = -(6) / (-10) = +0.6
Nature: Virtual and Erect.
Size of image, h' = m × h = 0.6 × 5 = +3 cm. (3 Marks)

Ans 9.
Snell's Law & Refractive Index:
Snell's Law: The ratio of sine of angle of incidence to the sine of angle of refraction is a constant, for the light of a given color and for the given pair of media. (sin i / sin r = constant).
Refractive index of medium (n) = Speed of light in vacuum (c) / Speed of light in medium (v)
n = (3 × 108 m/s) / (2 × 108 m/s) = 1.5. (1.5 + 1.5 = 3 Marks)

Lens Magnification & Concave Lens Numerical:
(a) Linear Magnification: It is defined as the ratio of the height of the image (h') to the height of the object (h).
Formula: m = h'/h = + v/u (where v is image distance and u is object distance). (1.5 Marks)
(b) Given for concave lens: f = -15 cm, v = -10 cm (Concave lens always forms a virtual, erect image on the same side as the object).
Lens Formula: 1/v - 1/u = 1/f ⇒ 1/u = 1/v - 1/f
1/u = (1/-10) - (1/-15) = -1/10 + 1/15 = (-3 + 2)/30 = -1/30
u = -30 cm. The object is placed 30 cm from the lens. (2 Marks)

Ans 13.
Power of Lens & Relative Refractive Index:
(a) 1 Dioptre: 1 Dioptre is defined as the power of a lens whose focal length is 1 metre. (1 D = 1 m-1). (1 Mark)
(b) Total Power (P) = P1 + P2 = +3.5 D + (-2.5 D) = +1.0 D. (1 Mark)
Focal length of combination (F) = 1 / P = 1 / (+1.0) = +1.0 m or +100 cm. (1 Mark)
(c) Given: nw (refractive index of water w.r.t air) = 4/3
ng (refractive index of glass w.r.t air) = 3/2
Refractive index of glass with respect to water (wng) = ng / nw
wng = (3/2) / (4/3) = (3 × 3) / (2 × 4) = 9/8 or 1.125. (2 Marks)
