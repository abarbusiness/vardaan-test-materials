SECTION A
Ans 1.
Coordinates & Axes:
The coordinates of the origin are (0, 0).
The horizontal line is called the x-axis and the vertical line is called the y-axis. (1+1 = 2 Marks)
Ans 2.
Quadrants & Axes:
(i) (3, -2): The x-coordinate is positive and the y-coordinate is negative. Therefore, it lies in the IV (Fourth) quadrant.
(ii) (0, 5): The x-coordinate is zero. Therefore, it lies on the y-axis. (1+1 = 2 Marks)
Ans 3.
Perpendicular Distance:
For point P(4, 3):
The perpendicular distance from the x-axis is the y-coordinate = 3 units.
The perpendicular distance from the y-axis is the x-coordinate = 4 units. (1+1 = 2 Marks)
Ans 4.
Abscissa & Ordinate:
Abscissa (x-coordinate) = -3. Ordinate (y-coordinate) = -5.
Coordinates of the point = (-3, -5).
Since both coordinates are negative, it lies in the III (Third) quadrant. (1+1 = 2 Marks)
Ans 5.
Value Calculation:
Abscissa of P(-2, 3) = -2.
Abscissa of Q(-3, 5) = -3.
(Abscissa of P) − (Abscissa of Q) = -2 - (-3) = -2 + 3 = 1. (2 Marks)
SECTION B
Ans 6.
Plotting & Shape Identification:
[Students are expected to plot the four points on a graph paper.]
Length of AB (horizontal distance from x=-2 to x=2) = 4 units.
Length of BC (vertical distance from y=-1 to y=2) = 3 units.
Since the opposite sides are equal and the angles formed are 90 degrees (lines are parallel to axes), the figure ABCD is a Rectangle. (2 Marks for plotting, 1 Mark for shape)
Ans 7.
Specific Points:
(i) A point lying on both axes is the origin. Coordinates: (0, 0).
(ii) Lying on y-axis means x = 0. Ordinate = -4. Coordinates: (0, -4).
(iii) Lying on x-axis means y = 0. Abscissa = 5. Coordinates: (5, 0). (1+1+1 = 3 Marks)
Ans 8.
Identifying Quadrants directly:
(i) Coordinates are (-3, 5). x is negative, y is positive → II (Second) Quadrant.
(ii) Coordinates are (-5, -3). x is negative, y is negative → III (Third) Quadrant.
(iii) Coordinates are (5, 3). x is positive, y is positive → I (First) Quadrant. (1+1+1 = 3 Marks)
Ans 9.
Finding the Fourth Vertex:
[Plotting A(2, 3), B(-3, 3), and C(-3, -2) on a graph].
Side AB is horizontal (length = 5 units). Side BC is vertical (length = 5 units).
For it to be a square, side CD must be horizontal (length 5 units) and AD must be vertical.
The x-coordinate of D will be same as A (x = 2). The y-coordinate of D will be same as C (y = -2).
Therefore, the coordinates of D are (2, -2). (1.5 Marks for plot, 1.5 Marks for D)
Ans 10.
Rectangle Vertices:
Length = 5 units, lying on the positive x-axis. Vertex 1 is at origin: (0, 0). Vertex 2 on positive x-axis: (5, 0).
Breadth = 3 units. One vertex is in the fourth quadrant (where y is negative). So the rectangle goes downwards.
Vertex 3 (below 5,0): (5, -3). Vertex 4 (below 0,0): (0, -3).
Coordinates are: (0, 0), (5, 0), (5, -3), (0, -3). (3 Marks)
SECTION C
Ans 11.
Rectangle & Area:
(a) [Students plot P(-2, 3), Q(-2, -4) and R(5, -4)]. (1.5 Marks)
(b) To form a rectangle, point S must align horizontally with P and vertically with R.
x-coordinate of S = x-coordinate of R = 5.
y-coordinate of S = y-coordinate of P = 3.
Therefore, coordinates of S are (5, 3). (1.5 Marks)
(c) Length of rectangle (PQ or RS) = vertical distance from y=-4 to y=3 = 7 units.
Breadth of rectangle (QR or PS) = horizontal distance from x=-2 to x=5 = 7 units. (Note: PQRS is actually a square, which is a special type of rectangle).
Area = Length × Breadth = 7 × 7 = 49 square units. (2 Marks)
Ans 12.
True/False Justification:
(a) False. The point (3, 0) lies on the x-axis, not in any quadrant. (1 Mark)
(b) False. (1, -1) lies in the IV quadrant, while (-1, 1) lies in the II quadrant. (1 Mark)
(c) True. The format is (abscissa, ordinate), so x=1, y=-1/2 gives (1, -1/2). (1 Mark)
(d) False. A point on the y-axis has x=0. If its distance from x-axis is 2, its coordinates are (0, 2) or (0, -2). (2, 0) lies on the x-axis. (1 Mark)
(e) False. The perpendicular distance from the x-axis is its y-coordinate, which is 3 units, not 4. (1 Mark)
Ans 13.
Triangle Plotting & Area:
(i) [Plotting A(-3, 2), B(-3, -3), C(4, -3) and joining them].
Side AB is parallel to y-axis and Side BC is parallel to x-axis. Thus, ∠B = 90°.
The triangle formed is a Right-angled triangle. (2 Marks)
(ii) Length of base BC = horizontal distance from x = -3 to x = 4 → 4 - (-3) = 7 units.
Length of height AB = vertical distance from y = 2 to y = -3 → 2 - (-3) = 5 units. (2 Marks)
(iii) Area of Triangle ABC = (1/2) × Base × Height
Area = (1/2) × 7 × 5 = 35 / 2 = 17.5 square units. (1 Mark)
