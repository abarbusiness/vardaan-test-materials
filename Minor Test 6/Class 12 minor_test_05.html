General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory. Read the questions thoroughly before answering.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Write your answers neatly, showing all thermodynamic equations and mathematical steps clearly.
  • Use of standard log tables is permitted. Calculate to two decimal places.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
A standard Daniel cell has a potential of 1.1 V. Predict the direction of flow of electrons and the chemical reaction taking place if an opposing external potential of (i) exactly 1.1 V and (ii) 1.2 V is applied to this cell.
2.
Explain, using interionic interactions, why the conductivity (κ) of an electrolytic solution decreases with dilution, whereas its molar conductivity (Λm) increases with dilution.
3.
During the electrolysis of aqueous sodium chloride, sodium ions are not reduced at the cathode, but hydrogen gas is evolved. However, chlorine gas is preferentially liberated at the anode despite oxygen having a lower oxidation potential. Give rigorous thermodynamic or kinetic reasons for these two observations.
4.
Consider the standard electrode potentials: E°A2+/A = -2.37 V, E°B2+/B = -0.76 V, E°C2+/C = 0.34 V, and E°D+/D = 0.80 V. Arrange these metals in increasing order of their reducing power and identify the strongest oxidizing agent among the ions. Justify your answer.
5.
How much charge is required for the complete reduction of 1 mole of MnO4- to Mn2+ in an acidic medium? Express your answer rigorously in Coulombs.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
Calculate the electrode potential of a standard hydrogen electrode placed in a buffer solution with a pH of 10 at 298 K. (Given: H2 gas is at 1 atm pressure).
7.
The resistance of a conductivity cell filled with 0.1 M KCl solution is 100 Ω. If the resistance of the same cell when filled with 0.02 M KCl solution is 520 Ω, calculate the conductivity and molar conductivity of the 0.02 M KCl solution. (Given: The conductivity of 0.1 M KCl solution is 1.29 S m-1).
8.
State Kohlrausch's law of independent migration of ions. How does this law practically help in determining the limiting molar conductivity (Λ°m) of a weak electrolyte like acetic acid, which cannot be accurately determined by graphical extrapolation?
9.
A concentration cell is constructed by dipping two identical copper electrodes in aqueous CuSO4 solutions of concentrations 0.001 M and 0.1 M respectively at 298 K.
(i) Write the standard cell representation and clearly assign the anode and cathode.
(ii) Calculate the EMF of this cell at 298 K.
10.
Corrosion of iron is essentially an electrochemical phenomenon. Explain this statement by writing the specific anodic and cathodic reactions that occur during the rusting of iron in the presence of water and atmospheric oxygen.
SECTION C (15 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
Three electrolytic cells A, B, and C containing solutions of ZnSO4, AgNO3, and CuSO4 respectively, are connected in series. A steady direct current of 1.5 Amperes was passed through them until exactly 1.45 g of silver was deposited at the cathode of cell B.
(a) State Faraday's First and Second Laws of electrolysis which govern this setup.
(b) Calculate the exact time for which the current flowed in the circuit.
(c) What exact mass of copper and zinc were deposited in cells C and A respectively? (Given atomic masses: Ag = 108 u, Cu = 63.5 u, Zn = 65.3 u, F = 96500 C mol-1).
12.
For the reaction occurring in a standard galvanic cell: Mg(s) + 2Ag+(aq) → Mg2+(aq) + 2Ag(s)
(a) Represent the cell schematically.
(b) Calculate the standard cell potential (E°cell) and the standard Gibbs free energy change (ΔG°) at 298 K. (Given: E°Mg2+/Mg = -2.36 V, E°Ag+/Ag = 0.80 V, F = 96500 C mol-1).
(c) Calculate the maximum electrical work that can be strictly obtained from this cell under standard conditions.
(d) Determine the equilibrium constant (Kc) for the reaction at 298 K.
13.
The conductivity (κ) of a 0.001028 M aqueous solution of acetic acid (CH3COOH) is experimentally found to be 4.95 × 10-5 S cm-1 at 298 K.
(a) Calculate the molar conductivity (Λm) of this specific solution.
(b) If the limiting molar conductivities (Λ°m) of H+ and CH3COO- ions are 349.6 S cm2 mol-1 and 40.9 S cm2 mol-1 respectively, calculate the degree of dissociation (α) of acetic acid at this given concentration.
(c) Calculate the acid dissociation constant (Ka) of the acetic acid.
(d) What mathematically happens to the degree of dissociation (α) if this solution is further diluted with pure water? Justify briefly using Le Chatelier's principle or relevant formulas.
