1.
Find the square of 45 without actual multiplication.
For a number ending in 5, i.e., (a5)², the formula is: [a × (a + 1)] hundred + 25
Here, a = 4.
Therefore, 45² = [4 × (4 + 1)] × 100 + 25
= (4 × 5) × 100 + 25 = 2000 + 25
Ans: 2025
2.
Is 216 a perfect cube? Give reason.
Prime factorization of 216 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 × 3 × 3
Grouping the factors into triplets: (2 × 2 × 2) × (3 × 3 × 3) = 2³ × 3³
Since all prime factors appear in groups of three, 216 is a perfect cube.
Ans: Yes, 216 is a perfect cube.

15.
Pythagorean triplet with one member 16. Explain algebraic form.
The general form of a Pythagorean triplet is (2m, m² - 1, m² + 1) for any integer m > 1.
Let 2m = 16. Therefore, m = 8.
Second member (m² - 1) = 8² - 1 = 64 - 1 = 63.
Third member (m² + 1) = 8² + 1 = 64 + 1 = 65.
Explanation: (2m)² + (m² - 1)² = 4m² + m4 - 2m² + 1 = m4 + 2m² + 1 = (m² + 1)². This satisfies the Pythagorean theorem a² + b² = c².
Ans: The triplet is (16, 63, 65).
