Q1.
Write the numeral for the following number name: "Two crore, thirty lakh, four thousand, five hundred".
Solution: The numeral is 2,30,04,500.
Q2.
Find the Least Common Multiple (LCM) of 12 and 18.
Solution:
Prime factorization of 12 = \( 2 \times 2 \times 3 = 2^2 \times 3 \)
Prime factorization of 18 = \( 2 \times 3 \times 3 = 2 \times 3^2 \)
LCM = Highest powers of prime factors = \( 2^2 \times 3^2 = 4 \times 9 = 36 \).
Q3.
Add the following fractions: \( \frac{2}{5} \) and \( \frac{1}{3} \).
Solution:
LCM of denominators 5 and 3 is 15.
\( \frac{2}{5} = \frac{2 \times 3}{5 \times 3} = \frac{6}{15} \)
\( \frac{1}{3} = \frac{1 \times 5}{3 \times 5} = \frac{5}{15} \)
Sum = \( \frac{6}{15} + \frac{5}{15} = \frac{11}{15} \).
Q4.
State the exact measure (in degrees) of:
(a) A straight angle
(b) A right angle
Solution:
(a) A straight angle measures exactly 180°.
(b) A right angle measures exactly 90°.
Q5.
Simplify using BODMAS rule: \( 15 + 5 \times 2 - 3 \).
Solution:
Applying BODMAS, multiplication comes first:
= \( 15 + (5 \times 2) - 3 \)
= \( 15 + 10 - 3 \)
Next, addition:
= \( 25 - 3 \)
= \( 22 \).
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
Q6.
A rectangular school garden is 45 m long and 30 m wide. Find its perimeter and area.
Solution:
Length (\( l \)) = 45 m, Breadth (\( b \)) = 30 m.
Perimeter = \( 2 \times (l + b) = 2 \times (45 + 30) = 2 \times 75 = 150 \) m.
Area = \( l \times b = 45 \times 30 = 1350 \) sq m.
Q7.
Find the Highest Common Factor (HCF) of 144 and 198 using the prime factorization method.
Solution:
Prime factorization of 144 = \( 2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 3 \times 3 = 2^4 \times 3^2 \)
Prime factorization of 198 = \( 2 \times 3 \times 3 \times 11 = 2 \times 3^2 \times 11 \)
HCF = Product of lowest powers of common factors = \( 2^1 \times 3^2 = 2 \times 9 = 18 \).
Q8.
In a class of 40 students, \( \frac{1}{5} \) of the total students like to study English, \( \frac{2}{5} \) of the total like Mathematics, and the remaining like Science. How many students like to study Science?
Solution:
Total students = 40.
Students liking English = \( \frac{1}{5} \text{ of } 40 = \frac{1}{5} \times 40 = 8 \).
Students liking Mathematics = \( \frac{2}{5} \text{ of } 40 = \frac{2}{5} \times 40 = 16 \).
Total students liking English and Math = \( 8 + 16 = 24 \).
Students liking Science = Total - (English + Math) = \( 40 - 24 = 16 \).
Q9.
The cost of 5 fountain pens is ₹ 75. Use the unitary method to find the cost of 12 such pens.
Solution:
Cost of 5 pens = ₹ 75.
Cost of 1 pen = \( \frac{75}{5} = 15 \) — so the cost is ₹ 15.
Cost of 12 pens = \( 15 \times 12 = 180 \) — so the cost is ₹ 180.
Q10.
Classify the following angles as acute, obtuse, right, or straight based on their degree measure:
(a) 45°
(b) 120°
(c) 90°
Solution:
(a) 45° is less than 90°, so it is an Acute angle.
(b) 120° is greater than 90° and less than 180°, so it is an Obtuse angle.
(c) 90° is exactly a Right angle.

Q11.
The population of a small town was 1,50,800 at the beginning of the year. During that year, the population increased by 4,290 due to new births. However, 8,973 persons died or left the town. What was the exact population of the town at the end of the year? Show all your working clearly.
Solution:
Initial population = 1,50,800.
Increase due to births = +4,290.

Decrease due to death/moving = -8,973.
Final population = \( 1,55,090 - 8,973 \).

Q12.
Solve the following:
(a) Arrange the fractions \( \frac{1}{2}, \frac{3}{4}, \) and \( \frac{5}{8} \) in ascending order. (3 Marks)
(b) Convert the mixed fraction \( 3\frac{2}{7} \) into an improper fraction. (2 Marks)
Solution:
(a) To arrange \( \frac{1}{2}, \frac{3}{4}, \frac{5}{8} \) in ascending order, find the LCM of denominators 2, 4, 8, which is 8.
Convert to equivalent fractions with denominator 8:
\( \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1 \times 4}{2 \times 4} = \frac{4}{8} \)
\( \frac{3}{4} = \frac{3 \times 2}{4 \times 2} = \frac{6}{8} \)
\( \frac{5}{8} = \frac{5 \times 1}{8 \times 1} = \frac{5}{8} \)
Comparing numerators: 4 < 5 < 6.
Ascending order: \( \frac{4}{8} < \frac{5}{8} < \frac{6}{8} \) which means \( \frac{1}{2} < \frac{5}{8} < \frac{3}{4} \).

(b) Mixed fraction = \( 3\frac{2}{7} \).
Improper fraction = \( \frac{(\text{Whole number} \times \text{Denominator}) + \text{Numerator}}{\text{Denominator}} \)
= \( \frac{(3 \times 7) + 2}{7} = \frac{21 + 2}{7} = \frac{23}{7} \).
Q13.
Bob wants to cover the floor of a room that is 3 m wide and 4 m long with square tiles. If each square tile has a side length of 0.5 m, calculate the total number of tiles required to completely cover the floor of the room.
Solution:
Length of the room = 4 m.
Breadth of the room = 3 m.
Area of the floor = \( \text{Length} \times \text{Breadth} = 4 \text{ m} \times 3 \text{ m} = 12 \text{ sq m} \).

Side of one square tile = 0.5 m.
Area of one square tile = \( \text{Side} \times \text{Side} = 0.5 \times 0.5 = 0.25 \text{ sq m} \).

Number of tiles required = \( \frac{\text{Total Area of Floor}}{\text{Area of One Tile}} \)
= \( \frac{12}{0.25} \)
To remove decimal, multiply numerator and denominator by 100:
= \( \frac{1200}{25} = 48 \).

Therefore, 48 tiles are required to completely cover the floor.
