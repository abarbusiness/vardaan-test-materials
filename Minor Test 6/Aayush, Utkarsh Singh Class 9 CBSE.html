Minor Test 6
Class: 9 CBSE Subject: Mathematics (Coordinate Geometry) Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 40
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly. Use a graph paper wherever required.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Write the coordinates of the origin. What are the names of the horizontal and vertical lines drawn to determine the position of any point in the Cartesian plane?
2.
In which quadrant or on which axis do the following points lie?
(i) (3, -2)
(ii) (0, 5)
3.
What is the perpendicular distance of the point P(4, 3) from the x-axis and the y-axis?
4.
The abscissa of a point is -3 and its ordinate is -5. Write the coordinates of the point and state its quadrant.
5.
If the coordinates of two points are P(-2, 3) and Q(-3, 5), then what is the value of (abscissa of P) − (abscissa of Q)?
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
Plot the points A(2, 2), B(-2, 2), C(-2, -1) and D(2, -1) on a Cartesian plane. Name the mathematical figure ABCD formed by joining these points in order.
7.
Find the coordinates of the point:
(i) Which lies on both the x and y axes.
(ii) Whose ordinate is -4 and which lies on the y-axis.
(iii) Whose abscissa is 5 and which lies on the x-axis.
8.
Without plotting the points, indicate the quadrant in which they will lie, if:
(i) Ordinate is 5 and abscissa is -3.
(ii) Abscissa is -5 and ordinate is -3.
(iii) Abscissa is 5 and ordinate is 3.
9.
Three vertices of a square are A(2, 3), B(-3, 3) and C(-3, -2). Plot these points on a graph paper and find the coordinates of the fourth vertex D.
10.
Write the coordinates of the vertices of a rectangle whose length and breadth are 5 units and 3 units respectively, having one vertex at the origin, its longer side lying on the positive x-axis and one of the vertices lying in the fourth quadrant.
SECTION C (15 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
(a) Plot the points P(-2, 3), Q(-2, -4) and R(5, -4) on a graph paper.
(b) Find the coordinates of point S such that PQRS forms a rectangle.
(c) Find the area of the rectangle PQRS.
12.
State whether the following statements are True or False. Justify your answer in one sentence each:
(a) The point (3, 0) lies in the first quadrant.
(b) The points (1, -1) and (-1, 1) lie in the same quadrant.
(c) The coordinates of a point whose ordinate is -1/2 and abscissa is 1 are (1, -1/2).
(d) A point lies on the y-axis at a distance of 2 units from the x-axis. Its coordinates can be (2, 0).
(e) The perpendicular distance of the point (4, 3) from the x-axis is 4.
13.
Plot the points A(-3, 2), B(-3, -3), and C(4, -3) on a Cartesian plane.
(i) Join them to form a triangle ABC. State the type of triangle formed.
(ii) Find the length of its base BC and height AB.
(iii) Calculate the area of the triangle ABC formed.
