Minor Test 6
Class: 10 CBSE Subject: Science (Physics - Light) Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 40
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Write your answers neatly and legibly. Draw neat and labeled ray diagrams wherever necessary.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
State the laws of reflection of light. Do these laws apply to spherical mirrors?
2.
Define absolute refractive index of a medium. Why does diamond have a high refractive index (2.42)?
3.
An object is placed at the centre of curvature of a concave mirror. State the position, nature, and size of the image formed.
4.
A convex lens has a focal length of 20 cm. Calculate the power of the lens.
5.
Why does a ray of light traveling from a rarer medium to a denser medium bend towards the normal?
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
An object 5 cm high is placed at a distance of 10 cm from a convex mirror of radius of curvature 30 cm. Find the position, nature, and size of the image.
7.
(a) What is lateral displacement?
(b) Draw a neat, labeled ray diagram to show the refraction of light through a rectangular glass slab, clearly marking the angle of incidence, angle of refraction, and emergent ray.
8.
A student wants to project the image of a candle flame on a screen 80 cm in front of a mirror by keeping the flame at a distance of 20 cm from its pole.
(a) Suggest the type of mirror he should use.
(b) Find the linear magnification of the image produced.
(c) What is the distance between the object and its image?
9.
State Snell's law of refraction. If the speed of light in vacuum is 3 × 108 m/s and the speed of light in a given medium is 2 × 108 m/s, find the refractive index of that medium.
10.
Draw a ray diagram to show the image formation by a convex lens when an object is placed between F1 and 2F1. State the characteristics of the image formed.
SECTION C (15 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
(a) Define linear magnification produced by a spherical lens. Write its formula in terms of object distance (u) and image distance (v).
(b) A concave lens of focal length 15 cm forms an image 10 cm from the lens. How far is the object placed from the lens? Draw the corresponding ray diagram.
12.
(a) Write two practical uses of concave mirrors and two uses of convex mirrors.
(b) It is desired to obtain an erect image of an object, using a concave mirror of focal length 20 cm.
    (i) What should be the range of distance of the object from the mirror?
    (ii) Will the image be bigger or smaller than the object?
    (iii) Draw a ray diagram to show the image formation in this case.
13.
(a) Define 1 dioptre of power of a lens.
(b) Two thin lenses of power +3.5 D and -2.5 D are placed in contact. Find the power and focal length of the lens combination.
(c) The refractive index of water is 4/3 and that of glass is 3/2 with respect to air. Calculate the refractive index of glass with respect to water.
