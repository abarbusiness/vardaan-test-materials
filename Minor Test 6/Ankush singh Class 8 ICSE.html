Minor Test 06
Class: 8 ICSE Subject: Mathematics (Profit, Loss & Discount) Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into four sections: A, B, C, and D.
  • Section A contains 10 Objective Type questions of 1 mark each.
  • Section B contains 5 Short Answer Type questions of 2 marks each.
  • Section C contains 5 Long Answer Type questions of 3 marks each.
  • Section D contains 3 Application-based questions of 5 marks each.
  • Show proper working/steps for questions in Sections B, C, and D.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 1 Mark Each)
1.
Write the formula to calculate Profit Percentage.
2.
Find the profit if Cost Price (C.P.) = Rs. 400 and Selling Price (S.P.) = Rs. 450.
3.
Find the loss if Cost Price (C.P.) = Rs. 500 and Selling Price (S.P.) = Rs. 420.
4.
Discount is always calculated on which price?
5.
Find the Selling Price if Marked Price (M.P.) = Rs. 100 and Discount = 10%.
6.
If Selling Price is greater than Cost Price (S.P. > C.P.), what does the seller make (Profit or Loss)?
7.
Find the Discount amount if Marked Price = Rs. 250 and Selling Price = Rs. 200.
8.
State True or False: "Loss percentage is calculated on the Selling Price."
9.
If Profit is Rs. 50 on a Cost Price of Rs. 200, find the Profit Percentage.
10.
Write the formula to find the Selling Price (S.P.) when Marked Price (M.P.) and Discount Percentage (D%) are given.
SECTION B (10 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 2 Marks Each)
11.
A man buys a toy for Rs. 150 and sells it for Rs. 180. Find his profit percent.
12.
A shopkeeper bought a chair for Rs. 500 and sold it at a loss of 10%. Find the selling price of the chair.
13.
An article marked at Rs. 800 is sold for Rs. 700. Find the discount amount and the discount percent.
14.
The selling price of an item is Rs. 1200 after giving a discount of Rs. 300. Find its marked price.
15.
Raju bought a book for Rs. 250. His overhead expenses (like transport) were Rs. 50. He then sold the book for Rs. 360. Find his total cost price and his profit.
SECTION C (15 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
16.
By selling a watch for Rs. 1140, a man loses 5%. For how much should he sell it to gain 5%?
17.
The marked price of a ceiling fan is Rs. 1250 and the shopkeeper allows a discount of 6% on it. Find the selling price of the fan.
18.
A dealer buys a table for Rs. 1500. He marks it at Rs. 2000 and then allows a discount of 10%. Find his selling price and his profit percentage.
19.
The cost price of 10 pens is equal to the selling price of 8 pens. Find the gain or loss percent.
20.
A trader marks his goods 20% above the cost price. If he allows a discount of 10% on the marked price, find his actual profit percentage.
SECTION D (15 Marks)(Application Based - 5 Marks Each)
21.
A shopkeeper allows two successive discounts of 20% and 10% on the marked price of a television. If the marked price of the television is Rs. 25,000, find the final selling price of the television.
22.
A dealer bought a washing machine for Rs. 12,000. He marked its price in such a way that after allowing a discount of 20% on the marked price, he still gains 20%. Find the marked price of the washing machine.
23.
Ram bought two bicycles for Rs. 3000 each. He sold one bicycle at a profit of 10% and the other bicycle at a loss of 10%. Find the total selling price of both bicycles and calculate his overall profit or loss percentage in the whole transaction.
