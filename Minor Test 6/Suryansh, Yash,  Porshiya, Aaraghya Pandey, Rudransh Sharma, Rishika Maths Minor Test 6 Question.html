Q2.
Find the product of \( \frac{3}{7} \) and \( \frac{14}{9} \).
Solution:
\( \frac{3}{7} \times \frac{14}{9} \)
Simplify by cancelling common factors:
\( = \frac{1}{1} \times \frac{2}{3} \)
\( = \frac{2}{3} \).
Q3.
Find the mean of the first 5 whole numbers.
Solution:
The first 5 whole numbers are: 0, 1, 2, 3, 4.
Sum = \( 0 + 1 + 2 + 3 + 4 = 10 \).
Mean = \( \frac{\text{Sum}}{\text{Total numbers}} = \frac{10}{5} = 2 \).
Q4.
Solve the simple equation for \( x \):
\( 2x - 3 = 7 \)
Solution:
\( 2x - 3 = 7 \)
\( 2x = 7 + 3 \)
\( 2x = 10 \)
\( x = \frac{10}{2} = 5 \).
Q5.
Find the complement of an angle measuring 35°.
Solution:
Two angles are complementary if their sum is 90°.
Complement of 35° = \( 90° - 35° \)
= 55°.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
Q6.
A car runs 16 km using 1 litre of petrol. How much distance will it cover using \( 2\frac{3}{4} \) litres of petrol?
Solution:
Distance covered with 1 litre of petrol = 16 km.
Quantity of petrol = \( 2\frac{3}{4} \) litres = \( \frac{11}{4} \) litres.
Distance covered = \( 16 \times \frac{11}{4} \)
= \( 4 \times 11 = 44 \) km.
Q7.
The scores in a mathematics test (out of 25) of 10 students is as follows:
19, 25, 23, 20, 9, 20, 15, 10, 5, 16.
Find the mode and median of this data.
Solution:
First, arrange data in ascending order:
5, 9, 10, 15, 16, 19, 20, 20, 23, 25.
Mode: The observation that occurs most frequently is 20 (occurs twice). So, Mode = 20.
Median: Since there are 10 observations (even), median is the average of 5th and 6th terms.
5th term = 16, 6th term = 19.
Median = \( \frac{16 + 19}{2} = \frac{35}{2} = 17.5 \).
Q8.
Find the value of the unknown exterior angle \( x \) in a triangle, if its interior opposite angles are 50° and 70°.
Solution:
According to the Exterior Angle Property of a triangle, the exterior angle is equal to the sum of the two interior opposite angles.
\( x = 50° + 70° \)
\( x = 120° \).
Q9.
Are the ratios 1:2 and 2:3 equivalent? Show your working.
Solution:
Write ratios as fractions: \( \frac{1}{2} \) and \( \frac{2}{3} \).
To compare them, make the denominators same. LCM of 2 and 3 is 6.
\( \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1 \times 3}{2 \times 3} = \frac{3}{6} \)
\( \frac{2}{3} = \frac{2 \times 2}{3 \times 2} = \frac{4}{6} \)
Since \( \frac{3}{6} \neq \frac{4}{6} \), the ratios are not equivalent.
Q10.
Find the area of a circle whose radius is 7 cm. (Use \( \pi = \frac{22}{7} \))
Solution:
Radius (\( r \)) = 7 cm.
Area of circle = \( \pi \times r^2 \)
= \( \frac{22}{7} \times 7 \times 7 \)
= \( 22 \times 7 \)
= 154 sq cm.
SECTION C (15 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
Q11.
In a class test containing 15 questions, 4 marks are given for every correct answer and (-2) marks are given for every incorrect answer.
(i) Gurpreet attempts all questions but only 9 of her answers are correct. What is her total score? (2.5 Marks)
(ii) One of her friends gets only 5 answers correct. What will be her score? (2.5 Marks)
Solution:
Marks for 1 correct answer = 4.
Marks for 1 incorrect answer = -2.

(i) Gurpreet's score:
Correct answers = 9.
Incorrect answers = Total - Correct = \( 15 - 9 = 6 \).
Marks from correct answers = \( 9 \times 4 = 36 \).
Marks from incorrect answers = \( 6 \times (-2) = -12 \).
Total score = \( 36 + (-12) = 24 \).

(ii) Friend's score:
Correct answers = 5.
Incorrect answers = Total - Correct = \( 15 - 5 = 10 \).
Marks from correct answers = \( 5 \times 4 = 20 \).
Marks from incorrect answers = \( 10 \times (-2) = -20 \).
Total score = \( 20 + (-20) = 0 \).
Q12.
Solve the following:
(a) Find the perimeter of a rectangle whose length is 40 cm and a diagonal is 41 cm. (3 Marks)
(b) State the Pythagoras property for a right-angled triangle. (2 Marks)
Solution:
(a) Let length (\( l \)) = 40 cm, diagonal (\( d \)) = 41 cm, breadth = \( b \).
According to Pythagoras theorem in the right-angled triangle formed by length, breadth, and diagonal:
\( d^2 = l^2 + b^2 \)
\( 41^2 = 40^2 + b^2 \)
\( 1681 = 1600 + b^2 \)
\( b^2 = 1681 - 1600 = 81 \)
\( b = 9 \) cm.
Perimeter of rectangle = \( 2 \times (l + b) \)
= \( 2 \times (40 + 9) = 2 \times 49 = 98 \) cm.

(b) The Pythagoras property states that in a right-angled triangle, the square of the hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides. (\( \text{Hypotenuse}^2 = \text{Base}^2 + \text{Perpendicular}^2 \))
Q13.
Anil's father's age is 5 years more than three times Anil's age.
(i) Set up an equation to find Anil's age, if his father is 44 years old. (2 Marks)
(ii) Solve the equation to find Anil's current age. (3 Marks)
Solution:
(i) Setting up the equation:
Let Anil's age be \( x \) years.
Three times Anil's age = \( 3x \).
Father's age is 5 years more than \( 3x \), so father's age = \( 3x + 5 \).
Given father's age is 44.
Equation: \( 3x + 5 = 44 \).

(ii) Solving the equation:
\( 3x + 5 = 44 \)
Subtract 5 from both sides:
\( 3x = 44 - 5 \)
\( 3x = 39 \)
Divide by 3:
\( x = \frac{39}{3} \)
\( x = 13 \).
Therefore, Anil's current age is 13 years.
