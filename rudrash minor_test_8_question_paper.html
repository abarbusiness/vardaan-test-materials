General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: Section A (15 Marks), Section B (15 Marks), and Section C (20 Marks).

SECTION A (15 Marks) (Objective / Very Short Answer Type)
1.
(Mathematics) Evaluate the following using laws of exponents:
(a) $(3^0 + 4^{-1}) \times 2^2$ [1.5]
(b) $(1/2)^{-2} + (1/3)^{-2} + (1/4)^{-2}$ [1.5]
2.
(Science) Classify the following devices based on whether they primarily utilize the 'Heating Effect' or 'Magnetic Effect' of electric current:
(i) Electric Iron    (ii) Electric Bell    (iii) Electric Fuse    (iv) Crane lifting scrap iron. [3]
3.
(Mathematics) Perform the indicated operations on algebraic expressions:
(a) Add: $7xy + 5yz - 3zx$ and $4yz + 9zx - 4y$ [1.5]
(b) Subtract: $3x^2 - 5x + 4$ from $5x^2 - 7x + 2$ [1.5]
4.
(Science) Answer in one or two sentences:
(a) Who was the first scientist to discover the magnetic effect of electric current? [1]
(b) Name the alloy predominantly used to make heating elements of appliances like room heaters. [1]
(c) What is the full form of MCB? [1]
5.
(Mathematics) Standard Form & Usual Form:
(a) Express the number $0.00000000837$ in standard scientific form. [1.5]
(b) Express $3.02 \times 10^{-6}$ in usual form. [1.5]
SECTION B (15 Marks) (Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
(Mathematics) Simplify the following expression and write the answer with a positive exponent: $$ \frac{8^{-1} \times 5^3}{2^{-4}} $$ [3]
7.
(Science) What is an electromagnet? State two ways in which you can increase the magnetic strength of an electromagnet. [3]
8.
(Mathematics) Find the product of the binomials $(2x + 5)$ and $(4x - 3)$. Show the expansion clearly. [3]
9.
(Science) Explain the working principle of an electric fuse. Why should we never replace a blown fuse with a thick piece of ordinary copper wire? [3]
10.
(Mathematics) Simplify the algebraic expression $3x(4x - 5) + 3$ and then find its numerical value for $x = 3$. [3]
SECTION C (20 Marks) (Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
(Mathematics - Algebraic Identities)
(a) Expand $(3a - 5b)^2$ using a suitable algebraic identity. [2.5]
(b) Evaluate $102 \times 98$ using a suitable algebraic identity. Mention the identity used. [2.5]
12.
(Science - Heating Effect & Magnetism)
(a) Describe an activity/experiment to show that a current-carrying wire behaves like a magnet (Oersted's experiment). [3]
(b) State two factors on which the amount of heat produced in a current-carrying wire depends. [2]
13.
(Mathematics - Mixed Applications)
(a) Find the value of $m$ for which $5^m \div 5^{-3} = 5^5$. [2]
(b) Subtract $3pq(p - q)$ from $2pq(p + q)$. [3]
14.
(Science - Electric Bell)
(a) Briefly explain the working mechanism of an electric bell when the switch is pressed. Which effect of electric current does it use? [3]
(b) Write two differences between an electromagnet and a permanent bar magnet. [2]
