SECTION A (12 Marks)
Part I: History (New Beginnings: Cities and States)
1. What does the term 'janapada' mean in Sanskrit? [2 Marks]
Solution: In Sanskrit, 'Janapada' literally means "the land where the 'jana' (tribe or people) sets its foot". It refers to the territories or small states established by various tribes.
2. Name the two main assemblies that functioned as early democratic traditions in the Vedic period. [2 Marks]
Solution: The two main assemblies were the Sabha and the Samiti.
3. Which two Mahajanapadas adopted a more democratic or republican form of government? [2 Marks]
Solution: The two Mahajanapadas were Vajji (with its capital at Vaishali) and Malla. They followed a 'Gana-Sangha' or republican system.
Part II: Mathematics (Rational Numbers & Integers)
4. Find the value of 'x' if 36⁄x and 2 are equivalent rational numbers. [2 Marks]
Solution: According to the question, 36⁄x = 2⁄1
By cross-multiplication: 2 × x = 36 × 1
2x = 36
x = 36 ÷ 2
x = 18
5. Write down the rational number in the form p⁄q whose numerator is [(−5) × 4] and whose denominator is [−5 + 4]. [2 Marks]
Solution: Numerator (p) = (−5) × 4 = −20
Denominator (q) = −5 + 4 = −1
Rational Number = −20⁄−1 = 20
6. Express 90⁄216 as an equivalent rational number with numerator 5. [2 Marks]
Solution: To get numerator 5 from 90, we divide by (90 ÷ 5) = 18.
Dividing both numerator and denominator by 18:
Numerator: 90 ÷ 18 = 5
Denominator: 216 ÷ 18 = 12
Result = 5⁄12
SECTION B (18 Marks)
Part I: History (New Beginnings: Cities and States)
7. State the main duties of a king (raja) in the Mahajanapadas. [3 Marks]
Solution: 1. Protection: Protecting the people from external attacks.
2. Law and Order: Maintaining peace and resolving disputes within the kingdom.
3. Welfare: Ensuring the prosperity of subjects through public works and irrigation.
8. Briefly differentiate between 'Varna' and 'Jati' in ancient Indian society. [3 Marks]
Solution: Varna: Refers to the four-fold classification (Brahmin, Kshatriya, Vaishya, Shudra) based primarily on occupation and status in the early Vedic period.
Jati: Refers to the sub-divisions or castes that emerged later, often based on birth and specific hereditary occupations, which became much more rigid.
9. What were the impacts of iron technology on agriculture and warfare? [3 Marks]
Solution: 1. Agriculture: Iron-tipped ploughshares allowed for deeper tilling of heavy soil, increasing crop yields.
2. Expansion: Iron axes helped in clearing dense forests for settlement.
3. Warfare: Iron weapons (swords, spearheads) were stronger and more durable than bronze, giving an edge in battle.
Part II: Mathematics (Rational Numbers & Integers)
10. Find common denominator equivalent forms for: 1⁄7 , 2⁄8 , 3⁄14 [3 Marks]
Solution: Denominators are 7, 8, and 14. LCM(7, 8, 14) = 56.
1. (1⁄7) × (8⁄8) = 8⁄56
2. (2⁄8) × (7⁄7) = 14⁄56
3. (3⁄14) × (4⁄4) = 12⁄56
11. True/False: (i) Every integer is a rational number. (ii) Every rational number is a fraction. [3 Marks]
Solution: (i) True: Any integer 'n' can be written as n⁄1. Example: 5 = 5⁄1.
(ii) False: Rational numbers include negative values, but fractions are typically parts of a whole (positive). Example: −3⁄4 is rational but not a fraction.
12. Evaluate: (−15) × 8 + (−15) × 2 [3 Marks]
Solution: Using Distributive Property of Multiplication over Addition:
a × b + a × c = a(b + c)
= (−15) × (8 + 2)
= (−15) × 10 = −150
SECTION C (30 Marks)
Part I: History (New Beginnings: Cities and States)
13. Describe the Varna system, roles, and its transformation. [5 Marks]
Solution: The Varna system divided society into four groups:
1. Brahmins: Priests and teachers, performed rituals.
2. Kshatriyas: Warriors and rulers, protected the people.
3. Vaishyas: Traders, farmers, and herders.
4. Shudras: Served the other three groups.
Transformation: Initially based on work, it became hereditary (based on birth). This led to social hierarchy, exclusion of Shudras from rituals, and the emergence of "untouchability," creating deep inequalities.
14. Significance of Uttarapatha and Dakshinapatha routes. [5 Marks]
Solution: Uttarapatha: The northern route connecting North-West India to the East (Pataliputra).
Dakshinapatha: The southern route connecting the North to South India.
Significance:
1. Trade: Allowed movement of goods like textiles, spices, and precious stones.
2. Cultural Exchange: Spread ideas, languages, and religions (Buddhism/Jainism).
3. Growth of Cities: Many major cities like Taxila and Ujjain developed along these trade junctions.
15. Technological and cultural advances of the Mahajanapada period. [5 Marks]
Solution: 1. Second Urbanisation: Emergence of walled cities and structured urban planning.
2. Iron Metallurgy: Widespread use of iron tools for agriculture and advanced weaponry.
3. Coinage: Introduction of punch-marked coins facilitated trade (moving away from barter).
4. Northern Black Polished Ware (NBPW): A luxury pottery style indicating specialized craft.
5. New Religions: Emergence of Buddhism and Jainism challenging the ritualistic Vedic tradition.
Part II: Mathematics (Rational Numbers & Integers)
16. (a) Check standard form of 4⁄−7 and 14⁄35. (b) 3⁄5 with numerator 150. [5 Marks]
Solution: (a) 4⁄−7: Not in standard form (denominator should be positive). Standard form: −4⁄7.
14⁄35: Not in standard form (they share a common factor 7). Standard form: 14÷7 / 35÷7 = 2⁄5.
(b) To get 150 from 3, we multiply by (150 ÷ 3) = 50.
(3⁄5) × (50⁄50) = 150⁄250
17. (a) Fill blanks: 105⁄? = ?⁄−99 = −5⁄−11 (b) Show 4⁄9 ≠ 16⁄27. [5 Marks]
Solution: (a) Base ratio: −5⁄−11 = 5⁄11.
For numerator 105: 105 ÷ 5 = 21. Denom = 11 × 21 = 231.
For denominator −99: −99 ÷ 11 = −9. Num = 5 × (−9) = −45.
(b) Cross-multiplication: 4 × 27 = 108. 9 × 16 = 144.
Since 108 ≠ 144, the numbers are not equivalent.
