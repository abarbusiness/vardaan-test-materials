Solution Key: Minor Test 02
Class: 9 ICSE Subject: Chemistry & Mathematics Max. Marks: 60
SECTION A (12 Marks)
Part I: Chemistry (Language of Chemistry)
1. Write the chemical formula for: (i) Calcium carbonate (ii) Ammonium sulphate. [2 Marks]
Solution: (i) Calcium carbonate: CaCO3
(ii) Ammonium sulphate: (NH4)2SO4
2. Define 'valency'. What is the valency of Oxygen in a water molecule (H2O)? [2 Marks]
Solution: Valency: It is defined as the combining capacity of an element or a radical. It is the number of hydrogen atoms that can combine with or displace one atom of that element.
In H2O: The valency of Oxygen is 2.
3. Balance the following basic chemical equation: H2 + O2 → H2O [2 Marks]
Solution: 2H2 + O2 → 2H2O
Part II: Mathematics (Factorization)
4. Factorize completely by taking out the common factors: 3x2 − 12x. [2 Marks]
Solution: Taking 3x as a common factor:
3x2 − 12x = 3x(x − 4)
5. Factorize by grouping the terms: a2 − b2 − 2a + 2b. [2 Marks]
Solution: (a2 − b2) − (2a − 2b)
= (a − b)(a + b) − 2(a − b)
= (a − b)(a + b − 2)
6. Factorize using the difference of two squares identity: x2 − 9. [2 Marks]
Solution: x2 − 32
= (x − 3)(x + 3)
SECTION B (18 Marks)
Part I: Chemistry (Language of Chemistry)
7. Calculate the relative molecular mass of: (i) H2O (ii) CO2 [H=1, O=16, C=12] [3 Marks]
Solution: (i) H2O = (2 × 1) + (1 × 16) = 2 + 16 = 18 amu
(ii) CO2 = (1 × 12) + (2 × 16) = 12 + 32 = 44 amu
8. Write the chemical symbol and standard valency for: (i) Sodium (ii) Chlorine (iii) Magnesium [3 Marks]
Solution: (i) Sodium: Symbol = Na, Valency = 1
(ii) Chlorine: Symbol = Cl, Valency = 1
(iii) Magnesium: Symbol = Mg, Valency = 2
9. Balance the equations: (a) N2 + H2 → NH3 (b) Mg + HCl → MgCl2 + H2 [3 Marks]
Solution: (a) N2 + 3H2 → 2NH3
(b) Mg + 2HCl → MgCl2 + H2
Part II: Mathematics (Factorization)
10. Factorize the given quadratic trinomial completely: x2 + 5x + 6. [3 Marks]
Solution: Find two numbers that multiply to 6 and add to 5 (2 and 3):
x2 + 2x + 3x + 6
= x(x + 2) + 3(x + 2)
= (x + 2)(x + 3)
11. Factorize by grouping the terms appropriately: ab + bc + ax + cx. [3 Marks]
Solution: b(a + c) + x(a + c)
= (a + c)(b + x)
12. Factorize using the difference of squares identity: 4x2 − 25y2. [3 Marks]
Solution: (2x)2 − (5y)2
= (2x − 5y)(2x + 5y)
SECTION C (30 Marks)
Part I: Chemistry (Language of Chemistry)
13. (a) Law of Conservation of Mass. (b) Balanced chemical equations for Carbon dioxide and Zinc sulphate. [5 Marks]
Solution: (a) Law: Mass can neither be created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction. The total mass of reactants is always equal to the total mass of products.
(b) (i) C + O2 → CO2
(ii) Zn + H2SO4 → ZnSO4 + H2
14. (a) Radical definition and examples. (b) Formula of Aluminium chloride (Criss-cross). [5 Marks]
Solution: (a) Radical: An atom or group of atoms that behaves as a single unit with a charge.
Example (Positive): NH4+ (Ammonium)
Example (Negative): Cl− (Chloride) or SO42− (Sulphate)
(b) Aluminium Chloride: Al (Valency 3), Cl (Valency 1).
Criss-cross: AlCl3
15. (a) Calculate mass of H2SO4. (b) Difference between '2H' and 'H2'. [5 Marks]
Solution: (a) H2SO4: (2×1) + (1×32) + (4×16) = 2 + 32 + 64 = 98 amu
(b) Difference: '2H' represents two independent atoms of Hydrogen. 'H2' represents one molecule of Hydrogen consisting of two atoms bonded together.
Part II: Mathematics (Factorization)
16. Factorize: (a) x2 − 7x + 12 (b) 2x2 + 7x + 3 [5 Marks]
Solution: (a) x2 − 3x − 4x + 12 = x(x − 3) − 4(x − 3) = (x − 3)(x − 4)
(b) 2x2 + 6x + x + 3 = 2x(x + 3) + 1(x + 3) = (x + 3)(2x + 1)
17. Factorize completely: (a) x4 − y4 (b) p2 − 10p + 25 [5 Marks]
Solution: (a) (x2)2 − (y2)2 = (x2 − y2)(x2 + y2) = (x − y)(x + y)(x2 + y2)
(b) (p)2 − 2(p)(5) + (5)2 = (p − 5)2
18. Factorize: (a) 5x2 − 20 (b) x2 − y2 − 4x + 4y [5 Marks]
Solution: (a) 5(x2 − 4) = 5(x − 2)(x + 2)
(b) (x − y)(x + y) − 4(x − y) = (x − y)(x + y − 4)
