SECTION B: Short Answer Type (3 Marks Each)
4.
Differentiate between contact and non-contact forces. Give one example of each.
(3)
Solution:
Contact Forces: Forces that act only when objects are in physical contact with each other.
Example: Muscular force, Frictional force.
Non-Contact Forces: Forces that can act between objects even when they are not in physical contact (act from a distance).
Example: Magnetic force, Gravitational force, Electrostatic force.
5.
State three ways by which we can increase friction to make movements safe and controlled. Give practical examples.
(3)
Solution:
Three ways to increase friction:
  1. Making surfaces rough: Treads are made on car tyres or spikes on sports shoes to increase grip.
  2. Using coarse powders/substances: Gymnasts and kabaddi players rub their hands with coarse powder or soil for better grip.
  3. Pressing surfaces harder: Brake pads in a bicycle are pressed hard against the moving rim to increase friction and stop the cycle.
6.
What is buoyancy? Why does an ordinary iron nail sink in water, but a large ship made of the same iron floats?
(3)
Solution:
Buoyancy: The upward force exerted by a fluid on an object immersed in it is called buoyancy (or upthrust).

Reason: An ordinary iron nail is solid, meaning its overall density is much higher than water. Its weight is greater than the buoyant force of the small amount of water it displaces, causing it to sink. A ship, however, is hollow and traps a lot of air. This makes its average density less than that of water. It displaces a massive volume of water, creating a large buoyant force equal to its weight, which allows it to float.
7.
State any three key differences between Mass and Weight.
(3)
Solution:
  1. Definition: Mass is the amount of matter contained in a body, whereas weight is the gravitational pull exerted by the Earth on that object.
  2. Constancy: Mass is constant everywhere in the universe, whereas weight varies from place to place depending on the local acceleration due to gravity.
  3. Units: The SI unit of mass is Kilogram (kg), whereas the SI unit of weight is Newton (N).
SECTION C: Long Answer Type (6 Marks Each)
8.
a) Explain the "turning effect" of force. Provide two everyday examples where this effect is observed. (3)
b) Friction is often called a "necessary evil". Justify this statement by stating two advantages and two disadvantages of friction. (3)
(6)
Solution:
a) Turning Effect of Force:
The turning effect of a force (also known as torque or moment of force) is its ability to rotate an object around a fixed point or pivot. It depends on the magnitude of the force and the perpendicular distance from the pivot.
Examples: 1) Pushing a door open using its handle (located far from the hinges). 2) Turning a steering wheel or opening a bottle cap.

b) Friction as a necessary evil:
Advantages (Necessary): Enables us to walk on the ground without slipping; allows vehicles to stop effectively when brakes are applied; enables us to write on paper.
Disadvantages (Evil): Causes wear and tear of moving parts in machinery and shoe soles; generates wasteful heat, reducing the efficiency of engines; opposes motion, requiring extra energy to overcome it.
9.
a) State the principle of flotation. (2)
b) Explain how a submarine uses the principle of buoyancy and average density to sink below the ocean surface and rise back up. (4)
(6)
Solution:
a) Principle of flotation:
An object will float in a fluid if the weight of the fluid displaced by its immersed part is equal to the total weight of the object.

b) Application in a Submarine:
Submarines have special hollow compartments called ballast tanks.
To sink (Dive): Water is allowed to enter the ballast tanks. This displaces the air and increases the submarine's average density and total weight. When its weight becomes greater than the buoyant upthrust, the submarine dives.
To rise (Surface): Compressed air is forcefully pumped into the ballast tanks, pushing the water out. This decreases the submarine's average density and total weight. When its weight becomes less than the buoyant upthrust, the submarine rises back to the surface.

SECTION A: Objective & Very Short Answer (2 Marks Each)
10.
Evaluate the following expression with negative exponents:
$\{ (\frac{1}{3})^{-1} - (\frac{1}{4})^{-1} \}^{-1}$
(2)
Solution:
Expression: $\{ (\frac{1}{3})^{-1} - (\frac{1}{4})^{-1} \}^{-1}$
Using the property $(\frac{a}{b})^{-n} = (\frac{b}{a})^n$:
$= \{ (\frac{3}{1})^1 - (\frac{4}{1})^1 \}^{-1}$
$= \{ 3 - 4 \}^{-1}$
$= \{-1\}^{-1}$
$= \frac{1}{-1} = -1$
Answer: $-1$
11.
Write the linear equation in one variable for the following statement:
"Three consecutive integers add up to 51."
(2)
Solution:
Let the first integer be $x$.
The next consecutive integers will be $(x + 1)$ and $(x + 2)$.
According to the statement, their sum is 51.
$x + (x + 1) + (x + 2) = 51$
$3x + 3 = 51$
Equation: $3x + 3 = 51$ (or $3x = 48$)

SECTION B: Short Answer Type (3 Marks Each)
13.
Simplify the following using the laws of exponents:
$\frac{3^{-5} \times 10^{-5} \times 125}{5^{-7} \times 6^{-5}}$
(3)
Solution:
Given expression: $\frac{3^{-5} \times 10^{-5} \times 125}{5^{-7} \times 6^{-5}}$
Step 1: Prime factorize composite bases ($10 = 2 \times 5$, $125 = 5^3$, $6 = 2 \times 3$).
$= \frac{3^{-5} \times (2 \times 5)^{-5} \times 5^3}{5^{-7} \times (2 \times 3)^{-5}}$
Step 2: Apply power of a product rule $(ab)^n = a^n b^n$.
$= \frac{3^{-5} \times 2^{-5} \times 5^{-5} \times 5^3}{5^{-7} \times 2^{-5} \times 3^{-5}}$
Step 3: Group and cancel common terms with the same base.
$= (\frac{2^{-5}}{2^{-5}}) \times (\frac{3^{-5}}{3^{-5}}) \times (\frac{5^{-5} \times 5^3}{5^{-7}})$
$= 1 \times 1 \times \frac{5^{-5+3}}{5^{-7}}$
$= \frac{5^{-2}}{5^{-7}}$
Step 4: Use quotient rule $a^m / a^n = a^{m-n}$.
$= 5^{-2 - (-7)} = 5^{-2+7} = 5^5$
Answer: $5^5$ (or $3125$)
14.
Solve the following linear equation for $y$:
$\frac{3y - 2}{4} - \frac{2y + 3}{3} = \frac{2}{3} - y$
(3)
Solution:
Equation: $\frac{3y - 2}{4} - \frac{2y + 3}{3} = \frac{2}{3} - \frac{y}{1}$
The LCM of the denominators ($4$, $3$, $3$, $1$) is $12$. Multiply the entire equation by $12$:
$12 \times \frac{(3y - 2)}{4} - 12 \times \frac{(2y + 3)}{3} = 12 \times \frac{2}{3} - 12 \times y$
$3(3y - 2) - 4(2y + 3) = 4(2) - 12y$
$9y - 6 - 8y - 12 = 8 - 12y$
$(9y - 8y) - 18 = 8 - 12y$
$y - 18 = 8 - 12y$
Transposing $y$ terms to one side and constants to the other:
$y + 12y = 8 + 18$
$13y = 26$
$y = \frac{26}{13} = 2$
Answer: $y = 2$
15.
Find the value of $m$ if:
$(\frac{-3}{5})^{-4} \times (\frac{-3}{5})^{-5} = (\frac{-3}{5})^{3m+1}$
(3)
Solution:
Equation: $(\frac{-3}{5})^{-4} \times (\frac{-3}{5})^{-5} = (\frac{-3}{5})^{3m+1}$
Using the law of exponents $a^x \times a^y = a^{x+y}$ on the LHS:
$(\frac{-3}{5})^{-4 + (-5)} = (\frac{-3}{5})^{3m+1}$
$(\frac{-3}{5})^{-9} = (\frac{-3}{5})^{3m+1}$
Since the bases are identical on both sides, their powers must be equal:
$-9 = 3m + 1$
$-9 - 1 = 3m$
$-10 = 3m$
$m = \frac{-10}{3}$
Answer: $m = -\frac{10}{3}$
16.
Simplify the algebraic expression:
$a(a^2 + a + 1) + 5$ and find its value for $a = -1$.
(3)
Solution:
Step 1: Simplify the expression
$= a(a^2 + a + 1) + 5$
Multiply '$a$' with each term inside the bracket:
$= (a \times a^2) + (a \times a) + (a \times 1) + 5$
$= a^3 + a^2 + a + 5$

Step 2: Find the value for $a = -1$
Substitute $a = -1$ into the simplified expression:
$= (-1)^3 + (-1)^2 + (-1) + 5$
$= -1 + 1 - 1 + 5$
$= 0 - 1 + 5 = 4$
Answer: Simplified Expression $= a^3 + a^2 + a + 5$ ; Value $= 4$
SECTION C: Long Answer Type (6 Marks Each)
17.
a) Simplify using laws of exponents: $(\frac{5}{8})^{-7} \times (\frac{8}{5})^{-5}$ (3)

b) Subtract the sum of $(3x - y + 11)$ and $(-y - 11)$ from $(3x - y - 11)$. (3)
(6)
Solution:
a) Simplification of Exponents:
Expression: $(\frac{5}{8})^{-7} \times (\frac{8}{5})^{-5}$
Convert the first term so the bases match using the rule $(\frac{a}{b})^{-x} = (\frac{b}{a})^x$:
$= (\frac{8}{5})^7 \times (\frac{8}{5})^{-5}$
Apply the product rule $a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}$:
$= (\frac{8}{5})^{7 + (-5)} = (\frac{8}{5})^2$
$= \frac{8^2}{5^2} = \frac{64}{25}$

b) Algebraic Subtraction:
Step 1: Find the sum of the first two expressions.
Sum $= (3x - y + 11) + (-y - 11)$
$= 3x - y - y + 11 - 11 = 3x - 2y$
Step 2: Subtract this sum from $(3x - y - 11)$.
$= (3x - y - 11) - (3x - 2y)$
$= 3x - y - 11 - 3x + 2y$
Group like terms:
$= (3x - 3x) + (-y + 2y) - 11$
$= y - 11$
Answers: a) $\frac{64}{25}$   b) $y - 11$
18.
Word Problem (Linear Equations):
The sum of the digits of a two-digit number is $9$. When we interchange the digits, it is found that the resulting new number is greater than the original number by $27$.
a) Formulate the algebraic equation for the given situation.
b) Find the original two-digit number.
(6)
Solution:
a) Algebraic Equation Formulation:
Let the unit's digit of the original two-digit number be $x$.
Since the sum of the digits is $9$, the ten's digit must be $(9 - x)$.
Value of the Original Number $= 10 \times (\text{ten's digit}) + (\text{unit's digit})$
$= 10(9 - x) + x = 90 - 10x + x = 90 - 9x$

On interchanging the digits, the new unit's digit is $(9 - x)$ and the new ten's digit is $x$.
Value of the New Number $= 10 \times (x) + (9 - x) = 10x + 9 - x = 9x + 9$

The condition states that the New Number is greater than the Original Number by $27$:
$(\text{New Number}) - (\text{Original Number}) = 27$
Equation: $(9x + 9) - (90 - 9x) = 27$

b) Finding the original number:
Solve the formulated equation:
$9x + 9 - 90 + 9x = 27$
$18x - 81 = 27$
$18x = 27 + 81$
$18x = 108$
$x = \frac{108}{18} = 6$
So, the unit's digit ($x$) is $6$.
The ten's digit is $(9 - 6) = 3$.
Answer: The original two-digit number is $36$.
