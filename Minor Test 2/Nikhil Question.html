Minor Test 02
Class: 8 ICSE Subject: Mathematics (Cubes & Cube Roots) Time: 1.5 Hours Max. Marks: 60
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Section A comprises 6 questions of 2 marks each.
  • Section B comprises 6 questions of 3 marks each.
  • Section C comprises 6 questions of 5 marks each.
  • Write your answers neatly and show clear mathematical steps where required.
SECTION A (12 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Find the cube of the numbers $1.2$ and $-0.04$.
2.
Determine whether $243$ is a perfect cube. Give mathematical reasons for your answer.
3.
Find the cube root of $-2744$ using the prime factorization method.
4.
What is the smallest number by which $392$ must be multiplied to make it a perfect cube?
5.
Determine the digit at the unit's place for the cube of the following numbers:
(i) $314$
(ii) $829$
6.
Evaluate the following expression: $\sqrt[3]{125 \times (-512)}$.
SECTION B (18 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
7.
Find the cube root of the decimal number $0.003375$.
8.
Find the smallest number by which $8640$ must be divided so that the quotient becomes a perfect cube. Also, find the cube root of the quotient.
9.
Three numbers are in the ratio $2 : 3 : 4$. The sum of their cubes is $33957$. Find the exact values of the three numbers.
10.
Evaluate the following fractional cube root:

$\sqrt[3]{\frac{-2197}{9261}}$
11.
The volume of a cubical box is $32.768 \text{ m}^3$. Find the length of its edge in meters.
12.
Using the properties of cubes and their roots, evaluate the following expression without expanding it completely:

$\sqrt[3]{-343} \times \sqrt[3]{-1000} \times \sqrt[3]{-125}$
SECTION C (30 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
13.
The difference between two perfect cubes is $189$. If the cube root of the smaller of the two numbers is $3$, find the cube root of the larger number. Explain your logical steps.
14.
A solid metallic cuboid of dimensions $9 \text{ cm} \times 12 \text{ cm} \times 16 \text{ cm}$ is melted and completely recast into a perfect cube.
(a) Find the length of the edge of the new cube. (3 Marks)
(b) Find the total surface area of the newly formed cube. (2 Marks)
15.
Find the smallest perfect cube which is exactly divisible by each of the numbers $5$, $10$, and $15$. Show the prime factorization of your final answer.
16.
Evaluate the following mathematical expression step-by-step:

$\sqrt[3]{27} + \sqrt[3]{0.008} + \sqrt[3]{0.064}$
17.
Find the cube root of $13824$ using the prime factorization method. Using this result, determine the value of:

$\sqrt[3]{13.824} + \sqrt[3]{0.013824}$
18.
A physical education teacher wants to arrange $4096$ students in a solid cubical formation for a 3D display.
(a) How many students will be there in each row of the front face of this formation? (3 Marks)
(b) If the teacher decides to add exactly one more student to each dimension (length, width, and height) of the formation, how many additional students will be needed? (2 Marks)
