Minor Test 02
Class: 6 ICSE Subject: Mathematics (Number System) Time: 1.5 Hours Max. Marks: 60
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Section A comprises 6 questions of 2 marks each.
  • Section B comprises 6 questions of 3 marks each.
  • Section C comprises 6 questions of 5 marks each.
  • Write your answers neatly and show clear mathematical steps where required.
SECTION A (12 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Insert commas suitably and write the number name according to the Indian System of Numeration: 87595762.
2.
Insert commas suitably and write the number name according to the International System of Numeration: 78921092.
3.
Write the following in numerical form: "Seven crore fifty-two lakh twenty-one thousand three hundred two".
4.
Find the difference between the place value and face value of the digit '7' in the number 27650934.
5.
Write the following Hindu-Arabic numerals in Roman Numerals:
(a) 73                      (b) 94
6.
Estimate the sum (5,290 + 17,986) by rounding off each number to its nearest thousands.
SECTION B (18 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
7.
Write the expanded form of the number 34,56,789.
8.
Arrange the following numbers in ascending order:
8435, 4835, 13584, 5348, 25843.
9.
Write the greatest and smallest 5-digit numbers using the digits 6, 2, 7, 4, 3 each only once, and find their absolute difference.
10.
A factory machine, on average, manufactures 2,825 screws a day. How many screws did it produce in the month of January 2024?
11.
Convert the following Hindu-Arabic numerals to Roman numerals and add them: 45 + 56. Express the final sum in Roman numerals.
12.
Find the product of 578 × 161 using suitable estimation (by rounding off each number to its greatest place value).
SECTION C (30 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
13.
Answer the following questions based on number systems:
(a) Write the number name for 54003211 in both the Indian and International systems of numeration. (2 Marks)
(b) How many thousands make a million? (1 Mark)
(c) How many lakhs make a crore? (1 Mark)
(d) Write the successor of the number 99,99,999. (1 Mark)
14.
Medicine is packed in boxes, with each box weighing 4 kg 500 g. How many such boxes can be loaded in a transport van which cannot carry a load beyond 800 kg? Show all your conversions and calculation steps clearly.
15.
Follow the instructions to form numbers:
(a) Form the largest 6-digit number using the digits 3, 0, 5, 8 (you may repeat any digits as required) and write its number name in the Indian system. (3 Marks)
(b) Write the predecessor of the smallest 7-digit number. (2 Marks)
16.
A merchant had ₹ 78,592 with her. She placed an order for purchasing 40 radio sets at ₹ 1,200 each.
(a) What is the total cost of purchasing the 40 radio sets? (2 Marks)
(b) How much money will remain with her after making this purchase? (3 Marks)
17.
Perform the following estimations:
(a) Round off 4,398 and 312 to the nearest hundreds and find their estimated sum. (2 Marks)
(b) Round off 8,325 and 491 to the nearest tens and find their estimated difference. (3 Marks)
18.
In a state election, the successful candidate registered 5,77,500 votes and his nearest rival secured 3,48,700 votes.
(a) By what margin did the successful candidate win the election? (3 Marks)
(b) Write the winning margin in words according to the International System of numeration. (2 Marks)
