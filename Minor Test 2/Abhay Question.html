Minor Test
Class: 9 ICSE Subject: Chemistry & Mathematics Time: 1.5 Hours Max. Marks: 60
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Section A comprises 6 questions of 2 marks each.
  • Section B comprises 6 questions of 3 marks each.
  • Section C comprises 6 questions of 5 marks each.
  • Write your answers neatly and show clear logical steps for mathematical calculations.
SECTION A (12 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
Part I: Chemistry (Language of Chemistry)
1.
Write the chemical formula for: (i) Calcium carbonate (ii) Ammonium sulphate.
2.
Define 'valency'. What is the valency of Oxygen in a water molecule (H2O)?
3.
Balance the following basic chemical equation:
H2 + O2 → H2O
Part II: Mathematics (Factorization)
4.
Factorize completely by taking out the common factors: 3x2 − 12x.
5.
Factorize by grouping the terms: a2 − b2 − 2a + 2b.
6.
Factorize using the difference of two squares identity: x2 − 9.
