MINOR TEST - MATHEMATICS (HARSH CLASS 8)
Class: 8 CBSE Subject: Mathematics Time: 1.5 Hours Max. Marks: 60
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory. Internal choices are provided in Section C.
  • Section A consists of 10 questions of 2 marks each.
  • Section B consists of 5 questions of 4 marks each.
  • Section C consists of 4 questions of 5 marks each.
  • Show all calculations clearly in the workspace provided on your sheet.
SECTION A (20 Marks)(Objective & Concept Recall - 2 Marks Each)
Q1.
Define a Whole Number. What is the smallest Whole Number?
Q2.
State the fundamental difference between a Prime Number and a Composite Number with one example of each.
Q3.
Identify the property: "The sum of any two Natural Numbers is always a Natural Number."
Q4.
Find the HCF of 12 and 18 using the listing method.
Q5.
Represent the number -3 and 5 on a single integer number line.
Q6.
What is the LCM of two co-prime numbers 7 and 11? Justify your answer.
Q7.
Can the HCF of two numbers be greater than the numbers themselves? Explain why or why not.
Q8.
Find the prime factorization of 48.
Q9.
True or False: "Every Natural Number is a Whole Number, but every Whole Number is not a Natural Number." Explain.
Q10.
Find the least number which is exactly divisible by 4 and 6.
SECTION B (20 Marks)(Short Answer & Application - 4 Marks Each)
Q11.
Find the HCF of 144 and 198 using the Long Division Method.
Q12.
Find the LCM of 24, 36, and 40 using the Common Division Method.
Q13.
Represent the following on a number line:
(a) A number 4 units to the right of -2.
(b) A number 3 units to the left of 1.
Q14.
The HCF of two numbers is 16 and their product is 3072. Find their LCM using the relationship formula.
Q15.
Express 90 as the product of its prime factors in exponential form. Identify if 90 is a perfect square.
SECTION C (20 Marks)(Long Answer & Problem Solving - 5 Marks Each)
Q16.
Three bells toll at intervals of 12, 15, and 18 minutes respectively. If they toll together at 9:00 AM, at what time will they next toll together?
Q17.
Find the greatest number which can divide 257 and 329 leaving a remainder of 5 in each case.
Q18.
Draw a number line and mark the following:
(i) The successor of -5.
(ii) The predecessor of 0.
(iii) The smallest prime number.
(iv) The additive inverse of 3.
(v) A whole number that is not a natural number.
Q19.
Two tankers contain 850 litres and 680 litres of petrol respectively. Find the maximum capacity of a container which can measure the petrol of either tanker in exact number of times.
