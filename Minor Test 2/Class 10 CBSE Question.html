Minor Test 02
Class: 10 CBSE Subject: Science & Mathematics Time: 1.5 Hours Max. Marks: 60
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Section A comprises 6 questions of 2 marks each.
  • Section B comprises 6 questions of 3 marks each.
  • Section C comprises 6 questions of 5 marks each.
  • Write your answers neatly, use standard scientific notations, and show clear steps for mathematical calculations.
SECTION A (12 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
Part I: Science (Chemical Reactions and Equations)
1.
Before burning in air, a magnesium ribbon is thoroughly cleaned with sandpaper. Give the scientific reason for this. Also, write the balanced chemical equation for its combustion.
2.
Identify the oxidizing agent and the reducing agent in the following redox reaction:
Fe2O3 + 3CO → 2Fe + 3CO2
3.
When silver chloride is exposed to sunlight for some time, its color changes from white to grey. Name the type of reaction involved and write the corresponding chemical equation.
Part II: Mathematics (Real Numbers)
4.
Given that HCF (306, 657) = 9, mathematically deduce the LCM of (306, 657).
5.
Check whether 6n can end with the digit 0 for any natural number n. Provide a fundamental mathematical justification for your answer.
6.
Explain why (7 × 11 × 13 + 13) and (7 × 6 × 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 + 5) are categorized as composite numbers.
SECTION B (18 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
Part I: Science (Chemical Reactions and Equations)
7.
When an aqueous solution of potassium iodide is added to an aqueous solution of lead nitrate, a significant change is observed.
(a) State the observation and name the insoluble substance formed.
(b) Write the balanced chemical equation for the reaction.
(c) What specific category of double displacement reaction is this?
8.
Translate the following skeletal equations into balanced chemical equations:
(a) HNO3 + Ca(OH)2 → Ca(NO3)2 + H2O
(b) BaCl2 + Al2(SO4)3 → BaSO4 + AlCl3
(c) NaOH + H2SO4 → Na2SO4 + H2O
9.
Decomposition reactions require energy either in the form of heat, light, or electricity to break down the reactants. Provide one balanced chemical equation each for decomposition reactions where energy is supplied in the form of: (i) Heat, (ii) Light, and (iii) Electricity.
Part II: Mathematics (Real Numbers)
10.
Prove that 3 + 2√5 is an irrational number, given the fundamental premise that √5 is an irrational number.
11.
Three school bells toll at regular intervals of 12 minutes, 15 minutes, and 18 minutes respectively. If they all start tolling together at 9:00 AM, at what precise time will they next toll together simultaneously?
12.
Find the largest possible positive integer that will divide exactly 70 and 125, leaving remainders 5 and 8 respectively.
SECTION C (30 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
Part I: Science (Chemical Reactions and Equations)
13.
In the context of the electrolysis of water:
(a) Explain the entire process briefly. (Diagram not strictly required if steps are precise, but state the setup).
(b) Identify the gases collected at the anode and the cathode.
(c) Why is the volume of gas collected at one electrode exactly double the volume of the gas collected at the other electrode?
(d) Why are a few drops of dilute sulphuric acid added to pure water before starting the electrolysis?
14.
Analyze the chemical behavior of metals and acids:
(a) What happens when zinc granules are dropped into a test tube containing dilute hydrochloric acid? Write the balanced chemical equation for the same.
(b) How will you conclusively test the presence of the gas evolved in the above reaction?
(c) "Respiration is universally considered an exothermic reaction." Scientifically justify this statement with the help of a relevant chemical equation.
15.
Identify the specific type of reactions taking place in each of the following cases and write the corresponding balanced chemical equations:
(a) Zinc metal reacts with aqueous silver nitrate to produce zinc nitrate and solid silver.
(b) Aqueous Potassium bromide reacts with aqueous Barium iodide to give aqueous Potassium iodide and solid Barium bromide.
(c) Define rancidity. Suggest any two effective industrial or domestic methods to prevent rancidity of food materials containing fats and oils.
Part II: Mathematics (Real Numbers)
16.
Prove by contradiction that √3 is an irrational number. Using this established result, further prove that the expression (2 - √3)/5 is also an irrational number.
17.
There is a circular pathway around a sports field. Sonia takes exactly 18 minutes to drive one complete round of the field, while Ravi takes 12 minutes for the same. Suppose they both start at the same designated starting point and at the exact same time, and go in the same direction.
(a) After how many minutes will they logically meet again at the starting point?
(b) Calculate the precise number of rounds completed by Sonia and Ravi respectively when they meet.
18.
Apply the concepts of Real Numbers to solve the following:
(a) Two premium brands of chocolates are available in wholesale packs of 24 and 15 respectively. If a retailer needs to buy an equal number of chocolates of both kinds for a festival hamper, what is the absolute least number of boxes of each kind he would need to purchase?
(b) Using the prime factorization method, calculate both the HCF and LCM of 144, 180, and 192. Ensure your final answer clearly displays the fundamental prime factors.
