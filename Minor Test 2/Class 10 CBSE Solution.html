1.
Before burning in air, a magnesium ribbon is thoroughly cleaned with sandpaper. Give the scientific reason for this. Also, write the balanced chemical equation for its combustion.
Reason: Magnesium ribbon is cleaned to remove the protective layer of basic magnesium carbonate / magnesium oxide from its surface so that it may readily react with oxygen. [1 Mark]
Equation: 2Mg(s) + O2(g) → 2MgO(s) + Heat + Light [1 Mark]

Fe2O3 + 3CO → 2Fe + 3CO2
Oxidizing Agent: Fe2O3 (as it is providing oxygen / undergoing reduction). [1 Mark]
Reducing Agent: CO (as it is taking oxygen / undergoing oxidation). [1 Mark]
3.
When silver chloride is exposed to sunlight for some time, its color changes from white to grey. Name the type of reaction involved and write the corresponding chemical equation.
Type of reaction: Photolytic Decomposition Reaction (or Photolysis). [1 Mark]
Equation: 2AgCl(s) (sunlight)→ 2Ag(s) + Cl2(g) [1 Mark]
Part II: Mathematics (Real Numbers)
4.
Given that HCF (306, 657) = 9, mathematically deduce the LCM of (306, 657).
Formula: LCM(a, b) × HCF(a, b) = a × b
LCM × 9 = 306 × 657 [1 Mark]
LCM = (306 × 657) / 9 = 34 × 657 = 22338 [1 Mark]
5.
Check whether 6n can end with the digit 0 for any natural number n. Provide a fundamental mathematical justification for your answer.
Prime factorization of 6n = (2 × 3)n = 2n × 3n. For a number to end with 0, it must have both 2 and 5 as prime factors. [1 Mark]
By the Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic, this prime factorization is unique. Since it does not contain the prime factor 5, 6n can never end with the digit 0. [1 Mark]
6.
Explain why (7 × 11 × 13 + 13) and (7 × 6 × 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 + 5) are categorized as composite numbers.
1st expression: 13 × (7 × 11 + 1) = 13 × 78. Since it can be expressed as a product of primes/integers other than 1 and itself, it is a composite number. [1 Mark]
2nd expression: 5 × (7 × 6 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 + 1) = 5 × 1009. Having factors other than 1 and itself, it is composite. [1 Mark]

Part II: Mathematics (Real Numbers)
10.
Prove that 3 + 2√5 is an irrational number, given the fundamental premise that √5 is an irrational number.
Let us assume 3 + 2√5 is rational. Therefore, we can find coprime integers a and b (b ≠ 0) such that 3 + 2√5 = a/b. [1 Mark]
Rearranging: 2√5 = (a/b) - 3 ⇒ √5 = (a - 3b) / 2b. [1 Mark]
Since a and b are integers, (a - 3b)/2b is rational, so √5 is rational. But this contradicts the given fact that √5 is irrational. Hence, our assumption is false and 3 + 2√5 is irrational. [1 Mark]
11.
Three school bells toll at regular intervals of 12 minutes, 15 minutes, and 18 minutes respectively. If they all start tolling together at 9:00 AM, at what precise time will they next toll together simultaneously?
To find the time when they toll together again, we calculate the LCM of 12, 15, and 18.
12 = 22 × 3
15 = 3 × 5
18 = 2 × 32 [1 Mark]
LCM = 22 × 32 × 5 = 4 × 9 × 5 = 180 minutes. [1 Mark]
180 minutes = 3 hours. They will toll together next at 9:00 AM + 3 hours = 12:00 PM (Noon). [1 Mark]
12.
Find the largest possible positive integer that will divide exactly 70 and 125, leaving remainders 5 and 8 respectively.
The required number divides (70 - 5) = 65 and (125 - 8) = 117 exactly. [1 Mark]
We must find the HCF of 65 and 117.
65 = 5 × 13
117 = 32 × 13 [1 Mark]
HCF = 13. Therefore, the largest possible integer is 13. [1 Mark]

Part II: Mathematics (Real Numbers)
16.
Prove by contradiction that √3 is an irrational number. Using this established result, further prove that the expression (2 - √3)/5 is also an irrational number.
Part 1: Let √3 be a rational number = a/b (where a, b are coprime integers, b ≠ 0).
√3 = a/b ⇒ 3 = a2 / b2 ⇒ a2 = 3b2.
Since 3 divides a2, 3 divides a. Let a = 3c.
Substituting: (3c)2 = 3b2 ⇒ 9c2 = 3b2 ⇒ b2 = 3c2.
Since 3 divides b2, 3 divides b. Thus, a and b have a common factor 3, contradicting the fact that they are coprime. Hence, √3 is irrational. [3 Marks]
Part 2: Let (2 - √3)/5 be a rational number p/q.
2 - √3 = 5p/q ⇒ √3 = 2 - (5p/q) = (2q - 5p) / q.
Since p, q are integers, (2q - 5p)/q is rational, meaning √3 is rational. This contradicts that √3 is irrational. Thus, (2 - √3)/5 is irrational. [2 Marks]
17.
There is a circular pathway around a sports field. Sonia takes exactly 18 minutes to drive one complete round of the field, while Ravi takes 12 minutes for the same. Suppose they both start at the same designated starting point and at the exact same time, and go in the same direction.
(a) After how many minutes will they logically meet again at the starting point?
(b) Calculate the precise number of rounds completed by Sonia and Ravi respectively when they meet.
(a) Time taken to meet again is the LCM of 18 and 12.
18 = 2 × 32; 12 = 22 × 3.
LCM = 22 × 32 = 4 × 9 = 36. They will meet again after 36 minutes. [3 Marks]
(b) Rounds by Sonia = Total Time / Time per round = 36 / 18 = 2 rounds.
Rounds by Ravi = 36 / 12 = 3 rounds. [2 Marks]
