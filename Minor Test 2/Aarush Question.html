Minor Test 02
Class: 7 CBSE Subject: SST & Mathematics Time: 1.5 Hours Max. Marks: 60
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Section A comprises 6 questions of 2 marks each.
  • Section B comprises 6 questions of 3 marks each.
  • Section C comprises 6 questions of 5 marks each.
  • Write your answers neatly and show clear logical steps for mathematical calculations.
SECTION A (12 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
Part I: Social Studies (The Story of Indian Farming)
1.
Define agriculture and name any two allied activities associated with it.
2.
Name the three main cropping seasons in India.
3.
Mention any two traditional methods used by Indian farmers to conserve soil.
Part II: Mathematics
4.
Insert commas suitably and write the number name of 5432908 according to the Indian System of Numeration.
5.
Simplify the given arithmetic expression: 15 − (4 × 3) + 8.
6.
Write an algebraic expression for the statement: "Five times a number 'x' decreased by 7."
SECTION B (18 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
Part I: Social Studies (The Story of Indian Farming)
7.
Differentiate between Kharif and Rabi crops, providing the time of sowing, harvest, and one example for each.
8.
Why is the monsoon considered a highly critical factor for Indian agriculture? Briefly explain the role of the Southwest Monsoon.
9.
Mention and briefly describe any three modern methods of irrigation used in agriculture today.
Part II: Mathematics
10.
Convert the decimal 4.75 into a fraction in its simplest form, and then add 2.5 to it. Express your final answer as a decimal number.
11.
Simplify the following arithmetic expression using the BODMAS rule:
24 ÷ [ 8 − (3 + 1) ] × 5
12.
If a = 3 and b = −2, find the exact numerical value of the algebraic expression:
2a2 + 3ab − b2
SECTION C (30 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
Part I: Social Studies (The Story of Indian Farming)
13.
Describe the major challenges faced by Indian farmers today. Provide at least five distinct points highlighting these difficulties.
14.
Explain the complete process of soil formation (weathering and inclusion of organic matter). In addition, list the main characteristics and one major crop of both Alluvial Soil and Black Soil.
15.
Compare traditional farming practices with modern farming methods. While modern farming has increased agricultural production, what are the serious demerits and environmental challenges associated with it?
Part II: Mathematics
16.
A large manufacturing company produced 45,67,890 pens in a single year. Out of these, 12,34,500 pens were sold in the domestic market, and 23,45,000 pens were exported.
(a) Estimate the remaining number of pens by rounding off each number to the nearest lakh. (3 Marks)
(b) Calculate the exact number of remaining pens left with the company. (2 Marks)
17.
A tailor needs exactly 1.25 meters of cloth to stitch one standard shirt. If he possesses a fresh roll containing exactly 30 meters of cloth:
(a) How many complete shirts can he logically stitch? (3 Marks)
(b) How much cloth (in meters) will be left over after stitching the maximum number of shirts? (2 Marks)
18.
Formulate clear algebraic expressions (using letter numbers) for the following situations:
(a) Ram's age is 3 years more than twice Shyam's age. If Shyam's age is given as 'y', what is the algebraic expression for Ram's age? (2 Marks)
(b) Write the expression for the perimeter of a rectangle whose length is 'l' units, and its breadth is exactly 5 units less than its length. Simplify the final expression. (3 Marks)
