Minor Test 02
Class: 7 ICSE Subject: History & Mathematics Time: 1.5 Hours Max. Marks: 60
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Section A comprises 6 questions of 2 marks each.
  • Section B comprises 6 questions of 3 marks each.
  • Section C comprises 6 questions of 5 marks each.
  • Write your answers neatly and show clear, logical steps for mathematical calculations.
SECTION A (12 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
Part I: History (The Advent of Christianity)
1.
Who was Emperor Constantine? State his major contribution towards the spread of Christianity in the Roman Empire.
2.
Differentiate between 'Monks' and 'Friars' in the context of the medieval Christian monastic order.
3.
What immediate political consequence occurred in the Roman Empire after the death of Emperor Theodosius?
Part II: Mathematics (Integers & Decimals)
4.
Evaluate using suitable properties of integers: 26 × (−48) + (−48) × (−36). State the property used.
5.
Divide 42.8 by 0.02 and write the final answer.
6.
A submarine is situated at 850 m below sea level. If it ascends 240 m, what is its new position? Represent your final answer using a proper integer sign.
SECTION B (18 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
Part I: History (The Advent of Christianity)
7.
Briefly explain the main teachings of Jesus Christ as outlined in the text. Mention the scriptures where these teachings are recorded.
8.
The decline of the Roman Empire was not a one-day event. State any three prominent internal causes that led to the decline of this vast empire.
9.
What were the Crusades? Quote the specific belief/promise sanctioned by Pope Urban II in 1095 that encouraged people to fight in these wars.
Part II: Mathematics (Integers & Decimals)
10.
Simplify the following expression using the BODMAS rule:
15 − [−3 − {4 − (5 − 2)}]
11.
The total cost of 15.5 kg of premium quality rice is ₹ 581.25. Calculate the exact cost of 1 kg of this rice.
12.
In a competitive quiz, positive marks are awarded for correct answers and negative marks for incorrect answers. If Rohit's scores in 5 successive rounds were 25, −5, −10, 15, and 10, what was his total score at the end of the quiz?
SECTION C (30 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
Part I: History (The Advent of Christianity)
13.
The fall of the Roman Empire created a void in the political and social life of Europe, bringing Christian Monasteries into prominence.
(a) Describe the daily spartan life and code of conduct of the monks and nuns in these monasteries. (2 Marks)
(b) Analyze the impact of these monasteries on medieval Europe, highlighting their role in education, society, and the preservation of knowledge. (3 Marks)
14.
The Crusades brought about significant transformations in medieval Europe. Elaborate on the results of the Crusades. How did they pave the path for the Renaissance and affect the power dynamics between kings and feudal lords?
15.
Write a detailed historical note on the Barbarian invasions of the Roman Empire. In your answer, include:
(a) The initial relationship between the Goths and the Romans. (2 Marks)
(b) The roles played by King Alaric and the Germanic leader Odoacer. (3 Marks)
Part II: Mathematics (Integers & Decimals)
16.
Solve the following decimal operations:
(a) Simplify: 2.5 × 4.8 + 5.6 ÷ 0.7 − 3.2. Show all working steps clearly. (3 Marks)
(b) Convert 0.045 into a fraction and reduce it to its absolute simplest form. (2 Marks)
17.
An elevator descends into a deep mine shaft at the uniform rate of 6 m/min.
(a) If the descent starts from 10 m above the ground level, how much time will it logically take to reach a depth of −350 m? (3 Marks)
(b) What will be the exact position of the elevator after 25 minutes of continuous descent from the same starting point? (2 Marks)
18.
Apply integer and decimal properties to solve:
(a) Evaluate (−45) × 102 using the distributive property of multiplication over addition. (2 Marks)
(b) A master tailor requires exactly 2.25 m of cloth to stitch one standard shirt. How many complete shirts can he logically stitch from a fresh roll of cloth measuring 31.5 m? (3 Marks)
