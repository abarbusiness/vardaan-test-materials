Class: 6 ICSE Subject: Mathematics (Number System) Max. Marks: 60
SECTION A (12 Marks)
1. Insert commas suitably and write the number name according to the Indian System: 87595762. [2 Marks]
Solution: Commas: 8,75,95,762
Number Name: Eight crore seventy-five lakh ninety-five thousand seven hundred sixty-two.
2. Insert commas suitably and write the number name according to the International System: 78921092. [2 Marks]
Solution: Commas: 78,921,092
Number Name: Seventy-eight million nine hundred twenty-one thousand ninety-two.
3. Write in numerical form: "Seven crore fifty-two lakh twenty-one thousand three hundred two". [2 Marks]
Solution: Numerical Form: 7,52,21,302
4. Find the difference between the place value and face value of '7' in 27650934. [2 Marks]
Solution: Place value of 7 in 2,76,50,934 = 70,00,000
Face value of 7 = 7
Difference = 70,00,000 - 7 = 69,99,993
5. Write in Roman Numerals: (a) 73 (b) 94 [2 Marks]
Solution: (a) 73 = 70 + 3 = LXX + III = LXXIII
(b) 94 = 90 + 4 = (100 - 10) + 4 = XC + IV = XCIV
6. Estimate the sum (5,290 + 17,986) by rounding off to nearest thousands. [2 Marks]
Solution: 5,290 rounds off to 5,000
17,986 rounds off to 18,000
Estimated Sum = 5,000 + 18,000 = 23,000
SECTION B (18 Marks)

3 × 10,00,000 + 4 × 1,00,000 + 5 × 10,000 + 6 × 1,000 + 7 × 100 + 8 × 10 + 9 × 1
8. Arrange in ascending order: 8435, 4835, 13584, 5348, 25843. [3 Marks]
Solution: Ascending Order: 4835 < 5348 < 8435 < 13584 < 25843
9. Greatest and smallest 5-digit numbers using 6, 2, 7, 4, 3 (once) and their absolute difference. [3 Marks]
Solution: Greatest number = 76,432
Smallest number = 23,467
Difference = 76,432 - 23,467 = 52,965
10. A machine produces 2,825 screws a day. Production in January 2024? [3 Marks]
Solution: Number of days in January = 31
Screws per day = 2,825
Total screws = 2,825 × 31
Calculation: 2825 × 30 = 84750; 2825 × 1 = 2825.
84750 + 2825 = 87,575 screws.
11. Convert to Roman numerals and add: 45 + 56. Express sum in Roman numerals. [3 Marks]
Solution: 45 in Roman = XLV
56 in Roman = LVI
Sum = 45 + 56 = 101
Sum in Roman = CI
12. Find the product of 578 × 161 using suitable estimation (greatest place value). [3 Marks]
Solution: 578 rounds off to nearest hundreds = 600
161 rounds off to nearest hundreds = 200
Estimated Product = 600 × 200 = 1,20,000
SECTION C (30 Marks)
13. Number Systems: (a) Name 54003211 (b) 1 Million = ? thousands (c) 1 Crore = ? lakhs (d) Successor of 99,99,999. [5 Marks]
Solution: (a) Indian: 5,40,03,211 - Five crore forty lakh three thousand two hundred eleven.
    Intl: 54,003,211 - Fifty-four million three thousand two hundred eleven.
(b) 1 Million = 1000 thousands.
(c) 1 Crore = 100 lakhs.
(d) Successor of 99,99,999 = 99,99,999 + 1 = 1,00,00,000 (One crore).
14. Boxes weight 4 kg 500 g. How many can fit in an 800 kg van? [5 Marks]
Solution: Weight of one box = 4 kg 500 g = 4,500 g
Max capacity of van = 800 kg = 8,00,000 g
Number of boxes = 8,00,000 ÷ 4,500
= 8,000 ÷ 45
= 177.77...
Since we cannot load a partial box, total boxes = 177 boxes.
15. (a) Largest 6-digit number using 3, 0, 5, 8. (b) Predecessor of smallest 7-digit number. [5 Marks]
Solution: (a) To form the largest 6-digit number, repeat the largest digit '8'.
Number: 8,88,530 (Eight lakh eighty-eight thousand five hundred thirty).
(b) Smallest 7-digit number = 10,00,000
Predecessor = 10,00,000 - 1 = 9,99,999.
16. Purchase of 40 radio sets @ ₹ 1,200 each from ₹ 78,592. [5 Marks]
Solution: (a) Cost of 1 radio = ₹ 1,200
Cost of 40 radios = 1,200 × 40 = ₹ 48,000
(b) Initial money = ₹ 78,592
Money remaining = 78,592 - 48,000 = ₹ 30,592
17. Estimation: (a) Nearest hundreds (4398+312). (b) Nearest tens (8325-491). [5 Marks]
Solution: (a) 4,398 rounds to 4,400; 312 rounds to 300.
Sum = 4,400 + 300 = 4,700
(b) 8,325 rounds to 8,330; 491 rounds to 490.
Difference = 8,330 - 490 = 7,840
18. Election votes: 5,77,500 vs 3,48,700. Margin in words (International System). [5 Marks]
Solution: (a) Margin = 5,77,500 - 3,48,700 = 2,28,800 votes.
(b) In International System: 228,800
Words: Two hundred twenty-eight thousand eight hundred.
