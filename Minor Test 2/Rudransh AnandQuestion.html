Minor Test 02
Class: 8 CBSE Subject: Mathematics Time: 1.5 Hours Max. Marks: 60
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Section A comprises 6 questions of 2 marks each.
  • Section B comprises 6 questions of 3 marks each.
  • Section C comprises 6 questions of 5 marks each.
  • Write your answers neatly and show clear mathematical steps where required.
SECTION A (12 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Without actual multiplication, find the square of the number $85$. Briefly state the property used.
2.
Express $81$ as the sum of $9$ odd numbers.
3.
Find the square root of $4096$ using the prime factorization method.
4.
Find the cube root of $3375$ using the prime factorization method.
5.
Observe the given pattern and find the missing value:
$11^2 = 121$
$101^2 = 10201$
$1001^2 = 1002001$
$1000001^2 = ?$
6.
Find the smallest whole number by which $252$ must be multiplied to get a perfect square number.
SECTION B (18 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
7.
Find the square root of $17.64$ by the long division method.
8.
Is $3087$ a perfect cube? If not, find the smallest number by which $3087$ must be divided so that the quotient is a perfect cube.
9.
The area of a square playground is $256.6404 \text{ m}^2$. Find the length of one side of the playground.
10.
Three numbers are in the ratio $1 : 2 : 3$ and the sum of their cubes is $4500$. Find the three numbers.
11.
Find the cube root of $0.000512$. Show the steps of your calculation clearly.
12.
Using the properties of squares, evaluate the following without doing actual multiplication:
(a) $54^2 - 53^2$
(b) $105^2 - 104^2$
SECTION C (30 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
13.
Find the least number that must be added to $1300$ so as to get a perfect square. Also, find the square root of the perfect square so obtained.
14.
Find the greatest $4$-digit number which is a perfect square. Also, find its square root.
15.
Find the smallest square number that is exactly divisible by each of the numbers $4$, $9$, and $10$. Detail your logical steps clearly.
16.
Evaluate the following expression step-by-step:

$\sqrt{156.25} + \sqrt{0.0081} - \sqrt{0.0361}$
17.
The volume of a cubical box is $13.824 \text{ m}^3$. Find the length of each edge of the box in meters. Also, convert the final edge length into centimeters.
18.
A General wishes to draw up his $7500$ soldiers in the form of a solid square. After arranging them, he found that some of them were left over.
(a) How many soldiers were left over? (3 Marks)
(b) How many soldiers are there in the front row? (2 Marks)
