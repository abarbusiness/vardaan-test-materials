Promise by Pope Urban II: He sanctioned them by preaching that fighting in a crusade would ensure the forgiveness of sins, quoting: "All who die by the way, whether by land or by sea, or in battle against the [Muslims], shall have immediate [forgiveness] of sins."

SECTION C (30 Marks)
Part I: History
Ans 13.
(a) Life in Monasteries: Monks and nuns lived a spartan, orderly life, shunning luxury, comfort, and marriage. They followed a very rigid code of conduct where their day was divided into three parts: one part for prayer, one for sleep, and the third for intellectual and manual labour.
(b) Impact on Medieval Europe:
1. Education & Knowledge: Monasteries emerged as the only source of education. Monks studied literature, science, art, and religion. Since printing was not yet invented, they wrote books by hand on parchment, acting as a crucial source of knowledge.
2. Social Work: They educated children, treated the sick, and extended help to the poor, needy, and destitute.
3. Art & Culture: They became important centers where architecture, mural painting, woodwork, and glass painting flourished.
Ans 14.
The results of the Crusades were highly impactful on medieval Europe:
1. Scientific Transfer & Renaissance: Knowledge in the areas of science, medicine, optics, engineering, and algebra was transferred from the Islamic and Greek worlds to the West. This influx of ancient and modern knowledge paved the path for the Renaissance in Europe.
2. New Inventions: Christians started the use of gunpowder, a technique they learned from the Muslims.
3. Towns & Universities: Inventions from the Arabs began to be taught as new courses in European universities. Many new towns and cities were established by traders.
4. Decline of Feudal Lords: Traders and manufacturers demanded better law and order, paying taxes directly to the king. This helped kings raise large armies, increasing their authority and leading to a significant decline in the power of feudal lords.
Ans 15.
(a) Relationship with Goths: The Goths were nomadic Germanic tribes fleeing from a more powerful enemy, the Huns. They initially sought shelter in Rome and found protection. However, they soon turned against the Romans who had provided them with that shelter.
(b) Role of Alaric and Odoacer:
• Under the young king Alaric, the West Goths attacked the Roman empire several times. After laying siege to Rome twice, he eventually captured and plundered the city the third time.
• Successive waves of tribes (Vandals, Saxons, Franks) ravaged the empire. In 476 CE, Odoacer, a Germanic leader, overthrew Romulus (the last Roman Emperor in the west), becoming the first Barbarian ruler of Rome and bringing a final end to the Western Roman Empire.
