Minor Test 07
Class: 9 ICSE Subject: Biology (Cell & Tissue) Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 40
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Section A contains 5 questions of 2 marks each.
  • Section B contains 5 questions of 3 marks each.
  • Section C contains 3 questions of 5 marks each.
  • Draw neat and labelled diagrams wherever necessary.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Who discovered the cell, and in which year was it discovered?
2.
State any two main postulates of the Cell Theory.
3.
Define a 'tissue'. Give one example of a plant tissue.
4.
Name the 'powerhouse' of the cell and the 'suicidal bags' of the cell.
5.
What is the main function of chloroplasts? Which green pigment do they contain?
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
State any three main differences between a plant cell and an animal cell.
7.
What are meristematic tissues? State any two characteristics of the cells of this tissue.
8.
Name the three types of muscle tissues found in animals. Which one of these is voluntary in nature?
9.
Briefly state one main function for each of the following cell parts:
(i) Ribosomes
(ii) Nucleus
(iii) Cell membrane
10.
Draw a neat, labeled diagram of a typical animal cell (label at least three parts).
SECTION C (15 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
(a) What is xylem tissue? State its primary function in plants. (2 Marks)
(b) Name the four different types of cells (elements) that make up xylem tissue. (2 Marks)
(c) Is xylem mostly a living tissue or a dead tissue? (1 Mark)
12.
(a) Draw a well-labeled diagram of a single nerve cell (Neuron). (3 Marks)
(b) What is the main function of nervous tissue in the human body? (2 Marks)
13.
(a) Differentiate between Prokaryotic and Eukaryotic cells based on: (i) Nucleus, (ii) Membrane-bound organelles, and (iii) Size. (3 Marks)
(b) Name the jelly-like fluid present inside the nucleus. (1 Mark)
(c) Name the type of plastid responsible for imparting colors (red, orange, yellow) to flowers and fruits. (1 Mark)
