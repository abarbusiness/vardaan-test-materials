SECTION B (15 Marks)
4.
Ans: Monocots vs. Dicots:
FeatureMonocotyledonsDicotyledons
SeedsHave only one cotyledon (seed leaf).Have two cotyledons (seed leaves).
Leaf VenationLeaves show parallel venation.Leaves show reticulate (net-like) venation.
Root SystemPossess a fibrous root system.Possess a tap root system.
[3 Marks: 1 Mark for each contrasting parameter]
5.
Ans: Characteristics of Mammalia:
1. They have mammary glands to produce milk and nourish their young ones. [1 Mark]
2. Their body is covered with hair or fur. [1 Mark]
3. They give birth to live young ones (viviparous) and are warm-blooded animals. [1 Mark]
6.
Ans: Amphibians:
Amphibians are cold-blooded vertebrates that can live both on land and in water. They generally lay their eggs in water. [1 Mark]
Examples: Frog, Toad, Salamander (Any two). [2 Marks: 1 Mark for each example]
7.
Ans: Cryptogams vs Phanerogams:
Cryptogams: These are non-flowering and non-seed-bearing plants. They reproduce by means of spores. [1 Mark]
Example: Fern, Moss, Spirogyra (Any one). [0.5 Mark]
Phanerogams: These are flowering and seed-bearing plants. [1 Mark]
Example: Mango, Pine, Rose (Any one). [0.5 Mark]
8.
Ans: Characteristics of Fungi:
1. They are non-green organisms (lack chlorophyll) and exhibit a heterotrophic/saprotrophic mode of nutrition. [1 Mark]
2. Their plant body is usually made up of thread-like structures called hyphae. [1 Mark]
3. They reproduce primarily by forming spores. [0.5 Mark]
Example: Mushroom, Yeast, Mucor, Rhizopus (Any one). [0.5 Mark]
SECTION C (20 Marks)
9.
Ans: Binomial Nomenclature:
(a) Binomial Nomenclature: It is the scientific method of naming living organisms with a two-part name (Genus and Species). [1 Mark]
It was introduced by Carolus Linnaeus. [1 Mark]

(b) Rules (Any two):
1. The first word is the Genus name and starts with a capital letter. [1 Mark]
2. The second word is the species name and starts with a small letter. [1 Mark]
(Also accept: Scientific names are usually derived from Latin. They must be printed in italics or underlined separately when handwritten).

(c) Scientific name of human beings: Homo sapiens (must be underlined or italicized). [1 Mark]
10.
Ans: Classification and Arthropoda:
(a) Classification:
(i) Earthworm: Phylum Annelida [1 Mark]
(ii) Starfish: Phylum Echinodermata [1 Mark]
(iii) Snake: Class Reptilia (Phylum Chordata) [1 Mark]

(b) Features of Arthropoda (Any two):
1. They have jointed legs/appendages. [1 Mark]
2. Their body is covered with a hard, protective exoskeleton made of chitin. [1 Mark]
3. The body is segmented and divided into head, thorax, and abdomen.
11.
Ans: Amoeba:
(a) Diagram: A neat, irregular drawing of Amoeba. [1.5 Marks for correct structure]
Labels: Pseudopodia, Nucleus, Food vacuole, Contractile vacuole, Cytoplasm. (Any three). [1.5 Marks: 0.5 for each label]

(b) Kingdom: Protista. [1 Mark]
Characteristic: Organisms in this kingdom are unicellular and eukaryotic (having a well-defined nucleus). [1 Mark]
12.
Ans: Gymnosperms vs Angiosperms:
(a) Gymnosperms: They are non-flowering plants that produce naked seeds (seeds are not enclosed inside a fruit). [1.5 Marks]
Angiosperms: They are highly developed flowering plants whose seeds are completely enclosed inside a fruit. [1.5 Marks]

(b) Examples of Gymnosperms: Pine, Cycas, Fir, Cedar (Any two). [2 Marks: 1 Mark for each example]
