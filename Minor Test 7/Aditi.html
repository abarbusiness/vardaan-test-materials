Minor Test 07
Class: 8 ICSE Subject: Biology (Transport in Plants & Circulatory System) Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 40
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Section A contains 5 questions of 2 marks each.
  • Section B contains 5 questions of 3 marks each.
  • Section C contains 3 questions of 5 marks each.
  • Draw neat and labelled diagrams wherever necessary.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Define transpiration. Name the specialized microscopic pores on leaves through which it mostly occurs.
2.
State the main function of red blood cells (RBCs) and the pigment responsible for it.
3.
Briefly explain the difference between diffusion and osmosis.
4.
In the ABO blood group system, which blood group is known as the 'Universal Donor' and which is the 'Universal Recipient'?
5.
What is the function of the bicuspid and tricuspid valves in the human heart?
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
State any three main structural and functional differences between arteries and veins.
7.
Describe the role of the following in the human body:
(i) White Blood Cells (WBCs)
(ii) Blood Platelets
(iii) Blood Plasma
8.
What is xylem? Explain its primary function in plants and mention whether it conducts substances upwards or downwards.
9.
Mention any three external factors that affect the rate of transpiration in plants, and state how they affect it.
10.
Explain how root hair cells are structurally adapted to absorb water from the soil efficiently.
SECTION C (15 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
(a) Draw a neat and well-labeled diagram showing the internal structure of the human heart. (3 Marks)
(b) Why is the wall of the left ventricle thicker than the wall of the right ventricle? (2 Marks)
12.
(a) Explain the term 'Ascent of Sap' in plants. Name the two main forces responsible for pushing and pulling water up a tall tree. (3 Marks)
(b) Apart from creating a pull for water, write two other ways in which transpiration is useful to plants. (2 Marks)
13.
(a) What is meant by 'Double Circulation' in the human body? Briefly explain its two main pathways. (3 Marks)
(b) Define pulse rate. What is the normal resting pulse rate of a healthy adult human? (2 Marks)
