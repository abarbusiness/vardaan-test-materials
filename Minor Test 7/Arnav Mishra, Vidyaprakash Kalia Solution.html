Since animals cannot make their own food and rely on plants, they do not require or possess chloroplasts. [1 Mark]

Pasteurization: It is the process of heating milk to about 70°C for 15 to 30 seconds and then suddenly chilling and storing it. This process prevents the growth of microbes without boiling. [2 Marks]
Scientist: Louis Pasteur. [1 Mark]
9.
Ans:
(i) Cell Membrane: Provides shape to the cell and controls the entry and exit of substances. [1 Mark]
(ii) Nucleus: Acts as the control center of all cell activities and contains genes for inheritance. [1 Mark]
(iii) Cytoplasm: Jelly-like substance where all other cell organelles are present and cellular chemical reactions take place. [1 Mark]

Ans:
Uses of microorganisms:
1. Making of curd, cheese, and bread (baking industry).
2. Commercial production of alcohol and wine (fermentation).
3. Preparation of medicines (antibiotics) and vaccines.
4. Cleaning up the environment by decomposing organic waste.
[1 Mark each for any three valid uses = 3 Marks]
SECTION C (25 Marks)
11.
Ans:
Diagram: A neat and appropriately shaped block diagram of a plant cell.
Mandatory Labels: Cell Wall, Cell Membrane, Large Central Vacuole, Nucleus, Chloroplasts, Cytoplasm.
[5 Marks Total: 2 Marks for correct structural drawing + 3 Marks for at least 4-5 correct labels (including cell wall & vacuole)]
12.
Ans:
(a) Vaccine: A substance containing dead or weakened (attenuated) microbes introduced into a healthy body to produce immunity. [1 Mark]
How it works: When the vaccine is introduced, the body's immune system recognizes the weakened microbes and produces antibodies to fight them. The body remembers the microbe, and these antibodies remain in the blood, protecting the body from future attacks by the actual disease-causing microbe. [2 Marks]

(b) Diseases prevented by vaccination: Polio, Cholera, Tuberculosis, Smallpox, Hepatitis, etc. [2 Marks: 1 Mark for each correct disease]
13.
Ans:
(a) Prokaryotes vs. Eukaryotes:
- Prokaryotes: Cells that do not have a well-organized nucleus (lack a nuclear membrane). The genetic material is free in the cytoplasm. Example: Bacteria or Blue-green algae. [1.5 Marks]
- Eukaryotes: Cells that have a well-organized nucleus surrounded by a distinct nuclear membrane. Example: Plant cells, Animal cells, Fungi. [1.5 Marks]

(b) Chromosomes as carriers of heredity: Chromosomes are thread-like structures found in the nucleus. They carry genes, which are the units of inheritance. Genes are responsible for transferring characters (traits) from the parents to the offspring, which is why chromosomes are called the carriers of heredity. [2 Marks]
14.
Ans:
(a) Role of Rhizobium: Rhizobium bacteria live in the root nodules of leguminous plants (like peas, beans). They are able to fix atmospheric nitrogen gas into usable nitrogenous compounds (like nitrates). This enriches the soil with nitrogen, thereby increasing soil fertility naturally. [3 Marks]

(b) Food Preservatives: Chemicals that check the growth of microorganisms in food items to prevent them from spoiling are called preservatives. [1 Mark]
Examples: Sodium benzoate, Sodium metabisulphite, common salt, sugar, oil, vinegar. [1 Mark for any two examples (½ + ½)]
15.
Ans:
(a) Organism: Amoeba. [1 Mark]
Functions of pseudopodia (false feet):
1. Facilitate cellular movement (locomotion). [1 Mark]
2. Help in capturing food (engulfing food particles). [1 Mark]

(b) Pathogen (causative microorganism): Plasmodium (a Protozoan). [1 Mark]
Carrier (vector): Female Anopheles mosquito. [1 Mark]
