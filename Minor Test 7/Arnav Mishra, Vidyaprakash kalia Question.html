Minor Test 07
Class: 8 CBSE Subject: Science (Cell & Microorganisms) Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Section A contains 5 questions of 2 marks each.
  • Section B contains 5 questions of 3 marks each.
  • Section C contains 5 questions of 5 marks each.
  • Draw neat and labelled diagrams wherever necessary.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Name the instrument used to observe cells. Who was the first scientist to discover the cell?
2.
What are antibiotics? Give one example of a widely used antibiotic.
3.
Why are chloroplasts found only in plant cells and not in animal cells?
4.
Name the microorganism that promotes the formation of curd from milk. Mention the major group (bacteria, fungi, protozoa, or algae) to which it belongs.
5.
Define a 'tissue' with respect to the structural organization of organisms.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
State three main ways by which communicable diseases can spread from an infected person to a healthy person.
7.
State any three key structural differences between a plant cell and an animal cell.
8.
What is pasteurization? Name the scientist who discovered this process.
9.
Explain the basic function of the following parts of a cell:
(i) Cell Membrane
(ii) Nucleus
(iii) Cytoplasm
10.
Microorganisms are not always harmful; they are our friends too. List any three uses of microorganisms in our daily life.
SECTION C (25 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
Draw a neat, well-labelled diagram of a plant cell. (Label at least 4 important parts including the cell wall and central vacuole).
12.
(a) What is a vaccine? How does vaccination work to protect our body against diseases? (3 Marks)
(b) Name any two diseases that can be effectively prevented by vaccination. (2 Marks)
13.
(a) Differentiate between Prokaryotes and Eukaryotes based on their nuclear structure, and give one example of each. (3 Marks)
(b) Chromosomes are often referred to as the 'carriers of heredity'. Explain the reason behind this statement. (2 Marks)
14.
(a) Describe the role of Rhizobium bacteria in agricultural soil. (3 Marks)
(b) What are food preservatives? Give two examples of common chemical preservatives used in our homes. (2 Marks)
15.
(a) While observing a drop of pond water under a microscope, Rahul observed a single-celled organism with irregular finger-like projections called pseudopodia. Identify the organism and state the two main functions of pseudopodia. (3 Marks)
(b) Name the specific pathogen (microorganism) that causes Malaria and the carrier insect that spreads it. (2 Marks)
