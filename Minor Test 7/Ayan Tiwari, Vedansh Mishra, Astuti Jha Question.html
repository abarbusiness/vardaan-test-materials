Minor Test 07
Class: 6 ICSE Subject: Biology (The Leaf) Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Section A contains 5 questions of 2 marks each.
  • Section B contains 5 questions of 3 marks each.
  • Section C contains 5 questions of 5 marks each.
  • Draw neat and labelled diagrams wherever necessary.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
What is the broad, flat, green part of the leaf called? What is its main function?
2.
Name the two main types of venation found in leaves.
3.
What are stomata? State their primary role.
4.
Define the term 'Transpiration'.
5.
What is the function of a petiole (leaf stalk)?
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
State any three main functions of a leaf.
7.
Differentiate between a simple leaf and a compound leaf with one example each.
8.
What is photosynthesis? Name the raw materials required by a plant to carry out this process.
9.
Why are leaves mostly green in colour? Name the green pigment and state its specific function.
10.
Identify the leaf modification in the following plants and state its purpose:
(i) Cactus
(ii) Pea plant
SECTION C (25 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
Draw a neat and well-labeled diagram of a typical leaf. Label the following parts: Lamina (Leaf blade), Petiole, Midrib, Veins, and Leaf base.
12.
(a) Differentiate between Reticulate venation and Parallel venation. (3 Marks)
(b) Give one example of a plant showing reticulate venation and one showing parallel venation. (2 Marks)
13.
(a) Explain the process of photosynthesis in simple words. Write the word equation for photosynthesis. (3 Marks)
(b) Why is photosynthesis considered a very important process for all living beings on Earth? (2 Marks)
14.
(a) What is meant by the 'modification of leaves'? (2 Marks)
(b) Describe three different ways in which leaves are modified to perform special functions, giving one example for each. (3 Marks)
15.
Describe a simple experiment to prove that leaves lose water through transpiration. (Include the materials required, the steps, and the observation).
