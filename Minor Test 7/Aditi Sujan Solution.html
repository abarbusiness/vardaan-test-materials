Class: 8 ICSE Subject: Biology (Transport in Plants & Circulatory System) Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 40
SECTION A (10 Marks)
1.
Ans:
Transpiration: It is the process of loss of water in the form of water vapor from the aerial parts of the plant (leaves and stems). [1 Mark]
Pores: Stomata. [1 Mark]
2.
Ans:
Main function: To transport oxygen from the lungs to all the cells of the body. [1 Mark]
Pigment: Haemoglobin (an iron-containing pigment). [1 Mark]
3.
Ans:
Diffusion: It is the movement of particles (solid, liquid, or gas) from a region of their higher concentration to a region of their lower concentration without any membrane. [1 Mark]
Osmosis: It is the movement of water molecules from a region of higher water concentration to lower water concentration strictly through a semi-permeable membrane. [1 Mark]
4.
Ans:
Universal Donor: Blood Group O. [1 Mark]
Universal Recipient: Blood Group AB. [1 Mark]
5.
Ans:
Valves are present between the auricles and ventricles. Their main function is to prevent the backward flow of blood into the auricles when the ventricles contract. [2 Marks]

7.
Ans:
(i) White Blood Cells (WBCs): They act as the body's defense system by fighting against germs and infections. [1 Mark]
(ii) Blood Platelets: They help in the clotting of blood at the site of a cut or wound to prevent excessive blood loss. [1 Mark]
(iii) Blood Plasma: It is the fluid part of the blood that transports digested food, carbon dioxide, and nitrogenous wastes. [1 Mark]
8.
Ans:
Xylem: It is a complex vascular plant tissue. [1 Mark]
Function: It conducts water and dissolved minerals absorbed by roots to the leaves and other aerial parts. [1 Mark]
Direction: Upwards (Unidirectional flow). [1 Mark]
9.
Ans:
Factors affecting transpiration (Any three):
1. Temperature: Higher temperature increases the rate of transpiration.
2. Light intensity: Transpiration is faster in bright sunlight as stomata open wider.
3. Wind speed: Fast-moving wind removes water vapor faster, increasing the rate.
4. Humidity: High humidity in the air reduces the rate of transpiration.
[3 Marks: 1 Mark each for naming the factor and correctly stating its effect]
10.
Ans:
Adaptations of root hair cells:
1. They have a large surface area which helps in maximum absorption. [1 Mark]
2. The cell wall is freely permeable and the cell membrane is semi-permeable, allowing water to pass through easily. [1 Mark]
3. The cell sap inside the root hair has a higher concentration of salts compared to soil water, ensuring water moves in by osmosis. [1 Mark]
SECTION C (15 Marks)
11.
Ans:
(a) Diagram of the Heart: A neat diagram showing 4 chambers, aorta, vena cava, pulmonary artery/vein, and valves.
Labels: Right auricle, Right ventricle, Left auricle, Left ventricle, Septum, Aorta.
[3 Marks Total: 1.5 Marks for correct structural diagram + 1.5 Marks for any 3 accurate labels (0.5 each)]

(b) Left Ventricle Wall: The left ventricle has thicker muscular walls because it has to pump blood with high pressure to the entire body, whereas the right ventricle only pumps blood to the nearby lungs. [2 Marks]
12.
Ans:
(a) Ascent of Sap: The upward movement of water and dissolved minerals from the roots to the tip of the stem/leaves through the xylem is called the ascent of sap. [1 Mark]
Forces responsible:
1. Root Pressure (a pushing force from the bottom). [1 Mark]
2. Transpiration Pull (a suction force created at the top by loss of water). [1 Mark]

(b) Usefulness of Transpiration:
1. It provides a cooling effect to the plant during hot weather. [1 Mark]
2. It helps in the continuous distribution of water and absorbed minerals to all parts of the plant. [1 Mark]
13.
Ans:
(a) Double Circulation: It means that blood flows twice through the heart during one complete cycle in the body. [1 Mark]
Pathways:
1. Pulmonary Circulation: Flow of blood from the heart to the lungs (for oxygenation) and back to the heart. [1 Mark]
2. Systemic Circulation: Flow of blood from the heart to all body organs and back to the heart. [1 Mark]

(b) Pulse Rate: It is the number of times the heart beats (or arteries throb) per minute. [1 Mark]
Normal Rate: 72 to 80 beats per minute in a normal resting adult. [1 Mark]
