Minor Test 07
Class: 9 CBSE Subject: Science (Biology) Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 40
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Section A contains 5 questions of 2 marks each.
  • Section B contains 5 questions of 3 marks each.
  • Section C contains 3 questions of 5 marks each.
  • Draw neat and labelled diagrams wherever necessary.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Who discovered cells, and what material did he observe under his self-designed microscope?
2.
Define the term 'tissue'. Give one example of an animal tissue.
3.
Why is the plasma membrane called a selectively permeable membrane?
4.
Name the two main categories of plant tissues based on their dividing capacity.
5.
Which cell organelle is known as the 'powerhouse of the cell' and why?
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
State any three key differences between a plant cell and an animal cell.
7.
What are stomata? State any two important functions performed by stomata in plants.
8.
Where are proteins synthesized inside the cell? Briefly describe the organelle responsible for this process.
9.
Identify the specific type of tissue present in the following:
(i) Skin
(ii) Bark of a tree
(iii) Bone
10.
What will happen if a living plant cell is placed in a concentrated sugar solution (hypertonic solution)? Name the process involved.
SECTION C (15 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
(a) Draw a neat and well-labelled diagram of a typical animal cell. (3 Marks)
(b) Name the organelle known as the 'suicide bags' of the cell. Why are they called so? (2 Marks)
12.
(a) Differentiate between Parenchyma, Collenchyma, and Sclerenchyma on the basis of their cell walls. (3 Marks)
(b) Name the complex permanent tissues in plants. State one primary function for each. (2 Marks)
13.
(a) Draw a neat, well-labelled diagram of a nerve cell (Neuron). (3 Marks)
(b) Name the three types of muscular tissues found in the human body. Which of these are voluntary in nature? (2 Marks)
