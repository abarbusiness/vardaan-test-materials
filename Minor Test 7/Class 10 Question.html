Minor Test 07
Class: 10 CBSE Subject: Science (Biology - Life Processes) Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 40
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Section A contains 5 questions of 2 marks each.
  • Section B contains 5 questions of 3 marks each.
  • Section C contains 3 questions of 5 marks each.
  • Draw neat and labelled diagrams wherever necessary.
SECTION A (10 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 2 Marks Each)
1.
Why is diffusion insufficient to meet the oxygen requirements of multicellular organisms like humans?
2.
Name the site of complete digestion of carbohydrates, proteins, and fats in the human body. Mention the two major secretions it receives to accomplish this.
3.
State any two major differences between aerobic and anaerobic respiration.
4.
How are the alveoli in human lungs designed to maximize the exchange of gases?
5.
What is the significance of the acidic medium in the human stomach? Name the gland that secretes it.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
6.
(a) Draw a neat, labeled diagram of a stomatal pore (open state).
(b) What are the two main functions of stomata?
7.
Explain the process of double circulation in the human heart. Why is this type of circulation necessary in humans?
8.
Describe the exact role of the following in the human digestive system:
(i) Salivary amylase
(ii) Bile juice
(iii) Villi
9.
List the three main biochemical events that occur consecutively during the process of photosynthesis.
10.
How is the amount of urine produced regulated in the human body? Mention two major factors influencing it.
SECTION C (15 Marks)(Long Answer Type - 5 Marks Each)
11.
(a) Draw a schematic representation of the transport and exchange of oxygen and carbon dioxide across the human body (alveoli to tissues). (3 Marks)
(b) Explain the components of the transport system in highly organized plants. How does the transport of water differ from the transport of food? (2 Marks)
12.
(a) Draw a well-labeled diagram of the human excretory system. (3 Marks)
(b) Explain the mechanism of urine formation in the nephron, detailing the processes of filtration and selective reabsorption. (2 Marks)
13.
(a) Trace the pathway of inhaled air from the nostrils to the alveoli in the human respiratory system. (3 Marks)
(b) Differentiate between arteries and veins on the basis of: (i) direction of blood flow, (ii) wall thickness, (iii) presence of valves, and (iv) type of blood carried. (2 Marks)
