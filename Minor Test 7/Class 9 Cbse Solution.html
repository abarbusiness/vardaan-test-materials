Minor Test 07 - MARKING SCHEME & SOLUTIONS
Class: 9 CBSE Subject: Science (Biology - Cell & Tissue) Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 40
SECTION A (10 Marks)
1.
Ans:
Cells were discovered by Robert Hooke in 1665. [1 Mark]
He observed a thin slice of cork (substance from the bark of a tree) under his microscope. [1 Mark]
2.
Ans:
Tissue: A group of cells that are similar in structure and work together to achieve a particular function forms a tissue. [1 Mark]
Example: Blood, Bone, Muscle, or Epithelium. [1 Mark for any valid example]
3.
Ans:
The plasma membrane is called a selectively permeable membrane because it allows or permits the entry and exit of only some selected materials in and out of the cell. [1 Mark]
It also prevents movement of some other materials. [1 Mark]
4.
Ans:
The two main categories of plant tissues are:
1. Meristematic Tissue (dividing tissue)
2. Permanent Tissue (non-dividing tissue)
[1 Mark each = 2 Marks]
5.
Ans:
Organelle: Mitochondria. [1 Mark]
Reason: They release energy required for various chemical activities of life in the form of ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate) molecules. [1 Mark]

7.
Ans:
Stomata are small pores present in the epidermis of leaves, enclosed by two kidney-shaped guard cells. [1 Mark]
Functions:
1. Exchange of gases (O2 and CO2) with the atmosphere. [1 Mark]
2. Transpiration (loss of water in the form of water vapor). [1 Mark]
8.
Ans:
Proteins are synthesized by Ribosomes. [1 Mark]
Description: Ribosomes are small, dense, spherical structures found freely suspended in the cytoplasm or attached to the surface of the Rough Endoplasmic Reticulum (RER). They are the sites of protein manufacture. [2 Marks]
9.
Ans:
(i) Skin: Stratified Squamous Epithelium (or Epithelial Tissue) [1 Mark]
(ii) Bark of a tree: Cork / Protective Tissue (contains suberin) [1 Mark]
(iii) Bone: Connective Tissue (Skeletal connective tissue) [1 Mark]
10.
Ans:
If placed in a concentrated sugar solution, the plant cell will lose water by osmosis and shrink. The cell contents will shrink away from the cell wall. [2 Marks]
Process involved: This phenomenon is known as Plasmolysis (or Exosmosis). [1 Mark]
SECTION C (15 Marks)
11.
Ans:
(a) Diagram of Animal Cell: Must be neat and appropriately shaped without a cell wall.
Labels: Cell membrane, Nucleus, Cytoplasm, Mitochondria, Golgi apparatus, Endoplasmic Reticulum.
[3 Marks: 1 for diagram structure, 2 for any 4 correct labels (½ mark each)]

(b) Suicide bags: Lysosomes. [1 Mark]
Reason: When the cell gets damaged, lysosomes may burst and the powerful digestive enzymes inside them end up digesting their own cell. [1 Mark]
12.
Ans:
(a) Difference on basis of cell wall:
1. Parenchyma: Cell wall is thin and made of cellulose. [1 Mark]
2. Collenchyma: Cell wall is thickened at the corners due to pectin deposition. [1 Mark]
3. Sclerenchyma: Cell wall is very thick due to lignin deposition (lignified walls). [1 Mark]

(b) Complex permanent tissues: Xylem and Phloem. [1 Mark (½ + ½)]
Function: Xylem transports water and minerals from roots to leaves. Phloem transports food (sugar) from leaves to other parts of the plant. [1 Mark (½ + ½)]

Ans:

Labels: Cell body (Cyton), Dendrite, Axon, Nucleus, Nerve ending.
[3 Marks: 1 mark for correct figure + 2 marks for 4 correct labels]

(b) Three types of muscular tissue:
1. Striated muscle (Skeletal)
2. Smooth muscle (Unstriated)
3. Cardiac muscle
[1 Mark for naming all three (deduct ½ mark if one is missing)]
Voluntary Muscle: Striated (Skeletal) muscles are voluntary in nature as they move as per our conscious will. [1 Mark]
