6.
Ans: Types of leaf venation:
1. Reticulate Venation: Veins form a net-like design. Example: Peepal, Mango, Rose (Any one). [1.5 Marks]
2. Parallel Venation: Veins run parallel to each other. Example: Grass, Banana, Wheat (Any one). [1.5 Marks]
7.
Ans: Definition and instrument for cells:
Cell: The cell is the smallest, basic structural and functional unit of all living organisms. [2 Marks]
Instrument: Microscope is used to observe cells clearly. [1 Mark]
8.
Ans: Functions of a leaf:
1. Photosynthesis: Leaves prepare food for the plant using sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide. [1 Mark]
2. Transpiration: Leaves help in losing excess water in the form of vapour, keeping the plant cool. [1 Mark]
3. Exchange of Gases: Leaves take in carbon dioxide and give out oxygen (during day) through stomata. [1 Mark]
SECTION C (20 Marks)
9.
Ans: Flower Diagram and Functions:
(a) Diagram: A neat diagram of a flower. [1.5 Marks for correct structure]
Labels needed: Sepal, Petal, Stamen (Anther + Filament), Pistil (Stigma + Style + Ovary). [1.5 Marks for accurate labelling]

(b) Function of Sepals: They protect the flower when it is in the bud stage. [1 Mark]
Function of Petals: Their bright colours and fragrance attract insects for pollination. [1 Mark]
10.
Ans: Plant Cell Diagram and Function:
(a) Diagram: A neat drawing of a plant cell showing a double outer boundary. [1.5 Marks for neat structure]
Labels: Cell wall, Nucleus, and Vacuole clearly pointed out. [1.5 Marks for correct labels]

(b) Function of Cell Membrane: It surrounds the cell and acts as a boundary. It controls the entry and exit of materials (like water and nutrients) into and out of the cell. [2 Marks]
11.
Ans: Simple vs Compound Leaf & Diagram:
(a) Simple Leaf: A leaf that has a single, entire, undivided lamina (leaf blade). Example: Mango leaf. [1.5 Marks]
Compound Leaf: A leaf in which the lamina is divided into smaller, separate pieces called leaflets. Example: Neem leaf. [1.5 Marks]

(b) Diagram: A drawing of a simple leaf. [1 Mark]
Labels: Lamina (the flat green part) and Petiole (the stalk attached to the stem). [1 Mark]
12.
Ans: Pollination:
(a) Pollination: It is the process of transfer of pollen grains from the anther (male part) to the stigma (female part) of a flower. [2 Marks]

(b) Differences:
Self-pollination: The transfer of pollen grains from the anther to the stigma of the same flower or another flower on the same plant. [1.5 Marks]
Cross-pollination: The transfer of pollen grains from the anther of one flower to the stigma of a flower on a different plant of the same kind. [1.5 Marks]
