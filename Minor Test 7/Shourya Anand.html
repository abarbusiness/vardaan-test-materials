Minor Test 07
Class: 6 ICSE Subject: Biology (Leaf, Flower & Cell) Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Section A contains Objective Type questions (15 Marks).
  • Section B contains Short Answer Type questions (15 Marks).
  • Section C contains Long Answer & Diagrammatic Type questions (20 Marks).
  • Draw neat and well-labelled diagrams wherever necessary.
SECTION A (15 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 1 Mark Each)
1.
Fill in the blanks with appropriate biological terms:
(a) The male reproductive part of a flower is called the __________.
(b) The green pigment present in leaves, which helps in making food, is __________.
(c) The __________ is known as the control center of the cell.
(d) The broad, flat, green part of a leaf is called the __________.
(e) The transfer of pollen grains from the anther to the stigma of a flower is called __________.
2.
State whether the following statements are True or False. If False, rewrite the statement correctly by changing the underlined word(s):
(a) The cell wall is present in both plant and animal cells.
(b) Reticulate venation shows a net-like design of veins in a leaf.
(c) The brightly coloured and attractive parts of a flower are called sepals.
(d) All living organisms are made up of basic units called cells.
(e) Stomata help the plant in the exchange of gases.
3.
Give one word or a brief biological term for the following:
(a) The scientist who first discovered and coined the term 'cell'.
(b) The process by which green plants make their own food using sunlight.
(c) The female reproductive part of a flower consisting of stigma, style, and ovary.
(d) The jelly-like substance present inside the cell, between the nucleus and the cell membrane.
(e) The loss of water in the form of water vapour from the surface of leaves.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
4.
State three main differences between a Plant Cell and an Animal Cell.
5.
Name the four main whorls (parts) of a typical flower in order from outside to inside.
6.
What are the two main types of venation found in leaves? Give one example of a plant for each type.
7.
Define a 'Cell'. Name the instrument used in laboratories to observe cells clearly.
8.
List any three important functions performed by a leaf in a plant.
SECTION C (20 Marks)(Long Answer & Diagrammatic Type - 5 Marks Each)
9.
(a) Draw a neat and well-labelled diagram of a typical Flower showing all its reproductive parts. (3 Marks)
(b) State the main function of Sepals and Petals in a flower. (2 Marks)
10.
(a) Draw a well-labelled diagram of a typical Plant Cell. (Label at least three parts: Cell wall, Nucleus, Vacuole). (3 Marks)
(b) What is the function of the cell membrane? (2 Marks)
11.
(a) Differentiate between a Simple Leaf and a Compound Leaf. (3 Marks)
(b) Draw a simple diagram of a leaf and correctly label the Lamina (Leaf blade) and the Petiole. (2 Marks)
12.
(a) What is meant by Pollination? (2 Marks)
(b) Clearly differentiate between Self-pollination and Cross-pollination. (3 Marks)
