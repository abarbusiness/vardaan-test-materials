2.
Ans:
Two main postulates of the Cell Theory are:
1. All living organisms are made up of one or more cells. (Cell is the basic structural and functional unit of life). [1 Mark]
2. All new cells arise from pre-existing cells (Omnis cellula e cellula). [1 Mark]
3.
Ans:
Tissue: A group of similar cells that perform a specific function and have a common origin. [1 Mark]
Example: Xylem, Phloem, or Meristematic tissue (Any one valid plant tissue). [1 Mark]
4.
Ans:
Powerhouse of the cell: Mitochondria. [1 Mark]
Suicidal bags of the cell: Lysosomes. [1 Mark]
5.
Ans:
Main function: Photosynthesis (synthesizing food for the plant using sunlight). [1 Mark]
Pigment: Chlorophyll. [1 Mark]

7.
Ans:
Meristematic Tissues: These are plant tissues made up of actively dividing cells responsible for the growth of the plant. [1 Mark]
Characteristics (Any two):
1. Cells have thin cell walls.
2. They have a prominent and large nucleus.
3. They have dense cytoplasm with mostly no vacuoles.
[1 Mark each for any two characteristics = 2 Marks]
8.
Ans:
Three types of muscle tissues:
1. Striated (Skeletal) Muscle
2. Unstriated (Smooth) Muscle
3. Cardiac Muscle
[2 Marks for naming all three correctly (0.5 mark deduction if one missed)]
Voluntary Muscle: Striated (Skeletal) muscle is voluntary in nature. [1 Mark]
9.
Ans:
(i) Ribosomes: Site of protein synthesis. [1 Mark]
(ii) Nucleus: Acts as the control center of the cell and carries genetic material (DNA). [1 Mark]
(iii) Cell membrane: It is selectively permeable and regulates the entry and exit of substances in and out of the cell. [1 Mark]
10.
Ans:
Diagram: A generalized animal cell drawing is required.
Key components to show: Irregular/round shape (no cell wall).
Labels: Cell membrane, Nucleus, Cytoplasm, Mitochondria, Vacuole (small).
[1.5 Marks for neat structure + 1.5 Marks for 3 correct labels (0.5 each) = 3 Marks]
SECTION C (15 Marks)
11.
Ans:
(a) Xylem Tissue: It is a complex permanent vascular tissue in plants. [1 Mark]
Function: It conducts water and dissolved minerals upwards from the roots to the stem and leaves. [1 Mark]

(b) Four elements of Xylem:
1. Tracheids
2. Vessels
3. Xylem parenchyma
4. Xylem fibres
[2 Marks: ½ Mark for each correct element]

(c) Xylem is mostly a dead tissue (only xylem parenchyma is living). [1 Mark]
12.
Ans:
(a) Diagram of Neuron: Must be neat and clearly show the star-shaped cell body and long axon.
Required Labels: Cyton (Cell body), Dendrites, Axon, Nucleus, Myelin sheath, Nerve endings.
[3 Marks: 1 for correct structural drawing + 2 for at least 4 correct labels (½ each)]

(b) Function of Nervous Tissue: It is highly specialized for receiving stimuli and rapidly transmitting impulses (messages) from one part of the body to another, thereby coordinating body functions. [2 Marks]
