1(b)
Ans: Exact location:
(i) Mitral valve: Located between the left auricle (atrium) and the left ventricle. [1 Mark]
(ii) Sino-atrial node (SAN): Located in the upper wall of the right auricle. [1 Mark]
(iii) Chordae tendineae: Attached at one end to the flaps of the bicuspid/tricuspid valves and at the other end to the papillary muscles of the ventricles. [1 Mark]
(iv) Hepatic portal vein: Located between the digestive tract (stomach/intestines) and the liver. [1 Mark]
(v) Spleen: Located in the abdomen, situated behind the stomach on the upper left side. [1 Mark]
1(c)
Ans: Main function:
(i) Thrombocytes (Platelets): Initiate the process of blood clotting (coagulation) at the site of a wound. [1 Mark]
(ii) Septum: Prevents the mixing of oxygenated blood (left side) and deoxygenated blood (right side) in the heart. [1 Mark]
(iii) Lymph nodes: Filter lymph to trap bacteria/foreign particles and produce lymphocytes (antibodies) for defense. [1 Mark]
(iv) Pulmonary vein: Carries oxygenated blood from the lungs to the left auricle of the heart. [1 Mark]
(v) Valves in veins: Prevent the backflow of blood, ensuring it flows only towards the heart. [1 Mark]

3.
Ans: Blood Vessels:
(a) Artery (A): Has a thick, elastic muscular wall and a narrow lumen to withstand the high pressure of blood flowing from the heart. [1 Mark]
Vein (B): Has a thin muscular wall and a wider lumen as blood flows under much lower pressure. [1 Mark]

(b) Artery: Carries blood away from the heart to the organs. [1 Mark]
Vein: Carries blood towards the heart from the organs. [1 Mark]

(c) Vein (B) has valves. [1 Mark]
Purpose: To prevent the backward flow of blood due to gravity, since the blood pressure in veins is very low. [1 Mark]

(d) Pulse: It is the alternate expansion and elastic recoil of the wall of the artery during ventricular systole. [1 Mark]
Location: Readily felt at the wrist (Radial artery) or the neck (Carotid artery). [1 Mark]

(e) Diagram: A neat diagram showing a single layer of squamous epithelial cells (endothelium) forming a narrow tube. [2 Marks: 1 for structure, 1 for label 'Endothelium / single cell layer']
4(a)
Ans: Biological Reasons:
(i) Lack of nucleus in RBCs: The absence of a nucleus provides more internal space for accommodating maximum haemoglobin to carry oxygen. It also gives the cell a biconcave shape, increasing the surface area for rapid oxygen diffusion. [2 Marks]

(ii) Arteries are deep-seated: Blood flows under extremely high pressure in arteries. Being deep-seated protects them from superficial cuts and injuries which could lead to severe, rapid blood loss. [2 Marks]

(iii) AB Universal Recipient: People with blood group AB have both antigens (A and B) on their RBCs and possess no antibodies (anti-A or anti-B) in their blood plasma. Thus, they can receive blood from any blood group without agglutination (clumping). [2 Marks]
4(b)
Ans: Differentiate:
(i) Diapedesis: The process by which WBCs squeeze out through the intact walls of capillaries. [1 Mark]
Phagocytosis: The process by which WBCs engulf and destroy foreign particles, bacteria, and dead cells. [1 Mark]

(ii) Erythrocytes (RBCs): Function to transport respiratory gases (mainly Oxygen). [1 Mark]
Leukocytes (WBCs): Function as the defense mechanism of the body, fighting infections and providing immunity. [1 Mark]
5.
Ans: Blood Pressure, Clotting & Lymph:
(a) Instrument: Sphygmomanometer. [1 Mark]
Normal BP: 120/80 mm Hg (Systolic 120, Diastolic 80). [1 Mark]

(b) Mechanism of Blood Clotting:
1. Injured tissues and platelets release the enzyme Thrombokinase (Thromboplastin). [1 Mark]
2. Thrombokinase, in the presence of Calcium ions, converts inactive Prothrombin (in plasma) into active Thrombin. [1 Mark]
3. Thrombin converts soluble Fibrinogen into insoluble Fibrin threads, which form a mesh trapping blood cells to form a clot. [1 Mark]

(c) Lymph: It is a clear to yellowish fluid, similar to blood plasma but lacking RBCs and large blood proteins, circulating in the lymphatic system. [1 Mark]
Functions (Any two): 1. Returns leaked fluid and proteins from tissues back to the blood. 2. Absorbs and transports digested fats from the intestine. 3. Lymphocytes present in it defend against infections. [2 Marks: 1 Mark for each function]
