Minor Test 07 - MARKING SCHEME & SOLUTIONS
Class: 6 ICSE Subject: Biology (The Leaf) Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 50
SECTION A (10 Marks)
1.
Ans:
The broad, flat, green part of the leaf is called the Lamina (or Leaf blade). [1 Mark]
Its main function is to prepare food for the plant through photosynthesis. [1 Mark]
2.
Ans:
The two main types of venation are:
1. Reticulate venation.
2. Parallel venation.
[1 Mark each = 2 Marks]
3.
Ans:
Stomata are tiny pores or openings present mainly on the lower surface of the leaf. [1 Mark]
Their primary role is to help in the exchange of gases (taking in carbon dioxide and giving out oxygen) and in transpiration. [1 Mark]
4.
Ans:
Transpiration is the process by which a plant loses excess water in the form of water vapour through the stomata of its leaves. [2 Marks]
5.
Ans:
The petiole (leaf stalk) connects the leaf blade (lamina) to the stem of the plant. [1 Mark] It also helps in holding the leaf out to get maximum sunlight. [1 Mark]
SECTION B (15 Marks)
6.
Ans:
Three main functions of a leaf are:
1. Photosynthesis: Manufacturing food for the plant using sunlight.
2. Transpiration: Losing excess water as vapour to keep the plant cool.
3. Respiration / Exchange of gases: Taking in and giving out oxygen and carbon dioxide through stomata.
[1 Mark for each correct function = 3 Marks]
7.
Ans:
Simple Leaf: A leaf that has a single, undivided lamina. Example: Mango leaf, Peepal leaf. [1.5 Marks]
Compound Leaf: A leaf in which the lamina is divided into smaller units called leaflets. Example: Rose leaf, Neem leaf. [1.5 Marks]
8.
Ans:
Photosynthesis: It is the process by which green plants make their own food (glucose) using carbon dioxide and water in the presence of sunlight and chlorophyll. [1.5 Marks]
Raw Materials required: Carbon dioxide, Water, Sunlight, and Chlorophyll. [1.5 Marks]
9.
Ans:
Leaves are mostly green because they contain a green-coloured pigment. [1 Mark]
Pigment name: Chlorophyll. [1 Mark]
Specific function: Chlorophyll absorbs or traps solar energy (sunlight) which is needed to prepare food. [1 Mark]
10.
Ans:
(i) Cactus: Leaves are modified into spines. [0.5 Mark] Purpose: To reduce water loss by transpiration and protect the plant from grazing animals. [1 Mark]
(ii) Pea plant: Leaves are modified into tendrils. [0.5 Mark] Purpose: They are thread-like structures that coil around supports to help the weak plant climb up. [1 Mark]
SECTION C (25 Marks)
11.
Ans:
Diagram: A neat and large diagram of a simple leaf (like a peepal or hibiscus leaf) must be drawn.
Labels Required:
1. Lamina (Leaf blade) - the flat part.
2. Petiole - the stalk connecting leaf to stem.
3. Midrib - the thick central vein.
4. Veins - branches coming off the midrib.
5. Leaf base - the part attached to the stem.
[5 Marks Total: 2 Marks for correct and neat structure + 3 Marks for all 5 correct labels]
12.
Ans:
(a) Difference:
Reticulate VenationParallel Venation
Veins form a net-like or web-like design on both sides of the midrib.Veins run parallel to each other from the base to the tip of the leaf.

(b) Examples:

[1 Mark each for correct examples = 2 Marks]
13.
Ans:
(a) Photosynthesis: Plants take in carbon dioxide from the air and water from the soil to make sugar (food) in their green leaves. This happens only in the presence of sunlight trapped by chlorophyll. [1.5 Marks]
Word Equation: Carbon dioxide + Water → (in presence of Sunlight and Chlorophyll) → Glucose (Food) + Oxygen. [1.5 Marks]

(b) Importance: It is important because it provides food (directly or indirectly) for all animals and humans. It also releases Oxygen gas into the air, which is essential for the survival of all living beings. [2 Marks]
14.
Ans:
(a) Modification of leaves: In some plants, leaves change their normal shape and structure to perform special functions other than photosynthesis (like protection, support, or food storage). This is called modification. [2 Marks]

(b) Three ways of modification:
1. Leaf Spines: Leaves become sharp spines to protect against animals and save water. (Example: Cactus).
2. Leaf Tendrils: Leaves become thin and wiry to coil around objects for support to climb. (Example: Sweet pea / Pea plant).
3. Scale Leaves (Food Storage): Leaves become thick and fleshy to store food and water. (Example: Onion bulb).
[1 Mark each for function + example = 3 Marks]
15.
Ans:
Experiment to show Transpiration:
Materials required: A small well-watered potted plant, a transparent polythene bag, and thread. [1 Mark]
Steps:
1. Take the potted plant and enclose one of its leafy branches inside the transparent polythene bag. [1 Mark]
2. Tie the mouth of the bag tightly with the thread at the base of the branch.
3. Leave the plant out in bright sunlight for a few hours. [1 Mark]
Observation: After a few hours, tiny droplets of water will be seen on the inner surface of the polythene bag. [1 Mark]
Conclusion: These water droplets prove that leaves give out water vapour continuously through the process of transpiration. [1 Mark]
