SECTION A (15 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 1 Mark Each)
1.
Fill in the blanks with appropriate biological terms:
(a) The growth in length of plants is brought about by ___________ meristem.
(b) ___________ are the dead cells of phloem tissue.
(c) Tendons connect muscles to ___________ in the animal body.
(d) The matrix of bone is rich in calcium and ___________ minerals.
(e) The respiratory tract is lined with ___________ epithelial tissue to move mucus.
2.
State whether the following statements are True or False. If False, rewrite the correct statement:
(a) Meristematic cells have large vacuoles to store food.
(b) Sclerenchyma consists of dead cells with heavily thickened cell walls.
(c) Cardiac muscles are voluntary in nature and never get fatigued.
(d) Blood is considered a fluid connective tissue.
(e) Collenchyma cells have deposit of pectin at their corners.
3.
Give one word for the following:
(a) The tissue that conducts manufactured food from leaves to different plant parts.
(b) The protective tissue that covers the outer surface of animal organs.
(c) The structural and functional unit of the nervous system.
(d) The connective tissue that joins two bones together at a joint.
(e) A group of similar cells performing a specific, common function.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
4.
Differentiate between the following pairs based on the key instruction given in the brackets:
(a) Apical Meristem and Lateral Meristem (Location)
(b) Parenchyma and Sclerenchyma (Living/Dead state of cells)
(c) Tendon and Ligament (Function)
5.
Describe the three main types of muscle tissues found in the animal body based on their structure and control of movement.
6.
What is protective tissue in plants? Describe how epidermic layer and cork act as protective structures.
7.
Mention any three important functions of epithelial tissue in the animal body.
8.
Explain the composition of mammalian blood by naming its liquid matrix and the three types of blood cells present in it.
