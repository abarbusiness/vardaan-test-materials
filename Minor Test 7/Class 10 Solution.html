7.
Ans:
Double Circulation: Blood travels twice through the heart in one complete cycle of the body. It consists of Pulmonary circulation (heart to lungs and back) and Systemic circulation (heart to body parts and back). [1.5 Marks]
Necessity: It ensures strict separation of oxygenated and deoxygenated blood. This allows for a highly efficient supply of oxygen to the body, which is essential to meet the high energy demands of warm-blooded animals like humans (to maintain constant body temperature). [1.5 Marks]
8.
Ans:
(i) Salivary amylase: It acts on starch (complex carbohydrate) and breaks it down into simple sugars like maltose. [1 Mark]
(ii) Bile juice: It makes the acidic food alkaline for pancreatic enzymes to act and emulsifies large fat globules into smaller ones, increasing the efficiency of enzyme action. [1 Mark]
(iii) Villi: These are finger-like projections in the small intestine that increase the surface area for maximum absorption of digested food into the blood. [1 Mark]

SECTION C (15 Marks)
11.
Ans:
(a) Schematic Representation:
Students must draw a box/flowchart indicating:
- Lungs (alveoli) exchanging O2 and CO2.
- Pulmonary vein carrying oxygenated blood to Left Atrium → Left Ventricle → Aorta → Body organs.
- Exchange at body organs (O2 in, CO2 out).
- Vena cava carrying deoxygenated blood to Right Atrium → Right Ventricle → Pulmonary artery → Lungs.
[3 Marks: 1 for correct flow, 1 for correct labeling of vessels, 1 for heart chambers/organs]

(b) Plant Transport:
Components: Xylem and Phloem. [½ Mark]
Water transport (Xylem): Unidirectional (upwards from roots to leaves), relies on physical forces like transpiration pull and root pressure, transports water and minerals. [1 Mark]
Food transport (Phloem): Bidirectional (leaves to storage organs and growing parts), requires energy (ATP) to load sucrose, a process called translocation. [½ Mark]

Ans:

(b) Mechanism of Urine Formation:
1. Filtration: Blood is filtered under high pressure in the glomerulus (within Bowman's capsule). The filtrate contains glucose, amino acids, urea, salts, and a large amount of water. [1 Mark]
2. Selective Reabsorption: As the filtrate flows along the tubular part of the nephron, useful substances like glucose, amino acids, salts, and a major amount of water are selectively reabsorbed into the blood capillaries surrounding the tubule, leaving the nitrogenous waste (urine) behind. [1 Mark]
