Minor Test 07
Class: 7 CBSE Subject: Science (Biology - Nutrition) Time: 1 Hour Max. Marks: 50
General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.
  • Section A contains Objective Type questions (15 Marks).
  • Section B contains Short Answer Type questions (15 Marks).
  • Section C contains Long Answer & Diagrammatic Type questions (20 Marks).
  • Draw neat and labelled diagrams wherever necessary.
SECTION A (15 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 1 Mark Each)
1.
Fill in the blanks with appropriate biological terms:
(a) The mode of nutrition in which organisms make their own food from simple substances is called _________ nutrition.
(b) The largest gland in the human body is the _________.
(c) Amoeba digests its food in the _________.
(d) The partially digested food in the rumen of herbivores is called _________.
(e) _________ is a parasitic plant with a yellow, slender, and tubular stem.
2.
State whether the following statements are True or False. If False, rewrite the statement correctly by changing the underlined word(s):
(a) Digestion of starch starts in the stomach.
(b) The gall bladder temporarily stores bile juice.
(c) The pitcher plant is an example of an autotroph only.
(d) Grass-eating animals (ruminants) have a four-chambered stomach.
(e) Assimilation is the process of removal of undigested food from the body.
3.
Give one word or a brief biological term for the following:
(a) The false feet of Amoeba used for movement and capturing food.
(b) The process of taking food into the body.
(c) The finger-like outgrowths present on the inner walls of the small intestine.
(d) A symbiotic association between an alga and a fungus.
(e) The acid secreted by the gastric glands in the human stomach.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
4.
Differentiate between Autotrophic and Heterotrophic modes of nutrition. Give one example of each.
5.
What are villi? State their location and exactly how they help in the process of digestion.
6.
Briefly describe the process of feeding and digestion in Amoeba.
7.
Why do farmers frequently grow leguminous crops (like peas or gram) in their fields? Explain the biological relationship involved.
8.
What is meant by 'rumination'? Name any two animals that exhibit this process.
SECTION C (20 Marks)(Long Answer & Diagrammatic Type - 5 Marks Each)
9.
(a) Draw a neat and well-labelled diagram of the human digestive system. (3 Marks)
(b) State the specific roles of the liver and the pancreas in the digestion of food. (2 Marks)
10.
(a) Describe the four different types of heterotrophic nutrition found in plants, providing one example for each type. (4 Marks)
(b) Why do insectivorous plants need to trap and digest insects if they are green and can perform photosynthesis? (1 Mark)
11.
Nutrition in animals is a complex process. Name and sequentially explain the five major steps involved in the process of animal nutrition (from taking in food to throwing out waste). (5 Marks)
12.
(a) Discuss the role of the mouth (buccal cavity) in human digestion. Mention the four types of teeth found in an adult human. (3 Marks)
(b) Explain the structural mechanism by which a pitcher plant catches its prey. (2 Marks)
