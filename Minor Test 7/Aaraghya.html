SECTION A (15 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 1 Mark Each)
1.
Fill in the blanks with appropriate biological terms:
(a) The tissue responsible for vertical growth at the root and shoot tips is _________ meristem.
(b) The fluid connective tissue in the human body is _________.
(c) _________ is a simple permanent plant tissue with heavily thickened, dead cells.
(d) The connective tissue that connects bone to bone at a joint is called a _________.
(e) Cardiac muscle tissue is found exclusively in the _________.
2.
State whether the following statements are True or False. If False, rewrite the statement correctly by changing the underlined word(s):
(a) Phloem conducts water and minerals from the roots to the leaves.
(b) Epithelial tissue forms the protective outer covering of the animal body and its internal organs.
(c) Tendons connect muscles to bones.
(d) Parenchyma cells are dead and provide rigid mechanical support.
(e) Striated muscles are involuntary in nature.
3.
Give one word or a brief biological term for the following:
(a) The complex permanent tissue that conducts manufactured food in plants.
(b) The structural and functional unit of nervous tissue.
(c) The tissue that stores fat and insulates the animal body.
(d) The only living cells present in the xylem tissue.
(e) The long, thread-like extension of a nerve cell that carries messages away from the cell body.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)

(a) Xylem and Phloem (Materials transported)

State three main characteristics of meristematic tissues in plants.
6.
Name the three types of muscular tissue found in animals. Mention one unique feature of cardiac muscles.
7.
Name the fluid matrix of blood and list its three main types of cellular components. State one function of Red Blood Cells.
8.
