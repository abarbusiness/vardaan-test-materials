General Instructions:
  • All questions are compulsory.
  • The paper is divided into three sections: A, B, and C.

  • Section B contains Short Answer Type questions (15 Marks).
  • Section C contains Long Answer & Diagrammatic Type questions (20 Marks).
  • Draw neat, labelled diagrams wherever necessary.
SECTION A (15 Marks)(Attempt all questions - 1 Mark Each)
1.
Fill in the blanks with appropriate biological terms:
(a) The exudation of water in the form of liquid droplets along the margins of leaves is called ___________.
(b) The fundamental structural and functional unit of the nervous system is the ___________.
(c) The tissue phloem conducts manufactured food through its non-nucleated living elements called ___________.
(d) The part of the hindbrain responsible for coordinating muscular activity and body balance is the ___________.
(e) The microscopic gap between the axon terminal of one neuron and the dendrites of the next neuron is called a ___________.
2.
State whether the following statements are True or False. If False, rewrite the statement correctly by changing the underlined word(s):
(a) Transpiration has no cooling effect on the plant body.
(b) Sensory nerves carry sensory impulses away from the central nervous system to the effector organs.
(c) Active transport is the movement of molecules from lower to higher concentration using ATP energy.
(d) Medulla oblongata is the seat of intelligence, memory, reasoning, and voluntary actions.
(e) Water-conducting channels in xylem, like vessels and tracheids, consist of living cells.
3.
Give one word or a brief biological term for the following:
(a) The movement of water molecules from a region of higher water concentration to lower water concentration across a semi-permeable membrane.
(b) An immediate, automatic, and involuntary response of an effector to a specific stimulus.
(c) The insulating, lipid-rich sheath wrapping around the axon of a neuron.
(d) The upward movement of water and dissolved minerals from roots up to the top leaves of tall plants.
(e) The three protective membranes wrapping around and cushioning the brain and spinal cord.
SECTION B (15 Marks)(Short Answer Type - 3 Marks Each)
4.
Differentiate between the following pairs based on the key instruction given in the brackets:
(a) Diffusion and Osmosis (Requirement of a membrane)
(b) Cerebrum and Cerebellum (Primary function)
(c) Xylem and Phloem (Direction of transport)
5.
What is transpiration pull? Explain how it plays a central role in conducting water to the uppermost canopy of exceptionally tall trees.
6.
Name the three functional categories of neurons present in the nervous system. Briefly describe the role of each.
7.
Trace the sequential pathway of a nerve impulse during a reflex action when a person accidentally touches a hot object. State this pathway in the form of a clear flow diagram.
8.
State three practical structural adaptations of plant root hairs that make them exceptionally efficient at absorbing soil water.
SECTION C (20 Marks)(Long Answer & Diagrammatic Type - 5 Marks Each)
9.
(a) Draw a neat, well-labelled diagram of a typical myelinated motor neuron. (3 Marks)
(b) State the exact physiological function of the following parts of a neuron: (i) Dendrites, (ii) Axon. (2 Marks)
10.
(a) Name the three primary anatomical subdivisions of the human brain. (1.5 Marks)
(b) Mention the respective functions of (i) Cerebrum, (ii) Cerebellum, and (iii) Medulla Oblongata. (3 Marks)
(c) State the location of the spinal cord in the human skeleton. (0.5 Marks)
11.
(a) Define Transpiration. List three important external factors that govern the rate of transpiration in plants. (3 Marks)
(b) Why do plants look wilted and droop down during hot, dry summer afternoons even if the soil has some moisture? Explain. (2 Marks)
12.
(a) Differentiate between Voluntary Actions and Involuntary Actions, providing one suitable biological example of each. (3 Marks)
(b) Define a 'Stimulus' and a 'Receptor'. Name the specialized receptor responsible for the sensation of (i) hearing, and (ii) vision. (2 Marks)
