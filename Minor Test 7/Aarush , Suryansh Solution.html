Ans:
Water is absorbed by the roots from the soil. The vascular tissue called xylem forms a continuous network of tubes connecting roots to the leaves through the stem. [1.5 Marks]
Transpiration from the leaves creates a suction pull, which pulls water upwards to great heights in tall trees. [1.5 Marks]
8.
Ans:
Menstruation: If the egg released by the ovary is not fertilized, the thickened inner lining of the uterus, along with its blood vessels, sheds off. [1.5 Marks]
This causes bleeding in females which is called menstruation. It occurs once in about 28 to 30 days. [1.5 Marks]
9.
Ans:
Plants need a transport system because:
1. Leaves need a constant supply of water and minerals (absorbed by roots) to carry out photosynthesis. [1.5 Marks]
2. The food prepared by the leaves must be transported to all other parts of the plant (like roots, stems, and fruits) for energy, growth, and storage. [1.5 Marks]
10.
Ans:
The pituitary gland is an endocrine gland attached to the brain. Its role during adolescence is crucial as it secretes hormones that control the production of sex hormones (testosterone from testes and estrogen from ovaries). [2 Marks]
It also secretes growth hormone which is responsible for the rapid physical growth during this period. [1 Mark]

Ans:
(a) Stomata: They are tiny pores present on the surface of the leaves. [1 Mark]

(b) Diagram: A neat diagram of a stomatal pore surrounded by two bean-shaped guard cells and epidermal cells. [2 Marks for diagram and labels]
Functions:
1. Exchange of gases (taking in CO2 and releasing O2 during photosynthesis). [1 Mark]
2. Transpiration (loss of excess water vapour to cool the plant). [1 Mark]
14.
Ans:
(a) Sex Determination: All human beings have 23 pairs of chromosomes. Two of these are sex chromosomes (X and Y). Females have two X chromosomes (XX), while males have one X and one Y chromosome (XY). [1 Mark]
When a sperm carrying an X chromosome fertilizes an egg (which always has an X chromosome), the zygote (XX) develops into a female child. [1 Mark]
If a sperm carrying a Y chromosome fertilizes an egg, the zygote (XY) develops into a male child. [1 Mark]

(b) Scientific reasoning: The mother always contributes an X chromosome to the child. It is the father's sperm (whether it carries an X or a Y chromosome) that determines the sex of the baby. Therefore, it is scientifically completely incorrect to blame the mother. [2 Marks]

Ans:
(a) Transport of Food: The food synthesized by the leaves through photosynthesis must reach all the cells of the plant for energy and growth. This transport is carried out by the vascular tissue called phloem. [2 Marks]
The specific process of transport of food is called Translocation. [1 Mark]
